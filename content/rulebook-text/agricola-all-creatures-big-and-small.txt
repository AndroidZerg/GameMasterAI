## OVERVIEW
Agricola: All Creatures Big and Small is a streamlined 2-player worker-placement game set on a small farm. Players compete to build the most valuable farm by collecting and breeding animals (sheep, pigs, cows, and horses), constructing buildings, and expanding their farmland with fences and pastures. It distills the core Agricola experience into a fast, focused 2-player game without the feeding mechanic.

## COMPONENTS
- 2 farm boards (3x2 grid of spaces each, expandable)
- 1 action board (central board with worker placement spaces)
- 3 workers per player (small farmer meeples)
- Animal tokens: sheep (white), pigs (black), cows (brown), horses (tan/beige)
- Fence pieces (small wooden sticks)
- Building tiles (special buildings with unique abilities)
- Feeding troughs (double animal capacity of a pasture)
- Border expansions (3 additional farm extensions per player — expand your grid)
- Resource tokens (wood, stone, reed)
- Starting player marker
- Score pad

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a **farm board** (3 columns x 2 rows = 6 spaces). Your farm starts with a cottage on one space.
2. Place the **action board** between both players with all action spaces visible.
3. Each player takes **3 workers** in their color.
4. Sort building tiles and place them near the action board. Some buildings are available from the start; others appear in later rounds.
5. Place all animal tokens, fences, and troughs in a general supply.
6. Set out resource tokens (wood, stone, reed) in supply.
7. Shuffle and deal round-specific action spaces (some are added each round, expanding options).
8. Determine first player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over **8 rounds**. Each round:

### Phase 1: REPLENISH
- Add resources/animals to accumulation spaces on the action board. These spaces gain more goods each round they go unclaimed (they build up over time).
- Reveal any new action spaces for this round (some become available in rounds 2-8).

### Phase 2: WORK (Worker Placement)
Players alternate placing **one worker at a time** on action spaces until all workers (3 each = 6 total placements) are placed:
- Each action space can hold only **one worker** (blocked once taken).
- You MUST place all 3 workers each round.

### Action Spaces Include:

1. **RESOURCE ACCUMULATION SPACES**: Gain wood, stone, or reed (these accumulate each round — unclaimed spaces grow in value).

2. **ANIMAL ACCUMULATION SPACES**: Gain sheep, pigs, cows, or horses (also accumulate).

3. **FENCES**: Take a number of fences and build pastures on your farm:
   - Place fences around empty farm spaces to create **enclosed pastures**.
   - Each enclosed pasture can hold **2 animals of the same type**.
   - A **feeding trough** in a pasture **doubles** its capacity (2 becomes 4 per space).
   - Multiple fenced spaces can be connected into one **large pasture** (capacity = 2 per space in the pasture, doubled with a trough).
   - Animals of only ONE type per pasture.

4. **BUILD**: Construct special buildings from the available supply:
   - Pay the building cost (combination of wood, stone, reed).
   - Place the building tile on an empty space on your farm.
   - Buildings provide various benefits: extra animal capacity, VP bonuses, resource conversion, etc.
   - Some buildings can hold specific animal types (e.g., a Shelter can hold 1 animal without fencing).

5. **EXPAND**: Take a farm **border expansion** tile and add it to your farm board, extending your grid by additional spaces. Each player has 3 expansions available. These give more room for pastures and buildings.

6. **STARTING PLAYER + BONUS**: Take the starting player marker for next round, plus a small bonus (resource or animal).

7. **SPECIAL ACTIONS**: Various spaces for trading, getting troughs, gaining specific animal/resource combos.

### Animal Housing Rules (CRITICAL):
Animals MUST have a valid place to live on your farm. If you cannot house an animal, you CANNOT take it (or must release excess animals back to supply).

Valid animal housing:
- **Fenced pasture**: 2 animals of one type per fenced space (4 with trough).
- **Buildings with animal capacity**: Some buildings hold specific animals.
- **Your cottage**: Can hold exactly **1 animal** of any type (your farmhouse pet).
- **Open (unfenced) farm spaces**: Can hold **1 animal each** (free-ranging), but only if the space has no building or fencing.

### Phase 3: BREEDING (at the end of each round, starting from round 2 or 3 — verify exact start)
- If you have **2 or more animals of the same type**, you gain **1 additional animal** of that type (they breed).
- Breeding happens for EACH animal type you have 2+ of.
- You must have room to house the new animal or it is returned to the supply.
- Breeding is a major engine — getting pairs of animals early leads to exponential growth.

### Phase 4: RETURN WORKERS
All workers return to their owners. Begin the next round.

## SCORING / WINNING
After **8 rounds**, final scoring:

**Animal Scoring (the primary score source):**
| Animals | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8+ |
|---------|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sheep** | -3 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |
| **Pigs** | -3 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |
| **Cows** | -3 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |
| **Horses** | -3 | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 | 8 |

- Each animal type you have **0** of = **-3 points** (harsh penalty for neglecting a type).
- Each animal of a type = 1 VP (roughly — scoring is approximately 1 per animal with penalties for 0).

**Building Scoring:**
- Many buildings have VP values printed on them.
- Some buildings provide bonus VP based on conditions (e.g., "2 VP per horse").

**Farm Extension:**
- Each unused (empty, unfenced, no building) farm space = **-3 VP** penalty (verify exact penalty).

**Additional Bonuses:**
- Feeding troughs and fully developed farm areas contribute to score.

**Highest total VP wins.** Tiebreaker: most animals total.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Get pairs of animals EARLY.** Breeding every round is how you fill your farm. A pair of sheep in round 2 can become 5+ sheep by round 8 through natural breeding.
- **You MUST have housing for all animals.** Plan your fencing and building BEFORE collecting animals. Nothing is worse than taking 4 cows you can't house.
- **Avoid -3 penalties.** Having at least 1 of each animal type eliminates 12 points of penalties (4 x -3). Even 1 of each is worth +1 instead of -3, a 4-point swing per type.
- **Feeding troughs are incredibly efficient.** A trough doubles a pasture's capacity. A 2-space pasture with a trough holds 8 animals instead of 4.
- **Farm expansions are essential.** Without expanding, you only have 6 spaces. Expanding gives you room for more pastures and buildings.
- **Buildings can be more point-efficient than raw animals.** A well-chosen building that provides 4+ VP and holds animals is extremely valuable.
- **Block your opponent.** With only 2 players, taking a space your opponent needs is just as important as taking one you need.
- **Accumulation spaces grow in value.** Sometimes it's worth letting a space accumulate for a round to get a bigger payout.
- **Round 8 animals don't breed.** Animals acquired in the last round have no breeding opportunity. Plan your final-round acquisitions accordingly.
- **Your cottage holds 1 animal.** Don't forget this free housing for a single creature — it can avoid a -3 penalty.
- **The game is tight at 8 rounds.** Every worker placement matters. Wasted turns are very costly.
