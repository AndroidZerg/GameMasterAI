## OVERVIEW
Chaos in the Old World is an asymmetric area control game set in the Warhammer Fantasy universe. Each player takes on the role of one of four Ruinous Powers (Chaos Gods) — Khorne, Nurgle, Tzeentch, or Slaanesh — competing to corrupt and dominate the Old World. Each god has unique abilities, units, upgrade paths, and victory conditions, creating a deeply asymmetric experience.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 game board (map of the Old World divided into regions)
- 4 god-specific sets of:
  - Cultist figures (3-4 per god)
  - Warrior figures (varying counts)
  - Greater Daemon figure (1 per god)
  - Chaos cards (unique deck of ~30 cards per god)
  - Upgrade cards (unique power progression)
  - Threat dial (tracks advancement toward dial victory)
  - Power/cost reference sheet
- Old World cards (event deck determining regional effects)
- Corruption tokens
- Victory point tokens
- Domination tokens
- Ruination tokens
- Warpstone tokens
- Hero tokens

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center. The board shows the Old World divided into 9 regions.
2. Each player chooses a Chaos God and takes ALL corresponding components: figures, Chaos card deck, upgrade cards, threat dial, and reference sheet.
3. Shuffle each player's Chaos card deck. Draw a starting hand of the specified size (varies slightly by god — VERIFY; typically 5-7 cards).
4. Set each god's threat dial to the starting position.
5. Shuffle the Old World card deck and place it face down.
6. Place corruption tokens, VP tokens, and other markers in a general supply.
7. Draw and resolve the first Old World card (it places Warpstone, heroes, and/or events in regions).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
Each round consists of these phases in order:

### 1. Old World Phase
- Draw the top Old World card and resolve it.
- These cards place Warpstone tokens in specific regions (making them more attractive), spawn Heroes (which fight Chaos forces), add peasant tokens (population), or trigger special events.
- If the Old World deck runs out, the game ends immediately.

### 2. Draw Phase
- Each player draws Chaos cards up to their hand limit (typically starting at 5; can be increased via upgrades).
- If your draw deck is empty, shuffle your discard to form a new deck.

### 3. Summoning Phase
- Starting with the player whose god is first in turn order (printed on the board: Khorne, Nurgle, Tzeentch, Slaanesh), each player takes a turn to summon figures and play Chaos cards.
- **Summoning**: Place cultists, warriors, or your greater daemon on the board in any region. Each figure has a Power Point cost. You have a limited pool of Power Points each round (typically 6-8 depending on upgrades).
- **Playing Chaos Cards**: Play Chaos cards from your hand by paying their Power Point cost. Cards provide combat bonuses, corruption, movement, or special effects.
- You may mix summoning and card playing freely within your Power Point budget.
- **Restrictions**: You cannot summon into regions where a Noble Hero is present (heroes block summoning). You must have a cultist in a region to summon warriors/daemons there (VERIFY this rule).

### 4. Battle Phase
- Resolve combat in each region that contains figures from 2+ different gods.
- Combat is simultaneous and resolved region by region.
- **Combat procedure**: Each figure rolls dice based on its combat value (Cultists = 0, Warriors = 1, Greater Daemons = 2-3 depending on god). Hits are scored on results of 4+ (VERIFY threshold).
- The player with figures in the region assigns hits to enemy figures. Cultists die to 1 hit, warriors to 2 hits, greater daemons to more.
- Heroes in the region also fight — they attack all Chaos players' figures.
- Killed figures return to the player's supply and can be resummoned in future rounds.

### 5. Corruption Phase
- Each **cultist** on the board places 1 corruption token in its region.
- Some Chaos cards or abilities add additional corruption.
- **Ruination check**: If any region has 12+ corruption tokens, it is RUINATED. When a region ruinates:
  - The player with the most corruption tokens in that region scores VP and advances their threat dial.
  - Second place also scores (lesser amount).
  - The region is devastated — all corruption tokens are removed, and the region may become less valuable or impassable.
  - Ruinating regions is one of the primary ways to score VP and advance toward dial victory.

### 6. Domination Phase
- Check each region for domination. The player with the highest "domination value" in a region dominates it.
- Domination value = number of figures + relevant bonuses (warriors and daemons count for more).
- The dominating player scores VP equal to the region's current VP value.
- This phase rewards military presence even without corruption.

### 7. End Phase / Threat Dial Check
- Check if any player has advanced their threat dial to the final tick. If so, they win via dial victory.
- Check if the VP threshold has been reached.
- Advance the round marker.

## THE FOUR CHAOS GODS

### Khorne (Blood God)
- **Strength**: Combat. Khorne excels at killing enemy figures.
- **Weakness**: Has NO cultists in the base game — cannot place corruption normally. Must kill to advance.
- **Dial Advancement**: Advances by killing enemy figures in battle.
- **Strategy**: Khorne wants constant warfare. Place warriors and the Bloodthirster where enemies cluster.

### Nurgle (Plague Lord)
- **Strength**: Corruption. Nurgle spreads corruption faster than anyone with special abilities that add extra corruption tokens.
- **Dial Advancement**: Advances by having 2+ corruption tokens in populous (high-population) regions.
- **Strategy**: Target heavily populated regions and pile on corruption. Nurgle wins by ruinating the most valuable areas.

### Tzeentch (Changer of Ways)
- **Strength**: Magic and manipulation. Tzeentch has powerful Chaos cards and abilities that warp the game state.
- **Dial Advancement**: Advances by having Warpstone tokens in regions where Tzeentch has presence.
- **Strategy**: Chase Warpstone and use superior card abilities to manipulate board position.

### Slaanesh (Prince of Pleasure)
- **Strength**: Seduction and targeted corruption. Slaanesh can place corruption on enemy figures and manipulate opponents' heroes.
- **Dial Advancement**: Advances by having corruption tokens in regions with enemy figures (corrupting other gods' followers).
- **Strategy**: Follow other players' forces and corrupt where they are strong.

## SCORING / WINNING
There are three ways to win:
1. **Dial Victory**: Advance your threat dial to the final position. This is the most thematic win — each god advances by fulfilling their unique condition. First player to complete their dial wins immediately.
2. **Victory Points**: If no one achieves dial victory, the player with the most VP at game end wins. VP is earned from domination and ruination.
3. **Game End Trigger**: The game ends when the Old World deck runs out or when a VP threshold is reached, and the highest VP total wins.

Dial victory takes priority — if achieved, VP totals are irrelevant.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Asymmetry is everything**: Each god plays completely differently. Learn your god's unique path to victory.
- **Khorne is the kingmaker**: Khorne's combat ability can destroy any player's position but needs to kill efficiently to advance its own dial.
- **Nurgle should target high-population regions**: That is how Nurgle advances its dial. Don't spread too thin.
- **Tzeentch's cards are overpowered by design**: The god of magic should leverage card advantage.
- **Slaanesh needs enemies nearby**: Follow the herd. Corrupt where others have committed forces.
- **Watch the dials**: If any player is close to completing their dial, the table must cooperate to stop them.
- **Ruination is a double-edged sword**: It scores big points but removes the region from future play.
- **Heroes are a nuisance**: They block summoning and fight in combat. Don't ignore them.
- **3-player game**: One god is not used, which significantly changes balance. Khorne is often the recommended exclusion at 3 players (VERIFY).
- **The Old World deck is a timer**: The game will end when it runs out. Plan accordingly.
