# Game: Small World
# Publisher: Days of Wonder
# Players: 2-5 | Time: 40-80 min | Weight: 2.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Small World is an area control game where players use fantasy races (Elves, Dwarves, Skeletons, Amazons, etc.) combined with special power badges to conquer regions on a map that is intentionally too small for everyone. When your active race runs out of steam, you put them into decline and pick a new race/power combo to continue your conquest. The game is light-hearted, fast-paced, and highly interactive.

## COMPONENTS
- Double-sided game boards (different maps for different player counts: 2, 3, 4, or 5 players)
- 14 Fantasy Race banners with matching race tokens (Amazons, Dwarves, Elves, Ghouls, Giants, Halflings, Humans, Orcs, Ratmen, Skeletons, Sorcerers, Tritons, Trolls, Wizards)
- 20 Special Power badges (Alchemist, Berserk, Bivouacking, Commando, Diplomat, Dragon Master, Flying, Forest, Fortified, Heroic, Hill, Merchant, Mounted, Pillaging, Seafaring, Spirit, Stout, Swamp, Underworld, Wealthy)
- Victory coins (values of 1, 3, 5, 10)
- Various tokens: Mountains, Troll Lairs, Fortresses, Encampments, Holes-in-the-Ground, Heroes, Dragon
- Lost Tribe tokens (neutral defenders in setup)
- Game turn track and marker
- Reinforcement die

## SETUP
1. Select the correct board side based on player count (2-player, 3-player, 4-player, or 5-player map).
2. Place Lost Tribe tokens on the regions marked with a Lost Tribe symbol.
3. Place Mountain tokens on regions marked with a mountain.
4. Shuffle the Race banners and deal 5 face-up in a column. Shuffle the Special Power badges and pair one with each Race banner (place a badge next to each race). Stack remaining races and powers face-down.
5. Place the Game Turn marker on space 1 of the turn track (number of turns varies by player count: 2P = 10 turns, 3P = 10, 4P = 9, 5P = 8).
6. Each player receives a set number of victory coins (kept hidden from other players throughout the game).
7. Place 1 victory coin on each race/power combo beyond the first (the 2nd combo gets 1 coin, the 3rd gets 2, etc. — this accumulates to make less desirable combos cheaper/sweeter).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### First Turn — Pick a Race/Power Combo
On your first turn, you must select a Race + Power combo from the visible column:
- The first combo in the column is free. Each subsequent combo costs 1 victory coin (placed on each skipped combo). These coins are collected by whoever eventually picks that combo.
- The combo tells you how many Race tokens you receive (race base + power bonus = total tokens).
- After a combo is picked, slide remaining combos down and reveal a new one at the top.

### Subsequent Turns — Conquer or Decline

**Option A: Conquer with your active race**
1. **Pick up tokens**: You may pick up all but 1 race token from each region you currently occupy (leave at least 1 behind to maintain control).
2. **Conquer new regions**: To conquer a region, you need 2 tokens + 1 for each token already there (enemy race tokens, Lost Tribes, Mountains, etc.). Mountains always require +1 regardless. Encampments, Fortresses, and Troll Lairs also add +1 each.
   - You must conquer from a region you control (adjacent) OR from the border of the map (first conquest of a new territory can be any border/coastal region).
   - Conquered enemy tokens: For each enemy token in a conquered region, 1 is returned to the owner (they choose which; remaining are removed permanently). Lost Tribe tokens are removed from the game.
3. **Final Conquest (Reinforcement Die)**: If you don't have enough tokens for one last conquest, you may attempt it by rolling the Reinforcement Die. The die shows 0, 1, 2, or 3 (with blanks = 0). Add the die result to your remaining tokens. If the total meets or exceeds the conquest requirement, you succeed. If not, the tokens stay where they were. You can only roll once per turn.
4. **Redeploy**: After all conquests, redistribute your race tokens among your controlled regions (minimum 1 per region). You can stack multiple tokens in key defensive positions.

**Option B: Put your race In Decline**
Instead of conquering, you may put your active race "In Decline":
1. Flip all your active race tokens to their declined (grayed-out) side.
2. Remove all but 1 token from each region you control with that race.
3. Discard the Special Power badge (unless it specifically says it persists in decline, like Spirit).
4. Your turn ends. You do NOT pick a new race this turn. On your NEXT turn, you will pick a new Race/Power combo and begin conquering with them.
5. You can only have one In Decline race at a time. If you already had an In Decline race, all those tokens are removed first.

### Scoring (End of Each Turn)
At the end of your turn, score 1 victory coin for EACH region you control (active race + declined race). Some powers and races grant bonus coins for specific conditions (e.g., Humans get +1 per Farmland region, Merchant gives +1 per occupied region).

Coins are kept face-down and secret until end of game.

## SCORING / WINNING
- After the last turn (determined by the turn track), all players reveal their victory coins.
- The player with the most total coin value wins.
- Ties broken by the player who last went In Decline (or has the most active race tokens, per specific tie-breaker rules).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Go In Decline at the right time**: This is the key strategic decision. Don't wait until your race is completely spent — go In Decline while you still hold several regions so those declined tokens keep scoring each turn.
- **Timing matters**: Going In Decline costs you a full turn of conquering. Think about whether the new combo will earn enough to compensate.
- **The board is intentionally cramped**: You WILL be conquering other players' territory. Don't take it personally. Attack where it's efficient, not personal.
- **Coins on skipped combos accumulate**: Sometimes an unpopular combo builds up 4-5 coins, making it worth grabbing for the coins alone.
- **Mountains are permanent**: Mountain tokens stay forever. Regions with mountains always cost +1 to conquer. They're great to hold defensively.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting to leave at least 1 token in each region you want to keep. If you pick up ALL tokens from a region, you abandon it.
- **Common mistake**: Conquering non-adjacent regions (unless your power/race allows it, like Flying).
- **Declined races are vulnerable**: They have only 1 token per region and don't get to redeploy. Opponents will pick them off. Factor this into your timing.
- **Hide your coins**: Don't let others see how many coins you have. Players will gang up on the leader.
- **Some race/power combos are much stronger than others**: Read both the race ability and the power ability carefully. Synergies (like Mounted + Amazons for massive conquering power) can be game-winning.
- **The Reinforcement Die is unreliable**: Don't count on rolling high. Plan conquests so the die roll is a bonus, not a necessity.
