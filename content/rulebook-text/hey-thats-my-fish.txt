# Game: Hey, That's My Fish!
# Publisher: Fantasy Flight Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 20 min | Weight: 1.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Hey, That's My Fish! is an abstract strategy game where players control teams of penguins on a board of hexagonal ice floe tiles. Each tile displays 1, 2, or 3 fish. Penguins move in straight lines across the ice, collecting the tile they leave behind. As tiles are removed, the ice field shrinks and fragments, trapping penguins and cutting off opponents. The player who collects the most fish wins.

## COMPONENTS
- 60 hexagonal ice floe tiles:
  - 30 tiles with 1 fish
  - 20 tiles with 2 fish
  - 10 tiles with 3 fish
- 16 penguin figures (4 per color: red, blue, green, yellow)

## SETUP
1. Shuffle all 60 hex tiles face-down and arrange them in a roughly rectangular grid of alternating rows (8 tiles per row, staggered). All tiles are then flipped face-up.
2. The number of penguins per player depends on player count:
   - 2 players: 4 penguins each
   - 3 players: 3 penguins each
   - 4 players: 2 penguins each
3. Starting with the first player and going clockwise, each player places ONE penguin on any tile showing exactly 1 fish. Continue placing one penguin at a time in turn order until all penguins are placed.
4. The player who placed first takes the first move.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn, you MUST do the following (in order):
1. **Move one penguin**: Choose one of your penguins and move it in a straight line along one of the 6 hex directions. The penguin may move any number of tiles in that direction, but:
   - It CANNOT pass through or land on a tile occupied by another penguin.
   - It CANNOT pass over empty spaces (gaps where tiles have been removed).
   - It MUST stop before any obstacle (penguin, gap, or board edge).

2. **Collect the tile**: After moving, pick up the tile the penguin was PREVIOUSLY standing on (not the destination tile). This tile and its fish are added to your score pile.

The space left behind becomes a gap in the ice field that no penguin can cross.

**If a penguin cannot move** (surrounded by gaps or penguins on all sides), that penguin is removed from the board. The player collects the tile that penguin was standing on and adds it to their score pile.

The game continues clockwise until no penguins on the board can make a legal move.

## SCORING / WINNING
- Each player counts the total number of fish on all tiles they collected.
- The player with the most fish wins.
- In case of a tie, the player with the most tiles wins. If still tied, players share the victory.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Penguins MUST be placed on 1-fish tiles during setup — this is mandatory, not optional.
- Movement is always in a straight line along one of the 6 hex directions — no turning mid-move.
- You collect the tile you LEAVE, not the tile you LAND on. The tile under your penguin is collected when you move away or when you are removed for being stuck.
- The board shrinks rapidly. By mid-game, the ice field usually fragments into isolated islands.
- A key strategy is isolating your penguin on a large island of high-value tiles with no opponents.
- Cutting off opponents by creating gaps is extremely powerful — position your penguins to fragment the board advantageously.
- In 2-player games with 4 penguins each, the game is deeply strategic. With more players, there is more chaos.
- Early game: focus on positioning near clusters of 3-fish and 2-fish tiles.
- Mid game: start creating gaps to isolate sections for yourself.
- Late game: maximize collection from your isolated islands.
- Despite its cute theme, this game has serious strategic depth and is used in some board game competitions.
