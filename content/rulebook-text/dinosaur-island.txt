# Game: Dinosaur Island
# Publisher: Pandasaurus Games
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 60-120 min | Weight: 2.9/5

## OVERVIEW
Dinosaur Island is a worker placement and engine-building game where players compete to build the most exciting (and dangerous) dinosaur theme park. You extract DNA from amber, combine it to create dinosaurs, build attractions and facilities, hire specialists, and manage the delicate balance between excitement (which draws visitors) and security (which keeps them alive). Inspired by a certain famous dinosaur franchise, the game features vibrant 90s-neon art and a satisfying mix of tactical decisions.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Research Center board (shared — for DNA acquisition and research)
- 1 Marketplace board (shared — for hiring specialists and buying attractions/upgrades)
- 4 Player park boards (personal — your theme park layout)
- 4 Player lab boards (personal — DNA storage and dinosaur creation)
- DNA dice (custom dice in 3 colors: basic, advanced, rare)
- DNA tokens (in multiple colors matching dinosaur types: herbivore green, small carnivore yellow, large carnivore red, plus basic/amber)
- Dinosaur recipe cards (showing DNA requirements to create each species)
- Attraction tiles (rides, food stands, merchandise shops)
- Paddock tiles (to house dinosaurs)
- Specialist cards (workers with special abilities)
- Worker meeples (scientists, in player colors)
- Visitor meeples (generic — drawn from a bag)
- Threat tokens (track danger level)
- Excitement tokens (track park appeal)
- Security upgrades
- Money tokens
- Objective cards (end-game and round-based)
- Round tracker
- Plot twist cards (optional variant for added chaos)
- Solo mode components

## SETUP
1. Place the Research Center and Marketplace boards in shared reach.
2. Each player takes a personal Park board and Lab board. Place starting DNA tokens on your lab.
3. Roll the DNA dice and place them on the Research Center board. These represent available DNA for the round.
4. Shuffle and deal Specialist cards to the Marketplace (number depends on player count).
5. Place Attraction tiles, Paddock tiles, and upgrades in the Marketplace supply.
6. Each player starts with: a set amount of money (varies by turn order), a starting number of workers/scientists, and one basic paddock.
7. Deal each player a starting hand of Dinosaur recipe cards (or lay them out in a public market — varies by game length setting).
8. Set each player's Excitement at 0 and Threat at 0 on their park board.
9. Choose a game length: Short (objective-based end), Medium, or Long. Set the round tracker accordingly.
10. Deal Objective cards (private end-game goals and/or public objectives).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each round consists of 5 phases:

### Phase 1: Research
- Roll all DNA dice and place them on the Research Center.
- Players take turns (in player order) placing scientists (workers) on the Research Center to:
  - **Acquire DNA dice**: Take a die from the Research Center. You gain the DNA type and amount shown on the die's face. Store DNA on your Lab board.
  - **Increase DNA storage capacity**: Some spaces let you expand how much DNA you can hold.
  - **Acquire Dinosaur recipes**: Take recipe cards that tell you what DNA combinations are needed to create specific dinosaurs.
  - **Pass**: You may pass and save workers for later phases.
- Turn order matters: better dice (showing rare DNA or high amounts) are claimed first.
- Worker placement is exclusive — each space can only hold one worker per round.

### Phase 2: Market
- Players take turns placing workers on the Marketplace board to:
  - **Hire Specialists**: Pay money to recruit Specialist cards. Specialists provide ongoing abilities, extra workers, resource discounts, or end-game VP.
  - **Buy Attractions**: Purchase attraction tiles (rides, food courts, shops) to place in your park. Attractions increase excitement and may generate income.
  - **Buy Paddocks**: Purchase larger or specialized paddock tiles for housing more/bigger dinosaurs.
  - **Buy Security Upgrades**: Increase your park's security level to mitigate the threat posed by dangerous dinosaurs.
- Again, worker placement is exclusive.

### Phase 3: Build (Simultaneous)
All players simultaneously manage their parks:

**A. Create Dinosaurs**
- Spend the required DNA tokens (as shown on a recipe card) to create a dinosaur.
- Place the dinosaur token/card in one of your paddocks.
- Each dinosaur increases your park's **Excitement** (visitors want to see dinosaurs!) but also increases **Threat** (dinosaurs are dangerous).
  - Herbivores: Low excitement, low threat.
  - Small Carnivores: Medium excitement, medium threat.
  - Large Carnivores: High excitement, high threat.

**B. Build Attractions and Facilities**
- Place purchased attraction tiles onto your park board in available spaces.
- Attractions increase excitement and may provide income or other bonuses.
- Your park board has a limited grid — spatial management matters.

**C. Upgrade Security**
- Increase your security level using purchased upgrades.
- Security must meet or exceed your threat level to keep visitors safe (see Visitor Phase).

**D. Adjust Workers**
- Assign workers/specialists to roles for the upcoming Visitor Phase.

### Phase 4: Visitors (Park Phase)
This is where your park "operates" for the round:

1. **Draw Visitors**: Draw a number of visitor meeples from the bag equal to your park's Excitement level. Visitors are color-coded:
   - **Regular visitors** (most common): Generate standard income.
   - **VIP visitors** (rarer): Generate more income or bonus VP.
   - **Hooligans** (bad): Cause problems, may not generate income, count against you.

2. **Collect Revenue**: Each regular and VIP visitor in your park generates income (money). Attractions may provide additional per-visitor bonuses.

3. **Security Check**: Compare your Threat level to your Security level.
   - If **Security >= Threat**: All visitors are safe. No penalties.
   - If **Security < Threat**: Visitors are eaten! For each point of threat exceeding security, one visitor is "killed" (removed). Each killed visitor costs you victory points at end of game (typically -1 VP each). Dead visitors are placed in a common "obituary" pile.
   - This is the core risk/reward tension: more exciting dinosaurs attract more visitors but are more dangerous.

### Phase 5: Cleanup
- Return all workers to their owners.
- Refresh the Research Center and Marketplace for the next round.
- Advance the round marker.
- Check for game-end conditions.

### Game Length Options
- **Short game**: Play until a specific number of objective cards are completed. ~60 minutes.
- **Medium game**: Fixed number of rounds (e.g., 7 rounds). ~90 minutes.
- **Long game**: More rounds and/or higher objective thresholds. ~120 minutes.

## SCORING / WINNING
At game end, score the following:

- **Victory points earned during the game** (from attractions, specialists, and bonuses).
- **Objective cards**: Score VP for each completed objective (private and/or public).
- **Dinosaurs in your park**: Many dinosaurs are worth VP directly.
- **Coins**: Remaining money converts to VP at a fixed rate (typically 3 coins = 1 VP).
- **Killed visitors penalty**: Lose 1 VP per visitor that was eaten in your park.
- **Specialist bonuses**: Some specialists provide end-game VP based on conditions (e.g., "2 VP per carnivore in your park").

The player with the most VP wins. Tiebreaker: most total excitement.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Balance excitement and security carefully. A park full of T-Rexes draws huge crowds, but if your security can't keep up, you'll hemorrhage VP from dead visitors.
- Herbivores are safe and provide a solid base. Sprinkle in carnivores for excitement once you have adequate security.
- The DNA dice draft in Phase 1 is critical. Going first lets you grab the best DNA. Consider turn-order manipulation.
- Specialists are very powerful — especially those that provide permanent workers or ongoing discounts. Hire them early.
- Don't neglect attractions. They boost excitement AND often provide per-visitor income multipliers.
- Paddock space is limited. Plan your park layout — you can't just stuff unlimited dinosaurs in.
- The visitor bag composition matters. In early rounds there are more regular visitors; the bag composition may change as the game progresses.
- Short game is great for learning; long game offers the most strategic depth.
- Money is tight, especially early. Don't overspend on the marketplace without a plan to generate income.
- Watch what opponents are building — if everyone drafts the same DNA type, competition will be fierce. Diversify when possible.
- Hooligans drawn from the bag are wasted visitors — you can't always control this, but high excitement mitigates it by drawing more total visitors.
