# Game: The Guild of Merchant Explorers
# Publisher: AEG (Alderac Entertainment Group)
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 45-60 min | Weight: 2.0/5

## OVERVIEW
The Guild of Merchant Explorers is a flip-and-write style exploration game where all players share the same map board but make independent placement decisions as exploration cards are revealed. Players place cubes on their personal map to explore terrain, connect cities, discover villages, and build trading posts. At the end of each of the four eras, most cubes are removed — but established structures remain, creating a satisfying engine-building arc across the game.

## COMPONENTS
- 4 double-sided map boards (each player gets one; all use the same side for a given game)
- Exploration cards deck (shared — terrain type cards flipped each era)
- Era cards (4 cards marking the eras)
- Player cubes (wooden cubes in 4 player colors — approximately 30+ per player)
- Trading post tokens (per player)
- Village tokens
- Treasure tokens
- Coin tokens (scoring currency)
- Investigate cards (unique special power cards)
- Objective cards (3 per game — shared goals)
- Score pad

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a map board (all players use the same map/side).
2. Each player takes all cubes and trading post tokens of their color.
3. Place one cube on the starting city (the city with the guild symbol on the map, typically in the center).
4. Shuffle the Exploration card deck. Separate the Era cards.
5. Deal out 3 shared Objective cards visible to all players. These provide bonus scoring conditions.
6. Each player receives a set of Investigate cards (special power cards unique to each player or shared, depending on variant).
7. Place coin tokens, village tokens, and treasure tokens in a shared supply.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Game Structure
The game is played over **4 Eras**. Each Era consists of several turns where exploration cards are flipped, followed by an era cleanup.

### Each Turn Within an Era
1. **Flip an Exploration Card**: One card is flipped from the shared deck. It shows a **terrain type** (grassland, desert, mountain, sea, or wild).
2. **All Players Simultaneously Place a Cube**: Each player places ONE cube from their supply onto their own map following these rules:
   - The cube must be placed on a space matching the terrain type shown on the card.
   - The cube must be **adjacent** (orthogonally — up/down/left/right, NOT diagonal) to an existing cube or established structure (trading post, village) on your map.
   - **Wild cards**: Can be placed on any terrain type.
   - If you cannot legally place a cube (no valid adjacent matching terrain), you skip that card.

3. **Resolve Discoveries**: When you place a cube, check if it triggers anything:
   - **City Connection**: If your cube network now connects two or more cities (by having a continuous path of cubes/structures), you earn coins based on the cities connected.
   - **Village Discovery**: If you place on a village space, place a village token there. Villages are permanent (survive era reset) and serve as starting points for future eras.
   - **Treasure Discovery**: If you place on a treasure space, collect a treasure token (worth coins at game end).
   - **Tower Spaces**: Some maps have tower spaces that provide special bonuses.

### Investigate Cards
- Each player has a hand of Investigate cards.
- On certain turns (usually once per era, or as the card allows), a player may play an Investigate card INSTEAD of or IN ADDITION to the normal exploration card placement.
- Investigate cards provide powerful special placements — like placing multiple cubes, placing on any terrain, or placing non-adjacent cubes.
- Used Investigate cards are typically discarded for the game.

### End of an Era
When all exploration cards for that era have been flipped:
1. **Score connections**: Earn coins for any completed city connections made during this era.
2. **Remove most cubes**: Pick up ALL cubes from the map EXCEPT those on spaces with established structures (trading posts and villages). This is the key mechanic — your network resets each era.
3. **Establish Trading Posts**: Before removing cubes, you may establish a trading post on one (or more, if you've earned the ability) of your cube positions. Trading posts are permanent and act as starting points for future eras. You typically get to establish 1 trading post per era.
4. **Prepare for next era**: Shuffle or prepare the next era's exploration cards.

### Building Your Network Across Eras
- **Era 1**: You start from only the guild city. Explore outward.
- **Era 2**: You start from the guild city + any villages discovered + any trading posts built. Multiple starting points allow branching exploration.
- **Era 3-4**: Even more starting points. Your network grows exponentially as you establish more permanent footholds.

## SCORING / WINNING
Scoring occurs throughout the game and at the end:

- **City Connections**: Each pair of connected cities scores coins (amount depends on the cities — typically printed on the map or referenced by a scoring chart). Larger networks connecting more cities score progressively more.
- **Discovered Villages**: Each village token is worth coins (typically 1-2 coins each).
- **Treasure Tokens**: Each treasure is worth coins (varies by token value).
- **Trading Posts**: May score based on placement or objectives.
- **Objective Cards**: The 3 shared objectives provide bonus scoring (e.g., "most trading posts in the desert," "connect all coastal cities," "discover all villages in a region"). These vary by game.
- **Investigate Cards**: Some unused investigate cards or achievements may provide points.

**The player with the most coins at the end of 4 eras wins.** Tiebreaker: most trading posts established, then most villages discovered.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Plan your trading post placement carefully**: Since cubes are removed each era, trading posts are your most important strategic decision. Place them where they'll give you the best starting positions for future eras.
- **Villages are free permanent anchors**: Discovering villages early gives you more starting points without spending your limited trading post placements.
- **Think about adjacency chains**: You need to reach distant locations through connected placement. Plan routes across the map.
- **Wild cards are precious**: Use them for hard-to-reach terrain or to bridge gaps in your network.
- **City connections are the main source of points**: Focus on connecting as many cities as possible each era. The scoring for multi-city networks grows significantly.
- **Watch the objective cards**: They can be worth substantial points. Don't ignore them.
- **Each era gets easier**: With more starting points from villages and trading posts, your reach grows. Eras 3 and 4 should see much more extensive exploration.
- **Don't spread too thin in Era 1**: You have only one starting point. Build a focused, useful path rather than trying to reach everything immediately.
- **All players use the same cards**: Since the exploration cards are shared, you're solving the same puzzle with different strategies. Watch what terrain types are likely to come up.
- **The game is low-conflict**: Players don't directly interact. Focus on optimizing your own map.
