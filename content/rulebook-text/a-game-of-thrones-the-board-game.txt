# Game: A Game of Thrones: The Board Game (2nd Ed)
# Publisher: Fantasy Flight Games
# Players: 3-6 | Time: 120-240 min | Weight: 3.7/5

## OVERVIEW
A Game of Thrones: The Board Game is a grand strategy game set in the world of George R.R. Martin's A Song of Ice and Fire. Players control one of the Great Houses of Westeros (Stark, Lannister, Baratheon, Greyjoy, Tyrell, Martell), vying for control of the Iron Throne by conquering territories and strongholds through diplomacy, betrayal, and military might. The game combines area control, hand management, and negotiation in a tense struggle across the map of Westeros.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board (map of Westeros divided into land and sea areas)
- House cards (7 unique combat cards per House, representing key characters)
- Order tokens (March, Defend, Support, Raid, Consolidate Power — each with a starred/special version)
- Footman, Knight, Siege Engine, and Ship units (plastic figures in each House's color)
- Power tokens (used for bidding, mustering, and special orders)
- Influence tracks (Iron Throne, Fiefdoms, King's Court) with dominance tokens
- Westeros deck (three decks of event cards, I/II/III)
- Wildling deck and Wildling threat track
- Supply track
- Victory track
- Neutral Force tokens (for areas with garrisons)
- Tides of Battle cards (optional variant)

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center of the table.
2. Each player chooses a House and takes all units, order tokens, power tokens, and House cards for that House.
3. Place starting units on the board as indicated on each House's setup card (these vary by House and player count).
4. Place each House's influence markers on the three influence tracks in the starting positions shown in the rulebook (varies by player count).
5. Set each House's supply marker on the supply track at its starting position.
6. Place each House's victory marker at its starting position on the victory track (number of starting castles/strongholds).
7. Shuffle the three Westeros decks separately and place them face down.
8. Shuffle the Wildling deck and place it face down. Set the Wildling threat marker to 2.
9. Place neutral Force tokens in the indicated areas.
10. The player on position 1 of the Iron Throne track receives the Iron Throne token; position 1 on Fiefdoms gets the Valyrian Steel Blade; position 1 on King's Court gets the Messenger Raven.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

The game is played over 10 rounds. Each round has three phases:

### Phase 1: Westeros Phase (skipped in Round 1)
Reveal the top card of each of the three Westeros decks and resolve them in order (Deck I, II, III):
- **Deck I** typically involves Supply or Mustering.
- **Deck II** typically involves Game of Thrones (collect power) or Clash of Kings (bid for influence tracks).
- **Deck III** typically involves Wildling Attacks or special effects.
- **Wildling Attack**: If triggered, all players simultaneously and secretly bid power tokens. The total bid is compared to the Wildling threat level. If the bid equals or exceeds the threat, players win (highest bidder gets a bonus). If not, players lose (lowest bidder suffers the worst penalty). Reset Wildling threat to 0.
- **Clash of Kings**: All players simultaneously bid power tokens on each of the three influence tracks. Highest bidder takes position 1, etc. Ties broken by current track position.
- **Mustering**: Players may recruit new units in areas containing a Castle (1 mustering point) or Stronghold (2 mustering points). Footman costs 1, Knight costs 2, Siege Engine costs 2, Ship costs 1 (in adjacent sea).
- **Supply**: Recalculate supply levels based on supply icons in controlled areas. Armies must be adjusted to comply with supply limits.

### Phase 2: Planning Phase
All players simultaneously and secretly place one Order token face-down on each area they control that contains at least one of their units. Orders are:
- **March** (move units to attack or reposition)
- **Defend** (+1 or +2 combat strength when defending)
- **Support** (add strength to an adjacent combat — can support either side!)
- **Raid** (remove an adjacent enemy Support or Consolidate Power order)
- **Consolidate Power** (gain power tokens)

Each order type has a regular and a starred (special/improved) version. The number of starred orders you can use is limited by your position on the King's Court influence track.

After all orders are placed, they are revealed simultaneously.

### Phase 3: Action Phase
Orders are resolved in a specific sequence:
1. **Raid Orders** — In Iron Throne track order, each player resolves one Raid order (remove an adjacent enemy Raid, Support, or Consolidate Power token; the starred Raid can also remove Defense). Continue cycling until all Raids are resolved.
2. **March Orders** — In Iron Throne track order, each player resolves one March order. Moving into an area with enemy units triggers combat. Continue cycling until all Marches are resolved.
3. **Consolidate Power Orders** — Each remaining Consolidate Power token generates 1 power token (or 2 with the starred version). A starred Consolidate Power may alternatively be used to Muster units.

### Combat
When a March order moves units into an area occupied by another player:
1. **Call for Support**: Both the attacker and defender check for adjacent friendly or enemy Support orders. A player with a Support order in an adjacent area may add their supporting units' strength to either side (negotiation!).
2. **Calculate initial combat strength**: Sum the strength of all participating units (Footman = 1, Knight = 2, Siege Engine = 4 against Strongholds/Castles or 0 otherwise, Ship = 1). Add any relevant March order bonus (+1 for starred March) or Defend bonus.
3. **Play House Cards**: Both players simultaneously select one House card from their hand and reveal. Each card has a combat strength value and may have special abilities. House cards are discarded after use — you cycle through all 7 before getting them back.
4. **Use Valyrian Steel Blade**: The holder may choose to add +1 to their total (once per round, flip token to show it's used).
5. **Resolve abilities**: Apply House card text abilities.
6. **Determine winner**: Highest total wins. Ties go to the defender (unless the attacker holds the Fiefdoms position 1 — Valyrian Steel Blade breaks ties in attacker's favor on the track, but the blade's +1 also plays a role).
7. **Casualties**: The loser must destroy units equal to the winner's House card skull icons. The loser retreats surviving units to an adjacent friendly or empty area. If they cannot retreat, all units are destroyed.

### Influence Tracks
- **Iron Throne** (position 1 = Iron Throne holder): Breaks non-combat ties, decides Wildling attack ties, determines order of action resolution.
- **Fiefdoms** (position 1 = Valyrian Steel Blade): Breaks combat ties, +1 strength once per round.
- **King's Court** (position 1 = Messenger Raven): Determines how many special/starred orders each player can use. Raven holder can change one placed order after reveal or look at the top Wildling card.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Instant Win**: The first player to control 7 areas containing Castles and/or Strongholds at the end of any Action Phase wins immediately.
- **End of Round 10**: If no one has 7 castles/strongholds after 10 rounds, the player with the most castles/strongholds wins. Ties broken by position on the three influence tracks (Iron Throne first, then Fiefdoms, then King's Court).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Supply limits are enforced strictly**: After a Supply event, you must disband units if your armies exceed your supply level. Plan conquests with supply in mind.
- **House cards don't come back until all 7 are used**: Track which cards opponents have played. Knowing they've used their strongest card is a huge strategic advantage.
- **Support can go to EITHER side**: A Support order doesn't automatically help your ally — you can support your enemy's enemy, or even an attacking enemy. This makes diplomacy and negotiation crucial.
- **The Messenger Raven is extremely powerful**: Being able to change an order after seeing everyone else's orders is a massive tactical advantage.
- **Starred orders are limited**: Lower King's Court positions mean fewer special orders. This can cripple your flexibility.
- **Power tokens are a resource**: You need them for bidding on influence tracks and Wildling attacks. Don't spend them recklessly.
- **Ports**: Ships in ports count as being in the adjacent sea area for support and can retreat to the port. Ports also protect ships from direct attack.
- **Siege Engines**: Worth 4 strength only when attacking (not defending) a Castle or Stronghold. Otherwise they contribute 0 combat strength. They are slow and vulnerable.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that March -1 orders reduce your total combat strength by 1. Use regular March (0) or starred March (+1) for attacks.
- **Player count matters**: With fewer than 6 players, certain Houses are removed, changing the strategic dynamics significantly. The 6-player game is considered the definitive experience.
- **Negotiation is everything**: Alliances, deals, and betrayals define the game. Nothing is binding — a promise made can be broken.
