# Game: The Search for Planet X
# Publisher: Renegade Game Studios
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 60-75 min | Weight: 2.5/5

## OVERVIEW
The Search for Planet X is a deduction game where players take on the role of astronomers using observations and logical deduction to locate a hypothetical planet (Planet X) hidden among the sectors of the night sky. The game uses a companion app to manage hidden information, and players must efficiently gather clues, eliminate possibilities, and be the first to correctly identify Planet X's location. It blends logic puzzle mechanics with a board game format.

## COMPONENTS
- Circular game board divided into 12 sectors (standard mode) or 18 sectors (expert mode)
- Companion app (required — available on iOS, Android, and web)
- Player screens (to hide note sheets)
- Note sheets (logic grid pads for tracking deductions)
- Theory tokens (numbered tokens for publishing theories)
- Sector tokens
- Earth board and pawn (tracks current position/season)
- Target tokens
- Action pawn
- Score track and scoring tokens

## SETUP
1. Place the circular game board in the center of the table.
2. Each player takes a player screen and a note sheet.
3. Open the companion app and select a new game. Choose standard (12 sectors) or expert (18 sectors) mode.
4. Enter the number of players. The app generates a unique arrangement of celestial objects for the game.
5. The app provides each player with a starting clue — enter your player number to receive your private initial information.
6. Place the Earth pawn on the starting position on the Earth board.
7. Each player takes a set of Theory tokens.
8. Mark your starting clue information on your note sheet.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

Players take turns in clockwise order. On your turn, you perform ONE action by selecting it in the app. Each action costs time (advances your pawn on the time track). The player furthest back on the time track takes the next turn (similar to Patchwork/Tokaido — you may take multiple turns in a row if you're behind).

### Actions

**Survey (4 time units)**: Choose a sector range and one object type (asteroid, comet, gas cloud, dwarf planet, or truly empty). The app tells you how many of that object type are in the chosen range. The range you can survey depends on the number of sectors (typically 3-4 adjacent sectors in standard mode).

**Target (4 time units)**: Select a single sector. The app tells you exactly what object is in that sector. This is the most precise action but only reveals one sector.

**Research (1-3 time units, varies by topic)**: Choose a research topic from the available options. Each topic provides a logical rule or clue about the arrangement (e.g., "Planet X is not adjacent to any dwarf planets," "There is at least one asteroid in sectors 3-6"). Research topics become available at different points in the game as the Earth moves through seasons. Some cost more time than others.

**Locate Planet X (5 time units)**: When you think you know where Planet X is, choose this action. You must identify the sector containing Planet X AND the objects in the two sectors adjacent to Planet X. If correct, the game enters the final phase. If incorrect, the game continues and you've lost time.

### Publishing Theories
At certain points on the time track (marked on the board), when you cross or land on a theory marker, you MAY publish a theory. To publish a theory:
1. Place one of your Theory tokens on a sector on the board, object-type side up, claiming what object is in that sector.
2. The app confirms (privately at end of turn or via app check) whether your theory is correct.
3. Correct theories earn points. Earlier theories about a sector score more than later ones.
4. You cannot publish a theory about Planet X directly — you must use the Locate Planet X action for that.

### Conferences
At certain positions on the time track, a Conference occurs. During a conference, each player reveals one piece of information they know (a specific fact about a sector). This is publicly shared, giving all players new data but also revealing that you know certain things.

### Seasons
The Earth board tracks the current season. As time advances, new Research topics become available in later seasons. Early research topics tend to be more general; later ones are more specific and useful.

## SCORING / WINNING
- When a player correctly locates Planet X (correct sector + correct adjacent objects), the game enters the final round. All other players get one more turn to also attempt to locate Planet X.
- **Scoring**:
  - Successfully locating Planet X: earn points based on being first (most points) or subsequent.
  - Published theories: each correct theory earns points. Earlier theories on a given sector earn more (first correct theory on a sector = more points than second).
  - The player with the most total points wins.
- Typical point values: First to locate Planet X = 10 points. Other players who also locate it in the final round = 5 points. Theory points vary (first correct theory on a sector = 2-3 points, subsequent correct = 1-2 points, depending on object type).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Use your note sheet methodically**: Cross-reference every clue. The game is essentially a logic puzzle — marking what CAN'T be in a sector is as important as marking what CAN.
- **Time management is critical**: Every action costs time. Efficient players survey broad areas early and narrow down. Don't waste time targeting individual sectors early when surveys give more information per time unit.
- **Research is often the best value**: Research clues cost less time and give logical rules that can eliminate many possibilities at once.
- **Publish theories early**: The first correct theory on a sector scores more. If you're confident, publish before opponents.
- **Conferences reveal information to everyone**: Be strategic about what you share (you must share something, but choose the least helpful-to-opponents piece of information you can).
- **Object distribution**: The app ensures a fixed number of each object type. The note sheet tells you how many of each object exist. Tracking how many you've found helps narrow remaining sectors.
- **Planet X rules**: Planet X is never adjacent to certain object types (this is always given as a starting constraint). Use this to eliminate sectors.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting to account for the wrap-around — sector 12 is adjacent to sector 1 on the circular board.
- **The app is required**: There is no way to play without the app. Make sure devices are charged.
- **Standard vs Expert**: Standard (12 sectors) is recommended for first games. Expert (18 sectors) adds more sectors and object types for a longer, more challenging deduction puzzle.
