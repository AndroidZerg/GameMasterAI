# Game: Orléans
# Publisher: dlp games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 90 min | Weight: 3.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Orléans is a bag-building game set in medieval France during the time of Joan of Arc. Players draw character tokens from their personal bags and assign them to action spaces on their player boards to perform various activities: trading, building, traveling, farming, and more. The unique bag-building mechanism (similar to deck-building but with tokens in a bag) creates a satisfying engine of growing capabilities and strategic depth.

## COMPONENTS
- Main game board (map of France with cities, waterways, and roads)
- Player boards (with action spaces for Farmhouse, Village, University, Castle, Monastery, Ship, Wagon, Guildhall)
- Character tokens (Farmers, Boatmen, Craftsmen, Traders, Knights, Scholars, Monks)
- Technology tiles
- Place tiles (city bonuses)
- Goods tiles (Cheese, Wine, Wool, Brocade, Grain)
- Coin tokens
- Beneficial Deeds board (communal board for permanent bonuses)
- Citizen tiles
- Hour Glass tiles (event cards for each round)
- Player bags (cloth drawstring bags)
- Trading stations (wooden houses per player)
- Merchant token (per player, for movement on the map)

## SETUP
1. Place the main board in the center. Set out goods tiles on their designated cities.
2. Give each player a player board, a bag, a merchant pawn (placed in Orléans), and a set of starting tokens: 4 Farmers, 4 Boatmen, 4 Craftsmen, 4 Traders, 1 Knight (these go in the player's supply, not the bag).
3. Each player puts their starting tokens into their bag: 1 Farmer, 1 Boatman, 1 Craftsman, 1 Trader.
4. Place the Beneficial Deeds board nearby with the citizen tiles on it.
5. Shuffle and stack the Hour Glass tiles (18 rounds). Reveal the first one.
6. Distribute starting coins (5 coins each).
7. Set out the technology tiles, place tiles, and goods on the board.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

The game is played over 18 rounds. Each round has 7 phases:

### Phase 1: Hour Glass
Reveal the current round's event tile. This may trigger an event at a specific phase (usually Phase 6), such as a Plague (lose followers), Pilgrimage, Taxes, or Income.

### Phase 2: Census
The player with the most Farmers gets a coin. The player with the fewest Farmers loses a coin (if tied, no one gains/loses). Check all players simultaneously.

### Phase 3: Followers
Each player draws character tokens from their bag. The number drawn equals 4 initially (can be increased to 5, 6, 7, or 8 by building certain structures). Place drawn tokens on your Market (a holding area on your player board).

### Phase 4: Planning
Simultaneously, all players place their drawn followers from the Market onto the action spaces on their player boards. Each action space requires specific combinations of character types (shown by icons). You don't have to fill all spaces — only fill those you want to activate.

### Phase 5: Actions
In turn order, players resolve their filled action spaces. You may resolve them in any order:

- **Farmhouse** (Farmer + Boatman): Gain a Farmer token and add it to your bag.
- **Village** (Farmer + Craftsman): Gain a Boatman token and add it to your bag.
- **University** (Farmer + Trader): Gain a Craftsman token and add it to your bag.
- **Castle** (Boatman + Trader): Gain a Knight token and add it to your bag.
- **Monastery** (Scholar + Trader): Gain a Scholar token and add it to your bag. (Scholars are also gained through Monk actions initially.)
- **Ship** (Farmer + Boatman + Knight): Move your merchant along a waterway. Collect any goods from the destination city. Place a trading station if you haven't already.
- **Wagon** (Craftsman + Knight + Trader): Move your merchant along a road. Collect any goods from the destination city. Place a trading station if you haven't already.
- **Guildhall** (various requirements based on technology): Gain coins or perform the special action of any technology tile you've acquired.
- **Town Hall**: Gain a technology tile (requires specific followers depending on the tile).

**Technology Tiles**: These add new action spaces to your board, providing additional or enhanced actions. They require specific follower combinations and give powerful abilities (extra draws, special movement, VP bonuses, etc.).

### Phase 6: Event
Resolve the Hour Glass event shown for this round (if any). Events include:
- **Plague**: Each player must return a follower from their Market/board.
- **Taxes**: Pay coins or lose VP.
- **Income**: Gain coins.
- **Pilgrimage**: Bonus actions.

### Phase 7: New Round
Used followers return to the player's bag (except those sent to the Beneficial Deeds board). Advance to the next round.

### Beneficial Deeds Board
At any point during your actions, you may send followers to the Beneficial Deeds board instead of using them. These followers are PERMANENTLY removed from your bag (thinning your bag for better draws). Each row of the Deeds board gives an immediate citizen tile (worth VP at end-game). Sending followers to the Deeds board is one of the key strategic paths — a lean bag draws better combinations more consistently.

## SCORING / WINNING
At the end of Round 18, calculate scores:
- **Trading stations**: Score points for each trading station placed on the board (more stations = more points, bonus for connected networks).
- **Citizen tiles**: Each citizen tile gained from the Beneficial Deeds board is worth VP (typically 1-7 VP each).
- **Goods**: Each set of different goods collected scores points. Individual goods are worth 1 coin each. Complete sets of 5 different goods score bonus VP.
- **Coins**: Every 5 coins = 1 VP.
- **Development track**: Players who have advanced on the development track score increasing VP (0, 1, 3, 6, 10, 15, 21...).

The player with the most VP wins.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Bag-thinning is powerful**: Sending followers to the Beneficial Deeds board removes them permanently, making your future draws more consistent and efficient. It's like card-thinning in deck-builders.
- **Don't neglect the Deeds board**: Citizen tiles are a major source of VP and the thinned bag improves your engine.
- **Technology tiles are game-changers**: Acquiring the right tech tile can define your strategy. Prioritize ones that complement your current followers.
- **Movement is crucial for goods**: You need Ship or Wagon actions to move your merchant and collect goods. Trading stations also score points.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that drawn followers not placed during Planning are wasted for the round — they go back into the bag unused.
- **Common mistake**: Not tracking the Hour Glass events. Plague rounds can be devastating if you're not prepared.
- **Monks are special**: Monks act as wild tokens — they can substitute for any one follower type when placed on an action space.
- **Knight timing**: Knights are needed for movement actions (Ship and Wagon) but can only be recruited through the Castle action. Plan ahead.
- **The Census (Phase 2) matters**: Having the most Farmers gives you consistent income; having the fewest costs you. Keep at least a moderate number.
