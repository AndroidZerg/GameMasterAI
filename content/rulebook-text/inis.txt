# Game: Inis
# Publisher: Matagot
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 3.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Inis is an area control game set in Celtic mythology where players lead clans vying for dominance over the island of Inis. Victory is achieved through one (or more) of three distinct win conditions checked at the start of each round. The game features a card drafting mechanism where players pass hands of action cards, ensuring deep familiarity with available options and creating tense, strategic play.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Capital tile and 16 territory tiles (double-sided)
- Clan figures (miniatures) in 4 player colors (12 per player)
- Citadel tokens (round wooden pieces)
- Sanctuary tokens (tall wooden pieces)
- 17 Action cards (main draft deck)
- 16 Advantage cards (territory-specific)
- 30 Epic Tale cards (powerful one-time effects)
- Deed tokens (track pretender claims)
- Brenn (chieftain) token
- Flock of crows token (first player marker)
- Clash tokens (for combat)
- Pretender tokens (red deed tokens)

## SETUP
1. Place the Capital tile in the center of the table. Each player places 2 clan figures on it.
2. Each player takes their 12 clan figures and places 2 on the Capital; the rest form their reserve.
3. Shuffle the Action cards, Advantage cards, and Epic Tale cards into separate decks.
4. The starting Brenn (chieftain) is chosen randomly; they receive the Brenn token.
5. Place citadel and sanctuary tokens nearby as a supply.
6. With 2 players, use 4 Action cards set aside (specific rules for 2p draft).
7. Each territory beyond the Capital will be added during play via the Exploration action.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
Each round has 3 phases:
1. **Assembly Phase** (check victory, draft cards)
2. **Season Phase** (take turns playing cards)
3. **Clean-up**

### Phase 1: Assembly
- **Check for Victory**: At the START of each assembly phase (after the first round), check if any player meets a win condition and has placed a Pretender token in a previous round. If they still qualify, they win. Multiple players may qualify simultaneously — if so, the one meeting the most conditions wins (ties = shared victory).
- **Pretender Claims**: Any player who currently meets at least one victory condition places a Pretender token. This announces their intent — they win NEXT round if they still qualify.
- **Card Draft**: The Brenn deals Action cards evenly to all players (typically 4 each). Players simultaneously look at their hands, choose 1 card to keep, and pass the rest to the left. Repeat until all cards are drafted. Players also keep any Advantage cards from territories they are chieftain of.

### Three Victory Conditions
1. **Leadership**: Be chieftain (have the most or tied-for-most clans) in territories containing at least 6 opponent clans total.
2. **Kingship**: Be present in at least 6 different territories.
3. **Chieftain of the Land**: Be chieftain in territories that collectively contain at least 6 sanctuaries.

A player who meets ANY ONE of these can place a Pretender token. Meeting multiple is advantageous for tiebreaking.

### Phase 2: Season (Main Gameplay)
Starting with the Brenn and going clockwise, each player plays ONE Action card from their hand and resolves it, OR passes. When all players pass consecutively, the Season ends.

**Key Action Cards include:**
- **Conquest**: Move clan figures from one territory to an adjacent territory. If opponents are there, clashes may occur.
- **Migration**: Move any number of your clans from one territory to another (not necessarily adjacent).
- **New Clans**: Add clan figures from your reserve to territories where you are present.
- **Exploration**: Draw a territory tile and place it adjacent to existing tiles. Place a clan on it. Take the corresponding Advantage card.
- **Sanctuary/Citadel**: Build a sanctuary or citadel in a territory where you are chieftain.
- **Brenn**: Take the Brenn token (become first player next round).
- **Master Craftsman**: Build citadels.
- Various other strategic action cards.

**Advantage Cards**: Each territory has a unique Advantage card held by its chieftain. These provide special powers while you remain chieftain of that territory.

**Epic Tale Cards**: Drawn through certain actions; they provide powerful one-time effects that can be played on your turn or in response to other actions (as specified on the card).

### Clashes (Combat)
When your clans enter a territory with opponents (or vice versa), a clash may occur:
1. The attacker initiates. Each involved player in the territory (starting with the attacker, then clockwise) must choose one action:
   - **Attack**: Remove one of the opponent's clans (your choice which opponent if multiple).
   - **Negotiate/Withdraw**: Move one of your clans to an adjacent territory.
   - **Play an Epic Tale card**: If applicable.
2. After all players have acted, any player may choose to **continue the clash** or **cease**. If ANY player wants to continue, another round of clash actions occurs.
3. Clashes end when all involved players agree to cease.
4. Citadels protect 1 clan each — a clan "inside" a citadel cannot be removed by attacks until the citadel is destroyed.

### Chieftain Rule
The player with the most clans in a territory is its chieftain. If tied, the current chieftain retains the title (or it remains uncontrolled). The chieftain holds that territory's Advantage card.

## SCORING / WINNING
- There is no point scoring. Victory is binary based on meeting at least one of the three victory conditions.
- A player must first declare as a Pretender (place token) when meeting a condition, then STILL meet a condition at the start of the NEXT assembly to win.
- If multiple players qualify simultaneously, the player meeting the most conditions wins. If still tied, those players share the victory.
- This delayed-win mechanism gives opponents a full round to disrupt the leader.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Defensive play is critical**: When an opponent places a Pretender token, the entire table must work to deny their conditions before next Assembly.
- **Card drafting creates information**: You know exactly which cards your opponents have (you passed them). Use this knowledge.
- **Clashes are costly for everyone**: Combat removes clans from both sides. Often the threat of a clash is more powerful than the clash itself.
- **Citadels are powerful defensively**: They protect 1 clan each and make territories much harder to contest.
- **Sanctuaries enable the Chieftain of the Land condition**: Building them in territories you control is key for that win path.
- **The Brenn card is deceptively important**: First player advantage in card play is significant.
- **Epic Tale cards can swing the game**: Save them for critical moments — they can cancel attacks, move clans unexpectedly, or provide other powerful effects.
- **Territory placement matters**: When exploring, where you place new tiles affects adjacency and movement for the rest of the game.
- **2-player Inis**: Uses a modified card draft and is significantly more confrontational.
- **You can meet multiple victory conditions simultaneously**: This is the surest path to winning, as opponents cannot block all of them.
