# Game: Pictomania
# Publisher: Czech Games Edition
# Players: 3-6 | Time: 25-35 min | Weight: 1.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Pictomania is a frantic party game where everyone draws and guesses simultaneously. Unlike most drawing games, every player is drawing their own secret word at the same time while also trying to guess what others are drawing. Speed matters — the faster you correctly guess another player's word, the more points your guess is worth. The twist: all players' secret words come from the same themed category list, so you must figure out exactly which word on the list each person is drawing.

## COMPONENTS
- 99 double-sided clue cards (6 difficulty levels), each with 7 numbered words
- 6 sets of drawing markers (1 per player)
- 6 drawing boards (double-sided dry-erase)
- 6 sets of guess cards (numbered 1-7, one set per player in each player's color)
- Scoring tokens (numbered chips: values 1, 2, 3... — awarded in order of guessing speed)
- 6 black bonus/penalty tokens
- Card holder/stand for clue cards
- 1 symbol card set (to assign secret words)

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a drawing board, a marker, and a set of 7 guess cards (numbered 1-7) in their color.
2. Select a clue card based on desired difficulty (easy/medium/hard — the card back indicates difficulty). Place it in the shared card holder so ALL players can see the list of 7 words.
3. Deal each player a secret assignment card that tells them which numbered word (1-7) on the clue card they must draw. Players look at their assignment secretly.
4. Place the scoring tokens in a row from highest to lowest value near each player's drawing area.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Pictomania is played over 5 rounds. Each round:

### Drawing & Guessing Phase (Simultaneous)
All players act at the same time:
- **Draw**: Begin drawing your assigned word on your board. You must draw — no letters, numbers, or symbols allowed.
- **Guess**: While drawing, look at what OTHER players are drawing. When you think you know which word (1-7) someone is drawing, place one of your numbered guess cards face-down next to their board. You can only submit ONE guess per player.
- **Speed matters**: Scoring tokens are stacked next to each player's board. The first person to place a guess next to a player's board takes the highest-value token; the second guesser takes the next, and so on.
- **Stop drawing to guess**: You may stop drawing at any time to focus on guessing, but a better drawing helps others guess YOUR word (which earns you points).

Players continue drawing and guessing simultaneously until everyone has either placed all their guess cards or decided to stop.

### Scoring Phase
1. **Reveal assignments**: All players reveal their secret word number.
2. **Check guesses**: Flip over the guess cards next to each player's board.
   - **Correct guesses**: If your guess card matches the artist's secret number, you keep the scoring token you claimed (the speed bonus).
   - **Incorrect guesses**: Return the scoring token. You also receive a black penalty token worth negative points.
3. **Artist bonus**: Each artist looks at the guesses placed on their board. If at least one person guessed your word correctly, you do NOT receive a penalty. If NO ONE guessed your word correctly, you receive a black penalty token (your drawing was too unclear).
4. **Fastest artist bonus**: The player who finished drawing and guessing first (placed all their guess cards earliest) may receive a bonus.

### Scoring Values
- Speed tokens are worth descending values (e.g., first correct guess = 3 points, second = 2, third = 1, etc., depending on player count).
- Each black penalty token is worth -1 point (in some editions, the penalty scales).
- Unused guess cards neither help nor hurt.

### End of Round
Clear boards, return guess cards, draw new clue card and secret assignments. Play another round.

## SCORING / WINNING
After 5 rounds, each player totals:
- Points from all collected speed tokens (for correct guesses)
- Minus points from black penalty tokens (for wrong guesses and unguessed drawings)

The player with the highest total wins.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- All 7 words on a clue card share a theme (e.g., "things in a kitchen," "famous landmarks," "types of vehicles"). This means guessing requires distinguishing between similar concepts.
- You cannot write letters, numbers, or recognizable symbols. Drawing only.
- Speed is critical but accuracy matters more — a wrong guess costs you a penalty AND you lose the scoring token.
- Balance drawing quality vs. guessing speed. If you spend too long perfecting your drawing, others will claim the high-value speed tokens before you.
- Draw the DISTINGUISHING features of your word first. If the category is "animals" and your word is "elephant," draw the trunk immediately — do not start with a generic body outline.
- Watch for players who stop drawing early — they are guessing aggressively. Their drawing might be simple enough to identify quickly.
- Higher difficulty cards have more similar words (e.g., easy might be "car, boat, plane" while hard might be "sedan, SUV, convertible, truck, van, bus, taxi"), making distinctions much harder.
- The 6-player game is the most chaotic and entertaining. At 3 players, it is more strategic and less frantic.
- Pictomania rewards quick visual communication — practice drawing concepts in 2-3 strokes.
