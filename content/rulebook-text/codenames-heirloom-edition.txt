# Game: Codenames
# Publisher: Czech Games Edition
# Players: 2-8 | Time: 15 min | Weight: 1.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Codenames is a team-based word association party game where two rival spymasters give one-word clues to help their teammates identify secret agents from a grid of words. The challenge lies in crafting clues that connect multiple words while avoiding the opposing team's agents and the deadly assassin. It is simple to learn but endlessly replayable.

## COMPONENTS
- 200 double-sided word cards (400 words total)
- 40 key cards (showing the secret grid layout)
- 25 agent cards: 8 red agents, 8 blue agents, 7 innocent bystanders, 1 assassin
- Card stand/holder for the key card
- Timer (optional, sand timer)

## SETUP
1. Divide players into two teams (Red and Blue). Each team selects one Spymaster; the rest are Field Operatives.
2. Shuffle the word cards and lay out a 5x5 grid of 25 word cards on the table, visible to all players.
3. Randomly draw one key card and place it in the card stand so that only the two Spymasters can see it.
4. The key card shows which of the 25 positions correspond to Red agents (red squares), Blue agents (blue squares), innocent bystanders (beige/tan squares), and the Assassin (black square).
5. The key card also indicates which team goes first (the border color). The starting team has 9 agents to find; the other team has 8.
6. Place the corresponding agent cards, bystander cards, and assassin card nearby for covering guessed words.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

Teams alternate turns, beginning with the starting team.

### Spymaster's Clue
The Spymaster gives a one-word clue followed by a number. Example: "Animal 3" — meaning 3 of their team's words relate to "Animal."

**Clue rules:**
- The clue must be a single word (no phrases, no hyphens unless it's a common compound word).
- The clue cannot be any of the 25 words currently visible on the grid (or a form of those words).
- The number indicates how many cards on the grid relate to the clue.
- The Spymaster may say "Unlimited" or "0" instead of a number. "0" means none of their words relate to the clue (used to steer teammates away from certain words). "Unlimited" means at least 1 word relates but the team can guess as many as they want.

### Field Operatives' Guesses
The Field Operatives discuss among themselves and then touch/point to a word card to guess.

- **Correct (own team's agent)**: The Spymaster covers the word with their team's agent card. The team may continue guessing.
- **Innocent Bystander**: The Spymaster covers it with a bystander card. The team's turn ends immediately.
- **Opponent's Agent**: The Spymaster covers it with the opponent's agent card. The team's turn ends AND they've helped the other team.
- **Assassin**: The game ends immediately. The team that contacted the Assassin LOSES.

**Number of guesses allowed**: The team may make up to (clue number + 1) guesses. They may stop guessing voluntarily at any time. The extra +1 guess allows teams to revisit words from previous clues.

### Turn End
After the team finishes guessing (either voluntarily, by hitting a bystander/opponent, or by using all their guesses), play passes to the other team.

## SCORING / WINNING
- A team wins immediately when all of their agents have been identified (contacted).
- The starting team must find 9 agents; the other team must find 8.
- A team loses immediately if they contact the Assassin.
- There are no points — it's simply a race to find all your agents first.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **The Spymaster must keep a straight face**: No reactions, no gestures, no hints beyond the one-word clue and number. Body language is considered cheating.
- **Common mistake**: Spymasters giving clues that are forms of visible words (e.g., saying "run" when "running" is on the grid). This is not allowed.
- **Common mistake**: Spymasters using proper nouns when the group hasn't agreed on allowing them. Clarify house rules before playing.
- **Strategy — Multi-word clues are risky**: Connecting 3+ words with one clue is powerful but increases the chance of teammates guessing a wrong word.
- **The +1 rule is important**: Teams can always make one extra guess beyond the number given, allowing them to catch up on previously missed words.
- **Zero clues**: Saying "Boats 0" means none of your words relate to boats — this is a defensive clue telling teammates to avoid boat-related words (which may be the assassin or opponent's agents).
- **Variant — Codenames Duet**: A cooperative 2-player version exists using separate key cards for each partner.
- **If using the timer**: The timer is optional and can be used to limit discussion time for the guessing team (about 2-3 minutes suggested). It's not used for the Spymaster's clue-giving.
- **Assassin awareness**: When giving clues, Spymasters should always consider whether the clue might lead teammates to the Assassin. Avoiding the Assassin takes priority over connecting multiple words.
