# SOURCE: AI knowledge summary — verify against official rulebook
# Game: Suburbia
# Publisher: Bezier Games
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.8/5

## OVERVIEW
Suburbia is a tile-laying city-building game where players develop their own borough by purchasing and placing hex tiles representing residential, commercial, civic, and industrial zones. Each tile interacts with adjacent tiles and sometimes all tiles in every player's borough, creating a dynamic economic engine of income and reputation. The player who attracts the most population to their borough wins.

## COMPONENTS
- Hex building tiles (approximately 100+ tiles across categories: Residential, Commercial, Civic, Industrial)
- 1 real estate market board (where tiles are available for purchase)
- 1 stacks board (for A, B, C tile stacks)
- Player borough boards (starting layout with 3 initial tiles: suburb, community park, heavy factory)
- Population track (shared board tracking each player's population — this IS the score track)
- Income marker and Reputation marker (per player, tracked on their player board)
- Goal tiles (public and secret goals)
- Coins (money supply)
- 1-player tile stack markers (A, B, C)
- Lake tiles (basic tiles always available)

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a player board showing the starting borough: **Suburb** (residential), **Community Park** (civic), and **Heavy Factory** (industrial) pre-placed.
2. Set each player's **Income** to 0 and **Reputation** to 1 on their player board dials/tracks.
3. Each player starts with **$15**.
4. Set each player's **Population** marker to **2** on the population track.
5. Separate building tiles into **A**, **B**, and **C** stacks (A = early game, C = late game with "1 More Round" tile in the C stack). Shuffle each stack. Stack them: C on bottom, B in middle, A on top.
6. Deal tiles from the top of the combined stack to fill the **real estate market** (7 face-up tiles in a row, with cost modifiers: the leftmost tiles cost more, the rightmost are cheapest or even discounted).
7. Place 3 basic **Lake tiles** in a separate always-available area.
8. Deal **public goal tiles** (visible to all) and **1 secret goal tile** per player (private objectives).
9. Determine first player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns clockwise. On your turn:

### Step 1: BUY A TILE
Choose one of these options:
- **Buy a tile from the Real Estate Market**: Pay the tile's base cost PLUS the market position premium. Tiles on the left cost $10 more, tiles in the middle are at face value, tiles on the right may cost $2 less. Immediately place the tile in your borough.
- **Buy a basic Lake tile**: Always available for $0. Lakes provide +2 income bonus for each adjacent tile already in your borough.
- **Buy from the market but place face-down as a Lake**: Any market tile can be purchased and placed as a generic lake (ignoring its printed ability), useful for cheap income boosts.

### Step 2: PLACE THE TILE
Place the purchased tile adjacent to any existing tile in your borough:
- Tiles must be placed edge-to-edge with at least one existing tile.
- **Adjacency bonuses/penalties trigger immediately** — many tiles care about what's next to them.
- **"When placed" effects trigger once** (e.g., "When placed, gain $5 for each adjacent Residential tile").
- **Some tiles have ongoing global effects** (e.g., "Each time any player places a Residential tile, gain +1 Reputation"). These trigger for the rest of the game.

### Step 3: ADJUST INCOME AND REPUTATION
After placing, check if any income (green $) or reputation (blue star) icons were activated by the new tile or its interactions. Adjust your markers accordingly.

### Step 4: COLLECT INCOME AND ADJUST POPULATION
- **Collect money equal to your current Income** value. (If income is negative, you pay the bank — or lose $1 per negative point you can't pay.)
- **Adjust Population by your current Reputation** value. Move your population marker up (or down if reputation is negative) by your reputation number.

### Step 5: SLIDE MARKET TILES
Slide all remaining market tiles to fill the gap, then draw a new tile from the stack to refill the rightmost (cheapest) position.

### Red Line Penalties:
- The population track has **red lines** at certain thresholds (e.g., population 5, 10, 15, etc.).
- **Each time your population crosses a red line going UP**, your Income decreases by 1 AND your Reputation decreases by 1.
- This simulates the challenges of growth — more people means more infrastructure strain.
- This is a critical balancing mechanism. Growing too fast without economic support can send you into a death spiral.

### Tile Types and Interactions:
- **Residential (green)**: Generate reputation (population growth). Want to be near parks, away from industry.
- **Commercial (blue)**: Generate income. Often benefit from being near residential.
- **Industrial (yellow)**: Generate income, often at the cost of reputation. Cheap but unpleasant.
- **Civic (grey)**: Varied effects. Parks, schools, airports — many have special abilities.

### Investment Markers:
- Some tiles provide investment markers allowing you to invest in other players' boroughs (placed on their tiles). You gain benefits when those tiles activate.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when the **"1 More Round"** tile is drawn from the C stack. Complete that round plus one more full round.

**Final Scoring:**
- Your **Population** (position on the population track) IS your base score.
- **Public goals**: Each public goal awards bonus population to the player(s) who best meet the condition (e.g., "most Residential tiles," "fewest Industrial tiles," "most money remaining"). Typically **+10 or +15 population** per goal met (only the leader, or in some versions, proportional).
- **Secret goal**: Each player reveals their secret goal tile and scores it (e.g., "most Civic tiles" — worth a significant population bonus, typically around +10-20).
- **Coins**: Every **$5 remaining** = +1 population.

**The player with the highest population wins.** Tiebreaker: most money.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Red lines are the most important mechanism to understand.** Every time you cross one, you lose 1 income AND 1 reputation permanently. Plan your growth to ensure your economy can sustain it.
- **Income before reputation in the early game.** You need money to buy good tiles. A strong early income lets you afford premium tiles later.
- **Tile placement is permanent.** Think about adjacency carefully — a poorly placed industrial tile can tank your reputation for the rest of the game.
- **Watch other players' boroughs.** Tiles with "all players" effects (e.g., Fast Food Restaurant: "-1 reputation for each player including you each time any player builds residential") affect everyone. Buying these tiles can be both offensive and engine-building.
- **Lake tiles are powerful and underrated.** A free tile that provides +2 income per adjacent tile is an excellent economic foundation.
- **Goals drive your strategy.** Check public goals AND your secret goal at the start and build toward them. Goals can be worth +10 to +20 population.
- **The C stack tiles are the most powerful** but also most expensive. Ensure you have income to afford them.
- **Don't neglect reputation.** It's your population growth rate. Even +1 reputation per turn over 15 turns is +15 population (victory points).
- **Negative income/reputation is a downward spiral.** If you go negative on either, correct it immediately.
- **In 2-player games,** there's less competition for tiles but "all players" effects hit your single opponent harder (relatively).
