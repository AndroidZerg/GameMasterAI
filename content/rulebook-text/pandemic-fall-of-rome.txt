# Game: Pandemic: Fall of Rome
# Publisher: Z-Man Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 45-60 min | Weight: 2.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Pandemic: Fall of Rome reimagines the Pandemic system with players as Roman leaders defending the empire from barbarian invasions. Instead of curing diseases, players must forge alliances with or battle against five barbarian tribes (Visigoths, Vandals, Ostrogoths, Anglo-Saxons, and Huns) that march toward Rome along invasion paths. The game retains Pandemic's cooperative tension while adding combat dice and an alliance-forging mechanic.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board showing the Roman Empire with cities connected by roads, plus invasion paths
- 5 Barbarian tribe cubes in different colors (Visigoths=green, Vandals=blue, Ostrogoths=yellow, Anglo-Saxons=purple, Huns=black)
- Barbarian wedge tokens (represent larger forces)
- Player pawns (Roman leaders)
- Roman Legion tokens (wooden soldiers)
- Player cards: City cards (matching cities on board), Event cards, Revolt cards
- Invasion cards (equivalent to Infection cards — determine where barbarians appear)
- Alliance tokens for each tribe
- Battle dice (custom dice with Swords and Eagle/Legion results)
- Role cards with unique abilities
- Decline tracker (equivalent to Outbreak tracker)
- Invasion rate marker

## SETUP
1. Place the board. Set the Decline tracker to 0. Set the Invasion rate to the starting position (2 cards per turn).
2. Create the Invasion deck — draw initial invasion cards to seed barbarians on the board:
   - Draw 3 cards: place 3 cubes of the matching tribe on each city drawn.
   - Draw 3 cards: place 2 cubes on each.
   - Draw 3 cards: place 1 cube on each.
3. Place starting Roman Legions in Rome and a few other key cities as specified.
4. Each player selects a Role and takes the role card with its unique ability. Place pawns in Rome.
5. Shuffle the Player card deck. Deal starting hands: 4 cards each (2-3 players), 3 cards each (4 players), 2 cards each (5 players).
6. Divide the Player deck and shuffle in Revolt cards (equivalent to Epidemic cards) — number depends on difficulty: 4 (Introductory), 5 (Standard), 6 (Heroic).
7. Place the 5 Alliance tokens nearby — not yet forged.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns. Each turn has 4 phases:

### Phase 1: Take 4 Actions
Choose from:
- **March (Move)**: Move to an adjacent connected city. You may take Legions from your city with you.
- **Sail**: Discard a City card to move to that city (or from any port to any port).
- **Recruit Legions**: Add Legions to your current city (take from supply; limited supply of ~20 Legions).
- **Battle**: Fight barbarians in your city. Roll battle dice equal to the number of Legions present (max typically ~3 dice per battle action). Each Sword result kills 1 barbarian cube. Each Eagle result also kills 1 but you lose 1 Legion too. Blank faces = miss. You can battle multiple times per turn if you have actions remaining.
- **Forge Alliance**: If you have 5 City cards matching a single tribe's color, discard them to forge an alliance with that tribe. Place the Alliance token. All cubes of that tribe on the board are immediately removed, and that tribe no longer invades. This is equivalent to "curing a disease."
- **Share Knowledge**: Exchange City cards with another player in the same city (standard Pandemic trading rules — must be in the city matching the card, or the role's special ability applies).

### Phase 2: Draw 2 Player Cards
- Draw 2 cards from the Player deck.
- Hand limit is 7 cards — discard down if exceeded.
- **Revolt cards** (when drawn): Resolve immediately.
  1. Move the Invasion rate marker up one step (future turns draw more invasion cards).
  2. Draw the bottom card of the Invasion discard pile — place 3 barbarian cubes in that city. If the city already has 3+ cubes, a **Sack** occurs (see below).
  3. Shuffle the Invasion discard pile and place it ON TOP of the Invasion deck (barbarians return!).

### Phase 3: Barbarian Invasion
- Draw Invasion cards equal to the current Invasion rate (starts at 2, increases with Revolts).
- For each card drawn, add 1 barbarian cube of the matching tribe to the matching city. Barbarians enter from the edges along invasion paths.
- If a city already has 3 cubes of any color and would gain another: a **Sack** occurs — the Decline marker advances 1, the excess cube is placed in an adjacent city (cascading like outbreaks in standard Pandemic), and if that city also has 3+, chain sacking occurs.

### Phase 4: Barbarian Migration
After placing new barbarians, existing barbarian cubes may migrate toward Rome along the invasion paths. Specific migration rules move cubes along their tribal path toward the center of the empire.

### Sacking Rome
If Rome itself is sacked (gets more than 3 cubes), it has immediate consequences and brings the game closer to defeat.

## SCORING / WINNING
**Players Win if:**
- They forge Alliances with ALL 5 barbarian tribes (collect and discard sets of 5 matching-color City cards for each).

**Players Lose if:**
- The Decline tracker reaches the end (too many sackings).
- The Player card deck runs out (time runs out).
- You need to place barbarian cubes but the supply of that tribe's color is exhausted.
- Rome is sacked too many times (specific threshold).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Legions are limited — there are only about 20 in the supply. If all Legions are on the board or eliminated, you cannot recruit more.
- Battle dice are risky: Eagle results kill a barbarian but cost you a Legion. Plan for attrition.
- Forging an Alliance removes ALL cubes of that tribe permanently — this is extremely powerful and the primary win condition.
- Unlike standard Pandemic, you fight barbarians (not just remove cubes) — this adds a push-your-luck combat element.
- Migration means barbarians actively advance toward Rome each turn. The Huns are especially aggressive.
- Common mistake: Neglecting Rome's defense — barbarians converge there and sacking Rome is devastating.
- Common mistake: Spending too many actions battling when you should be collecting cards for Alliances.
- Roles matter a lot: the Consul can recruit Legions more efficiently, the Magister Militum commands more dice in battle, the Mercator trades cards more easily.
- Events cards are powerful — save them for critical moments.
- In solo mode, you control multiple roles.
