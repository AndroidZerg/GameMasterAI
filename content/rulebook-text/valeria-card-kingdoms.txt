# Game: Valeria: Card Kingdoms
# Publisher: Daily Magic Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 30-45 min | Weight: 2.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Valeria: Card Kingdoms is an engine-building card game set in a fantasy kingdom under siege by monsters. Players recruit citizens, slay monsters, and build domains using a dice-activation mechanic similar to Machi Koro — dice rolled on EVERY player's turn can activate your citizen cards, generating resources. The goal is to accumulate the most victory points through diverse strategies.

## COMPONENTS
- Citizen cards (5 stacks, numbered 1-10, each with unique resource-generation abilities)
- Monster cards (5 stacks in a center row, each worth VP and loot)
- Domain cards (buildings that grant VP and special powers)
- Duke cards (secret end-game scoring objectives)
- 2 Six-sided dice
- Resource tokens: Gold, Magic (mana), Strength
- Player boards / reference cards
- Event cards (optional)

## SETUP
1. Lay out 5 stacks of Citizen cards in the center (randomly selected from available types, values 1-10). Each stack has multiple copies.
2. Lay out 5 stacks of Monster cards in a row (randomly selected, varying difficulty).
3. Lay out Domain cards in a market (number depends on player count, typically 5 face-up).
4. Shuffle Duke cards and deal 2 to each player. Each player secretly keeps 1 and discards the other. Dukes provide end-game scoring conditions.
5. Each player starts with: 1 Peasant card (value 1, generates gold) and 1 Knight card (value 2, generates strength), plus starting resources of 1 Gold, 1 Magic, 1 Strength.
6. Determine a starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each turn has 3 phases:

### Phase 1: Roll & Activate
The active player rolls both dice. ALL players (not just the active player) may activate citizens:

- **Individual die values**: Each die activates citizens matching that number. For example, rolling a 3 and a 5 activates all your citizens with values 3 and 5.
- **Sum of dice**: The sum of the two dice ALSO activates citizens matching that total. Rolling 3+5=8 activates your 8-value citizens too.
- **Active player bonus**: The active player gains the TOP (better) reward printed on activated citizens. All other players gain the BOTTOM (lesser) reward.
- Resources generated are Gold, Magic, and/or Strength, depending on the citizen card.

### Phase 2: Take Actions
The active player takes **2 actions** (may be the same action twice). Available actions:

- **Slay a Monster**: Pay the Strength cost shown on a face-up Monster card. Gain its VP value and any loot (Gold, Magic, or special rewards). Remove it and reveal the next monster in that stack.
- **Recruit a Citizen**: Pay the Gold cost to add a Citizen card from the center stacks to your tableau. This citizen will now activate on future dice rolls matching its number.
- **Build a Domain**: Pay the Gold and/or Magic cost to claim a Domain card. Domains provide VP and ongoing abilities (resource discounts, extra activations, wild resources, etc.).
- **Gain a Resource**: Take 1 Gold, 1 Magic, OR 1 Strength from the supply. Simple but sometimes necessary.

### Phase 3: End of Turn
- Check if any end-game conditions are met (see below).
- Pass dice to the next player clockwise.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends immediately when ANY of these conditions are met:
1. All Monster stacks are emptied (all monsters slain).
2. All Domain cards are claimed.
3. Any 2 Citizen stacks are emptied.

Final scoring:
- VP from slain Monsters
- VP from built Domains
- VP from your Duke card's secret objective (e.g., "2 VP per Domain you own," "1 VP per Monster you slain," etc.)
- Some citizens or domains grant bonus VP

Highest total VP wins. Ties broken by most total resources remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **All players activate on every roll** — this means your engine works even on opponents' turns. Recruiting citizens with common numbers (6, 7, 8 — the most likely sums on 2d6) ensures frequent activation.
- **Active player gets the better reward** — this is a significant advantage. Your own turns are more valuable.
- **Citizens stack** — if you have 2 copies of the same citizen, BOTH activate when their number is rolled. Doubling up on a key citizen is powerful.
- **Duke cards shape your strategy** — look at your Duke's scoring condition early and build toward it. A Duke that rewards Domains means you should prioritize building.
- **Monsters are the main VP source** — but they require Strength, which is harder to generate than Gold. Balance your citizen purchases accordingly.
- **Domains provide combo potential** — some domains give you extra actions, resource conversion, or activate like extra citizens. They can dramatically boost your engine.
- **Resource management** — there's no hand limit on resources. Stockpiling for an expensive monster or domain is valid.
- **Magic is the rarest resource** — fewer citizens generate it. Domains often cost Magic, so plan ahead if you want to build many domains.
- **The game can end suddenly** — monitor the Monster/Domain/Citizen supply. Don't get caught building an engine when someone triggers the end.
- **Number coverage is key** — try to have citizens covering a wide range of numbers (2-12) so every roll generates something for you.
