## OVERVIEW
Kutna Hora: The City of Silver is a historical city-building euro game set in the medieval Bohemian mining town of Kutna Hora, famous for its silver mines. Players develop the city by constructing buildings, mining silver ore, and managing a dynamic economy where supply and demand directly affect the prices of goods. The innovative economic system where every player action affects market prices is the game's signature feature.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 main game board (city grid, mine area, economy board with price tracks)
- 4 player boards
- Building tiles (various types: residential, commercial, civic, religious)
- Mine tiles/cards
- Economy board with sliding price markers for multiple goods (ore, food, wood, etc.)
- Silver ore tokens and refined silver tokens
- Worker meeples in 4 player colors
- Coin tokens (Groschen — the local currency)
- Guild cards
- Technology/upgrade tiles
- St. Barbara's Cathedral progress track
- Reputation track
- Round marker
- Various resource tokens

## SETUP
1. Place the main game board centrally. Set the economy board beside it with all price markers at their starting positions.
2. Lay out the city grid — place any starting building tiles as indicated.
3. Prepare the mine area with mine tiles arranged according to the setup rules.
4. Each player takes a player board, starting money (varies by player order — later players get more), workers in their color, and any starting resources.
5. Shuffle and deal guild cards for the initial draft or display.
6. Place St. Barbara's Cathedral track at its starting position.
7. Set the reputation track markers for each player.
8. Prepare the supply of building tiles, organized by type.
9. Determine starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over a series of rounds. Each round consists of players taking turns performing actions until all players have passed.

### On Your Turn
Choose ONE of the following actions:

#### 1. Construct a Building
- Select an available building tile and place it on an empty space in the city grid.
- Pay the construction cost in money and/or resources.
- **Building types** provide different benefits:
  - **Residential**: Increase population, generate income. More population drives demand on the economy board.
  - **Commercial/Production**: Produce goods (food, wood, etc.) or provide services. These affect supply on the economy board.
  - **Civic/Guild**: Provide special abilities, victory points, or ongoing effects.
  - **Religious**: Contribute to St. Barbara's Cathedral and provide reputation.
- Building placement matters — adjacency bonuses and neighborhood effects apply.
- When you build, the SUPPLY AND DEMAND on the economy board shifts, potentially changing prices for everyone.

#### 2. Mine Silver
- Send workers to the mine area to extract silver ore.
- Mining has a cost (pay workers, equipment).
- Ore can be refined into silver, which can be sold or used for construction.
- The mine has depth levels — deeper mines yield more ore but cost more to operate.
- Mine tiles may deplete over time.

#### 3. Trade / Sell at Market
- Buy or sell goods on the economy board at current market prices.
- **The dynamic economy**: Every transaction moves the price marker. Selling goods increases supply and lowers the price. Buying goods increases demand and raises the price.
- This is the core innovation — your trade actions directly affect what other players will pay on future turns.
- Timing your trades is critical: sell when prices are high, buy when low.

#### 4. Hire Guild Members / Acquire Abilities
- Recruit guild members from the available pool.
- Guild members provide ongoing special abilities, production bonuses, or end-game scoring.
- May require paying money or meeting prerequisite conditions.

#### 5. Contribute to St. Barbara's Cathedral
- Invest resources and/or money toward building the famous cathedral.
- Advancing on the cathedral track provides reputation and significant end-game victory points.
- This is a competitive area — positions on the cathedral track are relative to other players.

#### 6. Pass
- When you pass, you are done for the round. You may receive income based on your buildings and holdings.
- Once all players pass, the round ends.

### Economy Board (Key Mechanism)
- Multiple goods have price tracks (e.g., ore, food, wood, silver).
- Prices range from very low to very high.
- When a player PRODUCES a good (builds a production building, mines ore), supply increases and the price drops.
- When a player CONSUMES a good (builds something requiring resources, feeds workers), demand increases and the price rises.
- All players buy and sell at the CURRENT market price, which is constantly shifting.
- This creates a deeply interactive economy: overproducing a good crashes its price; scarcity drives prices up.

### Round End
- Collect income from buildings (residential income, commercial profits).
- Pay upkeep costs (worker wages, building maintenance).
- The economy may adjust based on round-end rules.
- Check for game-end conditions.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends after a set number of rounds or when specific conditions are met (e.g., city grid is full, cathedral is complete, mine is exhausted — exact trigger varies).

**Final Scoring includes:**
- Victory points accumulated during the game (from building, trading, cathedral contributions).
- Reputation track position — bonus points based on your standing.
- St. Barbara's Cathedral contributions — major end-game points for top contributors.
- Guild card scoring conditions.
- Money remaining (converted to VP at a rate, typically 5:1 or similar).
- Building portfolio — bonus points for sets or specific configurations of buildings.
- Penalties for unmet obligations or debts.

The player with the most victory points wins. Tiebreaker: most money remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- The dynamic economy is everything. Watch what other players are producing and consuming. If everyone mines silver, the price will crash — diversify or time your sales before the market floods.
- Building placement in the city grid matters for adjacency bonuses. Plan your neighborhoods.
- Early buildings generate value over more rounds. Front-load construction when possible.
- St. Barbara's Cathedral is a significant source of end-game points — don't ignore it, but don't overspend on it either.
- Cash management is tight. Upkeep costs can surprise you if you overextend with buildings and workers.
- Guild members are powerful force multipliers. Get them early to compound their benefits.
- In a 2-player game, the economy is more volatile (each action has a bigger impact on prices). In 4-player, prices move more frequently but in smaller relative terms.
- Pay attention to the timing of when you sell goods — selling right before someone else needs to buy them at a high price is ideal.
- Mining is a long-term investment. The ore must be extracted, refined, and sold — plan the full chain.
- The game rewards efficiency and timing more than any single dominant strategy.
