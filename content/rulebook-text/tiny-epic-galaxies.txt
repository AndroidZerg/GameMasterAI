# Game: Tiny Epic Galaxies
# Publisher: Gamelyn Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 30-45 min | Weight: 2.2/5

## OVERVIEW
Tiny Epic Galaxies is a dice-driven space empire game where players roll custom dice to take actions like moving ships, gathering resources, colonizing planets, and upgrading their galaxy. Players race to accumulate victory points by colonizing planets and upgrading their empire. A key "follow" mechanic lets players spend culture to copy other players' dice actions on their opponents' turns.

## COMPONENTS
- 5 Galaxy mats (one per player, showing empire level and ship slots)
- 7 Custom dice (action dice with 6 faces)
- Planet cards (the central market of planets to colonize)
- Energy tokens and Culture tokens
- Ship tokens (rockets) for each player
- Score track / VP tokens
- Solo variant cards (Rogue Galaxy)

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a Galaxy mat and places it at Level 1 (which gives 2 ships, 1 die to roll — actually Level 1 = 4 dice, 2 ships. See empire track).
2. Each player starts with 1 Energy and 1 Culture.
3. Shuffle the Planet deck and reveal a number of planets equal to the number of players + 2 (e.g., 5 planets for 3 players).
4. Each player places their ships on their Galaxy mat.
5. Determine a starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn:

### 1. Roll Dice
- Roll a number of dice equal to your empire level (Level 1 = 4 dice, Level 2 = 5, Level 3 = 6, Level 4 = 7).
- You may **re-roll** any number of dice up to 2 additional times (for a total of up to 3 rolls, like Yahtzee). Lock in dice you want to keep.

### 2. Activate Dice
Resolve your dice one at a time, in any order. The 6 die faces are:

- **Move Ship**: Move one of your ships to a planet (landing zone or colonization track) or back to your Galaxy mat. Ships on planets can use the planet's special ability or work toward colonizing it.
- **Acquire Energy**: Gain energy equal to the number of your ships on planets that produce energy (crystal symbol). Minimum 1 energy if no ships are on energy planets.
- **Acquire Culture**: Gain culture equal to the number of your ships on planets that produce culture (flag symbol). Minimum 1 culture if no ships are on culture planets.
- **Advance Colonization (Diplomacy)**: Move one of your ships one step forward on the **diplomacy** colonization track of the planet it occupies.
- **Advance Colonization (Economy)**: Move one of your ships one step forward on the **economy** colonization track of the planet it occupies.
- **Utilize a Colony**: Activate the special power of one planet you have already fully colonized. Colonized planets are tucked under your Galaxy mat.

### 3. Converter
- At any time during your turn, you may spend **1 Energy** to convert a die to any face you choose (instead of its rolled result). This is very powerful but costly.

### Follow Mechanic (Other Players' Turns)
- When another player activates a die, any OTHER player may immediately spend **1 Culture** to "follow" — they perform the same action as if they had rolled that die themselves.
- This keeps all players engaged on every turn and is a key strategic element.
- You can follow multiple dice in a single opponent's turn (spending 1 Culture each time).

### Colonizing a Planet
- Each planet has a colonization track (either Diplomacy or Economy type) with a number of steps (typically 3-6).
- When your ship advances past the final step, you **colonize** the planet: take the planet card, tuck it under your Galaxy mat, gain its VP, and return your ship to your mat.
- The planet's ongoing ability is now available to you via the "Utilize a Colony" die face.
- A new planet is drawn from the deck to replace the colonized one.

### Upgrading Your Empire
- Spend Energy and/or Culture (costs increase per level) to upgrade your Galaxy from Level 1 to Level 4.
  - Level 1: 4 dice, 2 ships (starting)
  - Level 2: 5 dice, 3 ships (cost: varies, ~3 resources)
  - Level 3: 6 dice, 3 ships (cost: ~5 resources)
  - Level 4: 7 dice, 4 ships (cost: ~7 resources)
- Each upgrade also grants victory points printed on the mat.

## SCORING / WINNING
- The game end is triggered when any player reaches **21 victory points** (or the threshold for the player count).
- Finish the current round so all players have equal turns.
- VPs come from: colonized planet values + empire level bonuses + any secret mission cards (if using that variant).
- Highest VP total wins. Ties broken by most colonized planets, then most total resources.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Following is the most important mechanic** — culture is extremely valuable because it lets you take extra actions on every opponent's turn. Never let your culture drop to 0 if you can help it.
- **Energy for converting dice** is powerful but expensive. Save it for critical moments when you need a specific die face to colonize.
- **Upgrade early** — more dice = more actions per turn = compounding advantage. Rushing to Level 2 or 3 is usually strong.
- **Planet abilities matter** — some colonized planet abilities are game-changing. Evaluate planets by both their VP value AND their ability.
- **Block opponents** — only one ship can be on each colonization track step at a time (in some variants). Landing on a planet someone else is colonizing can slow them down.
- **Resource planets vs. VP planets** — balance between colonizing high-VP planets and planets that produce the resources you need.
- **Solo mode** uses a Rogue Galaxy with an automated opponent. It's well-regarded and plays in about 20 minutes.
- **Re-rolling is free** — don't settle for mediocre dice. Use all 3 rolls to optimize.
