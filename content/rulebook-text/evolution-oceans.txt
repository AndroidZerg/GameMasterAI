# Game: Oceans
# Publisher: North Star Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.6/5

## OVERVIEW
Oceans is an evolution-themed engine-building card game where players create and adapt ocean species to survive in a dynamic ecosystem. Species must feed from the shared ocean (the Reef) or prey upon other species to grow their population. The game features two phases — a more structured early game (the Cambrian Explosion hasn't happened yet) and an explosive late game where powerful Deep cards become available.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Ocean board (with Reef zone holding fish tokens)
- 120 Surface cards (12 copies each of 10 basic trait types)
- 89 Deep cards (unique, powerful traits — used in the Cambrian Explosion phase)
- Fish tokens (wooden, representing food/population)
- Species boards (cardboard with population/aging tracks — unlimited supply, take as needed)
- Scenario cards (optional, for variant setups)
- Bonus tokens
- Cambrian Explosion marker

## SETUP
1. Place the Ocean board in the center. Fill the Reef with a starting number of fish tokens based on player count (e.g., in a 2-player game start with approximately 40 fish).
2. Shuffle the Surface deck and deal **6 Surface cards** to each player.
3. Shuffle the Deep deck and place it face-down near the board. Deep cards are NOT available until the Cambrian Explosion.
4. Each player creates **1 starting species** (take a species board, set population to 1).
5. Determine first player randomly.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, perform these steps in order:

### Step 1: PLAY CARDS (optional)
You may play **1 card** from your hand as a trait on one of your species (before or after the Cambrian Explosion, this limit may change):
- **Surface cards** are always playable. Each species can hold a limited number of trait cards.
- **After the Cambrian Explosion**, you may also play **Deep cards** (powerful unique abilities).
- Traits define how your species feeds, defends, or scores.
- Alternatively, you may play a card face-down to **create a new species** (new species board, population starts at 0, the card is discarded).

### Step 2: FEED ONE SPECIES
Choose one of your species and feed it using one of these methods:
- **Forage**: Take fish from the **Reef** zone on the board and place them on your species (number depends on traits — default is take 1 fish).
- **Attack**: If your species has an attack trait (like "Apex Predator"), you can prey on another species (yours or an opponent's). Remove population from the target species and gain fish for yours.
- Some traits modify feeding (e.g., filter feeding, parasitism, bottom feeding).

Fish placed on a species increase its population track.

### Step 3: AGING
**Every species you own** must age:
- Each species loses 1 population (1 fish is removed from it and placed in your **score pile** behind your player screen).
- This is how you score points — aging converts population into VP.
- If a species reaches 0 population, it goes **extinct**. Remove the species board and all its trait cards to the discard pile.

### Step 4: DRAW CARDS
Draw cards to refill your hand:
- Before Cambrian Explosion: Draw from the Surface deck only.
- After Cambrian Explosion: You may draw from either the Surface or Deep deck.
- Hand size depends on game phase and number of species (typically draw until you have a target number, or draw a set amount).

### THE CAMBRIAN EXPLOSION
- The Cambrian Explosion is triggered when the Reef's fish supply drops below a threshold (or a set number of fish have been scored collectively — varies by player count).
- Once triggered, the game shifts: Deep cards become available, and players may play **2 cards per turn** instead of 1.
- Deep cards are unique and dramatically powerful — they can break normal rules.
- The game accelerates significantly after the Cambrian Explosion.

### Key Surface Trait Types:
- **Filter Feeder**: Gain extra fish from the reef when foraging.
- **Apex Predator**: Attack other species to feed.
- **Symbiosis**: Gain food when an adjacent species feeds.
- **Shell/Ink Cloud**: Defensive traits that protect against attacks.
- **Speed**: Various benefits to feeding or defense.
- **Bottom Feeder**: Feed from the deep ocean instead of the reef.
- **Transparent**: Avoid being targeted by predators.
- **Schooling**: Population size provides defense bonuses.

### Reef Ecosystem:
- Each round, fish naturally flow into the Reef from the Ocean zone (the supply).
- If the Reef is overfished, less food is available for foraging species.
- The ecosystem self-balances: fewer fish in the reef = harder to forage = predation becomes more attractive.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when the **Ocean supply runs out of fish** or the **Surface and Deep decks are both exhausted**.

**Final Scoring:**
- Each fish in your **score pile** (behind your screen from aging) = 1 VP.
- Each fish still on your **living species** = 1 VP.
- Bonus tokens earned during the game = listed VP value.
- Some Deep cards provide end-game scoring bonuses.

**The player with the most VP wins.** Tiebreaker: most living species.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Aging is how you score.** A species with high population that ages many turns generates lots of VP. Keep your species alive and fed.
- **Don't over-invest in one species.** Extinction is devastating — you lose all trait cards and any built-up engine.
- **Predation is risky but powerful.** Attacking opponents' species can cripple their scoring while feeding yours, but defensive traits can backfire on you.
- **The Cambrian Explosion changes everything.** Plan for it. If you're too slow building your engine, opponents who leverage Deep cards will outpace you.
- **Watch the Reef.** When fish are scarce, foragers starve. Diversify your feeding strategies.
- **Creating new species costs a card and a turn of 0 population.** Time it carefully — you need to feed the new species before it ages to 0 and goes extinct.
- **Species can have at most approximately 3-4 traits** (verify exact limit). Choose synergistic traits.
- **Deep cards are unique and game-warping.** Reading them when drawn helps you plan dramatic combos.
- **2-player games** tend to be more predatory and interactive. With 4 players, the Reef depletes faster.
- **The game is from the creators of Evolution** but is a standalone redesign, not an expansion.
