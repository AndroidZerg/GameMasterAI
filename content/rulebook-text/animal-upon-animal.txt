# Game: Animal Upon Animal (Tier auf Tier)
# Publisher: HABA
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 15 min | Weight: 1.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Animal Upon Animal is a children's dexterity stacking game where players take turns carefully placing wooden animal figures on top of a growing, wobbling stack. The first player to get rid of all their animals wins. It is simple enough for young children (ages 4+) yet entertaining for all ages due to the physical challenge of balancing oddly shaped pieces.

## COMPONENTS
- 29 wooden animal figures total:
  - 1 large crocodile (the base of the stack)
  - 28 stackable animals (7 different species, 4 of each): hedgehog, penguin, toucan, monkey, snake, sheep, lizard
- 1 six-sided die with special symbols

## SETUP
1. Place the crocodile in the center of the table — it serves as the base/foundation.
2. Each player receives 7 animals (one of each type) and places them in a pile in front of them.
3. The youngest player goes first.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn:
1. **Roll the die.** The die has 6 faces with the following symbols:
   - **1 dot**: Place 1 of your animals on the stack.
   - **2 dots**: Place 2 of your animals on the stack (one at a time).
   - **Hand**: Give 1 of your animals to another player, who must place it on their next turn(s). That player chooses which animal to place.
   - **Snake**: Place the snake — you may extend the base by placing your animal adjacent to the crocodile (touching it nose-to-tail), effectively widening the foundation. Only snakes and long animals typically benefit from this.
   - **Crocodile (?)**: The other players collectively decide which of YOUR animals you must place on the stack (they pick the hardest one).

2. **Place your animal(s)**: Carefully stack the required animal(s) onto the pile. You may only use one hand. Animals must be placed on TOP of the stack (on other animals), not directly on the table or crocodile (unless the snake/crocodile face was rolled).

3. **If animals fall off**: If any animals tumble off the stack during your placement:
   - You must take back up to 2 of the fallen animals and add them to your personal supply.
   - Any remaining fallen animals beyond 2 are returned to the box (removed from the game).
   - The animal you were trying to place (if it fell) counts toward the fallen animals.

4. Play passes clockwise.

## SCORING / WINNING
- The first player to successfully place ALL of their animals (have none left in their personal supply) wins the game.
- There is no point scoring — it is a race to empty your supply.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- You may only use ONE hand to place animals.
- Animals must be placed on top of the existing stack (on other animals), not on the crocodile base, unless the snake symbol was rolled.
- The snake is uniquely shaped and can be draped over other animals, making it one of the easier pieces to place.
- The hedgehog and penguin are relatively flat and stable — good early placements.
- The toucan and monkey are trickier due to their irregular shapes.
- If the entire stack collapses, you still only take a maximum of 2 fallen animals.
- Strategy tip: When you roll the "hand" symbol and give an animal to an opponent, give them your most awkward-shaped animal.
- When opponents choose which animal you must place (crocodile symbol), they will pick the hardest to balance.
- Younger children may play with fewer animals per player (e.g., 5 instead of 7) for a shorter game.
- The game teaches fine motor skills, patience, and spatial reasoning in a fun, tactile way.
