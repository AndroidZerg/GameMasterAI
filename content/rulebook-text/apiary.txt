# Game: Apiary
# Publisher: Stonemaier Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 3.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Apiary is a worker-placement game set in a far-future universe where evolved spacefaring bees build hive civilizations across the galaxy. Players manage worker bees of varying strength levels (1-4), placing them on action spaces to explore planets, gather resources, advance technology, and build their hive. The signature mechanic is that workers grow stronger each time they're used, eventually "hibernating" when they reach maximum strength — creating a satisfying lifecycle management puzzle.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 main game board (with action spaces around the perimeter and central area)
- Player hive mats (individual player boards with storage, dance floor, and comb areas)
- Worker bee tokens (in player colors, with strength dials/values 1-4)
- Planet tiles (revealed during exploration)
- Faction tiles (unique player powers)
- Resource tokens: Pollen, Water, Fiber, and Honey (4 resource types)
- Seed cards (development cards)
- Farm tiles (placed on hive mat)
- Carve tiles (placed on hive mat)
- Dance tiles
- Frames (for the hive comb)
- Queen tiles (end-game scoring objectives)
- Hibernation comb (where maxed-out workers go)
- VP tokens
- Automa cards (solo mode)

## SETUP
1. Place the main game board centrally.
2. Each player selects a faction and takes the corresponding hive mat and faction tile. Each faction has a unique asymmetric ability.
3. Each player starts with **worker bees at strength 1-2** (typically 2-3 starting workers at varying strengths).
4. Distribute starting resources (small amounts of pollen, water, fiber — varies by faction and turn order).
5. Seed the planet display with face-up planet tiles.
6. Shuffle and prepare seed card deck, farm tiles, and carve tiles.
7. Place resource tokens in the supply.
8. Deal Queen tiles (end-game objectives — some public, one secret per player).
9. Determine turn order (later players receive compensation bonuses).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, perform **one** of two options:

### Option A: PLACE A WORKER
Take one of your available workers and place it on an action space on the board:

#### Worker Strength Mechanic:
- Each worker has a **strength value from 1 to 4**.
- When placing on an **occupied space** (another player's worker is there), your worker must be **equal to or higher strength** than the occupant.
- The displaced (bumped) worker returns to its owner with **+1 strength** (it grows from being bumped).
- If a worker reaches **strength 4 and would be bumped again** (or is recalled at strength 4), it **hibernates** — it's placed in your hibernation comb and scores VP at game end but is permanently retired from the workforce.

#### Action Spaces:

1. **EXPLORE** — Draw planet tiles and choose one to claim. Planets provide resources, VP, or ongoing bonuses. Place the planet tile near your hive mat.

2. **GROW** — Recruit new workers or increase worker strength:
   - Add a new worker to your supply at strength 1.
   - Or increase an existing worker's strength by 1.
   - Worker management is critical — you need enough workers for actions but they eventually hibernate.

3. **RESEARCH** — Acquire seed cards (development cards):
   - Draw from the seed deck or take from a display.
   - Seed cards provide VP, immediate bonuses, or ongoing effects when planted.

4. **ADVANCE** — Advance on a technology track or gain special abilities:
   - Multiple advancement tracks offering various bonuses.
   - Reaching milestones on tracks grants immediate rewards and end-game VP.

5. **FARM** — Collect resources:
   - Gain pollen, water, fiber, or honey based on the farm action space chosen and worker strength.
   - Stronger workers gather more resources.

6. **CARVE** — Build structures in your hive:
   - Place carve tiles onto your hive mat.
   - Carved sections provide VP, storage, or ability bonuses.
   - Spatial placement matters on your hive mat.

7. **CONVERT** — Trade resources or perform resource conversion:
   - Exchange resources at various rates.
   - Honey is the premium resource (hardest to get, most versatile).

### Option B: RETRIEVE ALL WORKERS
Instead of placing a worker, take ALL your workers back from the board to your supply:
- Each retrieved worker gains **+1 strength**.
- Workers at strength 4 that are retrieved **hibernate** (retire to your comb for VP).
- This is your "reset" — you need to retrieve to get workers back for future placement.
- **Timing your retrieval is crucial.** Retrieve too early, you waste potential actions. Too late, you run out of workers.

### Hive Mat Management:
Your hive mat has several areas:
- **Storage**: Limited space for resources. You cannot hold more than your storage capacity.
- **Dance Floor**: Dance tiles placed here provide ongoing abilities.
- **Comb**: Frames and carve tiles placed here form your hive's structure, providing VP and bonuses.
- **Hibernation Comb**: Where retired (strength 4+) workers go. Each hibernated worker scores VP.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends after a set number of rounds (determined by the hibernation trigger — when a certain number of workers have collectively hibernated across all players, or the planet/seed supply runs out).

**Final Scoring:**
- **Hibernated workers**: Each worker in your hibernation comb = VP (value varies, typically 2-4 VP each).
- **Hive comb structures**: VP from carve tiles and frames placed on your hive mat.
- **Planets collected**: VP printed on planet tiles.
- **Seed cards**: VP from planted/completed seed cards.
- **Technology tracks**: VP from advancement positions.
- **Queen tiles**: VP from meeting Queen objective conditions (public and secret).
- **Remaining resources**: Small VP conversion (varies — honey is worth more than basic resources).
- **Dance tiles**: Some provide end-game VP bonuses.

**Highest VP wins.** Tiebreaker: most resources remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Worker lifecycle is THE strategic puzzle.** Plan when workers will hibernate. Losing your strongest workers permanently is a tradeoff — you get VP but lose productivity.
- **Bumping is not purely negative.** When your worker is bumped, it comes back stronger. Sometimes you WANT to be bumped to power up a worker for free.
- **Time your retrievals carefully.** Retrieving powers up ALL workers (and hibernates 4s). Do it when the board state is unfavorable or when you need fresh workers.
- **Don't hoard workers.** Having many weak workers is less effective than having a few strong ones placed strategically.
- **Storage limits are real.** Build storage capacity early or you'll waste resources that exceed your limit.
- **Honey is the premium resource.** It's harder to acquire but can substitute for other resources and is worth more at game end.
- **Queen tiles shape your strategy.** Check your secret Queen objective early and build toward it. Public Queens are contested — evaluate whether to compete.
- **Faction asymmetry matters.** Lean into your faction's unique ability. Each faction excels at a different strategy.
- **Solo mode** uses an Automa system typical of Stonemaier Games — card-driven automated opponent.
- **At higher player counts (4-5)**, the board gets crowded quickly and bumping becomes more frequent. Prioritize having high-strength workers to ensure access to key spaces.
