# Game: Xia: Legends of a Drift System
# Publisher: Far Off Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 60-180 min | Weight: 3.2/5

## OVERVIEW
Xia: Legends of a Drift System is a sandbox-style space adventure game where players captain their own starship, exploring a modular board of hex tiles representing a drift system full of planets, asteroids, nebulae, and other dangers. Players earn Fame Points through trading, mining, combat, exploration, missions, and more — the first to reach the Fame Point goal wins. The game emphasizes player freedom: you choose your own path to victory.

## COMPONENTS
- Modular hex sector tiles (starting sectors + exploration tiles)
- Miniature starships (5 ship minis, one per player, with corresponding ship mats)
- Ship mats with energy tracks and cargo/outfit slots
- Outfit cards (engines, shields, blasters, missiles, hold expansions, etc.)
- Mission cards, Title cards, Event cards
- Exploration tokens
- Cargo cubes (multiple types representing different goods)
- Credit tokens (currency)
- Fame Point markers and track
- Custom dice (d6, d8, d12, d20 used for movement and combat)
- Damage markers, Ice Damage markers
- NPC ships (enforcer, scoundrel, merchant — in some editions)

## SETUP
1. Place the starting sector tiles in the center of the table as shown in the rulebook (typically 3-4 starting tiles forming the initial map).
2. Shuffle the remaining sector tiles face-down as the exploration deck.
3. Each player selects a Tier 1 ship (the smallest class), takes the corresponding ship mat and miniature.
4. Each player receives starting credits (typically 1,000 credits) and places their Fame Point marker at 0.
5. Shuffle and place the Mission deck, Title deck, and Event deck nearby.
6. Sort Outfit cards by tier and type, making them available for purchase.
7. Place cargo cubes in a general supply.
8. Determine the Fame Point goal based on player count (commonly 5 FP for a shorter game, up to 20 for a full game; the rulebook recommends varying thresholds).
9. Each player places their ship on the spawn point of a starting planet.
10. Determine first player randomly.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

Each player's turn consists of three phases:

### Phase 1: Action Phase
The active player takes actions by spending Energy from their ship's energy track. Actions include:

**Movement**: Spend energy to activate your engine. Roll the movement die associated with your engine (better engines roll higher dice — d6, d8, d12, d20). Move up to that many hexes. Moving into asteroid fields or nebulae may cause damage or other effects.

**Attack**: Spend energy to fire a weapon (blaster or missile). Blasters require line of sight and roll an attack die against the target. Missiles can sometimes arc. The target can spend energy on shields to roll defense dice. Damage is the difference between attack and defense rolls. Damage markers are placed on the target's ship mat, disabling outfits.

**Abilities**: Some outfits or ship abilities cost energy to activate.

**Pick Up / Drop Off Cargo**: When on a planet or station hex, you can pick up cargo cubes that planet produces (for free or at a cost) or sell cargo that the planet demands (earning credits). Buy low, sell high — different planets produce and consume different goods.

**Mine**: When adjacent to or on an asteroid field, you can mine for cargo cubes. Roll a die; on certain results you gain specific cargo, on others you may take damage.

**Complete a Mission**: If you are at the location specified on a mission card you hold and meet its requirements, you can complete it for Fame Points and/or credits.

### Phase 2: Business Phase
After completing actions, you may conduct business if your ship is on a planet or space station:
- **Buy Outfits**: Purchase outfit cards and install them on your ship mat. Each ship has limited slots.
- **Sell Outfits**: Sell installed outfits back for partial credit value.
- **Upgrade Ship**: Trade in your current ship plus credits for a higher-tier ship (Tier 2 or Tier 3). Higher-tier ships have more energy, more outfit slots, more cargo space, and better base stats.
- **Repair**: Pay credits to remove damage markers from your ship.
- **Draw Missions**: Draw mission cards (usually up to a hand limit).
- **Respawn (if destroyed)**: If your ship was destroyed, you respawn at a planet during this phase with a new Tier 1 ship and some starting credits (you lose some Fame Points).

### Phase 3: Status Phase
- Recharge your energy track to full.
- Check if any end-of-turn effects trigger.
- If an Event is triggered (certain conditions or tiles), resolve the Event card.
- Pass the turn to the next player.

### Exploration
When you fly your ship off the edge of a known sector tile into unexplored space, draw a new sector tile and place it. You gain 1 Fame Point for exploring a new sector. Some tiles have special features (gates, dead zones, unique planets).

### Combat Details
- When you destroy another player's ship, you gain 1 Fame Point and may salvage some of their cargo.
- Destroyed players lose 1 Fame Point (minimum 0) and respawn next turn.
- Bounty system: attacking peaceful players may place a bounty on you; destroying a bountied player is worth extra FP.

### Titles
Title cards are earned by being the first to achieve certain milestones (e.g., first to destroy another ship, first to complete a mission, first to reach a certain sector). Each Title is worth 1 Fame Point.

## SCORING / WINNING
- The first player to reach the agreed-upon Fame Point goal wins immediately.
- Fame Points are earned by: exploring new sectors (1 FP each), completing missions (1-3 FP), destroying other ships (1 FP), earning Titles (1 FP each), and occasionally through special events or achievements.
- If a player is destroyed, they lose 1 FP (minimum 0).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Energy management is critical**: You start each turn with full energy, but every action costs energy. Plan your turn carefully — don't blow all your energy on movement and have none left for combat or mining.
- **Ship upgrades matter enormously**: Tier 1 ships are fragile and slow. Prioritize earning credits to upgrade to Tier 2 as quickly as possible.
- **Cargo trading is the safest income**: Learn which planets buy what, and establish trade routes.
- **Rolling a 1 on movement in a dangerous area (asteroid/nebula)**: This can cause damage. Don't fly through asteroids in a damaged ship.
- **Blind jumps**: You can fly off the map edge to explore, but the new tile might be dangerous.
- **The bounty system**: Attacking players who haven't attacked anyone puts a bounty on your head. This can make you a target.
- **Respawn penalty**: Losing your ship costs 1 FP and resets you to Tier 1. Avoid destruction if you're close to winning.
- **Mission cards are private**: Don't reveal your missions until you complete them.
- **Multiple paths to victory**: You can win by pure trading, pure combat, exploration focus, or any mix. Adapt your strategy to your ship and the board state.
