# Game: PARKS
# Publisher: Keymaster Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 40-70 min | Weight: 2.1/5

## OVERVIEW
PARKS is a beautifully illustrated game celebrating the U.S. National Parks system. Players take on the role of hikers walking along trails throughout the four seasons of a year, collecting memories (resources) along the way and using those resources to visit National Parks. The game features stunning artwork from the Fifty-Nine Parks Print Series and combines light resource management with a unique trail mechanism.

## COMPONENTS
- Trail tiles (site tiles that form the hiking trail each season)
- Hiker pawns (2 per player)
- Resource tokens: Mountains (grey), Forests (green), Sunshine (yellow), Water (blue), Wildlife (red/brown)
- Park cards (each depicting a real National Park with a cost and VP value)
- Gear cards (provide ongoing abilities)
- Canteen cards (convert water into other benefits)
- Year cards (4 cards that add a special rule each season)
- Season board
- Camera token
- First player marker (lantern)
- Campfire tokens (allow sharing trail sites)
- Trail-end tile and Trailhead tile
- Personal supply boards (optional, for resource organization)
- Photo tokens

## SETUP
1. Place the Trailhead tile at the start and the Trail End tile at the end.
2. Shuffle the basic trail site tiles and lay out a trail based on the season (Season 1 has fewer tiles; the trail grows each season by adding 1 more tile).
3. Each player places both of their Hiker pawns on the Trailhead.
4. Each player takes 1 Canteen card (from a drafted selection or randomly dealt).
5. Shuffle the Park cards and lay out 3 face-up Parks available for visiting.
6. Shuffle Gear cards and display 3 face-up.
7. Place all resource tokens in a general supply.
8. Reveal the Year card for Season 1 (this adds a special trail tile or rule).
9. Give each player a Campfire token (flipped to the fire side — unspent).
10. Determine the first player; give them the first player lantern and the Camera.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

The game is played over 4 seasons. Each season, all hikers walk along the trail from the Trailhead to the Trail End.

### On Your Turn:
Move ONE of your two hikers forward along the trail to any unoccupied site (you must move forward, never backward):

1. **Choose a hiker**: Pick one of your two hikers that hasn't yet reached the Trail End.
2. **Move forward**: Move that hiker to any site ahead of it on the trail. You may skip over sites. You CANNOT land on a site occupied by another hiker (yours or an opponent's) UNLESS you spend your Campfire token (flip it to the spent side). Each player can only use their Campfire once per season.
3. **Resolve the site**: Gain the benefit shown on the trail site. Common site benefits include:
   - Gain specific resource tokens (Mountains, Forests, Sunshine, Water, Wildlife)
   - Exchange one resource for another
   - Draw a Canteen card
   - Reserve a Park card
   - Take photos (use the Camera)
4. **Fill your Canteen (optional)**: At any point during your turn, you may spend 1 Water to activate your Canteen card's ability (gain resources, VP, etc.). Canteens can be used once per season, then flip to show they're used.

### Trail End
When a hiker reaches the Trail End, the player must choose ONE of three actions:
- **Visit a Park**: Pay the resource cost shown on an available Park card to claim it (it's worth VP).
- **Buy Gear**: Pay the resource cost of an available Gear card. Gear provides an ongoing ability for the rest of the game.
- **Reserve a Park**: Take a face-up Park card into your personal reserve (you can visit reserved Parks later).

After choosing, your hiker is done for the season.

### Season End
When all hikers have reached the Trail End:
1. Return all hikers to the Trailhead.
2. Add one new trail site tile to the trail (making the trail longer each season).
3. Reveal the next Year card (adding another special site or rule).
4. Refresh Canteens (flip them back to the ready side).
5. Refresh Campfire tokens.
6. Refill the Park and Gear displays.
7. Pass the first player lantern (and Camera) to the next player.

### The Camera
The player with the Camera can take photos at certain sites (marked with a camera icon) by spending 1 resource of any type to gain a Photo token. Photos are worth 1 VP each at game end.

### Gear
Gear cards provide permanent abilities such as: gaining an extra resource when visiting certain site types, converting resources more efficiently, or earning bonus VP for certain actions.

### Resources and Wild
Wildlife tokens (red/brown) are wild — they can substitute for any 1 resource when visiting Parks. Resources come in five types matching natural features: Mountains, Forests, Sunshine, Water, Wildlife.

## SCORING / WINNING
At the end of Season 4 (after all hikers reach the Trail End for the fourth time):
- **Park cards**: Each Park card is worth its printed VP value (typically 1-4 VP).
- **Photos**: Each Photo token = 1 VP.
- **Personal bonus**: In some variants, the player who visited the most Parks gets a bonus.
- The player with the most VP wins.
- Ties broken by most resources remaining, then most Gear cards.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Two hikers give you flexibility**: You can "leapfrog" your hikers — move one far ahead to grab a key site, then move the other to pick up sites you skipped. This is the core tactical decision.
- **Campfire is precious**: You only get one shared-site use per season. Save it for when you absolutely need a contested site.
- **The trail grows each season**: Seasons 3 and 4 have the longest trails with the most sites. Plan resource collection around the Parks you want to visit.
- **Reserve Parks early**: If you see a Park you want, reserve it before someone else visits it. Visiting from your reserve is just as valid.
- **Wildlife is wild**: Wildlife tokens are the most flexible resource. Don't undervalue them.
- **Canteens are strong**: Getting Water to fuel your Canteen each season is often efficient.
- **Common mistake**: Trying to move a hiker backward or staying on the same site. Hikers must always move forward.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting you can only use the Campfire once per season (not once per hiker).
- **Gear compounds over 4 seasons**: Gear bought in Season 1 gives you 3 more seasons of benefit. Prioritize early Gear purchases.
- **End-game Parks are expensive but high-VP**: Save premium resources for Season 3-4 Parks worth 3-4 VP.
- **Watch opponents' resources**: If someone is stockpiling for a specific Park, consider visiting it or reserving it first.
