# Players: 1-6 | Time: 120-180 min | Weight: 3.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Arkham Horror (Third Edition) is a cooperative Lovecraftian investigation game where players take on the roles of investigators working together to solve mysteries and prevent an Ancient One from devastating the city of Arkham. The game features a modular board built from neighborhood tiles, a scenario-driven narrative system with branching storylines, and a streamlined action system compared to earlier editions. Each scenario tells a unique story with multiple possible endings.

## COMPONENTS
- Modular neighborhood tiles (5 double-sided tiles that form the game board)
- Investigator sheets (12+ unique investigators with different stats and abilities)
- Scenario sheets and Codex (branching narrative documents)
- Ancient One sheets
- Monster tokens and monster cards
- Clue tokens
- Doom tokens and doom track
- Anomaly tokens
- Spell cards, Item cards, Ally cards, Condition cards
- Mythos cup (opaque bag) with scenario-specific tokens
- Event cards (headline events that occur each round)
- Action tokens
- Health and Sanity tokens
- Damage and Horror tokens
- Dice (custom d6 with success/failure/special faces: blank, 1 success, 1 success + elder sign)
- Reference cards

## SETUP
1. Choose a scenario from the scenario guide. The scenario dictates which Ancient One you face, which neighborhood tiles to use, and how to arrange them.
2. Build the modular game board by placing the specified neighborhood tiles. Each tile shows several neighborhoods (e.g., Easttown, Merchant District, Rivertown, etc.) with streets connecting them.
3. Place starting clue tokens, anomaly tokens, and monster tokens on the board as directed by the scenario.
4. Each player chooses an investigator. Take that investigator's sheet, starting possessions (items, spells, allies as listed), and set starting Health and Sanity values.
5. Place investigator figures on their starting locations (as specified or player's choice).
6. Prepare the Mythos cup by adding the tokens specified by the scenario.
7. Shuffle and prepare all card decks (Spells, Items, Allies, Conditions, Event cards).
8. Set the Doom track to its starting position.
9. Read the scenario introduction aloud.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each round consists of 4 phases:

### Phase 1: Action Phase
Each investigator, in any agreed order, takes up to 2 actions (from the following):

**Move**: Move your investigator to an adjacent neighborhood (connected by a street). You may move through multiple neighborhoods if you spend multiple actions on movement.

**Research**: If you are in a neighborhood with a clue token, attempt to gather it. Perform an Observation test (roll dice equal to your Observation skill). Successes let you claim clue tokens. Clues are needed to advance the scenario and solve mysteries.

**Attack**: If you are in a neighborhood with a monster, engage it in combat. Perform a Strength test (melee) or other relevant skill test depending on your weapon. Apply damage to the monster; the monster may fight back (see Monster Phase).

**Ward**: Attempt to close an anomaly in your neighborhood. Perform a Lore test. Successes remove anomaly tokens. Anomalies spawn monsters and cause bad effects if left unchecked.

**Focus**: Gain 1 focus token (can be spent later to add +1 die to any skill test, or to reroll dice). Max focus varies by investigator.

**Component Actions**: Use an action granted by a card you possess (an item, spell, ally, or condition may provide unique actions).

**Investigate/Interact**: Some scenario-specific locations allow special interact actions (reading a codex entry, talking to an NPC, etc.).

### Phase 2: Monster Phase
- All monsters on the board activate.
- Monsters in the same neighborhood as an investigator ATTACK: the investigator suffers damage (reduce Health) and/or horror (reduce Sanity) based on the monster's stats.
- Monsters not engaged with investigators may MOVE toward the nearest investigator (or follow other movement rules on their card).
- Some monsters have special abilities (flying, spawning, etc.).
- **Engagement**: When a monster and investigator share a space, they are engaged. Investigators must fight or evade to disengage.
- **Evade**: Instead of fighting, an investigator can attempt an Agility test to evade a monster, allowing them to move away without being attacked.

### Phase 3: Encounter Phase
- Each investigator in a neighborhood without monsters may have an ENCOUNTER.
- Draw the top card of the neighborhood deck matching your location type (or use a scenario-specific encounter).
- Encounter cards present narrative situations with skill tests and choices. Outcomes range from gaining items, allies, or clues to suffering damage, horror, or gaining harmful conditions.
- Encounters are a primary way to gain resources and advance personal stories.

### Phase 4: Mythos Phase
- Draw 2 tokens from the Mythos cup.
- Each token has an effect:
  - **Doom tokens**: Advance the Doom track (bringing the Ancient One closer to awakening).
  - **Clue tokens**: Place new clues on the board (per scenario rules).
  - **Monster tokens**: Spawn new monsters.
  - **Anomaly tokens**: Spawn anomalies.
  - **Headline token**: Draw and resolve a Headline event card (global effects — new threats, rule changes, etc.).
  - **Scenario-specific tokens**: Trigger narrative events.
- After resolving tokens, return them to the cup (some stay out — depends on the token type and scenario).
- If the Doom track reaches its threshold, the scenario may escalate or the Ancient One may awaken.

### Skill Tests
- When a test is required, roll a number of dice equal to your relevant skill (Strength, Agility, Observation, Lore, Influence) plus any modifiers (items, focus tokens, ally bonuses).
- Each die has 3 possible faces: blank (failure), 1 success, or 1 success + Elder Sign (counts as success plus may trigger special card effects).
- You need a certain number of successes (set by the test difficulty) to pass.
- Focus tokens: spend 1 to either add 1 die to the roll or reroll any number of dice.

### Scenario Progression / Codex System
- Each scenario has a Codex — a branching narrative document.
- When specific conditions are met (enough clues gathered, specific encounters completed, doom thresholds reached), the scenario directs you to read a specific Codex entry.
- Codex entries advance the story, change the board (add/remove tiles, tokens, monsters), and present new objectives.
- Scenarios have MULTIPLE possible endings depending on player performance and choices.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win condition**: Complete the scenario's objectives before the doom track runs out or before the Ancient One achieves its goal. This typically involves gathering enough clues, solving mysteries (narrative checkpoints), and ultimately confronting the main threat.
- **Lose conditions**: All investigators are eliminated (Health or Sanity reduced to 0 — an investigator reduced to 0 Health is defeated; reduced to 0 Sanity goes insane). If all investigators are eliminated, the game is lost. OR the Doom track completes and the Ancient One's victory condition is met.
- There is no point scoring — it is a binary win/loss cooperative game. Some scenarios have "degrees" of success depending on the ending achieved (best ending vs. bittersweet ending).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Prioritize anomalies — they spawn additional monsters every round if left unchecked, creating a snowball effect.
- Clue gathering is essential. Without clues, you cannot advance the scenario. Spread out to cover clue-rich neighborhoods.
- Health and Sanity are both critical. Losing either eliminates you. Carry healing items and avoid unnecessary fights.
- Focus tokens are extremely powerful. Stockpile a few before difficult tests.
- Coordinate movement — don't leave investigators isolated. Monsters can overwhelm a lone investigator.
- Read encounter cards carefully. Some choices seem minor but have major consequences in the branching narrative.
- Each investigator has strengths: fighters should handle monsters while researchers gather clues. Play to your investigator's best stats.
- The Mythos cup becomes more dangerous as the game progresses. Speed matters — don't waste rounds.
- Spells are powerful but often carry risk (backlash effects on failed rolls).
- The scenario system provides high replay value — the same scenario can play out very differently based on choices and the Codex branches taken.
- At higher player counts (5-6), the game runs longer and more monsters appear. Consider this when deciding group size.
- Investigators who are defeated may continue as a new investigator (restart with a new character, losing all previous progress).
