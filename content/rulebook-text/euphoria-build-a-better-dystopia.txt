# Game: Euphoria: Build a Better Dystopia
# Publisher: Stonemaier Games
# Players: 2-6 | Time: 60 min | Weight: 3.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Euphoria is a dice-based worker placement game set in a dystopian future where players are faction leaders using workers (dice) to gain resources, build markets, and advance their authority. Your workers are dice — their face values represent their knowledge (awareness). If your workers become too smart, they realize they're being exploited and leave. The first player to place all 10 of their authority tokens wins.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 game board featuring 4 faction territories (Euphorian, Subterran, Wastelander, Icarite) plus a central area
- Worker dice: each player gets 2 starting dice (workers) plus up to 2 more that can be gained during the game, in their player color
- 48 Recruit cards (ethical and hidden recruits with faction allegiances)
- 36 Artifact cards (6 types: Bear, Bat, Balloons, Bifocals, Book, Box)
- Resource tokens: Gold, Stone, Clay (commodity resources); Bliss, Food, Water, Energy (allegiance/basic resources)
- Knowledge tokens (track for worker awareness)
- Morale track markers
- 10 Authority stars per player (the stars you place to win)
- Market tiles (building markets with penalties/benefits)
- Progress tokens for faction tracks (Euphorian, Subterran, Wastelander, Icarite tunnels)
- Miner meeples (for the faction tunnel tracks)
- Ethical dilemma cards
- Multiplier markers

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center.
2. Each player takes their 4 worker dice, 10 authority stars, and morale/knowledge markers of their color.
3. Each player rolls 2 of their worker dice — these are your starting workers. Set the other 2 aside (they can be gained later).
4. Place morale and knowledge markers on the starting positions of their respective tracks (typically morale at 1 and knowledge at 1 — check specific setup rules).
5. Deal each player 1 Ethical Recruit card (kept face-up — this is your active recruit with a known ability) and 1 Hidden Recruit card (kept face-down until revealed).
6. Distribute starting resources based on player count and faction allegiance.
7. Place commodity resources (Gold, Stone, Clay) in the appropriate territory markets.
8. Shuffle and place Artifact cards in their deck area.
9. Set up Market tiles (face-down in construction zones — these will be built during the game).
10. Place miner meeples on the starting positions of the tunnel tracks.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### On Your Turn
You must do ONE of the following:

#### Option A: PLACE A WORKER
Take one of your available worker dice, roll it, and place it on an unoccupied (or shared, depending on the space) action space on the board. Then perform the action of that space.

**Action spaces include**:

**Commodity Territories** (Euphorian, Subterran, Wastelander):
- **Generator/Farm/Mine spaces**: Place a worker to gain the territory's basic resource (Energy, Food, Water respectively). The amount may depend on how many workers are already there or your morale.
- **Commodity spaces**: Use basic resources to acquire commodities (Gold, Stone, Clay).
- **Construction sites**: Pay resources to help build a Market. When a Market is completed, all contributors place an authority star on it. Players who did NOT contribute face the Market's penalty (ongoing negative effect).
- **Tunnel spaces**: Place a worker to advance the faction tunnel track. When the tunnel is complete, all players with workers on that track gain a bonus and the faction allegiance bonus triggers.

**Icarite Cloud Territory**:
- Cloud spaces let you gain Bliss and trade it for Artifact cards or use special Icarite actions.

**Central/Shared Spaces**:
- **Artifact Market**: Trade sets of Artifact cards for authority star placement.
- **Allegiance tracks**: Some spaces let you advance faction allegiance.

#### Option B: RETRIEVE WORKERS
Take back ALL of your workers from the board to your active pool. Then:
1. Roll all retrieved workers.
2. **Knowledge check**: Sum the face values of ALL your workers (active + any on the board if applicable). If the total exceeds your knowledge threshold (based on your morale track), your smartest worker (highest die) LEAVES — remove it from the game temporarily (it goes back to the unused worker pool). This represents your worker figuring out the dystopia is terrible and escaping.
3. You may pay resources or use abilities to mitigate knowledge gain.

### Key Mechanics

**Worker Dice Values**:
- When you place or retrieve a worker, you roll it. The face value (1-6) matters:
  - **Lower values = dumber workers = safer**. Low totals avoid the knowledge check penalty.
  - **Higher values = smarter workers = risky** but may provide bonuses on certain spaces (some actions give better rewards for higher die values).
  - Some spaces have a **doubles bonus**: if you place a worker and its value matches another worker already on that space, you get a bonus action.

**Morale Track**:
- Determines how many Recruit/Artifact cards you can hold.
- Higher morale = more cards in hand.
- If morale drops to 0, you lose cards and suffer penalties.

**Knowledge Track**:
- Determines your knowledge threshold for the worker escape check.
- Higher knowledge threshold = workers can know more before escaping.
- But gaining knowledge is risky — you want to balance it.

**Recruits**:
- Each player has an Ethical Recruit (face-up, ability always active) and a Hidden Recruit (face-down).
- You may reveal your Hidden Recruit during the game when its trigger condition is met, gaining a powerful one-time or ongoing ability.
- Recruits have faction allegiances that interact with the faction allegiance tracks.

**Faction Allegiance**:
- Each faction (Euphorian, Subterran, Wastelander, Icarite) has an allegiance track.
- Advancing a faction's track benefits all players aligned with that faction.
- Allegiance bonuses include: extra resources, authority star placement, special abilities.

**Markets (Building)**:
- Markets are built cooperatively (any player can contribute resources to construction).
- When completed, contributors place authority stars. Non-contributors suffer the Market's penalty for the rest of the game (e.g., "you cannot use the Artifact Market," "you pay double for commodities").
- Avoiding Market penalties is a strong motivation to participate in construction.

**Artifact Sets**:
- Collecting sets of Artifact cards (specific combinations) allows you to trade them in to place an authority star.
- Different sets are required at different locations on the board.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **First player to place all 10 of their authority stars wins immediately.**
- Authority stars are placed by:
  1. Helping build Markets (1 star per Market you helped construct)
  2. Trading in Artifact card sets
  3. Reaching faction allegiance milestones
  4. Completing certain special actions or Recruit abilities
  5. Other board spaces that award star placement

- There is no second place. The game ends immediately when someone places their 10th star.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Manage worker knowledge**: The sum of your dice values determines if a worker escapes. Keep totals low when possible. Losing a worker is a significant setback.
- **Markets are critical**: The penalty for not contributing to a Market can be crippling. Always watch what's being built and contribute when possible.
- **Balance morale and knowledge**: Both tracks affect your capabilities. Don't neglect either.
- **Hidden Recruits are powerful**: Reveal them at the right moment for maximum impact. Study your Recruit's trigger condition and plan around it.
- **Doubles bonuses**: When placing a worker on a space with another worker showing the same number, you get a bonus. This can be extremely valuable — sometimes it's worth rerolling or choosing a space specifically for the doubles match.
- **Faction allegiance is a shared benefit**: Advancing a faction helps all aligned players. Cooperate when it benefits you, but watch who else benefits.
- **Don't hoard resources**: Resources are means to an end (placing authority stars). Convert them efficiently.
- **The Icarite territory** is often overlooked but offers unique advantages through Bliss and Artifact manipulation.
- **Worker dice are random**: You can't control what you roll. Build strategies that are resilient to variance.
- **Aggression through Markets**: One of the best offensive strategies is racing to complete Markets that penalize opponents who didn't contribute.
- **2-player games** play differently (tighter, more direct) than 5-6 player games (more competition for spaces, faster Market building).
