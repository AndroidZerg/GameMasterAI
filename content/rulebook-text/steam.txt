# Game: Steam: Rails to Riches
# Publisher: Mayfair Games
# Players: 3-6 | Time: 90 min | Weight: 3.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Steam: Rails to Riches is a strategic train and economic game where players build rail networks, deliver goods cubes to cities, and manage income versus victory points on a clever dual-track system. Designed by Martin Wallace, it is the refined successor to Age of Steam, featuring a tight economic model where players must balance taking income (money) against converting deliveries into victory points. Every delivery forces a choice: points or cash?

## COMPONENTS
- Game board (double-sided with different maps; base game typically includes the Northeast USA map and a European map)
- Track tiles (straights, curves, crossovers, towns — various types for building rail connections)
- Goods cubes (colored wooden cubes representing cargo: red, blue, purple, yellow, black)
- City tiles (placed on the map, showing which color goods they accept)
- Goods growth tiles / demand markers
- Player locomotives (train tokens in player colors)
- Income/VP track (shared track around the board edge — one marker for income, one for VP)
- Turn order tokens
- Action selection tiles (roles each round)
- Money tokens

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center. Choose which map side to use.
2. Place initial goods cubes on cities as indicated by the map setup (each city starts with 1-3 cubes of specific colors).
3. Each player starts with: an income marker at position 0 on the income track, a VP marker at position 0, and a starting amount of money (varies by map — often $10-15).
4. Each player takes a locomotive marker set to Level 1 (can deliver goods across 1 link).
5. Shuffle and prepare goods growth tiles for future rounds.
6. Place track tiles in organized piles by type.
7. Determine initial player order randomly.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game typically lasts 6-8 rounds (depending on map). Each round:

### Phase 1: Goods Growth
Add new goods cubes to the board. Draw goods growth tiles or roll to determine which cities receive new cubes and of what color. This replenishes the supply of deliverable goods.

### Phase 2: Select Actions (Role Selection)
Players bid for turn order / select special actions. In the standard game:
- Players bid money for turn order position (first player gets first pick of actions, but pays more).
- Each player selects ONE special action for the round:
  - **First Move**: You deliver goods before other players (critical for snatching cubes).
  - **First Build**: You build track before other players.
  - **Engineer**: Build an extra track tile this round (normally you build 2-3 tiles; this lets you build an additional one).
  - **Locomotive**: Upgrade your locomotive by 1 level for free (normally costs money to upgrade).
  - **Urbanization**: Place a new city tile on a town space, converting it into a city that accepts specific goods colors.
  - **Production**: Place 2 new goods cubes on cities of your choice.
  - **Turn Order**: Guarantee a specific turn position.

Each action can only be selected by one player per round.

### Phase 3: Build Track
In turn order, each player builds 2-3 track tiles (depending on game version and map). Rules:
- Track tiles must connect to existing track or a city.
- Each tile costs money ($2-4 typically, varies by terrain — mountains and rivers cost more).
- Track must form valid connections (no dead ends within a tile, proper connections at edges).
- Players may build through towns (small hexes) but towns only connect, they don't accept goods.
- You can build over other players' track — track is shared infrastructure. But the builder of a link is marked as its owner.

### Phase 4: Deliver Goods
In turn order (modified by First Move action), players deliver goods cubes.

**Delivery rules:**
- Pick up a goods cube from a city and deliver it to another city that matches the cube's color.
- The delivery route must follow connected track links from the origin city to the destination city.
- The number of links you can traverse is limited by your locomotive level (Level 1 = 1 link, Level 2 = 2 links, etc., up to Level 6).
- You may use track links built by ANY player — track is shared.
- Each delivery scores 1 point on the income/VP track per link used in the delivery route. You choose whether to move your INCOME marker or your VP marker by that amount. This is the critical economic decision.
- Each link used generates $1 for the link's owner (if another player built a link you use, they get paid).
- A player may make multiple deliveries per turn (as many as they can legally execute).

### Phase 5: Income and Expenses
- Each player receives money equal to their income track position.
- Each player pays expenses: $1 per locomotive level they have.
- Players may also pay to upgrade their locomotive (cost varies, often $2-3 per level).
- If you cannot pay expenses, you must reduce your income marker (potentially going into debt).

### Phase 6: Cleanup
Advance the round marker. If the game is over, proceed to final scoring.

## SCORING / WINNING
- VP are tracked on the VP track throughout the game (from choosing VP during deliveries).
- At game end, the income track position is converted to VP at a reduced rate (income position / 2, rounded down, added as VP) in some versions, or simply VP track alone determines the winner.
- The player with the most VP wins.
- Ties broken by most money remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Income vs. VP is THE decision**: Every delivery forces you to choose. Early game, you need income to fund track building and locomotive upgrades. Late game, you need VP. The transition timing is critical.
- **Locomotive upgrades are essential**: A Level 1 locomotive can only deliver across 1 link — very limiting. Upgrade to Level 3-4 as quickly as affordable to reach distant, profitable deliveries.
- **Track ownership pays off**: When other players deliver across your track links, you get $1 per link used. Building in high-traffic corridors generates passive income.
- **Goods scarcity**: There are limited goods on the board. Delivering a cube removes it. Don't assume goods will still be there next round — the First Move action lets you grab them before opponents.
- **Urbanization is powerful**: Creating a new city opens up new delivery destinations and can make previously useless goods cube colors deliverable.
- **Going into debt is dangerous**: If your expenses exceed income, your income marker drops. This creates a death spiral. Keep income ahead of locomotive costs.
- **Common mistake**: Building expensive track without a plan to deliver goods along it immediately. Every round of unused track is wasted investment.
- **Common mistake**: Neglecting to upgrade the locomotive. You can't make long, high-value deliveries without it.
- **Common mistake**: Choosing VP too early. Without income, you can't build track or upgrade, and you fall behind economically.
- **Map differences**: Different maps play very differently. Some have tight corridors, others have open terrain. Adjust strategy accordingly.
