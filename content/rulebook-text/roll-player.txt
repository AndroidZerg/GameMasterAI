# Game: Roll Player
# Publisher: Thunderworks Games
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Roll Player is a dice-drafting, character-creation game where each player builds an RPG character by placing dice into attribute rows on their character sheet. Players draft colored dice to fill Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma rows, trying to hit target attribute scores while also purchasing skills, traits, weapons, and armor from the market. The game is essentially the character-creation mini-game of an RPG turned into a full board game.

## COMPONENTS
- Character sheets (double-sided player boards with 6 attribute rows, 3 slots each)
- Class cards (determine target attribute score values)
- Backstory cards (determine which color dice you want in which rows)
- Alignment card and alignment tracker (grid of 9 positions)
- 73 custom dice in 5 colors (gold, red, green, blue, purple — each color associated with certain attributes)
- Market board with 4 card slots
- Market cards: Skills, Traits, Weapons, Armor
- Gold tokens (currency)
- Charisma tokens
- Initiative cards (determine draft order based on dice value)

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a character sheet (player board). The board has 6 rows (attributes), each with 3 dice slots.
2. Deal each player 1 Class card (determines target values for each attribute — e.g., Strength should total 14).
3. Deal each player 1 Backstory card (shows a pattern of dice colors across the grid — matching the pattern scores VP).
4. Each player takes an Alignment card, placing their marker in the center of the 3x3 alignment grid.
5. Roll initial dice and distribute: Roll a set number of dice, each player drafts in order and places them. (Specific setup depends on player count — 2p: 5 dice per attribute setup round; 3p: 4 dice; 4p: 3-4 dice).
6. Players begin with their attribute rows partially filled from setup.
7. Prepare the market: Shuffle skill, trait, weapon, and armor decks separately. Place 1 card of each type face-up in the 4 market slots.
8. Place gold supply and remaining dice in the bag.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game lasts a number of rounds until all players have filled all 18 dice slots on their character sheets (6 attributes x 3 slots). Each round:

### Phase 1: Roll Dice
Draw dice from the bag equal to the number of players + 1. Roll them all and place them on the initiative cards in ascending order (lowest die on card 1, next on card 2, etc.).

### Phase 2: Draft Dice
In player order, each player selects one initiative card, taking the die on it. The initiative card number also determines that player's market shopping position this round (lower initiative number = earlier market access but a lower-value die, typically).

### Phase 3: Place Die
Place your drafted die into any open slot on your character sheet. When placed, the die triggers that attribute's special power:

- **Strength (Row 1)**: Flip any one die on your sheet to its opposite face (1<->6, 2<->5, 3<->4).
- **Dexterity (Row 2)**: Swap any two dice on your character sheet (they stay in the same rows but switch columns, or switch between rows).
- **Constitution (Row 3)**: Increase or decrease any one die on your sheet by 1 (cannot go below 1 or above 6).
- **Intelligence (Row 4)**: Re-roll any one die on your sheet.
- **Wisdom (Row 5)**: Move any one die from its current slot to a different open slot on your sheet.
- **Charisma (Row 6)**: Take 1 gold OR gain a charisma token (used for certain bonuses).

### Phase 4: Market
In initiative order, each player may buy ONE card from the market by paying its gold cost:
- **Skills**: Provide end-game scoring conditions or ongoing abilities. Placed below an attribute row — they often key off that attribute's total.
- **Traits**: Provide VP and shift your alignment marker one space in a direction shown on the card. You may have at most one trait (buying a new one replaces the old).
- **Weapons**: Placed in your hand slots (each player has weapon/shield slots). Provide VP, especially in sets.
- **Armor**: Placed on your character. Provides VP based on matching die colors in specific positions.

After purchasing, refill the market from the corresponding deck.

### Phase 5: Cleanup
Discard any remaining unpurchased dice. Begin next round.

## SCORING / WINNING
At game end (all dice placed), score the following:

1. **Class Card**: For each attribute, compare the total (sum of 3 dice in that row) to the target value on your class card. Exact match = most VP (varies, often 4 VP). Close matches may score less; far off scores 0.
2. **Backstory Card**: Score VP for each die that matches the color indicated on the backstory card in the correct position on your grid. (Typically 1 VP per match, up to ~12 VP.)
3. **Alignment**: Your alignment marker's final position on the 3x3 grid scores VP based on your class card (each class has a target alignment or scoring region).
4. **Armor Cards**: Score VP per armor card, often based on conditions met (e.g., matching dice colors adjacent to armor).
5. **Weapon Cards**: Score VP, often in sets or based on type combinations.
6. **Skill Cards**: Score VP based on their conditions (e.g., "2 VP per attribute that totals exactly its target").
7. **Trait Card**: Score its printed VP if conditions met.
8. **Gold**: Every 2 remaining gold = 1 VP.
9. **Charisma Tokens**: Each token = 1 VP.

Most VP wins. Ties broken by most gold remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Attribute powers fire on placement**: Every time you place a die, you MUST use the row's power (it's not optional in most cases — check your edition's rules, some make it optional).
- **Backstory colors are hidden information**: You don't have to reveal your backstory card. Other players can see your class card, though.
- **Attribute totals range**: With 3 dice (values 1-6), each attribute totals between 3 and 18. Class targets are usually 8-16.
- **Trait replacement**: You can only have one trait at a time. Buying a new trait discards the old one (and its VP).
- **Alignment shifts**: Traits and some other effects shift your alignment. Plan where you want to end up based on your class card's alignment scoring.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting to trigger the attribute power when placing a die.
- **Common mistake**: Not planning backstory colors early — it's easy to fill slots with wrong-colored dice and lose significant VP.
- **Initiative trade-off**: Taking a low-value die means you shop first in the market. Sometimes a key skill or weapon is worth the weaker die.
- **Solo mode**: Uses an automa-style system where a virtual player takes cards and dice according to specific rules.
