# Players: 1-8 | Time: 120-240 min | Weight: 3.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Eldritch Horror is a cooperative adventure game where players take on the roles of investigators traveling the globe to stop an Ancient One from awakening and destroying the world. Drawing heavily from H.P. Lovecraft's Cthulhu Mythos, the game combines narrative encounters, dice-based skill tests, and strategic resource management across a world map. Players must solve mysteries, close gates to other dimensions, and fight monsters while managing their health and sanity.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 large game board (world map with named cities and locations connected by paths)
- Ancient One sheets (Azathoth, Cthulhu, Yog-Sothoth, Shub-Niggurath in base game)
- Mystery cards (4 per Ancient One, 3 used per game)
- Investigator sheets (12 in base game) with plastic stands
- Investigator tokens and starting equipment cards
- Research Encounter cards
- Other World Encounter cards
- General Encounter cards (by region: Americas, Europe, Asia/Australia)
- Special Encounter cards
- Expedition Encounter cards
- Gate tokens
- Monster tokens (variety of Lovecraftian horrors)
- Clue tokens
- Eldritch tokens
- Spell cards, Condition cards, Asset cards, Artifact cards
- Mythos cards (3 types: green/yellow/blue)
- Health and Sanity tokens
- Travel tickets (train and ship)
- Dice (custom d6: blank and success faces)
- Doom track
- Omen track
- Reference cards

## SETUP
1. Place the world map board centrally.
2. Choose an Ancient One. Place its sheet nearby and set up its specific doom track starting value.
3. Shuffle and place the chosen Ancient One's Mystery deck. Draw the first Mystery — this is the investigators' current objective.
4. Each player chooses an Investigator, takes their sheet, starting items, health, sanity, and places their figure on their starting location.
5. Sort and shuffle all encounter decks, asset deck, spell deck, condition deck, artifact deck, and Mythos deck.
6. Build the Mythos deck according to the Ancient One's instructions (specific mix of green, yellow, blue cards for Stage I, II, III).
7. Place starting clues, gates, and monsters as indicated by the Ancient One setup.
8. Set the Doom track to the Ancient One's starting value.
9. Set the Omen track to the starting position.
10. Choose a Lead Investigator (first player).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
Each round has 3 phases:

### Phase 1: Action Phase
Each investigator, in player order, performs **2 actions** (may take the same action twice):

1. **Travel**: Move to a connected location. If crossing an ocean/long path, you need a Ship or Train ticket.
2. **Rest**: Recover 1 Health AND 1 Sanity.
3. **Trade**: Exchange possessions (assets, clues, artifacts, tickets) with another investigator on your space.
4. **Prepare for Travel**: Gain 1 travel ticket (Ship or Train) from the supply.
5. **Acquire Assets**: Attempt to gain an asset card by rolling dice equal to your Influence skill. Each success (5 or 6) provides $1 of purchasing power. Assets have costs ranging from 1-4.
6. **Component Actions**: Some cards, possessions, or game effects provide unique actions (marked with an action icon).

### Phase 2: Encounter Phase
Each investigator resolves ONE encounter based on their current location:

- **City/Wilderness (no gate/clue)**: Draw a General Encounter card for your region (Americas, Europe, Asia/Australia). Read the entry matching your location type. Typically involves a skill test with a narrative reward or penalty.
- **Clue token present**: You may take a Research Encounter instead, which advances the current Mystery.
- **Gate present**: You may encounter the Gate — draw an Other World Encounter card. These are often dangerous but can close the gate (removing it from the board) if you succeed.
- **Expedition token present**: Draw an Expedition Encounter.
- **Defeated Monster**: If you defeated a monster this round, you skip the encounter phase (VERIFY — combat may happen during encounters).

### Phase 3: Mythos Phase
The Lead Investigator draws and resolves a Mythos card. Mythos cards come in three colors:
- **Green (general effects)**: Spawn gates, monsters, and clues on the board.
- **Yellow (ongoing effects)**: Create persistent effects that last until resolved.
- **Blue (immediate effects)**: Trigger one-time events.

Mythos cards typically:
1. Advance the Omen track (moves to the next symbol).
2. Spawn a Gate and Monster at the indicated location.
3. Trigger a Reckoning effect (each Reckoning icon on active cards/monsters resolves).
4. Execute the card's specific effect.
5. **Monster Surge**: If the Omen track matches a gate's symbol, a Monster Surge occurs — additional monsters spawn at matching gates.
6. **Doom Advance**: Certain Mythos effects advance the Doom track. If Doom reaches 0, the Ancient One awakens.

### Skill Tests
- When a card or encounter calls for a skill test, you roll dice equal to your skill value (Lore, Influence, Observation, Strength, Will).
- Each die showing a 5 or 6 is a SUCCESS.
- You need a specified number of successes to pass the test.
- Spells, assets, and abilities can modify the number of dice rolled or add bonuses.
- Blessed condition: 4, 5, 6 count as successes. Cursed condition: Only 6 counts.

### Combat
When you share a space with a monster, you must fight it (during the Encounter Phase or when a monster moves onto you):
1. **Horror Check (Will test)**: Roll Will dice. If you fail, lose Sanity equal to the monster's horror value.
2. **Combat Check (Strength test)**: Roll Strength dice. You need successes equal to the monster's toughness to defeat it. If you fail, lose Health equal to the monster's damage value.
3. Defeated monsters are placed on your investigator sheet as trophies (some mysteries require monster trophies).

### Gates
- Gates appear on the board when spawned by Mythos cards.
- Each gate has an Omen symbol (matching the Omen track for surges).
- To close a gate, you must be on the gate's space and resolve an Other World Encounter successfully.
- Closing gates is essential — too many open gates can trigger additional doom or game-ending conditions.

### Investigator Defeat
- If your Health OR Sanity reaches 0, you are **defeated**.
- You are eliminated for the rest of the round. At the start of the next round, you may choose a new investigator (or the same one with fresh stats if playing casually — VERIFY).
- Possessions may be dropped at the defeat location or lost entirely (depends on the defeat type).

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win Condition**: Solve 3 Mysteries before the Doom track reaches 0. Each Mystery has specific requirements (e.g., close 2 gates, collect 3 clues at specific locations, defeat a specific monster, accumulate Eldritch tokens, etc.).
- **Loss Conditions**:
  1. The Doom track reaches 0 and the Ancient One awakens. Most Ancient Ones then present a final battle — if investigators fail this too, the world is destroyed.
  2. Azathoth has no final battle — if he awakens, you lose immediately.
  3. If the Mythos deck runs out, you may also lose (VERIFY specific condition).
  4. Some Ancient Ones have additional loss conditions.

There is no scoring system — you either save the world or you don't.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Prioritize the current Mystery**: Everything you do should advance the active Mystery objective. Don't wander aimlessly.
- **Clue tokens are precious**: Research Encounters (on clue spaces) often advance Mysteries and provide powerful bonuses. Race to claim clues.
- **Close gates early**: Open gates enable Monster Surges, which can quickly overwhelm the board. Closing gates is usually a priority.
- **Manage Health and Sanity carefully**: Resting costs actions, but being defeated is far worse. Stay topped up when possible.
- **Asset acquisition is expensive in actions**: Each purchase attempt costs an action, and you might fail the Influence test. Plan your shopping trips.
- **Split up geographically**: The board is huge. Investigators should spread out to cover multiple regions and reach clues/gates quickly.
- **Travel tickets matter**: Ship and Train tickets let you cross the world quickly. Stock up when you have downtime.
- **Spells are powerful but risky**: Many spells have Reckoning effects that can backfire.
- **The Mythos deck is your timer**: Each round, the situation gets worse. You typically have 12-15 rounds to win.
- **Player count affects difficulty**: More players means more actions but also more monsters and encounter pressure. 3-4 investigators is often considered the sweet spot.
- **Ancient One selection**: Azathoth (instant loss on awakening) is the base difficulty. Yog-Sothoth is often considered the hardest in the base game.
