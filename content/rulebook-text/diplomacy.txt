# Game: Diplomacy
# Publisher: Avalon Hill
# Players: 2-7 | Time: 240-360 min | Weight: 3.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Diplomacy is a classic strategic board game set in pre-World War I Europe where players control one of seven Great Powers vying for continental dominance. The game is unique among strategy games in that it uses NO dice or random elements — outcomes are determined entirely by negotiation, alliances, and simultaneous order resolution. Victory requires skillful diplomacy, strategic planning, and the willingness to betray allies at the right moment.

## COMPONENTS
- Large map board of Europe divided into 75 provinces (56 land, 19 sea)
- 34 supply center markers on the map
- Army and Fleet units for each of the 7 powers (wooden or plastic pieces)
- 7 Great Power cards/sheets
- Order sheets and pencils for writing secret orders
- Rulebook and reference cards

## SETUP
1. Lay out the map of Europe on the table.
2. Each player chooses (or is assigned) one of the 7 Great Powers:
   - **England**: Fleets in Edinburgh and London, Army in Liverpool (3 units)
   - **France**: Fleet in Brest, Armies in Paris and Marseilles (3 units)
   - **Germany**: Fleet in Kiel, Armies in Berlin and Munich (3 units)
   - **Italy**: Fleet in Naples, Armies in Rome and Venice (3 units)
   - **Austria-Hungary**: Fleet in Trieste, Armies in Vienna and Budapest (3 units)
   - **Russia**: Fleets in St. Petersburg (south coast) and Sevastopol, Armies in Moscow and Warsaw (4 units)
   - **Turkey**: Fleet in Ankara, Armies in Constantinople and Smyrna (3 units)
3. Place starting units on their designated home supply centers.
4. Distribute order sheets and pencils to all players.
5. The game begins in Spring 1901.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Year Structure
Each game year consists of the following phases:
1. **Spring Diplomacy Phase** — Negotiate with other players (no time limit, but tournaments use 15-30 minutes)
2. **Spring Order Phase** — All players write orders simultaneously and secretly
3. **Spring Resolution Phase** — Orders are revealed and resolved simultaneously
4. **Fall Diplomacy Phase** — Negotiate again
5. **Fall Order Phase** — Write orders
6. **Fall Resolution Phase** — Resolve orders
7. **Fall Retreat Phase** — Dislodged units retreat or are disbanded
8. **Winter Adjustment Phase** — Build or disband units based on supply center count

### Order Types
Each unit receives exactly ONE order per turn:

- **Hold**: Unit stays in place and defends with strength 1.
- **Move (Attack)**: Unit attempts to move to an adjacent province. Written as: Army Paris -> Burgundy. Strength 1 unless supported.
- **Support**: Unit adds +1 strength to another unit's hold or move. The supporting unit must be adjacent to the TARGET province. Written as: Army Munich supports Army Paris -> Burgundy. A support order is CUT if the supporting unit is attacked from any province EXCEPT the one it is supporting into.
- **Convoy**: A fleet in a sea zone can transport an army across water. Written as: Fleet English Channel convoys Army London -> Belgium. Multiple fleets can chain convoys.

### Resolution Rules
- All orders resolve SIMULTANEOUSLY — there is no turn order advantage.
- **Strength**: A move has strength 1 plus any valid supports. A hold has strength 1 plus any valid supports.
- **Conflict**: If two units move to the same province, the one with higher strength succeeds; the other bounces back. If equal strength, BOTH bounce (neither moves).
- **Dislodgement**: A unit is dislodged if attacked by a force with strength greater than its hold/defense strength. Dislodged units must retreat to an adjacent empty province (not the province the attacker came from, and not a province where a standoff occurred). If no valid retreat exists, the unit is destroyed.
- **Self-dislodgement**: You CANNOT dislodge your own units. Your own units cannot support an attack against your other units.
- **Cutting Support**: Any attack on a supporting unit CUTS its support (except an attack from the province being supported into).
- **Convoys**: If any fleet in a convoy chain is dislodged, the convoy fails and the army stays put.
- **Standoffs**: Two units of equal strength moving to the same province both fail and stay where they are. A unit already IN a province is NOT dislodged by a standoff in that province.

### Fleets vs. Armies
- **Armies** move on land provinces only (but can be convoyed across sea zones).
- **Fleets** move on sea zones and coastal land provinces. Fleets CANNOT move inland.
- Some provinces have multiple coasts (e.g., Spain has north and south coasts, St. Petersburg has north and south coasts). Fleets must specify which coast when moving to such provinces.

### Winter Adjustments
- After the Fall turn, count supply centers controlled by each power (you control a supply center if you were the last to have a unit in it during a Fall turn).
- If you have MORE supply centers than units, you may BUILD new units (armies or fleets) in your unoccupied HOME supply centers only.
- If you have FEWER supply centers than units, you must DISBAND units (your choice which).
- You can never have more units than supply centers.
- You can only build in your original home supply centers, and only if they are unoccupied.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Solo Victory**: A player wins by controlling **18 of the 34 supply centers** (a majority). This is the official victory condition.
- **Draw**: If no single player achieves 18 centers, players may vote to end the game in a draw. All players who still have units on the board share the draw equally (typically). Players with no units are eliminated and do not share in the draw.
- **Elimination**: If a player loses all supply centers, they are eliminated from the game.
- In practice, many games end in negotiated draws between 2-4 surviving powers, as achieving a solo victory requires significant dominance.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Negotiation is everything**: You can (and should) talk to every other player. Promises are NOT binding — lying and betrayal are core mechanics. Make deals, but be ready for them to be broken.
- **Support is the key tactic**: A lone unit has strength 1. Getting support from adjacent units is how you overwhelm defenders. Coordinate with allies for supported attacks.
- **Write clear orders**: Ambiguous or illegal orders are treated as Hold orders. Double-check your order syntax.
- **No randomness**: Every outcome is deterministic based on the orders written. Skill, persuasion, and strategic planning determine the winner.
- **Stalemate lines**: Experienced players know defensive formations that cannot be broken. Recognizing and establishing these is critical in the endgame.
- **Don't burn all bridges early**: Betraying an ally too soon leaves you exposed. Timing of betrayal is crucial.
- **Russia starts with 4 units** but is surrounded on all sides — it's powerful but vulnerable.
- **England is protected by water** but needs to convoy armies to the continent to win.
- **Turkey has a strong corner position** that is easy to defend but hard to break out of.
- **Watch the supply center count**: Always plan your builds. An opponent nearing 18 should trigger coalition defense.
- **Common house rule**: Many groups use a time limit on negotiation phases (15 minutes is standard in tournaments).
- **Retreats rarely matter strategically**, but Winter builds and disbands are critical decisions.
