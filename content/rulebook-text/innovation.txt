# Game: Innovation
# Publisher: Asmadi Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 45-60 min | Weight: 2.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Innovation is a fast-paced civilization card game spanning 10 ages of human history, from Prehistory to the Information Age. Players build tableaus of technology cards, using their "dogma" effects to score achievements, attack opponents, and advance through the ages. The game is highly interactive and swingy — a clever play can dramatically shift the game state. It rewards adaptability, timing, and tactical card play over long-term planning.

## COMPONENTS
- 110 cards divided into 10 ages (Ages 1-10, 10 cards per age with some variation)
- Each card shows: title, age number, color (one of 5: blue, red, green, yellow, purple), icons (6 types: crowns, leaves, lightbulbs, castles, factories, clocks), and 1-3 dogma effects
- Achievement cards (5 standard achievements + 5 special achievements)
- Score pile area for each player (cards tucked under as "points")
- Supply piles (10 draw piles, one per age)

## SETUP
1. Separate all cards by age and create 10 face-down draw piles (Age 1 through Age 10).
2. Place the 5 standard achievements in a row: Age 1 achievement, Age 2 achievement, etc. (These require accumulating a certain number of points and having a top card of the matching age or higher to claim.)
3. Set the 5 special achievements aside (these are earned by meeting specific conditions).
4. Each player draws 2 Age 1 cards to their hand.
5. Each player chooses 1 card from their hand to play as their first card (meld it to their tableau). This happens simultaneously.
6. Determine first player (youngest, random, etc.). In a 2-player game, the first player takes only 1 action on their first turn.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn, take exactly 2 actions (first player takes 1 action on their very first turn in a 2-player game). Actions available:

### 1. Draw
Draw a card from a supply pile. You draw from the pile matching your HIGHEST top card age in your tableau. If that pile is empty, draw from the next higher pile. Drawn card goes to your hand.

### 2. Meld
Play a card from your hand to your tableau. Cards are organized by COLOR — each player has up to 5 stacks (one per color). Playing a new card of a color covers the previous card of that color, but cards can be SPLAYED to reveal icons on covered cards.

### 3. Dogma
Activate the dogma effects on one of your TOP cards (the visible card on any of your color stacks). This is the core of the game.

**Dogma execution rules:**
- Each dogma effect has a FEATURED ICON (shown to the left of the effect text).
- Before you execute, compare your count of that icon (across all visible icons in your tableau) with each opponent's count.
- **Sharing**: Any opponent who has EQUAL OR MORE of the featured icon than you ALSO executes the effect (they benefit too). After all sharing opponents execute, the active player executes. If any opponent shared, the active player gets a free Draw action as a bonus.
- **Demand effects** (aggressive): These affect opponents who have FEWER of the featured icon than you. They don't share; they suffer the effect. (Demand effects have "I demand" in their text.)
- Non-demand effects are cooperative/beneficial.

### 4. Achieve
Claim a standard achievement. Requirements:
- Your SCORE (total age values in your score pile) must be at least 5x the achievement's age number (e.g., Age 1 achievement requires 5+ points, Age 2 requires 10+, Age 3 requires 15+, etc.).
- You must have a top card of that age or higher in your tableau.
- Claiming an achievement is one of your 2 actions for the turn.

### Card Anatomy & Icons
Each card has 4 icon positions (3 on the left edge, 1 on the bottom right). Some positions show icons, others are blank. The 6 icon types:
- Crown, Leaf, Lightbulb, Castle, Factory, Clock
- Icon counts matter for dogma sharing/demand thresholds.

### Splaying
Some dogma effects let you SPLAY a color stack in a direction:
- **Splay Left**: Fan cards so the RIGHT icons of covered cards are visible.
- **Splay Right**: Fan cards so the LEFT icons of covered cards are visible.
- **Splay Up**: Fan cards so the BOTTOM icons of covered cards are visible.
- Splaying reveals more icons, making you stronger for dogma sharing/demand.

### Scoring Cards
"Score" means take a card and tuck it into your score pile. The card's AGE NUMBER is its point value. Your total score is the sum of all ages in your score pile.

### Tucking
"Tuck" means place a card UNDER a stack (at the bottom, not on top). The top card remains the same, but the tucked card may reveal icons if the stack is splayed.

### Returning
"Return" means put a card back in the supply pile of its age.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends immediately when any player achieves enough achievements:
- **2 players**: 6 achievements to win
- **3 players**: 5 achievements to win
- **4 players**: 4 achievements to win

Achievements include the 5 standard achievements plus 5 special achievements (earned by meeting unique conditions during play, like having 5 top cards of the same icon type, or having a score pile with specific properties).

**Alternative end**: If a player needs to draw a card and there are no cards remaining in ANY pile (even above the required age), the game ends immediately. The player with the most achievements wins. Ties broken by score.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Sharing is double-edged**: If opponents share your dogma, they get the benefit too — but you get a free draw. Sometimes you WANT opponents to share.
- **Demand effects target the weak**: If you have more of the featured icon than an opponent, your demand effects hit them. Build icon counts defensively.
- **Icon management is crucial**: Splaying stacks to reveal more icons protects you from demands and enables powerful sharing.
- **The game can end suddenly**: Special achievements and the draw-pile-exhaustion condition can end the game unexpectedly. Always be aware of achievement counts.
- **Higher ages are more powerful**: Age 10 cards have game-warping effects. But you need to build up to draw them (you draw from your highest top card age).
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that sharing triggers a bonus draw for the active player. This makes activating dogmas with widely-held icons quite efficient.
- **Common mistake**: Not checking icon counts before activating dogma. Count carefully — one icon difference changes whether an effect is shared or demanded.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting you must take exactly 2 actions per turn (not 1, not 3).
- **Melding covers your current top card**: Think carefully before covering a powerful card. Its dogma becomes inaccessible (though its icons may still show if splayed).
- **Score pile vs. hand vs. tableau**: These are three separate areas. "Score" always means the score pile. "Draw" goes to hand. "Meld" goes to tableau.
