## OVERVIEW
Pulsar 2849 is a dice-drafting, space-exploration euro game set in the year 2849 where players are explorers claiming pulsars, developing technologies, building transmitters on planets, and flying through space on gyrodyne ships. Players draft dice each round from a common pool, using them to perform various actions across multiple interconnected systems. The clever dice-drafting mechanism forces players to balance the value of dice taken against their position on the engineering and initiative tracks.

## COMPONENTS
- Main board with space map (hex grid), pulsar locations, HQ board with technology tracks
- Dice (multiple colored dice forming the common pool — typically 2 per player + 1)
- Player boards with gyrodyne ship and personal technology tracks
- Pulsar claim tokens
- Transmitter tokens
- Technology tiles (3 tiers)
- Planet tokens with various bonuses
- Engineering track and Initiative track markers
- Goal cards (end-game scoring objectives)
- Round marker (game plays over 8 rounds)
- Exploration tokens, patent markers

## SETUP
1. Place the main board in the center. It shows the space hex map with numbered pulsars, planets, and connections.
2. Place technology tiles in their designated areas on the HQ board by tier.
3. Each player takes a player board, places their gyrodyne ship at the starting location, and takes markers for the engineering and initiative tracks (both start at the median/center position).
4. Roll all dice for the round and organize them by value on the dice draft track (from 1 to 6). The median value is calculated and marked.
5. Deal goal cards — each player selects personal end-game scoring goals (draft or deal depending on variant).
6. Place transmitter and pulsar tokens in supply areas.
7. Determine first player; set round marker to Round 1.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game plays over 8 rounds. Each round:

### Phase 1: Roll and Sort Dice
- Roll all dice for the round (2 per player + 1 extra).
- Sort them by face value on the dice track. Mark the median value.

### Phase 2: Dice Drafting (2 dice per player)
Players take turns (in initiative order) selecting one die at a time, going around twice so each player gets exactly 2 dice.

**Median Penalty/Bonus**: When you take a die:
- If its value is ABOVE the median: move your marker down on engineering track, initiative track, or both (you must pay the difference).
- If its value is BELOW the median: move your marker up on engineering/initiative tracks (you gain the difference).
- If its value EQUALS the median: no adjustment.

This is the core tension — high-value dice are powerful but cost you track position; low-value dice are weaker but improve your position.

### Phase 3: Action Phase
Each player uses their 2 drafted dice to perform actions. Each die is placed on one action space. Actions include:

**Fly Gyrodyne Ship**: Move your ship along the hex map. The die value determines how far you can move (each connection costs movement points). Landing on planets or pulsars triggers bonuses.

**Claim a Pulsar**: Place your marker on an unclaimed pulsar. The die value must meet or exceed the pulsar's requirement. Claimed pulsars generate points each round (the pulsar "spins" — you rotate its dial each round for ongoing income/VP).

**Build a Transmitter**: Place a transmitter on a planet you've visited. The die value determines which transmitter level you can build. Transmitters score VP and provide network bonuses.

**Develop Technology**: Place a die on the HQ board to advance along a technology track or acquire a technology tile. Technologies provide ongoing abilities, end-game VP, or one-time bonuses. Three tiers of increasing power.

**Use Engineering Track**: Certain action spaces on the HQ board or player board allow spending engineering track position for bonuses.

**Modifier Tokens**: Some acquired abilities let you modify dice values (+1/-1) before placement.

### Phase 4: Production / Pulsar Spin
- Each claimed pulsar "produces" — players gain VP, resources, or bonuses based on pulsar output for the current round.
- Advance round marker.

### Phase 5: Cleanup
- Return dice to supply. If it's not the final round, proceed to next round.

## SCORING / WINNING
Final scoring after 8 rounds includes:
- VP accumulated during the game (from pulsars, transmitters, technologies)
- End-game goal card scoring (personal objectives like "most transmitters," "longest network," etc.)
- Position on engineering track (higher = more VP)
- Bonus VP from completed technology tracks
- Transmitter network bonuses (connected transmitters score more)
- Patent bonuses from certain technologies

The player with the most VP wins. Ties broken by initiative track position (higher is better).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Dice median is critical**: Taking a 6 when the median is 3 costs you 3 positions split between engineering and initiative. Plan whether you need the high die or if a lower die with track bonuses serves you better.
- **Initiative order matters**: Higher initiative means you draft dice earlier, getting first pick. This is especially important in later rounds.
- **Engineering track**: Don't neglect it — it provides end-game VP and can be spent for powerful bonuses during the game.
- **Pulsars are engines**: Claiming pulsars early means more rounds of production. A pulsar claimed in round 1 produces 8 times; one claimed in round 7 produces only twice.
- **Transmitter networks**: Connected transmitters on adjacent planets score bonus VP. Plan your ship movement to build efficient networks.
- **Technology synergies**: Technologies can combo powerfully. Look for tier-3 technologies that multiply your strategy.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting to adjust engineering/initiative tracks when drafting dice above or below the median.
- **Common mistake**: Not spinning pulsars each round — you must rotate the dial to get the current round's benefit.
- **Movement planning**: Your gyrodyne can only move when you spend a die on movement. Plan multi-round routes to reach distant pulsars and planets.
