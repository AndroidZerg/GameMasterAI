# Game: Century: Golem Edition
# Publisher: Plan B Games
# Players: 2-5 | Time: 30-45 min | Weight: 2.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Century: Golem Edition is a streamlined engine-building card game where players collect and upgrade gems (soul gems) to acquire powerful golem cards worth victory points. It is a re-themed version of Century: Spice Road, replacing spices with colorful gems and merchants with golem-crafting wizards. The game is praised for its elegant simplicity, quick turns, and satisfying engine-building arc.

## COMPONENTS
- Gem crystals in 4 types (plastic gems):
  - **Yellow** (least valuable, most common)
  - **Green** (second tier)
  - **Blue** (third tier)
  - **Pink/Red** (most valuable, rarest)
- Merchant cards (form your hand/engine — used to gain and upgrade gems)
- Golem cards (victory point cards you acquire by spending gems)
- Victory point coins (gold = 3 VP, silver = 1 VP)
- Player caravan cards (reference card showing gem values)
- Gem supply bowls (one per gem type)
- Card trays or designated areas for the market rows

## SETUP
1. Shuffle the Merchant card deck. Deal a row of 6 face-up Merchant cards to form the Merchant market. Place the remaining deck at the end of the row.
2. Shuffle the Golem card deck. Deal a row of 5 face-up Golem cards to form the Golem market. Place the remaining deck at the end of the row.
3. Place gold VP coins (worth 3 VP each) above the first and second Golem cards in the row. Place silver VP coins (worth 1 VP each) above the third and fourth Golem cards. (The 5th card has no bonus coin.)
4. Create a supply of all 4 gem types accessible to all players.
5. Each player takes a caravan card (player mat) and receives 3 starting gems: typically 3 yellow gems for the first player, with later players receiving slightly better starting gems:
   - Player 1: 3 yellow
   - Player 2: 4 yellow
   - Player 3: 3 yellow + 1 green (or 4 yellow)
   - Player 4: 3 yellow + 1 green
   - Player 5: 3 yellow + 1 green (check edition for exact starting resources)
6. Each player receives 2 starting Merchant cards that form their initial hand (typically one basic "gain 2 yellow gems" card and one basic "upgrade" card — exact cards may vary by edition).
7. Determine starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn, perform exactly ONE of these 4 actions:

### 1. Play a Merchant Card
- Play a Merchant card from your hand face-up in front of you (into your play area / discard pile).
- Resolve its effect immediately. Merchant cards do one of three things:
  - **Production**: Gain the depicted gems from the supply and add them to your caravan (gem area). Example: "Gain 2 yellow and 1 green."
  - **Upgrade**: Upgrade gems you own to the next tier. Each upgrade step moves 1 gem up one level: yellow -> green -> blue -> pink. An "upgrade 2" card lets you perform 2 upgrade steps (on the same gem twice, or on 2 different gems once each). Example: "Upgrade 3" means you can upgrade any combination of your gems 3 steps total.
  - **Trade/Exchange**: Trade specific gems from your caravan for other specific gems from the supply. Example: "Pay 2 yellow, gain 1 blue" or "Pay 1 green + 1 blue, gain 1 pink + 1 yellow."
- The card remains in your played area (not in your hand) until you Rest.

### 2. Acquire a Merchant Card
- Take 1 Merchant card from the market row and add it to your hand.
- **The first card (leftmost) is free.**
- For every card further right in the row, you must place 1 gem from your caravan onto each card you skip over (one gem per skipped card, placed on the card itself).
  - Example: To take the 3rd card, place 1 gem on the 1st card and 1 gem on the 2nd card.
- When you take a card that has gems on it (placed by other players), you also take all those gems — bonus!
- Slide remaining cards left to fill the gap. Draw a new card from the deck to fill the rightmost position.
- **Gem limit**: You can never have more than 10 gems in your caravan. If taking a card with gems would exceed 10, you must return excess gems to the supply.

### 3. Rest
- Take ALL previously played Merchant cards from your play area back into your hand.
- This is how you "recharge" your engine. You do nothing else this turn.
- Timing your Rest is important: rest too often and you waste turns; rest too late and you run out of playable cards.

### 4. Claim a Golem Card
- Pay the gem cost shown on one of the 5 face-up Golem cards by returning those gems from your caravan to the supply.
- Take that Golem card and add it to your score pile.
- If there is a VP coin above that card's position (gold or silver), take it as well.
- Slide remaining Golem cards left to fill the gap. Draw a new Golem card for the rightmost position. Move VP coins back to their fixed positions (above cards 1 and 2 for gold, 3 and 4 for silver).

### Gem Hierarchy (Important)
- Yellow (value 1) -> Green (value 2) -> Blue (value 3) -> Pink (value 4)
- Upgrades always move one step up this chain. You cannot downgrade.
- Pink is the most valuable and hardest to obtain.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game end is triggered when any player claims their **5th Golem card** (in a 2-3 player game) or their **6th Golem card** (in a 4-5 player game — check your edition, some use 5 for all player counts). Finish the current round so all players have equal turns.

**Final Scoring:**
- Add up VP values printed on all your claimed Golem cards.
- Add VP from bonus coins (gold = 3 VP, silver = 1 VP).
- Each remaining non-yellow gem in your caravan is worth 1 VP (green, blue, and pink each = 1 VP; yellow = 0 VP).

The player with the most VP wins. Tiebreaker: fewest total gems remaining, then the player later in turn order.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Build your engine (acquire Merchant cards) in the early game, then pivot to claiming Golem cards in the mid-to-late game. Don't rush Golems before your engine can efficiently generate the needed gems.
- Upgrade cards are extremely efficient. A single "upgrade 3" card can turn 3 yellow gems into 3 green, or push gems up multiple tiers.
- Watch the Golem market: if a cheap Golem appears on position 1 or 2 (with a gold VP coin bonus), it may be worth grabbing even if it's not optimal for your engine.
- Placing gems to acquire distant Merchant cards is a real cost. The leftmost free card is often good enough.
- Taking a card loaded with other players' gems is a powerful swing — you get the card AND free resources.
- Rest efficiently: play all (or most) of your useful cards before resting. Resting with unplayed cards wastes potential.
- The 10-gem caravan limit matters. Don't hoard gems needlessly — convert them to Golem cards.
- Pink gems are rare and valuable. Any Merchant card that produces or trades for pink is very strong.
- In 2-player games, the Golem market refreshes more slowly — plan around what's available.
- The game is quick to teach and plays in 30 minutes. Excellent gateway to engine-building games.
