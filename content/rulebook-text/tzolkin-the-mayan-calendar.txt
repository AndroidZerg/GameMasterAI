# Game: Tzolk'in: The Mayan Calendar
# Publisher: Czech Games Edition
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 90 min | Weight: 3.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Tzolk'in: The Mayan Calendar is a worker placement game featuring an ingenious interlocking gear mechanism. Players place workers on rotating gears representing different Mayan cities; as the central calendar gear turns each round, workers advance to more powerful action spaces. The tension between placing workers early (for better rewards later) and removing them (to take actions now) creates a deeply strategic experience. The theme revolves around pleasing the Mayan gods through resource management and temple advancement.

## COMPONENTS
- Central game board with the large Tzolk'in calendar gear and 5 smaller interlocking gears (Palenque, Yaxchilan, Tikal, Uxmal, Chichen Itza)
- Worker tokens (3-6 per player in player colors; corn-shaped)
- Corn tokens (denominations of 1 and 5)
- Wood, Stone, Gold resource tokens
- Crystal Skulls
- Player boards
- Technology tracks (4 levels each for Agriculture, Extraction, Architecture, Theology)
- Temple tracks (3 temples: blue/Chaac, green/Quetzalcoatl, brown/Kukulkan)
- Buildings tiles
- Monuments
- Starting wealth tiles
- First player marker

## SETUP
1. Assemble the gear mechanism on the board. The central Tzolk'in gear starts at position 0 (the starting tooth).
2. Each player receives a player board, 3 workers (can gain up to 3 more, for a max of 6), and starting resources (varies by player order — later players get more corn).
3. Shuffle and deal starting wealth tiles — each player picks one for an asymmetric starting bonus.
4. Place all workers on player boards initially (none start on the gears).
5. Set up the 3 temple tracks; all players start at the bottom of each temple.
6. Lay out buildings and monuments for the game.
7. Place corn, wood, stone, gold, and crystal skulls in the supply.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

The game is played over 26 rounds (one full rotation of the central gear, with 4 food days interspersed).

### Each Round:
1. **Turn the Gear**: The first player advances the central Tzolk'in gear by one notch. This automatically rotates all 5 city gears, moving all placed workers to the next (higher-value) action space.

2. **Player Turns** (in order): On your turn, you MUST do exactly ONE of the following:
   - **Place one or more workers** on the gears, OR
   - **Remove one or more workers** from the gears

   You CANNOT both place and remove in the same turn.

### Placing Workers
- Choose one or more workers from your supply and place each on the lowest available space of any gear.
- **Corn cost**: The lowest space on each gear costs 0 corn. However, if you skip to a higher space (because lower ones are occupied or you want to start higher), you pay extra corn. Placing on position 0 = free; position 1 = 1 corn extra; position 2 = 2 corn extra; etc.
- Placing multiple workers in one turn: The first worker is free (base cost); each additional worker costs escalating corn (2nd worker = +1 corn, 3rd = +2 corn, etc.) in addition to the placement cost.

### Removing Workers
- Remove one or more workers from any gear(s) and perform the action of the space each worker currently occupies.
- Workers on higher-numbered spaces get better actions (because they've been on the gear longer).
- Removing is free; you take the action immediately.

### The Five City Gears:

**Palenque (Agriculture/Food)**:
- Spaces provide corn and/or wood in increasing amounts.
- Higher spaces: gain corn + wood, or burn forest for large corn payouts.

**Yaxchilan (Resources)**:
- Spaces provide wood, stone, gold, or combinations.
- Higher spaces give more valuable resources (stone, gold).

**Tikal (Building/Technology)**:
- Spaces let you advance on technology tracks or construct buildings.
- Technology tracks (4 types): Agriculture (better food), Extraction (better resources), Architecture (building discounts), Theology (temple benefits).
- Buildings provide ongoing or end-game benefits.

**Uxmal (Trading/Flexible)**:
- Spaces let you trade corn for resources, use any one action from another gear, or gain corn.
- Includes the Market (trade 3 corn for any resource, or sell resources).
- The flexible "any action" space is very powerful at high positions.

**Chichen Itza (Religion/Skulls)**:
- Spaces let you place crystal skulls to advance on temple tracks.
- Each skull placed advances you on one or more temples and earns immediate VP.
- Higher spaces give more temple steps and VP.

### Food Days (Rounds 7, 13, 19, 26):
On these rounds, after the gear turns:
1. **Feed workers**: Pay 2 corn per worker you own (on the board or in your supply). If you can't pay, you lose 3 VP per unpaid corn.
2. **Temple scoring**: Advance on temples provides VP. On food days, each temple rewards the players at the top steps and may penalize those at the bottom.

### Technology Tracks
Each of the 4 technology tracks has 4 levels:
- **Agriculture**: Level bonuses reduce feeding costs, give free corn during harvests.
- **Extraction**: Level bonuses provide free resources when gathering.
- **Architecture**: Level bonuses reduce building costs.
- **Theology**: Level bonuses enhance temple rewards and skull placements.

Advancing costs resources (corn, wood, stone, etc.) and provides immediate or ongoing benefits.

## SCORING / WINNING
VP is earned throughout the game and at the end:
- **During play**: Crystal skull placements, buildings, temple scoring on food days.
- **End-game scoring** (after the 4th food day):
  - Final temple scoring: Each temple rewards the top positions with significant VP. Being at the bottom of a temple may cost VP.
  - Monuments: Each player may have built a monument during the game; these score VP based on specific conditions (e.g., 1 VP per technology level reached, 2 VP per building, etc.).
  - Remaining resources: 4 resources of any type = 1 VP.

Highest VP wins. Tiebreaker: most remaining corn.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Timing is everything**: Workers gain value the longer they stay on gears. But they also cost you an action to remove, and you need to balance placement with removal.
- **Corn is life**: You need corn to place workers, feed them on food days, and trade for resources. Running out of corn is crippling.
- **The place-or-remove rule is crucial**: You MUST either place or remove — you cannot pass, and you cannot do both. Planning 2-3 turns ahead is essential.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting food days and being unable to feed workers. Mark rounds 7, 13, 19, 26.
- **Common mistake**: Placing a worker on a high gear position and forgetting it will rotate to the end (falling off) — workers that pass the last space on a gear are removed and you get a consolation reward (usually corn).
- **Crystal skulls are limited**: There are only a fixed number of skulls available. Getting them early matters.
- **Temple positioning**: Being at the top of a temple during food days provides large VP; being at the bottom can cost VP. Prioritize at least 1-2 temples.
- **More workers = more actions but more feeding cost**: Gaining extra workers (up to 6) is powerful but expensive to maintain. Evaluate based on your corn engine.
- **Starting wealth tiles matter**: They can dictate your opening strategy. A tile that gives extra corn supports early worker placement; a resource tile supports rushing a technology track.
