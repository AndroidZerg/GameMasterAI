# Game: Expeditions
# Publisher: Stonemaier Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.8/5

## OVERVIEW
Expeditions is a sequel to Scythe, set in the same alternate-history 1920s Europa. After the events of Scythe, players venture beyond the Factory into the mysterious lands of Siberia, exploring with their mech and unique worker companions. The game is a competitive action-selection and tableau-building game focused on playing cards, gathering resources, and completing quests — all while moving across a modular hex map.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 base board (central hub with card rows)
- Modular hex map tiles (Siberian wilderness — arranged around the base board)
- 5 player mats (double-sided for variable setup)
- 5 mech miniatures (one per player)
- 10 worker miniatures (2 per player — unique characters)
- Item cards, Quest cards, Meteorite cards (3 card types in the display)
- Glory tokens (for completed quests and achievements)
- Power tokens (corruption/power currency)
- Coins (VP currency used during the game)
- Guile, Power, and Insight tokens (3 resource types)
- Refresh tokens
- Solo mode Automa cards

## SETUP
1. Assemble the hex map around the base board. The exact layout varies by player count (specific tiles used/removed).
2. Place location tokens face-down on designated hexes (these are revealed when explored).
3. Shuffle and lay out the 3 card rows on the base board: Items, Quests, and Meteorites.
4. Each player takes: 1 player mat, 1 mech, 2 unique workers, starting cards (2 starting item cards), and starting resources.
5. All mechs begin on the base board's central starting hex.
6. Each player places their workers on their player mat (workers start at home, not on the map).
7. Determine first player. Give later players a small resource bonus for balance.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns clockwise. On your turn, you perform exactly **2 steps**:

### Step 1: MOVE (optional)
Move your mech 1-3 hexes on the map:
- Your mech carries your workers with it when you move.
- **You may drop off or pick up workers** on hexes as you move through them.
- When you enter a hex with a **face-down location token**, flip it to reveal it (explore). Revealed locations may provide immediate bonuses or become action spaces.
- **Workers placed on hexes** allow you to use those locations' abilities on future turns.
- You cannot end movement on a hex with another player's mech (but you can pass through).

### Step 2: PLAY or GATHER

**Option A — PLAY a card:**
- Play 1 card from your hand into your tableau (your play area in front of you).
- Pay the card's cost (resources — Guile, Power, and/or Insight).
- Cards provide ongoing abilities, end-game scoring, and/or immediate effects.
- **Item cards** attach to your workers or mech, granting abilities.
- **Quest cards** represent objectives. To complete a quest, you must meet its requirements (specific resources, locations visited, etc.) when you play it. Completed quests earn Glory tokens.
- **Meteorite cards** provide powerful bonuses and are tied to meteorite tokens on the map.

**Option B — GATHER resources:**
- Gain resources based on your current hex and worker positions.
- Each location type produces specific resources (Guile, Power, or Insight).
- Workers left on locations can generate resources when you gather.
- You may also gain cards from the display rows as part of gathering.

### Card Management:
- Your hand has a limit (typically around 3-5 active cards, verify exact number).
- Cards in your tableau are permanent and contribute to scoring.
- You may **refresh** (pick up) cards from your tableau back to your hand under certain conditions, allowing you to replay them for additional value.
- The refresh mechanic is key — cards can be played, refreshed, and replayed for their bonuses multiple times.

### Corruption (Power Tokens):
- Some actions let you take corruption (power tokens with a negative side).
- Corruption gives you short-term power but counts as negative VP at game end if not managed.
- Certain cards and actions let you remove corruption.

### Vanquish:
- You can vanquish challenges on certain map hexes by spending power, earning rewards.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game end is triggered when **any player has placed their 4th Glory token** (from completing quests, achievements, or other goals). Finish the current round so all players have equal turns.

**Final Scoring:**
- **Coins** accumulated during the game (1 coin = 1 VP).
- **Glory tokens** — each token is worth VP based on the achievement.
- **Cards in tableau** — many cards have end-game VP values, especially if conditions are met.
- **Remaining resources** may convert to VP (small amounts).
- **Corruption penalty** — lose VP for unresolved corruption tokens.
- **Map presence bonuses** — some tiles or positions provide end-game VP.

**Highest VP total wins.** Tiebreaker: most Glory tokens.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Worker placement on the map is strategic.** Leaving workers on key hexes gives you resource generation even when your mech moves away. But your workers also travel with your mech by default, so plan drop-offs deliberately.
- **The refresh mechanic is central to the game.** Playing a powerful card, refreshing it, and replaying it maximizes value. Build your strategy around cards worth refreshing.
- **Movement is limited to 1-3 hexes.** Map position matters — don't get stranded far from valuable locations.
- **Quests are the primary game-end trigger and scoring mechanism.** Prioritize completing quests, but don't ignore the resource engine needed to play cards.
- **Corruption is a calculated risk.** Taking some corruption for powerful short-term gains is fine if you plan to clear it before game end.
- **Card synergies in your tableau** are how you build your engine. Look for combos between items, quests, and meteorites.
- **In solo mode,** the Automa simulates an opponent with a card-driven AI.
- **Unlike Scythe, there is no combat between players.** Interaction is mostly through map positioning, card competition, and racing to complete quests.
- **The game is lighter than Scythe** despite being a sequel. It focuses more on card play and less on area control.
- **Explore early** to reveal location tokens and open up resource-gathering options.
