# Game: The Search for Lost Species
# Publisher: Renegade Game Studios
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 45-75 min | Weight: 2.7/5

## OVERVIEW
The Search for Lost Species is a competitive deduction game and the spiritual successor to The Search for Planet X. Players are scientists exploring a mysterious region to locate a lost species by gathering clues, conducting surveys, and narrowing down the possible location of the creature. The game uses a companion app that generates unique puzzles each session and provides clues through various research actions, creating a logic puzzle that players race to solve.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 circular game board (divided into sectors, representing the search area — typically 12 sectors)
- 1 companion app (required — available on iOS, Android, and web browser)
- Player screens (to hide notes and deductions)
- Note sheets (for recording clues and logic)
- Sector tokens / markers
- Theory tokens (for making intermediate claims)
- Action pawns (one per player)
- Time track / turn order track
- Terrain/biome tiles (placed on the circular board to define the landscape)
- Clue tokens
- Scoring tokens
- Conference tokens

## SETUP
1. Launch the companion app and select a new game. Choose difficulty level and number of players.
2. The app generates a unique puzzle — it determines the secret location of the lost species and the distribution of terrain types, evidence, and clues around the board.
3. Set up the circular board with terrain/biome tiles as directed by the app. Each sector has a specific biome type (jungle, mountains, river, grassland, etc.).
4. Each player takes a player screen, note sheet, pencil, theory tokens, and an action pawn.
5. Place all players' action pawns on the time track at the starting position.
6. The app provides each player (or all players) with a starting clue to begin deductions.
7. Determine starting player (or the app may designate this).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns performing actions. The turn order is determined by position on the time track — the player furthest BACK on the track goes next (similar to Patchwork/Tokaido). Actions cost different amounts of time, so choosing efficient actions is key.

### On Your Turn
Choose ONE action:

#### 1. Survey a Region
- Select a contiguous group of sectors (the number you can survey depends on the action cost).
- The app reveals what types of terrain or evidence exist in those sectors (but NOT the exact position of the lost species directly).
- Example: "There are 2 jungle sectors and 1 mountain sector in sectors 3-5."
- Surveys give broad information to narrow your deductions.
- Cost: Varies by the number of sectors surveyed (more sectors = more time on the time track).

#### 2. Research / Gather a Clue
- The app provides a specific logical clue based on the type of research you choose.
- Clues are definitive logical statements like: "The lost species is NOT in a jungle sector" or "The lost species is within 2 sectors of a river" or "The lost species is not adjacent to sector 7."
- Different research types cost different amounts of time.
- Clues are private — only you see the clue (on the app, you enter it behind your screen).

#### 3. Investigate a Sector
- Choose a specific sector and ask the app what is there.
- The app tells you the exact contents of that sector (but this does NOT reveal the lost species unless you are making a final guess).
- This is more targeted than a survey but provides narrow information.
- Cost: Moderate time.

#### 4. Publish a Theory
- Place a theory token on a sector where you believe a specific type of evidence or creature exists.
- The app does NOT immediately confirm or deny your theory.
- Theories are scored at conference phases (see below): correct theories earn points, incorrect ones may cost points or waste opportunities.
- You can publish multiple theories over the game.

#### 5. Locate the Lost Species (Final Guess)
- When you believe you have deduced the exact sector containing the lost species, you can attempt to locate it.
- Enter your guess in the app.
- **If correct**: You earn a large point bonus and the game enters its final phase. Other players get one more turn to also attempt to locate the species.
- **If incorrect**: You lose points and continue playing (but you have wasted time and revealed that you were wrong).

### Time Track
- Every action advances your pawn on the time track by a certain number of spaces.
- The player FURTHEST BACK always goes next — so taking small actions means you get more turns, while big actions skip you ahead.
- The game ends when all players reach the end of the time track (or after someone locates the species and final turns are taken).

### Conferences
- At certain points on the time track (marked spaces), a Conference occurs.
- During a Conference, all published theories are checked:
  - **Correct theories**: Earn victory points for the publishing player.
  - **Incorrect theories**: May be removed or penalized (depending on rules version).
- Conferences incentivize publishing early theories — you score points for being right even before finding the lost species.

### Logic and Deduction
- The core of the game is keeping detailed notes on your note sheet.
- Use process of elimination: cross off sectors that cannot contain the lost species based on your gathered clues.
- Track what terrain types are in which sectors.
- The puzzle is always solvable with enough clues — the race is solving it with fewer clues (less time spent) than your opponents.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Locating the lost species**: First player to correctly locate it earns the largest bonus (typically 10+ points). Other players who also locate it on their final turn earn a smaller bonus.
- **Theory points**: Points earned at each Conference for correct theories. Earlier correct theories are worth more.
- **Efficiency bonus**: Some scoring may reward players who solved the puzzle in less time (fewer actions taken).
- **Penalties**: Incorrect final guesses and incorrect theories may cost points.

The player with the most total points wins. Tiebreaker: the player who located the species first (or who was further back on the time track — indicating greater efficiency).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Take good notes! The game is a logic puzzle — organized deduction on your note sheet is essential.
- Surveys are most efficient early when you know little. As you narrow down possibilities, targeted investigations become more valuable.
- Publish theories as early as you can justify them — earlier correct theories score more at Conferences.
- Don't rush to locate the species with too little information. An incorrect guess wastes precious time and scores no points.
- Pay attention to what sectors other players are investigating or theorizing about — this is indirect information you can use.
- The time track mechanic means you can take several small actions in a row if you fall behind. Use this to gather multiple clues efficiently.
- Different difficulty levels change the board size and clue complexity. Start with an easier difficulty to learn the logic framework.
- The app is required and generates unique puzzles every game — high replayability.
- If you played The Search for Planet X, the mechanics are similar but the theme and some clue types differ. The deduction framework carries over well.
- In solo mode, you compete against a target score — try to locate the species in as few actions as possible.
