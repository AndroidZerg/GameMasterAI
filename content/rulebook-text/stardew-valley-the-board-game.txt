## OVERVIEW
Stardew Valley: The Board Game is a cooperative farming game based on the beloved video game. Players work together as farmers in Pelican Town, growing crops, raising animals, mining, fishing, and foraging to complete community goals and restore the Community Center before the end of the 4th season (year-end). It captures the cozy, multi-activity feel of the video game in a collaborative board game format.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Game board (Pelican Town, Farm, Mine, Forest, Beach areas)
- 4 Player boards (individual farms)
- Season deck (Grandpa's Goals / event cards for Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter)
- Crop tiles, Animal tiles, Artisan Good tiles
- Mine cards (dungeon encounters and loot)
- Fish cards
- Foraging tokens
- Villager / Heart track cards (friendship)
- Community Center bundles (goal cards)
- Tool upgrade tokens
- Resource tokens (wood, stone, gold, etc.)
- Die / dice for various checks
- Season track marker

## SETUP
1. Place the game board centrally.
2. Each player takes a Player board and a starting set of resources (seeds, basic tools, small amount of gold).
3. Shuffle and set up the Season decks (Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter) — each contains event cards and Grandpa's Goals.
4. Draw and reveal the Grandpa's Goals for the game — typically 4 goals that must ALL be completed to win.
5. Lay out the Community Center bundles — each bundle requires specific items to complete.
6. Prepare the Mine deck, Fish deck, and Foraging token supply.
7. Set up the Villager friendship cards along the board.
8. Place the Season marker on Spring, Day 1.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game plays over 4 seasons. Each season consists of multiple turns (days). On each turn:

### 1. Season Event (Start of each new season)
- Draw and resolve the top card of the current Season deck. These introduce events, weather changes, festivals, or new availability of crops/fish/forage.

### 2. Planning Phase
- Players discuss and plan their actions cooperatively. Communication is open and encouraged.

### 3. Action Phase
Each player takes **2 actions** per turn (some upgrades grant additional actions). Actions include:

- **Plant Crops**: Place seed tiles on your farm board. Different crops are available in different seasons.
- **Water Crops**: Water planted crops to advance them toward harvest. Crops typically need to be watered across multiple turns.
- **Harvest Crops**: Collect mature crops from your farm. These can be used for bundles, sold for gold, or converted to artisan goods.
- **Raise Animals**: Buy and tend animals (chickens, cows, etc.) that produce goods over time.
- **Mine**: Enter the mine — draw Mine cards to fight monsters, find gems/ore, and descend deeper. Combat uses dice. Deeper levels yield better resources.
- **Fish**: Go to a fishing location and draw Fish cards. Different fish are available by season and location. Fishing uses a simple check mechanic.
- **Forage**: Collect forage tokens from the forest, beach, or mountain areas. Available items change by season.
- **Visit Town**: Buy seeds, upgrade tools, or sell goods at Pierre's shop.
- **Befriend Villagers**: Spend gifts or actions to increase friendship hearts with villagers. Reaching heart thresholds grants rewards and may be required for goals.
- **Complete Bundles**: Turn in the required items to complete a Community Center bundle.
- **Craft Artisan Goods**: Convert raw materials into processed goods (wine, jam, cheese, etc.) using equipment.

### 4. End of Day
- Advance the day marker. Some crops grow, some events resolve.
- Check if the season is ending (after a set number of days). If so, transition to the next season.

### Season Transition
- Unharvested seasonal crops may wilt/die.
- New seasonal crops, fish, and forage become available.
- Advance the Season marker.

## SCORING / WINNING
- This is a **cooperative, pass/fail game** — there is no score.
- **Win Condition**: Complete ALL of Grandpa's Goals before the end of Winter (the 4th season). Goals typically include things like: complete X Community Center bundles, reach Y friendship hearts with villagers, earn Z gold, catch specific fish, reach a certain mine level, etc.
- **Lose Condition**: Winter ends and any Grandpa's Goal remains incomplete.
- The difficulty is adjustable by choosing harder Grandpa's Goal combinations.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Season-specific resources** — many crops, fish, and forage items are ONLY available in certain seasons. Plan ahead so you don't miss seasonal items needed for bundles.
- **Crop timing is critical** — if you plant too late in a season, crops won't mature before the season changes and they'll die. Count the remaining days.
- **Specialization helps** — in multiplayer, having each player focus on different activities (one farms, one mines, one fishes) is more efficient than everyone doing the same thing.
- **Community Center bundles are the core objective** — always check what items bundles need and prioritize collecting them.
- **The Mine gets harder but more rewarding** — deeper levels have tougher monsters but rarer gems needed for bundles and upgrades.
- **Tool upgrades save actions** — upgrading your watering can, pickaxe, or fishing rod lets you do more per action. Invest early.
- **Villager friendships have compounding rewards** — high-friendship villagers may give you free items or unlock special abilities.
- **Don't hoard items** — turn them in for bundles as soon as possible. Holding items "just in case" is a common trap.
- **Winter is the last chance** — by Fall, you should have a clear plan for what's left. Winter has fewer crops and resources available.
