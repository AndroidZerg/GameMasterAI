## OVERVIEW
Wyrmspan is an engine-building game where players are dragon enthusiasts enticing dragons to settle in their cavernous habitats. As a spiritual successor to Wingspan, it shares a similar card-driven engine-building core but introduces a cave-based habitat system, dragon guild mechanics, and excavation. Players play dragon cards into three different cave types, chaining abilities together to build increasingly powerful action combos.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Game Arc
The game is played over 4 rounds. Each round, players have a limited number of turns (determined by available action cubes/markers — VERIFY mechanism; in Wingspan it is action cubes that decrease each round).

### On Your Turn
Choose ONE of three actions corresponding to one of the three cave rows:

**1. Excavate (First Cave Row)**
- Pay the cost shown on the next open space in this row.
- Place an excavation marker or dragon in the cave.
- Activate the abilities of all dragons previously played in this row, from left to right (engine activation).
- This action typically focuses on gaining cave cards, exploring new areas, and expanding your habitat capacity.

**2. Entice a Dragon (Second Cave Row)**
- Pay the cost to play a dragon card from your hand into the next available space in any cave row (VERIFY if dragons must go in the row of the action taken or any row).
- The dragon must meet habitat requirements (some dragons require specific cave types).
- Pay the dragon's resource cost (combination of meat, crystals, milk, coins, eggs).
- Gain any immediate "when played" abilities.
- Activate the abilities of all dragons previously played in this row, from left to right.

**3. Explore (Third Cave Row)**
- Pay the cost shown on the next open space.
- Advance your marker on the Dragon Guild board, gaining the benefit shown.
- Activate the abilities of all dragons previously played in this row, from left to right.
- The Dragon Guild track provides resources, cards, eggs, and special bonuses.

### Cave Spaces
- Each cave row has spaces that may have printed costs or bonuses.
- As you fill spaces left to right, later spaces may cost more but unlock more powerful engine activations (since you activate ALL dragons in the row).
- The leftmost spaces are cheapest, incentivizing early engine building.

### Dragon Guild

- Each space on the track provides a specific benefit (resources, cards, eggs, VP).
- Players can potentially block or race ahead on this track.

### Eggs

- Dragons can hold a limited number of eggs (shown on the card).

### Round End

- Score points for the round objective (e.g., most eggs in Crimson Cavern, most dragons of a certain type, etc.).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Engine building is cumulative**: Each dragon you add to a row makes that row's action more powerful, since you activate ALL dragons in the row each time. Prioritize building one row early.
- **Resource management is tight**: You never have enough of everything. Focus your engine on generating the resources you need most.
- **Dragon selection**: Not every dragon in your hand will be playable. Choose dragons that synergize with your existing engine and resource production.
- **Cave row specialization**: Each row's base action does different things. Consider specializing in 1-2 rows rather than spreading evenly across all three.
- **The Dragon Guild track provides essential resources**: Don't neglect the Explore action, especially in early rounds.
- **Eggs are dual-purpose**: Using eggs as currency means fewer end-game VP. Balance spending vs. hoarding.
- **Round-end objectives guide strategy**: Check what objectives are coming and plan your engine accordingly.
- **Differences from Wingspan**: While similar in spirit, Wyrmspan has meaningful mechanical differences. The cave/excavation system, Dragon Guild, and resource types are distinct. Players familiar with Wingspan should learn the new systems rather than assuming identical rules.
- **Solo mode**: Uses an Automa deck that simulates an opponent. Scoring is compared against the Automa's total.
