# Game: Onirim
# Publisher: Z-Man Games
# Players: 1-2 | Time: 15 min | Weight: 1.8/5

## OVERVIEW
Onirim is a solo or cooperative card game where you are a Dreamwalker lost in a mysterious labyrinth and must find the 8 oneiric doors (2 of each color) before the deck runs out. You explore the labyrinth by playing cards in sequences of 3 matching colors, but Nightmare cards lurk in the deck, threatening to undo your progress. It is a tense puzzle of hand management and calculated risk.

## COMPONENTS
- 76 Labyrinth cards in 4 colors (red, blue, green, brown/tan), each with one of 3 symbols (sun, moon, key):
  - Each color has: 6 sun cards, 4 moon cards, 3 key cards = 13 per color, minus a few (exact distribution: 8 suns, 6 moons, 4 keys across the deck — verify counts)
  - Approximately: 9 red, 8 blue, 7 green, 6 brown suns; similar distribution across types
- 8 Door cards (2 per color: red, blue, green, brown)
- 10 Nightmare cards
- (Second edition includes 7 expansion modules in the box)

## SETUP
1. Shuffle all 76 cards together (Labyrinth cards, Door cards, and Nightmare cards) into a single deck.
2. Draw an opening hand of 5 cards. If you draw any Door cards or Nightmare cards during this initial draw, set them aside, draw replacement cards, then shuffle the set-aside cards back into the deck.
3. Your goal: discover all 8 Door cards before the deck runs out.
4. In a 2-player cooperative game, each player has their own hand of 5 cards but you share the labyrinth row and work together.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On each turn, you perform these steps in order:

### Phase 1: Play or Discard
Choose ONE:
- **Play a card** from your hand to the Labyrinth row (the line of cards in front of you): The card you play must NOT have the same SYMBOL as the previously played card. (Colors can repeat; symbols cannot be consecutive.) If you play a third card of the same COLOR in a row (3 consecutive cards of the same color, regardless of symbol), you immediately discover a Door of that color — take it from the deck or discard pile and place it in your discovered area.
- **Discard a card** from your hand to the discard pile: If you discard a KEY card, you may activate its Prophecy power (see below).

### Phase 2: Draw a Card
Draw 1 card from the deck to refill your hand to 5. Then resolve what you drew:
- **Labyrinth card** (sun, moon, or key): Add it to your hand. Done.
- **Door card**: You may immediately discover it IF you have a Key card of the matching color in your hand — discard the Key to claim the Door. Otherwise, shuffle the Door card back into the deck.
- **Nightmare card**: You MUST resolve one of these four penalties (your choice):
  1. **Discard your hand**: Discard all 5 cards in your hand, then draw 5 new cards (resolving any Doors/Nightmares drawn normally).
  2. **Discard the top 5 cards of the deck**: Remove 5 cards from the top of the deck to the discard pile. (Door cards found this way are placed in Limbo, not discarded — they get shuffled back in.)
  3. **Lose a discovered Door**: Return one of your already-discovered Doors back into the deck and shuffle.
  4. **Discard a Key card from your hand**: If you have one, discard it.

### Prophecy Power (Key discard ability)
When you discard a Key card from your hand (during Phase 1), you may look at the top 5 cards of the deck, discard 1 of them, and rearrange the remaining 4 on top of the deck in any order.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win**: Discover all 8 Doors (2 of each color) before the deck runs out.
- **Lose**: If the deck runs out and you have not discovered all 8 Doors, you are lost in the labyrinth forever.

There is no point scoring — it is a pass/fail game.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- The symbol restriction is critical: you cannot play two cards with the same symbol consecutively (e.g., sun-sun is illegal), but you CAN play same color consecutively (that is how you build sequences of 3).
- Keys are the most valuable cards — they open Doors when drawn AND provide Prophecy when discarded. Do not waste them carelessly.
- The Prophecy power (discarding a Key) is extremely powerful for avoiding Nightmares and finding Doors. Use it strategically.
- When resolving Nightmares, the best option varies by situation. Discarding from the deck is often safest if your hand is strong. Losing a Door is painful but sometimes necessary.
- Keep track of which colors you still need Doors for. Prioritize building 3-card sequences in those colors.
- In the 2-player variant, players take turns but share the labyrinth row. Communication about hand contents is allowed.
- The second edition box includes 7 expansion modules (The Towers, Happy Dreams, The Book of Steps Lost and Found, etc.) that add new card types and mechanics for variety and increased difficulty.
- The game is quite difficult — expect to lose frequently. A typical win rate for experienced players is around 50-60%.
- Count cards mentally if possible — knowing how many of each color/symbol remain helps planning enormously.
