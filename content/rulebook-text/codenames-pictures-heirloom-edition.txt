# Game: Codenames: Pictures
# Publisher: Czech Games Edition
# Players: 2-8 | Time: 15 min | Weight: 1.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Codenames: Pictures is a party deduction game where two teams compete to identify their agents hidden among a grid of picture cards. One player on each team is the Spymaster, who gives one-word clues to guide their teammates to pick the correct pictures while avoiding the opposing team's pictures and the deadly Assassin. This version uses abstract and whimsical artwork on cards instead of the word cards from original Codenames.

## COMPONENTS
- 140 double-sided picture cards (280 unique pictures total)
- Key cards (grid cards showing the secret layout — which pictures belong to which team)
- Card stand/holder for the key card
- Agent cards (red and blue — placed on correctly guessed pictures)
- Innocent Bystander cards (beige)
- Assassin card (black)
- Timer (sand timer, optional for enforcing time limits on clue-giving)
- Rulebook

## SETUP
1. Divide players into two teams (Red and Blue). Teams should be roughly equal in size. Each team selects one Spymaster; the remaining players are Field Operatives (guessers).
2. The two Spymasters sit on one side of the table; the Field Operatives sit across from them.
3. Shuffle the picture cards and lay out a 5x4 grid (20 pictures) in the center of the table, visible to all players.
4. Randomly draw one Key card and place it in the card stand so only the Spymasters can see it. The Key card orientation matters — a small indicator on the card shows which side corresponds to which edge of the grid.
5. The Key card shows which pictures belong to Red (typically 8 or 9), which to Blue (8 or 9), which are Innocent Bystanders (7), and which is the Assassin (1). The team with 9 agents goes first.
6. Place the agent cards, bystander cards, and assassin card nearby.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

Teams alternate turns, starting with the team that has 9 agents (indicated by the Key card border color).

### Spymaster's Clue
The active team's Spymaster gives a clue consisting of:
- **One word** — this word relates to one or more of their team's pictures.
- **One number** — this tells the team how many pictures on the grid relate to the clue.

**Clue restrictions**:
- The clue must be a single word (no phrases, no hyphenated words unless they appear as one word in a dictionary).
- The clue cannot be something physically visible in any of the pictures (you can't say "blue" while pointing at a blue picture).
- You cannot use a word that is part of the picture's visible content as a direct description.
- The number can be 0 (meaning "none of our pictures relate to this word — but I'm steering you away from dangerous pictures") or "unlimited" (meaning "I'm not specifying a count; guess as many as you want").

### Field Operatives Guess
The Field Operatives discuss among themselves (the Spymaster must remain silent and stone-faced!) and then touch/point to one picture as their guess:

- **Correct (team's agent)**: The Spymaster covers that picture with their team's agent card. The team may continue guessing. They can make up to (number + 1) guesses total — the extra guess allows them to catch up on a previous round's missed clue.
- **Innocent Bystander**: The Spymaster covers it with a bystander card. The team's turn ends immediately.
- **Opposing team's agent**: The Spymaster covers it with the opposing team's agent card. The team's turn ends, AND they've helped the other team by revealing one of their agents.
- **Assassin**: The game ends immediately. The team that touched the Assassin LOSES. The other team wins.

After each guess, the team can choose to stop guessing voluntarily (even if they have guesses remaining). This is wise when uncertain.

### End of Turn
After the active team finishes guessing (by choice, by hitting a wrong card, or by using all their guesses), play passes to the other team's Spymaster.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win by finding all agents**: The first team to have all of their agent pictures identified wins.
- **Win by Assassin**: If the opposing team touches the Assassin picture, your team wins instantly.
- There is no point scoring — it's a win/lose game.
- For tournament or series play, teams can play multiple rounds and track wins.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **The Spymaster must keep a poker face**: No reactions to guesses, no nodding, no wincing. Any body language is essentially cheating.
- **Pictures are more abstract than words**: Unlike original Codenames, the pictures are often surreal or multi-layered. Clues tend to be more thematic or conceptual. A picture of a tree with a clock might connect to "time" or "nature."
- **The Assassin is game-ending**: Always be aware of where you think the Assassin is. When in doubt, DON'T guess.
- **Multi-picture clues are powerful but risky**: Giving a clue for 3+ pictures is high-reward but increases the chance of a misinterpretation.
- **Common mistake**: Spymasters giving clues that relate to the PICTURE on an opponent's card or the Assassin card. Always double-check your clue against dangerous cards.
- **The +1 rule**: If the Spymaster says "ocean, 2," the team can guess up to 3 pictures (2 + 1). The extra guess allows catching up on a previously missed clue.
- **Zero clues**: Saying "birds, 0" means "none of our pictures relate to birds." This helps the team avoid certain pictures (perhaps the Assassin looks bird-related).
- **Unlimited clues**: Saying a word and "unlimited" means the team can keep guessing until they want to stop or hit a wrong picture. The Spymaster can't specify a number.
- **2-3 player variant**: With fewer players, one player can be Spymaster for both teams (cooperative/competitive variants exist), or play the Duet-style cooperative variant if available.
- **The grid orientation matters**: Make sure the Key card is oriented correctly. There are marks on the Key card and the card stand that must align with the grid.
- **Don't overthink**: Sometimes the simplest interpretation of a clue is the right one. Operatives who second-guess too much often pick the wrong picture.
