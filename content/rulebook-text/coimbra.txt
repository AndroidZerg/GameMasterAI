# Game: Coimbra
# Publisher: eggertspiele
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 3.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Coimbra is a dice-drafting and set-collection game set during the Portuguese Renaissance. Players invest in the city's four districts by drafting dice and using them to recruit influential citizens, advance on pilgrimage tracks, and earn victory points. The game features a unique mechanism where the color of the die determines what you pay with (money or guards) while the value determines turn order and influence.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 main game board (depicting Coimbra's 4 city districts and pilgrimage map)
- Player boards (1 per player)
- 15 dice (3 each in 5 colors: grey, purple, orange, green, white — but only certain colors used based on player count)
- Citizen cards (divided into 4 districts: University, Upper City, Lower City, Monastery)
- Voyage cards (for pilgrimage track bonuses)
- Crown tokens, favor tokens
- Coins and guard tokens
- Monasteries and diplomas
- Round marker

## SETUP
1. Place the game board centrally. Set out coin and guard supply.
2. Sort citizen cards by round (I, II, III, IV indicated on card backs). Shuffle each round's deck separately.
3. Deal citizen cards face-up to each of the 4 district rows (number depends on player count: typically player count + 1 per row).
4. Each player takes a player board, starting coins (varied by turn order), starting guards, 1 crown marker per track, and their colored dice.
5. Place crown markers at the bottom of each of the 4 influence tracks (Commerce, Farming, Culture, Religion — these correspond to the 4 card colors).
6. Shuffle and deal voyage cards on the pilgrimage map spaces.
7. Determine first player randomly.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over **4 rounds**, each with 3 phases:

### Phase 1: DICE DRAFTING
1. Roll all dice for the round into the shared pool (number of dice depends on player count).
2. In turn order, each player drafts **one die at a time** and places it on one of the 4 district rows on the board.
3. Each player drafts a total of **3 dice** over multiple turns (so 3 drafting turns each).
4. **Key mechanism:** The die COLOR determines payment type, and the die VALUE determines both turn order within that district and your bidding power.

### Die Colors and Their Meaning:
- **Orange dice** = Pay with GUARDS (military strength)
- **Grey dice** = Pay with COINS (wealth)
- **Purple dice** = Pay with EITHER guards or coins (your choice)
- **Green dice** = Special — associated with various bonuses
- **White dice** = Wild — can count as any color

### Phase 2: CITY COUNCIL (Resolving Districts)
Resolve each of the 4 districts in order (from top to bottom):
1. Within each district, dice are resolved from **highest to lowest value** (this is your turn order).
2. When your die is resolved, you may **recruit one citizen card** from that district row.
3. **Cost:** The die's VALUE is what you must pay (in coins or guards, determined by die color).
4. If you cannot or choose not to pay, you instead take a "favor" (small consolation bonus — typically 1 coin or 1 guard).
5. Place recruited citizens on your player board, organized by type.

### Citizen Card Types (4 colors matching the influence tracks):
- Cards provide **immediate bonuses**, **ongoing abilities**, and/or **end-game scoring**.
- Each citizen also advances your **crown marker** on the corresponding influence track by 1 step.

### Phase 3: PILGRIMAGE & INCOME
1. **Pilgrimage:** Players may advance their pilgrim on the pilgrimage map. Movement is based on position on the influence tracks. Reaching voyage card locations grants bonuses.
2. **Income:** Each player earns income based on their position on the 4 influence tracks:
   - Track positions grant coins, guards, victory points, or pilgrimage steps.
   - Higher positions yield better rewards.

### End of Round:
- Remove any remaining citizen cards from the districts.
- Deal new citizen cards for the next round.
- Pass the first-player marker.

## SCORING / WINNING
Victory points are earned throughout the game and at game end:

**During the game:**
- Some citizen cards give immediate VP.
- Certain influence track positions award VP during income.
- Voyage cards on the pilgrimage map can award VP.

**End-game scoring:**
- **Set collection:** Bonus VP for collecting citizens of different colors (variety is rewarded).
- **Influence track positions:** VP for final positions.
- **Monasteries and diplomas:** VP from special objectives collected during the game.
- **Remaining coins and guards:** Small VP conversion (typically 3:1 or similar).
- **Personal objective cards** (if applicable): VP for meeting secret conditions.

The player with the most VP wins. Tiebreaker: most remaining coins + guards.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Dice value is a double-edged sword:** High dice give you priority in turn order and first pick of cards, but they also cost more to recruit citizens. Low dice are cheap but you pick last.
- **Color choice matters greatly.** If you're short on guards, avoid orange dice. If short on coins, avoid grey dice. Purple dice give flexibility.
- **Influence tracks are crucial** — they determine your income each round AND contribute to end-game scoring. Neglecting a track can be costly.
- **The pilgrimage map** provides powerful bonuses. Plan your influence track advancement to enable good pilgrim movement.
- **Set collection is a major scoring mechanism.** Try to recruit citizens of all 4 colors for maximum bonus VP.
- **Plan your dice drafting considering all 3 dice placements.** Think about which districts you want and what you can afford.
- **In 2-player games,** there is a neutral "ghost" player whose dice block district spots, creating tension.
- **Citizen cards from later rounds (III, IV) are generally more powerful** but also more expensive. Build your economy early.
