## OVERVIEW
Descent: Journeys in the Dark (2nd Edition) is a one-versus-many dungeon-crawling adventure game. One player takes on the role of the Overlord, controlling monsters and springing traps, while up to four other players each control a hero exploring dungeons, fighting monsters, and completing quests. The game features a full campaign mode with branching quest paths where the results of each quest affect the storyline.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Rulebook and 1 Quest Guide
- 48 map tiles (double-sided, interlocking)
- 8 hero figures and hero sheets
- 40+ monster figures (various types in master/minion versions)
- Overlord cards (deck of traps, events, and powers)
- Class decks (for hero archetypes: Warrior, Mage, Scout, Healer — each with subclasses)
- Custom dice: blue (ranged attack), red (melee attack), yellow (bonus), brown/grey/black (defense)
- Search tokens, condition tokens (poison, stun, immobilize, etc.)
- Door tokens, objective tokens, hero tokens
- Health and fatigue trackers
- Lieutenant packs and relics (for campaign play)

## SETUP
### Quest Setup:
1. The Overlord reads the quest introduction from the Quest Guide.
2. Assemble the map using the specified tiles and tile layout shown in the quest.
3. Place monsters, search tokens, doors, and objective tokens as indicated.
4. Hero players choose their heroes and equip their starting gear.
5. The Overlord prepares their Overlord deck.

### Campaign Setup (First Quest):
1. Each hero player selects a hero and an archetype class (Warrior, Mage, Scout, or Healer).
2. Each class starts with a basic class card and a starting weapon.
3. The Overlord chooses a starting Overlord class and takes its basic cards.
4. Begin with the introductory quest.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each quest is played over multiple rounds until the quest's victory condition is met by either the heroes or the Overlord.

### Round Structure:
1. **HERO TURNS** — Each hero takes a full turn (in any order the hero players agree on).
2. **OVERLORD TURN** — The Overlord activates all monster groups.

### Hero Turn:
Each hero gets **2 actions** per turn (can repeat the same action except Rest):

1. **MOVE** — Move up to your Speed value in spaces. You can move through friendly figures but not enemies. Diagonal movement is allowed (each diagonal = 1 space). Terrain costs may apply.

2. **ATTACK** — Perform an attack against a monster:
   - Declare target and weapon.
   - Roll attack dice (blue for ranged, red for melee, plus any bonus yellow dice from equipment).
   - The target rolls defense dice (brown, grey, or black based on their defense value).
   - Compare: Hearts (damage) minus Shields (defense) = wounds dealt.
   - For ranged attacks, the range rolled (on blue die) must equal or exceed the distance to the target.
   - A miss symbol (X on the blue die) means the attack automatically misses regardless of other results.
   - Surges (lightning bolt symbols) can be spent on special abilities listed on weapon/class cards.

3. **SEARCH** — If adjacent to or on a search token, pick it up. Draw a search card (usually items or gold).

4. **REST** — Recover all fatigue at the end of this turn. Cannot be taken twice.

5. **STAND UP** — If knocked out, use both actions to stand up with partial health.

6. **REVIVE** — If adjacent to a knocked-out hero, revive them (they recover some health).

7. **SPECIAL** — Some quests have unique actions (open doors, interact with objectives, etc.).

### Fatigue System:
- Heroes can **suffer fatigue to gain extra movement** (1 fatigue = 1 extra space) at any point during their turn.
- Many class abilities cost fatigue to use.
- Fatigue cannot exceed your Stamina value. Excess fatigue becomes damage.

### Overlord Turn:
1. **Draw 1 Overlord card** from the Overlord deck.
2. **Activate each monster group** — each monster in every group on the map gets 1 activation:
   - Each monster can perform **2 actions** (same options as heroes: move and attack primarily).
   - **Master monsters** (red base) have enhanced stats and abilities vs. **Minion monsters** (white base).
3. **Play Overlord cards** — can be played at various timing windows (some during hero turns as interrupts).

### Line of Sight:
- Needed for ranged attacks and some abilities.
- Draw an imaginary line from any corner of your space to any corner of the target space. If the line doesn't pass through a blocked space (wall, closed door, obstacle), you have LOS.

### Knocked Out:
- When a hero reaches 0 health, they are **knocked out** (not eliminated).
- The hero's figure is replaced with a token. They drop all search items.
- On their next turn, they must use a Stand Up action (costs both actions) to recover with health equal to half their max (rounded up).
- The Overlord may win quests by knocking out all heroes or achieving objectives while heroes are down.

## SCORING / WINNING
### Quest Victory:
Each quest has specific victory conditions for both sides, described in the Quest Guide:
- Heroes might need to escape, defeat a boss, retrieve an object, or protect NPCs.
- The Overlord might need to kill heroes a certain number of times, advance a doom track, or achieve map objectives.
- Most quests have 2 encounters. The loser of Encounter 1 often gets a small advantage in Encounter 2.

### Campaign Progression:
- After each quest, the winning side gets rewards (gold, relics, XP, Overlord cards).
- Both sides spend XP to buy new class cards (new abilities).
- Heroes can buy new equipment from the shop between quests.
- The campaign branches based on which side won each quest.
- The campaign ends with a finale quest — the ultimate winner is whoever wins the finale.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Heroes should coordinate their turns.** Order matters — a healer going first to restore health before the warrior charges in can be critical.
- **Fatigue for movement is powerful.** Don't forget you can push 1-2 extra spaces at the cost of fatigue.
- **The Overlord's card draws are their most powerful tool.** Cards like "Dark Fortitude" (heal a monster) or "Dash" (extra monster movement) can swing encounters.
- **Search tokens are worth grabbing** but don't waste critical turns on them when objectives are time-sensitive.
- **Line of sight and positioning matter enormously.** Blocking corridors with hero figures (large figures block movement) can control monster flow.
- **Master vs. Minion distinction** — always target master monsters first if possible; they hit harder and have special abilities.
- **Large monsters (2x2)** occupy 4 spaces but only need 1 space free to move into (they expand). They also block LOS and movement.
- **Conditions like Stun are devastating** — a stunned monster loses 1 action on its next activation.
- **The campaign is designed to be close.** Don't give up if you lose early quests — the catch-up mechanisms keep it competitive.
- **For 2 players:** One player is the Overlord, one controls all 2 heroes (minimum 2 heroes required for balance).
