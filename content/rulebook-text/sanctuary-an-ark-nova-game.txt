## OVERVIEW
Sanctuary: An Ark Nova Game is a streamlined 2-player (or solo) spinoff of the hit game Ark Nova. Players build and manage wildlife sanctuaries, playing animal and sponsor cards, building enclosures, and advancing conservation efforts. It distills Ark Nova's core card-play and action-selection mechanisms into a tighter, faster experience designed specifically for 1-2 players.

## COMPONENTS
- 2 sanctuary boards (player boards with enclosure spaces)
- Action cards (5 per player — similar to Ark Nova's action card system)
- Animal cards (deck of animals with various requirements, abilities, and appeal/conservation values)
- Sponsor cards (provide ongoing benefits and bonuses)
- Conservation cards (advance conservation goals)
- Enclosure tiles (various sizes: 1-space up to 4-space enclosures)
- Appeal track and Conservation track (shared or per-player)
- Tokens: money, X-tokens (reputation/multiplier tokens), break tokens
- Map board (for conservation projects)
- Solo mode components

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a sanctuary board and places it in front of them.
2. Shuffle the main card deck and deal each player a starting hand (typically 4-6 cards).
3. Each player arranges their 5 action cards in a row (numbered positions 1-5, determining action strength).
4. Set out the enclosure tile supply, sorted by size.
5. Place money and token supplies within reach.
6. Set appeal and conservation markers at their starting positions.
7. Prepare the conservation map/track.
8. For solo: set up the solo opponent's automated system.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players alternate turns. On your turn, you perform **one action** by choosing one of your 5 action cards.

### The Action Card System (Core Mechanism):
- You have **5 action cards** arranged in a row, positioned at strength levels 1 through 5.
- When you choose an action card, **its position determines its strength** (position 5 = strongest, position 1 = weakest).
- After using an action card, it moves to **position 1** (weakest), and all cards below it shift up by one position.
- This creates a cycling system where recently used actions are weak and gradually regain strength.

### The 5 Actions:

1. **ANIMALS** — Play an animal card from your hand to your sanctuary:
   - Pay the animal's cost in money.
   - The animal must be placed in an appropriate enclosure (matching size and sometimes biome/type requirements).
   - Animals provide **Appeal** (VP track) and/or **Conservation** points.
   - Higher action strength = ability to play more expensive/powerful animals or gain bonuses.
   - Animal abilities may trigger when played (immediate effects) or provide ongoing bonuses.

2. **BUILD** — Construct enclosures on your sanctuary board:
   - Take an enclosure tile from the supply and place it on your board.
   - Enclosures come in sizes (1, 2, 3, 4 spaces) — larger ones house bigger animals.
   - Higher action strength = build larger enclosures or gain discounts.
   - Enclosure placement is spatial — you must fit tiles onto your grid.

3. **CARDS** — Draw cards or manage your hand:
   - Draw cards from the deck or a display row.
   - Higher action strength = draw more cards or access better selection.
   - You may also use this action to snap up cards from a shared market/display.

4. **SPONSORS** — Play sponsor cards for ongoing benefits:
   - Sponsors provide income, discounts, or special abilities.
   - Higher action strength = play more powerful sponsors or gain extra benefits.

5. **CONSERVATION** — Advance conservation projects:
   - Spend resources to contribute to conservation goals on the shared map/track.
   - Earns conservation points (crucial for winning — see scoring).
   - Higher action strength = greater contribution or access to better projects.

### X-Tokens:
- X-tokens are a versatile bonus currency earned from various sources.
- They can be spent to boost action strength, pay costs, or trigger special abilities.
- Managing X-tokens efficiently is key to optimizing turns.

### Break System:
- After a set number of turns, a **break** occurs (similar to Ark Nova's break mechanism).
- During a break: collect income, refill card displays, and check conservation milestones.
- Breaks pace the game and provide regular income injections.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game uses the **dual-track system** from Ark Nova:

- **Appeal track** — advances when you play attractive animals and build impressive facilities. Moves your Appeal marker forward.
- **Conservation track** — advances when you complete conservation projects and play conservation-focused animals. Moves your Conservation marker forward.

**The game ends** when one player's Appeal marker and Conservation marker **cross each other** (or meet). The moment this happens, that player wins (or final scoring is triggered).

**If the game end is triggered by deck depletion or break limit:**
- Calculate the **difference** between each player's Appeal and Conservation positions.
- The player whose markers are closest together (or have crossed) wins.
- If comparing two players, the higher combined position wins.

This means you must balance BOTH Appeal and Conservation — maximizing one while ignoring the other will not win the game.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Balance Appeal and Conservation.** This is the most critical strategic consideration. An all-appeal or all-conservation strategy loses because you need both markers to converge.
- **Action card cycling is the heart of the game.** Plan 2-3 turns ahead. If you need a strong Build action, wait until it cycles to position 4-5 before using it.
- **Enclosure planning is spatial.** Map out which animals you want and build enclosures to match BEFORE drawing those animals.
- **X-tokens are extremely valuable.** Never waste them. Boosting a position-3 action to effective position-5 can be game-changing.
- **Sponsors early, animals later.** Sponsors provide ongoing benefits that compound over the game. Playing them early maximizes their value.
- **Card draw is not glamorous but essential.** You need cards in hand to have options. Don't neglect the Cards action.
- **Conservation projects often have requirements** (specific animal types in your sanctuary, specific enclosures built). Check requirements before committing.
- **The 2-player game is interactive** — you compete for cards in the shared display and conservation projects on the map.
- **Solo mode** provides a competitive automated opponent with scaling difficulty.
- **If you've played Ark Nova,** the core mechanisms will feel familiar but the tighter card pool and 2-player focus create a distinct strategic experience. Games are roughly half the length.
