# Game: Keyflower
# Publisher: R&D Games
# Players: 2-6 | Time: 90-120 min | Weight: 3.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Keyflower is a game of auctions, worker placement, and tile-laying played over four seasons (rounds). Players use colored meeples (red, blue, yellow, and green) as both currency for bidding on village tiles and as workers to activate those tiles. The game combines tight resource management with spatial puzzle elements as players build their own village tableau.

## COMPONENTS
- 64 hexagonal village tiles (16 per season: Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter)
- 48 wooden meeples (red, blue, yellow — 16 each)
- 18 green meeples (earned through specific tiles)
- Skill tokens (iron, stone, wood, gold)
- Home tiles (1 per player, numbered 1-6)
- Player screens
- Turn order tiles
- Boat tiles (6, bringing workers to players each season)
- Transport tokens (wheelbarrow counters)

## SETUP
1. Each player receives a home tile, a player screen, and 8 random meeples drawn from the bag (kept hidden behind their screen).
2. Shuffle the boat tiles and deal a number equal to the player count. Place them face up — these show the meeples and skill tokens arriving this season.
3. Separate village tiles by season (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter). Shuffle the current season's tiles and reveal a number equal to the player count plus a few extras (varies by player count).
4. Each player receives 1 Winter tile dealt randomly (kept secret behind screen). Winter tiles are end-game scoring objectives.
5. Place the turn order tile in the center.
6. The starting player is determined randomly.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Season Flow
The game is played over 4 seasons (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter). Each season consists of multiple rounds of turns until all players have passed. Between seasons, won tiles are added to villages, new tiles and boats are revealed, and new meeples arrive.

### On Your Turn (choose one action or pass)
1. **Bid on a tile**: Place one or more meeples on the border of any unclaimed village tile in the center. You must use meeples of a SINGLE COLOR, and that color is now locked for that tile — all future bids on this tile must use the same color. To outbid, place MORE meeples of that same color than the current highest bid. Outbid meeples are returned to their owner.

2. **Use a tile (worker placement)**: Place one or more meeples ON a tile (in the center OR in any player's village, including your own). The meeples must match the color already established on that tile (if any workers are already there). A tile can be activated at most 3 times per season (max 6 meeples on it — placed in groups of 1-2). You gain the tile's benefit immediately.

3. **Pass**: Once you pass, you cannot take any more actions this season. The first player to pass takes the turn order tile and goes first next season.

### Color Rules (Critical)
- Once a meeple of a certain color is placed on or next to a tile, ALL subsequent meeples placed on/next to that tile this season must be the same color.
- Bidding meeples (on the edge) and worker meeples (on the tile face) are tracked separately but must share the same color.
- This creates intense competition — if someone places a blue worker on a tile, you must also use blue to bid on it or place workers.

### End of Season
- Highest bidder on each tile wins it. Winning bid meeples go to the general supply. Losing bid meeples return to their owners.
- Workers placed on central tiles go to the winning bidder (or stay on player village tiles).
- Won tiles are added to the player's village, connecting roads edge-to-edge.
- New boats arrive — players collect meeples/resources shown on their boats.
- New season tiles are revealed.

### Tile Placement Rules
- Tiles must be placed adjacent to existing tiles in the player's village.
- Road connections do not need to match (unlike Carcassonne), but roads are used for transporting resources.

### Transporting Resources
- Resources (wood, stone, iron, gold) must be moved along connected roads from the tile they are on to the tile that needs them.
- Each road segment costs 1 transport (moving a resource from one tile to an adjacent tile = 1 transport). Players start with limited transport capacity.

### Green Meeples
- Green meeples are earned only through specific tiles (e.g., the Keythedral).
- They function identically to other colors but are rarer and give more flexibility since they can match any established color on a tile (they are wild for color matching purposes — VERIFY THIS RULE).

### Winter Season (Final Round)
- Winter tiles are revealed from behind player screens and bid/used like normal tiles.
- Winter tiles often provide end-game scoring conditions (e.g., points per connected road, points per meeple type, etc.).
- After the final season resolves, the game ends.

## SCORING / WINNING
- Points from village tiles (many tiles have VP values printed on them).
- Points from Winter tile scoring conditions.
- Points from gold on tiles (1 VP per gold).
- Points from skill tokens upgraded and placed on tiles.
- The player with the most victory points wins. Ties are broken by the player with the most meeples remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Meeple management is paramount**: Your meeples are both your bidding currency and your workforce. Spending too many on bids leaves you unable to activate tiles, and vice versa.
- **Color scarcity drives strategy**: If you are low on blue meeples and an opponent starts a blue bid on a tile you want, you may be locked out. Diversify your meeple colors.
- **Using opponent's tiles**: You can place workers on tiles in other players' villages, but those workers stay there — your opponent keeps them at season's end. This is a meaningful cost.
- **Boats matter**: The meeples and resources from boats are a major source of income. Bidding on favorable boats (which work like tile auctions) is important.
- **Village layout**: Plan your road network. Resources need connected roads to move, and transport is limited.
- **Winter tiles**: Keep your secret Winter tile in mind from the start. It shapes your entire strategy.
- **Turn order**: Passing early gives you first-player advantage next round but costs you actions this round.
- **2-player games**: Tend to be tighter and more directly competitive with less tile variety.
