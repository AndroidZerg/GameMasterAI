# Game: The Colonists
# Publisher: Lookout Games
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 30-360 min | Weight: 4.1/5

## OVERVIEW
The Colonists is an epic worker-placement and resource-management game spanning four eras of a growing colonial settlement. Players develop their communities by constructing buildings, managing citizens of increasing skill levels, and trading goods across a modular hex-based market board. The game is notable for its scalability — players can choose to play just 1 era (about 30-60 min) or all 4 eras for the full multi-hour experience.

## COMPONENTS
- Modular hex market tiles (different sets for each era)
- Player community boards (personal tableau areas)
- Ambassador pawns (1 per player, moved around the hex market)
- Resource tokens (wood, clay, ore, food, cloth, tools, bricks, iron, coal, etc. — resources evolve across eras)
- Citizen tokens in 3 tiers: Farmers (basic), Citizens (mid), Merchants (advanced)
- Building cards across 4 eras (residential, production, storage, public)
- Storage limits tiles and warehouse markers
- Era decks and setup cards
- Victory point tokens
- Solo mode components

## SETUP
1. Select which eras to play (Era I only for introductory game, or any combination up to all 4).
2. Build the hex market: Place the starting hex tiles for your chosen era(s) as shown in the setup guide. Each hex represents a location with a specific action.
3. Each player takes a community board, starting buildings (a basic house and basic storage), starting resources (varies by era), and 3 Farmer citizens.
4. Place Ambassador pawns on the designated starting hex.
5. Sort building cards by era and type; place them in accessible stacks.
6. Place resource supply tokens in a general supply.
7. Determine starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over a series of years (rounds), with each era containing multiple years. Each year consists of:

### Year Structure
1. **Add New Market Tiles**: At the start of certain years, add new hex tiles to the expanding market board, providing new action spaces.
2. **Player Turns**: Players take turns in order. On your turn, move your Ambassador pawn to an adjacent hex tile and perform the action on that tile. You MUST move (you cannot stay on the same tile).
3. **Year End**: After all players have taken a set number of turns (typically 3 turns per year), the year ends. Perform upkeep — feed citizens, check storage limits.

### Key Actions (Hex Tile Types)
- **Production Buildings**: Produce resources using citizens assigned to them. Better citizens produce more.
- **Market/Trading**: Exchange resources at market rates.
- **Construction**: Build new buildings from available cards by paying resource costs. Buildings are placed in your community.
- **Upgrade Citizens**: Promote Farmers to Citizens, Citizens to Merchants. Upgraded citizens are more productive but have higher upkeep.
- **Storage**: Expand your storage capacity. Each resource type has a storage limit; excess is lost.
- **Special Actions**: Era-specific hex tiles offering unique abilities.

### Community Management
- Buildings in your community are organized in a grid. Some buildings require adjacency to others for bonuses.
- Each building has slots for citizens. Citizens assigned to buildings can activate them for production.
- Residential buildings house citizens. You need housing capacity before recruiting new citizens.
- Storage buildings determine how many resources you can hold of each type.

### Era Progression
- After all years in an era are complete, perform Era scoring.
- If playing multiple eras, set up the next era's hex tiles and continue.
- Buildings and progress carry over between eras.

## SCORING / WINNING
Victory points are earned through:
- **Buildings**: Each building in your community has a VP value. More advanced era buildings are worth more.
- **Citizens**: Higher-tier citizens (Citizens, Merchants) are worth VP.
- **Stored Resources**: Some resources convert to VP at game end.
- **Public Buildings**: Special buildings that score based on conditions (e.g., number of Merchants, types of buildings built).
- **Embassy Points**: Gained through certain actions during play.

The player with the most Victory Points at the end of the final era wins. Tiebreaker is the player with the most remaining resources.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- You MUST move your Ambassador each turn — you cannot stay put or revisit the same tile immediately.
- Storage limits are strict. If you produce resources beyond your storage capacity, excess is discarded. Expanding storage early is crucial.
- Citizens require food each year during upkeep. Farmers need 1 food, Citizens need 1 food + 1 cloth, Merchants need 1 food + 1 cloth + 1 tool. Failure to feed causes penalties (losing citizens or VP).
- Upgrading citizens is powerful but increases upkeep costs — balance growth with sustainability.
- The hex market grows each year, so action spaces that were far away become accessible as the board expands.
- Blocking: When your Ambassador is on a hex, other players cannot use that hex. Positioning is strategic.
- Common mistake: Neglecting storage — you can lose critical resources if you produce beyond capacity.
- Common mistake: Over-upgrading citizens without enough food/cloth/tool production to sustain them.
- For shorter games, playing just Era I or Era I+II is recommended for learning. The full 4-era game can take 4-6 hours.
- In solo mode, you play against a simulated opponent that blocks certain hex tiles.
