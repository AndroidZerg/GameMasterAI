# Game: Biblios
# Publisher: Iello
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 30 min | Weight: 1.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Biblios is a card game set in a medieval monastery where players are abbots competing to amass the greatest library of sacred books. The game is played in two distinct phases: a Gift phase (card drafting) and an Auction phase. Players collect cards in five colored categories (pigments, holy books, manuscripts, forbidden tomes, and monks) and manipulate the value dice to maximize their scoring.

## COMPONENTS
- 87 cards: Category cards in 5 colors (values 1-4), Gold cards (values 1-3), Church cards (manipulate dice)
- 5 colored dice (one per category), each showing values 1-6
- 1 game board (small board to hold the dice)

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center and set each of the 5 category dice to 3.
2. Shuffle all 87 cards into a single deck.
3. Based on player count, remove a specific number of random cards face-down from the game (unseen): 2 players remove 22 cards; 3 players remove 15 cards; 4 players remove 8 cards. These removed cards are not used and remain secret.
4. The starting player is determined (youngest player or random).
5. No cards are dealt to start — all cards come from the Gift phase.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Phase 1: Gift Phase (Card Drafting)
The active player draws cards one at a time from the deck, equal to the number of players plus one (e.g., 3 cards in a 2-player game, 5 cards in a 4-player game). For each card drawn, the active player must immediately assign it to one of three destinations before drawing the next card:
- **Keep it** (place face-down in their hand) — may only keep exactly 1 card per turn
- **Give it away** (place face-up in front of another player) — each opponent must receive exactly 1 card per turn
- **Auction pile** (place face-down on the auction pile) — exactly 1 card goes here per turn

The key tension: cards are drawn one at a time and must be assigned immediately. You might give away a great card early hoping for better, only to be disappointed. After all cards are assigned, the next player clockwise becomes active. The Gift Phase ends when the draw deck is empty.

### Phase 2: Auction Phase
Shuffle the auction pile. Reveal cards from the auction pile one at a time and auction each:
- **Category cards**: Players bid using Gold cards from their hand (highest bid wins; gold cards spent are discarded). Minimum bid is 1 gold. Players bid in clockwise order starting left of the auctioneer; you may bid or pass. Once you pass, you are out for that card.
- **Gold cards**: Players bid using Category cards from their hand (highest total value wins; cards spent are discarded). This reversal is critical — you pay with your scoring cards to gain gold.
- **Church cards**: Auctioned with Gold cards, same as category cards. The winner plays the Church card effect immediately.

Church card effects allow the player to adjust one die up or down by 1 (within 1-6 range), effectively changing how many victory points a category is worth.

The Auction Phase ends when all auction pile cards have been resolved.

## SCORING / WINNING
After the Auction Phase, each player totals the values of their cards in each of the five color categories. The player with the highest total in a category wins that category's die. In case of a tie, no one wins that die. The player with the highest total value of dice won is the winner. If tied, the player who won more dice wins. If still tied, players share the victory.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- During the Gift Phase, you MUST assign each card immediately before drawing the next — you cannot look ahead.
- Church cards played from your hand (not auctioned) can be played at any time during either phase on your turn as a free action.
- In a 2-player game, you draw 3 cards: keep 1, give 1 to opponent, 1 to auction. The opponent does the same on their turn.
- Gold cards have no value at end of game — spend them all in auctions.
- Manipulating dice is extremely powerful. Raising a die you're winning or lowering one you're losing can swing the game dramatically.
- Focus on 2-3 categories rather than trying to win all 5.
- Track what colors your opponents are collecting to avoid bidding wars.
- The auction pile is face-down, so remember what you put there during the Gift Phase.
