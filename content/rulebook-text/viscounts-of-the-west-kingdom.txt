## OVERVIEW
Viscounts of the West Kingdom is the third game in the West Kingdom trilogy, combining deck building with a rondel movement mechanism. Players move their viscount around a circular board, constructing buildings, placing workers in the castle, trading at the trading post, and transcribing manuscripts in the workshop. The game features a virtue/corruption track that influences scoring and access to certain actions.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 main board (circular rondel with sections)
- Castle board (3 tiers)
- Player boards (1 per player)
- Townsfolk cards (large shared deck for deck building)
- Starting deck cards (8 per player)
- Viscount meeples (1 per player)
- Workers (small cubes in player colors)
- Building pieces (3D cardboard buildings in player colors)
- Debt cards
- Deed cards
- Manuscript tiles
- Resource tokens (gold, stone, ink, provisions — VERIFY exact types)
- Virtue/Corruption track markers
- Hero and leader cards (for solo mode)
- End-game scoring cards

## SETUP
1. Place the main board in the center. It shows a circular rondel of connected areas.
2. Place the castle board nearby.
3. Each player takes their player board, starting deck of 8 cards, workers, buildings, and viscount meeple.
4. Shuffle starting decks and draw 3 cards as starting hand.
5. Place viscount meeples on the designated starting space of the rondel.
6. Populate the rondel spaces with townsfolk cards (specific distribution per rules).
7. Place manuscript tiles, debt cards, and deed cards in their supply areas.
8. Set each player's marker on the middle of the virtue/corruption track.
9. Determine starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Turn Overview
On your turn, you perform these steps in order:
1. **Place your Viscount** (move along the rondel)
2. **Resolve the Area** (take the area's main action)
3. **Play/Activate Cards** (play cards from hand for effects and resources)
4. **Collide with other Viscounts** (if applicable)
5. **End of Turn** (draw back up, manage hand)

### Step 1: Move Your Viscount
- Move your viscount 1 to 3 spaces clockwise around the rondel (you must move at least 1).
- You may pick up townsfolk cards from spaces you pass through or land on (they go to your discard pile — this is how you acquire new cards for your deck).
- Cards on the rondel have a cost that must be paid to recruit them.

### Step 2: Resolve the Area
Each area on the rondel corresponds to one of the main action types. The area you LAND on determines which action you can take:

**Castle**: Place one of your workers in the castle. The castle has 3 tiers — bottom tier costs less but scores less, higher tiers require more resources but score more. Workers in the castle score VP at end of game based on position and neighbors.

**Trading Post**: Gain resources, exchange goods, or take other economic actions. The specific benefit depends on the trading post space.

**Workshop (Transcribe)**: Transcribe a manuscript by paying the required resources (typically ink and gold). Manuscripts provide immediate bonuses and end-game scoring.

**Build**: Construct a building in the area where your viscount currently is. Buildings require resources (stone, wood, etc.) and provide ongoing benefits or end-game points. Buildings placed on the rondel create bonuses for future visits.

### Step 3: Play Cards
- Play townsfolk cards from your hand to generate resources, gain bonuses, or enhance your action.
- Cards have types (Cleric, Knight, Merchant, Scholar — VERIFY exact types) that synergize with different actions.
- Cards with matching types to the area action provide enhanced benefits.
- Some cards have "collision" powers that trigger when your viscount shares a space with an opponent's.

### Step 4: Collisions
- If your viscount ends on a space with another player's viscount, a collision occurs.
- Collision effects from your played cards activate, which can provide benefits or hinder the other player.

### Step 5: End of Turn
- Discard played cards and any remaining hand cards to your discard pile (or some stay in your "tableau" area — VERIFY).
- Draw 3 new cards from your deck. If your deck is empty, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck.

### Virtue and Corruption
- Certain actions move your marker on the virtue/corruption track.
- **Virtuous side**: Unlocks access to certain castle areas and scoring bonuses. More "honorable" actions.
- **Corrupt side**: Unlocks different powers and debt cards. Debts provide short-term benefits but negative end-game VP if not resolved.
- Your position on this track significantly affects end-game scoring.

### Debt Cards
- Gained when taking corrupt actions or as a cost for certain powerful effects.
- Debt cards clog your deck (they do nothing when drawn) and cost VP at end of game.
- Can be removed from your deck through specific actions.

### Deed Cards
- Earned through virtuous actions or specific achievements.
- Provide end-game VP bonuses.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when a certain number of buildings have been placed, workers placed in the castle, or manuscripts transcribed (specific trigger conditions — typically when any two of three "end game" tracks are filled).

Final scoring includes:
- VP from buildings constructed
- VP from workers placed in the castle (based on tier and adjacency)
- VP from transcribed manuscripts
- VP from deed cards
- VP (or negative VP) based on position on virtue/corruption track
- Penalty VP from unresolved debt cards
- VP from any end-game bonus cards

The player with the most VP wins. Ties broken by most remaining resources.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Deck thinning is valuable**: Your deck cycles frequently with only 3-card hands. Removing weak starting cards and debts makes your draws more consistent.
- **Plan your rondel movement**: You can only move 1-3 spaces, so plan ahead to reach the areas you need.
- **Card type synergy matters**: Playing Clerks when doing castle actions (for example) provides enhanced benefits. Build your deck around your strategy.
- **Virtue vs. Corruption is a strategic choice**: Neither is inherently better — both paths have strong scoring potential. But committing to one side is generally better than sitting in the middle.
- **Buildings on the rondel benefit everyone who lands there**: Be mindful of what you build and where.
- **Castle placement is a spatial puzzle**: Higher tiers score more but cost more. Surrounding opponent workers can matter.
- **Watch the end-game triggers**: The game can end faster than expected when players aggressively pursue two of the three tracks.
- **Recruiting townsfolk as you move**: Don't forget this is how you build your deck — the cards on the rondel are your "market."
