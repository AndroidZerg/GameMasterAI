# Game: Final Girl
# Publisher: Van Ryder Games
# Players: 1-1 | Time: 20-60 min | Weight: 2.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Final Girl is a solo horror survival card game inspired by classic slasher films. You play as the Final Girl — the last survivor who must defeat the killer before time runs out or everyone dies. The game uses a unique card-driven system where you manage action cards, horror cards, and a countdown timer across multiple thematic Feature Film boxes paired with a core box.

## COMPONENTS
- Core Box: Action cards, Horror cards, Item cards, Event cards, Final Girl cards, Time track board, Terror track, dice (multiple d6), heart tokens (health), player board
- Feature Film Box (one required): Killer board/miniature, Victim cards, Location board (unique per film), Special killer cards, Killer-specific Horror cards, Setup card, Finale cards, Location tiles
- Various tokens: bloodlust markers, search tokens, victim standees, killer standee

## SETUP
1. Choose one Final Girl character card and one Feature Film box (killer + location).
2. Place the Location board in the center; populate it with Victim standees according to the setup card.
3. Place the Killer standee on its starting location as indicated.
4. Set the Time marker on the time track (typically starts around 7-10 depending on scenario).
5. Set your Final Girl's health to her starting value (usually 6 hearts).
6. Set the Terror track to 0.
7. Shuffle the Action card deck, Horror card deck, and Item deck separately.
8. Draw your starting hand of Action cards (typically 5).
9. Place the Bloodlust marker on the killer board at its starting position.
10. Follow any additional Feature Film-specific setup instructions.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each round consists of several phases:

### Phase 1: Action Phase
- You have a hand of Action cards. Each card can be used in one of two ways:
  - **Play for its action**: Use the card's effect (attack, move, search, rescue, etc.). Many actions require spending the card plus rolling dice.
  - **Discard for its time value**: Each card has a time value (1-3). You can discard cards to "buy time" — adding their values together to take generic actions.
- Generic actions (cost time points): Move (1 time), Search a location (2 time), Attack the killer (2 time), Heal (2 time), Rescue a victim (1 time).
- You may play/discard as many cards as you wish during the Action Phase.
- **Dice rolls**: When attacking, roll dice equal to your attack value. Hits are scored on 4+ (typically). The killer has a health track that must be depleted.

### Phase 2: Horror Phase
- Draw Horror cards equal to the current Horror level (increases as terror rises).
- Resolve each Horror card — these represent the killer acting: moving, attacking victims, attacking you, triggering events.
- The killer gains Bloodlust as victims die, making it more dangerous over time.

### Phase 3: Killer Phase
- The killer acts according to its Bloodlust level and any special rules on its board.
- Higher Bloodlust = more actions, more damage, special abilities unlocked.
- The killer moves toward victims or the Final Girl and attacks.
- Victims in the killer's location may be killed (removed from the board, raising Terror).

### Phase 4: Upkeep Phase
- Advance the Time marker by 1 (time is running out).
- Draw back up to your hand limit of Action cards (usually 5).
- Check for Finale conditions: if the Time track reaches 0, a Finale event triggers.
- Adjust Terror and Bloodlust tracks as needed.

### Key Mechanisms
- **Terror Track**: Rises as victims die. Higher Terror means more Horror cards drawn, making the game harder. If Terror maxes out, you may lose.
- **Bloodlust**: The killer's rage meter. As it increases, the killer becomes faster and deadlier.
- **Items**: Found by searching locations. Provide one-time or ongoing benefits (weapons, keys, flashlights).
- **Victims**: NPCs on the board. You can try to rescue them (move them to safety) or they may be killed by the killer.
- **Finale**: When time runs out, you enter a climactic final confrontation with the killer under special rules.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win condition**: Reduce the killer's health to 0 before all victims (and you) are dead or before the Finale kills you.
- **Lose conditions**: Your Final Girl's health reaches 0 (you die), OR the Terror track maxes out and the loss condition triggers, OR the Finale event kills you.
- After winning, you can track your score based on: number of surviving victims, health remaining, time remaining, and items collected. Higher scores represent more heroic victories.
- The game includes a scoring rubric on the back of the setup card for ranking your performance.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Prioritize keeping victims alive early — each death raises Terror AND Bloodlust, creating a death spiral.
- Search actions are crucial: items like weapons dramatically increase your combat effectiveness.
- Don't engage the killer too early unless you have good weapons. Early turns are better spent searching and rescuing.
- Pay attention to the killer's unique mechanics — each Feature Film killer plays very differently (e.g., some teleport, some spawn minions, some set traps).
- Horror cards are the killer's "turns" — reducing Terror means fewer Horror cards, which means less killer activity.
- The Finale is extremely difficult. Try to weaken the killer significantly before time runs out.
- Each Final Girl character has unique abilities — lean into her strengths when choosing actions.
- The game is highly modular: any Final Girl can face any killer at any location, creating huge replayability.
