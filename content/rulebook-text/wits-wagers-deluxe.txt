# Game: Wits & Wagers
# Publisher: North Star Games
# Players: 3-7 | Time: 25 min | Weight: 1.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Wits & Wagers is a party trivia game where you don't need to know the answers — you just need to know who does. Each round, players write numerical answers to a trivia question, then bet on which answer they think is closest to the correct one without going over. It combines trivia knowledge with betting strategy, making it accessible to all skill levels.

## COMPONENTS
- 100+ trivia question cards (each card has multiple questions)
- Betting mat (felt mat with payout odds: 4:1, 3:1, 2:1, 2:1, 3:1, 4:1, and a center row)
- Answer cards/dry-erase boards and markers (1 per player/team)
- Poker-style betting chips in two denominations (small and large)
- 2 betting tokens per player (small and large — "Meeple" shaped)
- Cardboard point chips or scoring track
- Sand timer

## SETUP
1. Give each player (or team) an answer card/dry-erase board and marker.
2. Give each player 2 betting chips/meeples (1 small, 1 large).
3. Place the betting mat in the center of the table.
4. Shuffle the question cards and place them in a draw pile.
5. Each player starts with a set of starting chips (or tokens representing points).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over 7 rounds. Each round has 3 phases:

### Phase 1: Question & Answer
- One player reads a trivia question aloud. All answers are numerical (e.g., "In what year was the Eiffel Tower completed?").
- All players simultaneously write their best guess on their answer card. Use the sand timer (30 seconds) to keep pace.
- Reveal all answers simultaneously and arrange them in numerical order on the betting mat, from lowest to highest. Each answer occupies one row on the mat.

### Phase 2: Place Bets
- Players now bet on which answer they believe is closest to the correct answer WITHOUT going over.
- Each player has 2 betting tokens: a small bet (worth 1 chip payout) and a large bet (worth 2 chips payout).
- Place your betting tokens on any answer row(s) on the mat. You may place both on the same answer or split them.
- You may also bet on "Smaller than all answers" if you think every guess is too high.
- Betting is simultaneous — all players place bets at the same time.

### Phase 3: Reveal & Pay Out
- Read the correct answer aloud.
- The winning row is the answer that is closest to the correct answer WITHOUT exceeding it. If all answers are too high, the "Smaller" row wins.
- **Payouts**: The payout odds are printed on the mat. Center answers (most popular range) pay 2:1. Outer answers pay 3:1 or 4:1.
  - Small bet on winning row: earn chips equal to the row's payout ratio.
  - Large bet on winning row: earn chips equal to double the row's payout ratio.
- **Bonus**: The player(s) whose written answer is on the winning row receive a bonus of 3 chips, regardless of where they bet.
- Losing bets earn nothing (you do not lose your betting tokens — they are reused each round, not wagered away).

### Round End
- Collect winnings. Return betting tokens to players.
- Proceed to the next question.

## SCORING / WINNING
- After 7 rounds, the player with the most chips wins.
- If tied, the tied players share the victory (or play a tiebreaker question if desired).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- You do NOT lose chips on wrong bets — you simply don't gain anything. This keeps the game fun and low-stress.
- Answers must be numerical. Questions are always designed to have a specific number as the answer.
- "Closest without going over" — like The Price is Right. An answer of 1900 beats an answer of 2000 if the correct answer is 1950, because 2000 goes over.
- You CAN bet on your own answer. If your answer is on the winning row, you get the 3-chip bonus PLUS any betting winnings.
- The payout odds reward risky bets on outlier answers: an answer on the edge of the mat pays 4:1 if correct.
- Common mistake: Forgetting that answers that go OVER the correct number are eliminated — only answers at or below count.
- Strategy tip: You don't need to know trivia — watch who at the table is confident and bet on their answer.
- Teams of 2-3 work well for larger groups, with each team sharing an answer card and betting tokens.
- The "Smaller" space catches cases where everyone overshoots.
- This game works great as a party game or warm-up game due to its simple rules and quick playtime.
