# Game: 6 Nimmt! (Take 5!)
# Publisher: Amigo
# Players: 2-10 | Time: 25 min | Weight: 1.1/5

## OVERVIEW
6 Nimmt! (also known as Take 5! or Take 6!) is a simultaneous card selection game where players try to avoid collecting cards with bull heads (penalty points). Each round, all players secretly choose a card from their hand and reveal simultaneously, then cards are placed in ascending order onto four rows. When a row reaches 6 cards, the player who triggered it must take the entire row as penalty points. Simple to learn but surprisingly strategic with larger groups.

## COMPONENTS
- 104 number cards (numbered 1-104), each with 1-7 bull heads printed on it:
  - Cards ending in **0**: 3 bull heads (10, 20, 30... 100)
  - Cards ending in **5**: 2 bull heads (5, 15, 25... 95)
  - Cards that are **doubles** (11, 22, 33, 44, 55, 66, 77, 88, 99): 5 bull heads
  - Card **55**: 7 bull heads (both a multiple of 5 and a double)
  - **All other cards**: 1 bull head

## SETUP
1. Shuffle all 104 cards thoroughly.
2. Deal **10 cards** face-down to each player. Players pick up their hand and look at it secretly.
3. Flip **4 cards** face-up from the deck and place them in a column to start 4 separate rows. Each row begins with one card.
4. Set aside the remaining cards — they are not used this round.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
The game is played over **10 turns** per round (one for each card in your hand).

### Each Turn
1. **Choose a card**: All players simultaneously and secretly select one card from their hand and place it face-down on the table.
2. **Reveal**: All players flip their chosen cards face-up at the same time.
3. **Place cards**: Starting with the LOWEST revealed card and proceeding in ascending order, each card is placed according to these rules:

### Placement Rules
Each card must be placed at the end of the row whose last card has the **highest value that is still LOWER than the played card**. In other words, the card goes to the row where it is closest to (but higher than) the current end card.

- **Example**: The four rows end in 12, 35, 67, and 89. A player plays card 42 — it goes to the row ending in 35 (closest lower value).

**Two special situations**:

**A. Sixth Card Rule**: If your card would be the **6th card** in a row, you do NOT place it. Instead:
   - You must take all 5 cards currently in that row (these are your penalty points — count the bull heads).
   - Your played card then becomes the new first card of that now-empty row.

**B. Lowest Card Rule**: If your card is **lower than ALL four row-ending cards** (it can't legally go on any row), you must:
   - Choose any one of the four rows.
   - Take ALL cards from that row as penalty points.
   - Your played card becomes the new first card of that row.
   - **Strategy**: Pick the row with the fewest bull heads!

### End of Turn
After all revealed cards have been placed (in ascending order), the turn is complete. Players choose another card from their remaining hand for the next turn.

## SCORING / WINNING

### Round Scoring
- After all 10 cards have been played, count the **bull heads** on all penalty cards you collected.
- Each bull head = 1 penalty point.

### Game End
- Play multiple rounds. After each round, add up bull heads.
- The game ends when any player reaches **66 bull heads** (or another agreed threshold).
- The player with the **fewest total bull heads** wins.

### Quick Game Variant
- Play just one round. Lowest bull heads wins.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Card 55 is the most dangerous card in the deck** (7 bull heads). Avoid rows where it might end up in your penalty pile.
- **Cards are placed in ascending order within a turn**: This matters because a lower card might fill up a row before a higher card gets placed, changing where the higher card goes.
- **Being forced to pick a row (lowest card rule) is usually bad**: Avoid playing extremely low cards unless you can predict a safe opening.
- **Count the rows**: Pay attention to how many cards are in each row. A row with 4 cards is dangerous — the next card placed there will trigger the 6th-card penalty for whoever plays card #6.
- **Watch what's been played**: As the game progresses, you can deduce what numbers other players might still hold. High-number cards are generally safer for avoiding the lowest-card penalty.
- **Mid-range cards are tricky**: They have many possible rows to land on, making their placement harder to predict.
- **With more players (6-10), chaos increases**: There's less control and more penalty-taking. Lean into risk management.
- **With fewer players (2-4), it's more strategic**: You have more control over which rows fill up.
- **Playing the lowest card when rows are short is strategic**: If all rows have just 1-2 cards, being forced to pick a row costs fewer bull heads.
- **The game scales beautifully** from 2 to 10 players, but plays best with 5-8.
- **Doubles (11, 22, 33, etc.) are 5 bull heads each** — extremely painful to collect. Watch for rows accumulating these.
- **No communication allowed** about what card you're playing — it's simultaneous and secret.
