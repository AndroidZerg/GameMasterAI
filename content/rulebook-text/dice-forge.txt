# Game: Dice Forge
# Publisher: Libellud
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 40 min | Weight: 1.9/5

## OVERVIEW
Dice Forge is a dice-crafting game set in a mythological realm where players are heroes competing for the favor of the gods. The unique twist is that players physically customize their dice by popping off and replacing die faces with upgraded ones, creating increasingly powerful dice over the course of the game. Players roll dice to earn gold, sun shards, and moon shards, which they spend to buy better die faces or heroic feat cards worth victory points.

## COMPONENTS
- 2 customizable dice per player (with removable faces)
- Die face tokens (stored in the Temple/Garden tray — organized by cost and type)
- Player boards with resource/VP tracks (gold, sun shards, moon shards, glory/VP)
- Heroic Feat cards (two types: instant effects and ongoing/permanent powers)
- Islands board showing available heroic feats
- Temple tray (die face marketplace) — a 3D cardboard insert that holds all purchasable faces
- Hammer token (starting player marker)
- Chest markers for resource tracking

## SETUP
1. Assemble the Temple tray and populate it with die face tokens organized by type and cost.
2. Set up the Islands board with Heroic Feat cards — each portal/space gets a stack of cards as indicated by the setup guide (different cards for 2, 3, or 4 players).
3. Each player takes a player board, 2 starting dice, and sets initial resources: Gold = 0, Sun Shards = 0, Moon Shards = 0, Glory Points = 0.
4. Starting dice have faces showing: one die with 1 gold on each face; one die with 1 gold on 5 faces and 1 sun shard on 1 face (exact configuration varies slightly).
5. Give each player starting gold (amount varies by player count and turn order — later players get a small gold bonus to compensate).
6. Determine starting player; give them the Hammer.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over a set number of rounds (9 rounds with 2 players, 10 rounds with 3-4 players).

### Divine Blessing (EVERYONE rolls — every turn!)
At the START of the active player's turn, ALL players roll both of their dice and gain the resources shown. This is critical — you benefit from your dice on EVERY player's turn, not just your own. Track resources on your player board.

### Active Player's Turn
The active player then performs ONE of the following actions (and may also perform a second action by spending 2 Sun Shards):

**Option A: Visit the Temple (Buy Die Faces)**
- Spend Gold to purchase die face tokens from the Temple tray.
- Pop off a face from one of your dice and replace it with the purchased face. Removed faces go to a discard area (they cannot be reused).
- You may buy multiple faces in one visit if you have enough gold.
- Die faces range from basic (more gold, 2-3 gold cost) to powerful (glory points, resource combos, or special effects — costing 8-12 gold).

**Option B: Visit an Island (Buy a Heroic Feat Card)**
- Move your hero pawn to an Island portal and pay the cost (Sun Shards, Moon Shards, or a combination) to take a Heroic Feat card.
- **Instant cards**: Resolve their effect immediately (gain VP, gain resources, forge a die face, etc.) and discard.
- **Permanent cards**: Place next to your board for ongoing effects. Some trigger during Divine Blessings, some give end-game scoring.
- If another player's pawn is on the portal you want to visit, you "oust" them — they are sent back to the starting island and immediately receive a bonus Divine Blessing (roll both dice and gain resources).

### Extra Action (2 Sun Shards)
Once per turn, you may spend 2 Sun Shards to perform a SECOND action (Temple or Island). This is extremely powerful and encourages investing in sun shard generation.

### Resource Limits
- Gold: maximum 12
- Sun Shards: maximum 6
- Moon Shards: maximum 6
- Glory Points: no maximum
- Resources beyond the cap are lost.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Glory Points (VP)** are tracked on the player board throughout the game.
- VP come from: die faces that show VP (rolled during Divine Blessings), Heroic Feat cards (instant VP or end-game VP), and some special die face effects.
- After the final round, add up: glory points from the track + any end-game scoring from permanent Heroic Feat cards.
- The player with the most Glory Points wins.
- Tiebreaker: the player with the most remaining gold, sun, and moon shards combined.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- ALL players roll dice at the start of EVERY player's turn. This means your dice produce resources much more frequently than you might expect — upgrading die faces early has compounding returns.
- Resource caps are tight (especially Sun/Moon at 6). Upgrade storage or spend resources before they cap out.
- Ousting opponents from islands gives them a free dice roll — sometimes it's worth NOT ousting if their dice are strong.
- The extra action for 2 Sun Shards is key to efficient play. Build toward sun shard production early.
- Common mistake: Forgetting that ALL players roll at the start of each turn, not just the active player.
- Common mistake: Losing resources to caps — watch your maximums.
- Replacing die faces is permanent — plan your dice carefully. You can't undo a forge.
- Mirror faces (copy another face result) and x3 multiplier faces are extremely powerful when combined with high-value faces.
- In 2-player games, you get fewer total turns but the same number of rounds, so efficiency matters even more.
