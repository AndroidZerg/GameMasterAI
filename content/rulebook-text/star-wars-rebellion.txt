# Game: Star Wars: Rebellion
# Publisher: Fantasy Flight Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 180-240 min | Weight: 3.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Star Wars: Rebellion is an epic asymmetric strategy game set in the Star Wars universe. The Empire player commands overwhelming military forces and must find and destroy the hidden Rebel base before time runs out. The Rebel player works to survive, complete secret objectives, and inspire the galaxy to rise up. The game combines area control, hidden movement, and a unique mission system using iconic characters as leaders.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board (32 systems across the Star Wars galaxy)
- Over 150 plastic miniatures (Star Destroyers, X-Wings, AT-ATs, Mon Calamari cruisers, etc.)
- Leader standees and rings (Luke, Vader, Leia, Palpatine, Han, Boba Fett, etc.)
- Mission cards (Rebel and Imperial)
- Objective cards (Rebel)
- Probe deck (for narrowing Rebel base location)
- Action cards, combat cards (space and ground)
- Project cards (Death Star plans, etc.)
- Time marker and reputation track
- Loyalty markers, subjugation markers
- Dice (custom combat dice)

## SETUP
1. Place the board and populate starting systems: Empire gets Coruscant, several loyal systems, and starting fleet (2 Star Destroyers, 3 Assault Carriers, various ground units). Rebels get a smaller starting force.
2. The Rebel player secretly selects a Rebel base location from the probe deck and places it face-down. The base can be in any system (including Imperial-loyal ones, though risky).
3. Each side takes their starting leaders (Empire: 4, Rebels: 4).
4. Rebel player draws a hand of objective cards and selects the starting objective.
5. Set the time marker to space 1 and the Rebel reputation marker to space 14. If reputation reaches the time marker, Rebels win.
6. Shuffle probe cards — the Empire uses these to eliminate possible base locations.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

Each round has three phases:

### 1. Assignment Phase
Players simultaneously and secretly assign their leaders to mission cards. Each mission requires a leader with specific skill icons (diplomacy, intel, spec ops, logistics). Assigned leaders are placed face-down on the chosen mission cards.

### 2. Command Phase
Players alternate turns. On your turn, you either:
- **Reveal a Mission**: Flip a leader and their assigned mission face-up, resolve it in a chosen system. The opponent may attempt to oppose the mission by sending a leader from their leader pool (unassigned leaders) to the same system. If opposed, both players roll dice based on relevant skill icons; the mission succeeds if the active player rolls enough successes.
- **Activate a System**: Take an unassigned leader from your leader pool, place them in a system, and move units from adjacent systems into that system. This is how you deploy and reposition fleets. If both sides have units in the system, combat begins.

The command phase continues until all leaders have been used or both players pass.

**Key Missions Include:**
- Empire: Gather Intel (draw probe cards), Build (deploy new units), Rule by Fear (subjugate systems), Capture Rebel Operatives
- Rebels: Sabotage, Incite Rebellion (flip loyalty), Hit and Run, Plant False Leads, Seek Yoda

### 3. Refresh Phase
- Retrieve all leaders from the board.
- Draw new mission cards (2 each).
- Recruit new leaders (based on the recruitment track; certain leaders require specific conditions, e.g., Luke needs "Seek Yoda" to unlock Jedi Luke).
- Launch probe droids (Empire draws probe cards to eliminate systems as possible base locations).
- Build units: Each loyal or subjugated system with a building queue produces units. Place units in the build queue; they deploy next round.
- Advance the time marker by 1.

### Combat
When both sides have units in a system, combat occurs in rounds:
1. Roll dice for each unit (different units roll different dice — red, black, etc.).
2. Assign damage: hits destroy health points of ships/ground, direct hits bypass shields.
3. Ships fight in space; ground units fight on the ground. You must have transport capacity to land ground troops.
4. Space combat and ground combat happen simultaneously each round.
5. Either side may retreat after any combat round.
6. Combat cards add special abilities (drawn at battle start, 1 per leader present).
7. Tactical cards can be played if a leader is present in the battle.

### The Death Star
The Empire can build and deploy the Death Star (and later the Death Star Under Construction). The completed Death Star can destroy entire systems — removing the planet and all units/loyalty there. This is devastating but also advances Rebel objectives.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Empire wins** by finding and conquering the Rebel base (moving ground troops into the base system and destroying all Rebel ground units there).
- **Rebels win** when the reputation marker meets or passes the time marker on the track. Reputation advances by completing secret objective cards (e.g., "Rebel forces destroyed an Imperial unit in combat this round," "Three systems are loyal to the Rebellion").
- The time marker advances each round (rounds 1-2-3... up to ~16). The reputation marker starts at 14 and moves down toward 1. If they meet, Rebels win.
- This creates increasing urgency for the Empire.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Probe cards are critical for the Empire**: Systematically eliminate systems. Focus probes on clusters of systems.
- **Rebels should move the base if discovered**: The "Rapid Mobilization" objective card lets you relocate the base. Keep it as insurance.
- **Leaders are dual-use**: A leader assigned to a mission can't activate a system (move fleets) that round, and vice versa. Balance carefully.
- **Subjugation vs. Loyalty**: The Empire can subjugate a system (military control without loyalty) or convert loyalty. Subjugated systems still produce for the Empire but may count for Rebel objectives.
- **Transport capacity**: Ground units need transport ships (Assault Carriers, Mon Cal cruisers) to move between systems. You can't invade a planet without transports.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that newly built units go into the build queue and don't deploy until the NEXT refresh phase.
- **Common mistake**: Attempting to oppose a mission with a leader who is already assigned to a mission (only leaders in the leader pool can oppose).
- **Rebel strategy**: Don't try to match the Empire militarily. Use hit-and-run tactics, complete objectives, and run out the clock.
- **Imperial strategy**: Don't spread too thin. Concentrate forces and use probe droids aggressively. The clock is your enemy.
- **In 3-4 player games**, each side splits responsibilities between two players (one commands fleet/ground, the other handles missions/diplomacy).
