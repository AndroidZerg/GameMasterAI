# SOURCE: AI knowledge summary — verify against official rulebook
# Game: Formula D
# Publisher: Asmodee
# Players: 2-10 | Time: 60 min | Weight: 1.8/5

## OVERVIEW
Formula D is a high-octane racing game where players compete in Formula 1-style cars around a track, managing their speed through a unique gear/dice system. Each gear corresponds to a different die with a different range of movement values. Players must navigate corners at safe speeds or risk damaging their car. The game supports both a simplified family mode and a more strategic advanced mode.

## COMPONENTS
- Double-sided game board (two different racing circuits)
- Car pawns (10 different colors)
- 6 gear dice (different sizes/colors for each gear): 1st gear (d4), 2nd gear (d6), 3rd gear (d8), 4th gear (d12), 5th gear (d20), 6th gear (d30)
- Dashboard/car mats (1 per player showing damage categories)
- Damage point (DP) markers or pegs
- Gear indicator (lever or dial on dashboard showing current gear)
- Starting grid tokens
- Lap counter tokens
- Advanced mode: character sheets with special abilities, pit stop rules

## SETUP
1. Choose a circuit (side of the board).
2. Each player selects a car and takes the matching dashboard.
3. Set all damage categories to their starting values on the dashboard: Tires (typically 6 DP), Brakes (3 DP), Gearbox (3 DP), Body (3 DP), Engine (3 DP), Suspension (3 DP). (Exact values vary by car in advanced mode.)
4. Determine starting positions: roll a die, or agree on positions. Place cars on the starting grid.
5. Set gear indicators to 1st gear for all players.
6. Decide number of laps (typically 1-2 for a standard game).
7. Place the appropriate gear dice nearby.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns in order based on track position (leader goes first). Each turn:

### Step 1: Shift Gears
- You may shift UP or DOWN by 1 gear, or stay in your current gear.
- Moving up: 1st -> 2nd -> 3rd -> 4th -> 5th -> 6th.
- Moving down: You cannot drop more than 1 gear per turn normally. Dropping gears costs Brake DP or Gearbox DP in some cases.
- You cannot skip gears when shifting up. Going from 2nd to 4th is not allowed.

### Step 2: Roll the Gear Die
- Roll the die that corresponds to your current gear:
  - 1st gear: d4 (range 1-2)
  - 2nd gear: d6 (range 2-4)
  - 3rd gear: d8 (range 4-8)
  - 4th gear: d12 (range 7-12)
  - 5th gear: d20 (range 11-20)
  - 6th gear: d30 (range 21-30)
- Move your car forward exactly the number of spaces rolled.

### Step 3: Handle Corners
Each corner on the track has two values: a **required number of stops** and a **speed limit** (maximum movement to end your turn on or past the corner entrance).

- You must STOP (end your movement) in the corner zone the required number of times (usually 1, 2, or 3 stops depending on corner severity).
- If you overshoot a corner (move past the corner zone without making the required stops), you take damage. For each stop you miss, you lose Tire DP. If you overshoot by more spaces, additional damage to body/suspension may apply.
- If Tire DP reaches 0, you spin out and must stop immediately, losing your next turn.

### Step 4: Collisions and Drafting
- If your path is blocked by another car, you stop behind them (some rules allow spending Body DP to push through or take alternate lines).
- **Slipstreaming/Drafting**: If you end your movement directly behind another car, you may get a drafting bonus of +3 spaces on your next turn (simplified rule).

### Pit Stops (Advanced Mode)
- You may enter the pit lane instead of continuing on the main track.
- In the pit, you move at reduced speed (specific die/rule) and can repair damage categories. The amount repaired depends on how many turns you spend in the pit.

## SCORING / WINNING
- The first car to cross the finish line after completing the required number of laps wins.
- If a car loses all DP in any one damage category, it is eliminated from the race.
  - Exception: If Tires reach 0, you spin out; if Engine reaches 0, your engine blows and you're eliminated; etc.
- Elimination = DNF (Did Not Finish). Last car standing wins in an elimination scenario.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Gear selection is the core strategy. Higher gears cover more ground but make cornering dangerous. Downshifting before corners is essential.
- You CANNOT skip gears when shifting up — plan several turns ahead as you approach corners.
- Each corner has a specific number of required stops displayed on the board. Read the track carefully.
- Tire damage from overshooting corners is the most common way to wreck your car.
- Common mistake: Staying in high gear too long and destroying your tires on a tight corner.
- Common mistake: Forgetting that you can only shift 1 gear per turn — you can't slam from 6th to 2nd.
- The 6th gear die (d30) has huge variance (21-30). Only use it on long straights.
- In multiplayer, track position determines turn order — being in front is advantageous for blocking.
- Advanced mode adds weather conditions, different car setups, tire compounds, and character abilities for more depth.
- The game plays up to 10 players, making it excellent for large groups, though downtime increases with more players.
