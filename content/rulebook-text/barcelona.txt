# Players: 1-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 3.2/5

## OVERVIEW
Barcelona is a tile-placement and action-selection game set during the 19th century urban planning revolution of Barcelona's Eixample district, designed by Ildefons Cerda. Players build the iconic grid-shaped city blocks, construct modernist buildings, establish public services, and gain the favor of Barcelona's citizens. The game combines spatial tile placement with multi-use cards and an innovative intersection-based action system.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 large game board (depicting Barcelona's grid of streets and intersections)
- Building tiles (residential, commercial, and public — various shapes)
- Citizen tokens (various types: workers, bourgeoisie, etc.)
- Player boards (1 per player)
- Action cards (multi-use cards for each player)
- Street tiles and tramway tokens
- Modernisme tokens (for landmark buildings)
- Cobblestone tokens
- Coins
- VP track
- Sagrada Familia board / track
- Solo mode components

## SETUP
1. Place the main board centrally. The board shows Barcelona's grid of city blocks with intersections.
2. Prepare building tile stacks sorted by type and place them near the board.
3. Distribute citizen tokens to designated starting areas on the board.
4. Each player takes their player board, starting hand of action cards, initial coins, and player markers.
5. Place scoring markers on the VP track at 0.
6. Set up the Sagrada Familia track and any public goal cards.
7. Populate the board with initial elements as indicated (some intersections start with services or citizens).
8. Determine first player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over a series of rounds. On your turn, you perform these steps:

### Step 1: CHOOSE AN INTERSECTION
- Select an **intersection** (a crossroads where streets meet) on the board to place your action marker.
- The intersection you choose determines **which city blocks** you can build in (the 1-4 blocks adjacent to that intersection) AND triggers actions based on what's at the intersection.
- **You cannot choose an intersection already occupied** by another player (blocking mechanism).

### Step 2: BUILD (Primary Action)
Place a **building tile** into one of the city blocks adjacent to your chosen intersection:
- City blocks are subdivided into smaller plots. You fill plots with building tiles.
- **Building types** include:
  - **Residential** (houses for citizens)
  - **Commercial** (generate income)
  - **Public services** (hospitals, schools, markets — attract citizens from adjacent areas)
- When a city block is **fully completed** (all plots filled), it scores bonus VP for contributors.
- Buildings may attract **citizens** who move into adjacent completed blocks.

### Step 3: PERFORM INTERSECTION ACTIONS
Based on the intersection's features and any tokens/services there, you gain bonuses:
- **Tramway**: Advance on the tramway track for bonuses and VP.
- **Citizens**: Interact with citizens at the intersection — recruit them, gain their bonuses.
- **Cobblestones**: Collect cobblestone tokens used for construction or bonuses.
- **Public services**: If a public service is at the intersection, gain its benefit.

### Step 4: PLAY ACTION CARDS (optional)
You may play **action cards** from your hand for additional effects:
- Cards are **multi-use** — each card has multiple possible uses (build, gain resources, special actions).
- Spent cards go to your discard pile and are eventually recycled.
- Some cards have powerful combos with specific board positions or building types.

### Additional Mechanisms:

#### Citizens:
- Citizens appear on the board and move/settle into completed city blocks.
- Different citizen types provide different benefits:
  - **Workers** help with construction.
  - **Bourgeoisie** provide income and VP.
- Attracting citizens to your buildings is a major scoring mechanism.

#### Modernisme:
- Certain buildings qualify as Modernist masterpieces (inspired by Gaudi and contemporaries).
- Building these earns Modernisme tokens and advances on the Sagrada Familia track.
- The Sagrada Familia track provides milestone bonuses and end-game VP.

#### Street Development:
- Streets between blocks can be developed (paved, tramway lines added).
- Developing streets provides immediate bonuses and contributes to area scoring.

#### Cerda's Plan Bonuses:
- Bonus objectives relate to Cerda's urban planning principles (green space, services distribution, etc.).
- Meeting these objectives earns bonus VP.

## SCORING / WINNING
VP is earned throughout the game and at game end:

**During the Game:**
- Completing city blocks (all players who contributed score based on their contribution).
- Citizen interactions and recruitment.
- Tramway advancement.
- Modernisme achievements.
- Card bonuses.

**End-Game Scoring:**
- **Sagrada Familia track** position (significant VP).
- **Remaining citizens** in your buildings.
- **Completed objective cards** / Cerda's Plan bonuses.
- **Modernisme tokens** collected.
- **Leftover resources** converted to small VP amounts.

**Highest VP wins.** Tiebreaker: most coins remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Intersection selection is the key decision.** It determines both WHERE you can build and WHAT bonuses you receive. Think spatially.
- **Blocking matters.** Since you cannot choose an occupied intersection, denying opponents access to key intersections is a valid strategy.
- **Complete city blocks for big points.** Partially built blocks score nothing until completed. Contribute to blocks others are building if you can share the completion bonus.
- **Citizens are a major scoring engine.** Build residential buildings near citizens to attract them.
- **The Sagrada Familia track rewards commitment.** If you invest in Modernisme, commit to climbing the track for its steep VP curve.
- **Multi-use cards force tough choices.** Every card has multiple uses, so deciding which use to burn it for is a constant tension.
- **Balance income and VP.** Commercial buildings generate coins needed for construction, but residential buildings attract citizens for scoring.
- **Watch for area-majority opportunities** in large city blocks where multiple players contribute.
- **Solo mode** uses an automated opponent that blocks intersections and builds competitively.
- **The game has a steep learning curve** due to the many interconnected systems, but turns are relatively fast once you understand the flow.
