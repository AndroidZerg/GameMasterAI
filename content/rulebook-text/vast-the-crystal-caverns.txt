# Game: Vast: The Crystal Caverns
# Publisher: Leder Games
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 60-120 min | Weight: 3.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Vast: The Crystal Caverns is a fully asymmetric adventure game where each player controls a completely different role with unique mechanics, abilities, and win conditions. The five roles are the Knight, the Dragon, the Goblins, the Thief, and the Cave itself. Each role plays like an entirely different game, yet all interact in a tightly interlocked ecosystem of predator and prey.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 large game board (7x7 grid for cave tiles)
- Cave tiles (dark tiles, lit tiles, crystal tiles, event tiles)
- Knight board and cubes (Grit, Perception, Strength, etc.)
- Dragon board, Power crystals, Shriek tokens
- Goblin boards (3 tribes: Fangs, Bones, Eyes), Goblin figures, War/Monster cards
- Thief board, Treasure tokens, upgrade cards
- Cave board, Omen tokens, terrain tokens
- Various tokens, cards, and standees for each role
- Custom dice

## SETUP
1. Place the Entrance tile in the center of the grid. The Knight starts on the Entrance.
2. Each player takes their role board, pieces, and reference card.
3. Place dark (face-down) tiles around the Entrance as specified.
4. The Cave player (or a randomized deck if no Cave player) prepares their tile stack.
5. Place treasure tokens, Goblin tribes, and the Dragon in their starting positions as indicated.
6. Each role has specific setup instructions on their player board.
7. Turn order is always: Knight, Goblins, Dragon, Thief, Cave (skip roles not in the game).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each role has a completely different turn structure:

### The Knight (Goal: Kill the Dragon)
The Knight explores the cave, gains stats, and fights. On each turn:
- **Move**: Move up to your Speed stat through revealed tiles. Moving onto a dark tile reveals it (the Cave player or deck determines what it is).
- **Encounter**: Resolve encounters on tiles — fight Goblins, find treasure, trigger events.
- **Manage Stats**: The Knight has stats (Grit, Perception, Strength, Speed, Health) tracked on their board. Grit is spent to take extra actions, improve rolls, and resist damage. Perception lets you peek at dark tiles. Strength improves combat.
- **Combat**: Roll dice equal to Strength; each hit symbol deals 1 damage. Bomb symbols deal area damage.
- **Side Quests**: Completing side quest cards grants permanent upgrades.
- To fight the Dragon, the Knight must find the Dragon (who may be hidden) and reduce it to 0 health.

### The Goblins (Goal: Kill the Knight)
The Goblins control three tribes (Fangs, Bones, Eyes), each with a tribal board and unique Monster cards.
- **Populate**: Place Goblin figures from your supply onto the board in dark or Goblin-friendly tiles.
- **Rage**: Each tribe has a Rage track. As Goblins are killed, Rage increases, unlocking powerful War and Monster cards.
- **Scatter**: Move Goblin groups around the map to set up ambushes.
- **Attack**: When Goblins share a tile with the Knight, they fight. Each Goblin rolls 1 die; Fangs are melee fighters, Bones are ranged, Eyes have special abilities.
- **Monster Cards**: Spend Rage to play powerful one-time Monster cards (giant spiders, rockfalls, etc.).
- **War Cards**: Permanent upgrades for tribes.
- Goblins win by reducing the Knight to 0 health.

### The Dragon (Goal: Wake Up and Escape the Cave)
The Dragon starts asleep and must awaken before escaping.
- **Sloth Track**: The Dragon has a Sloth track. Eating Goblins, gaining Power Crystals, and taking certain actions reduce Sloth.
- **Wakefulness**: The Dragon progresses through stages: Deep Sleep, Sleep, Drowsy, Awake. Each stage unlocks more powerful actions (Shriek, Flame, Claw).
- **Power Crystals**: Collected from crystal tiles. Used to activate abilities.
- **Movement**: When awake, the Dragon moves through the cave (ignoring walls in some cases).
- **Spirit Track**: Represents the Dragon's ghostly presence while sleeping; used to eat Goblins and interact with the map before physically waking.
- **Escape**: Once fully Awake (Sloth at 0), the Dragon must reach the Entrance tile and spend an action to escape. The Dragon wins upon escaping.

### The Thief (Goal: Collect Treasure and Escape)
The Thief sneaks through the cave collecting loot.
- **Stealth**: The Thief has a stealth token and is hidden from other players unless revealed. Other players cannot target the Thief while hidden.
- **Move and Loot**: Move through tiles, pick up treasure tokens.
- **Upgrade Cards**: Spend treasure to buy upgrade cards that grant abilities (lockpicking, backstab, shadow step, etc.).
- **Interaction**: The Thief can steal from the Dragon's hoard, pick Goblin pockets, or backstab the Knight.
- **Escape**: The Thief wins by collecting a required number of treasure (6 dragon gems) and reaching the Entrance to escape.

### The Cave (Goal: Collapse)
The Cave itself is a player, controlling the environment.
- **Place Tiles**: When players explore dark tiles, the Cave player chooses which tile to place (from a hand of tiles), controlling the dungeon layout.
- **Omen Track**: The Cave has an Omen track that advances each turn. Certain thresholds trigger events.
- **Terrain Tokens**: Place rockslides, rivers, crystals, and hazards on tiles to hinder or redirect players.
- **Collapse**: The Cave wins by placing enough Crystal tiles and then triggering a Collapse. The Cave must have a certain number of Crystal tiles on the board and spend Omens to collapse, destroying everything inside.
- **Tile Manipulation**: The Cave can rotate tiles, block passages, and create dead ends.

## SCORING / WINNING
There is no shared scoring. Each role has a unique win condition:
- **Knight**: Kill the Dragon (reduce to 0 HP while Awake).
- **Goblins**: Kill the Knight (reduce to 0 HP).
- **Dragon**: Wake up fully and escape through the Entrance.
- **Thief**: Collect 6 dragon gems and escape through the Entrance.
- **Cave**: Collapse (have enough crystal tiles placed and trigger collapse).

The game ends immediately when any player achieves their win condition.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- The game is highly interactive with a rock-paper-scissors dynamic: the Knight hunts the Dragon, Goblins hunt the Knight, the Dragon eats Goblins, the Thief exploits everyone, and the Cave threatens all.
- Player count heavily affects balance. The game includes detailed guidelines for which roles to include at each player count. 4-5 players is the fullest experience.
- Each role has its own detailed reference card — players should study their role before starting.
- The learning curve is steep because every player is essentially learning a different game. Teach each role separately.
- The Cave player role is the most unusual — it's essentially a dungeon master who is also trying to win.
- At lower player counts, some roles are run by automated "difficulty" cards (e.g., a solo Knight vs. automated Goblins, Dragon, and Cave).
- Communication between players about temporary alliances is allowed and encouraged — the Goblins might help the Cave by slowing the Knight, for instance.
- The Dragon's progression from sleeping to awake is the game's central clock — if the Dragon wakes up too quickly, the Knight may not be ready.
