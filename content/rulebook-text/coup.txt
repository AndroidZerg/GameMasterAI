COUP - Official Rulebook

In Coup, you want to be the last player with influence in the game, with influence being represented by face-down character cards in your playing area.

Each player starts with two coins and two influence (two face-down character cards). The fifteen card deck consists of three copies of five different characters:

- Duke: Take three coins from the treasury. Block someone from taking foreign aid.
- Assassin: Pay three coins and try to assassinate another player's character.
- Contessa: Block an assassination attempt against yourself.
- Captain: Take two coins from another player, or block someone from stealing coins from you.
- Ambassador: Draw two character cards from the Court (the deck), choose which (if any) to exchange with your face-down characters, then return two. Block someone from stealing coins from you.

On your turn, you can take any character action above regardless of which characters you actually have, or take one of three other actions:

- Income: Take one coin from the treasury.
- Foreign aid: Take two coins from the treasury.
- Coup: Pay seven coins and launch a coup against an opponent, forcing that player to lose an influence. (If you have ten coins or more, you must take this action.)

When you take a character action (actively or defensively), it automatically succeeds unless an opponent challenges you. If challenged and you can't reveal the appropriate character, you lose an influence (turn one card face-up). Face-up characters cannot be used. Both face-up = out of the game.

If you do have the character and reveal it, the challenger loses an influence. You shuffle that character into the deck and draw a new one.

The last player with influence (a face-down character) wins!

COMPONENTS: 15 character cards (3 each of 5 characters), 50 coins, summary cards.

OPTIONAL - INQUISITOR (replaces Ambassador): Draw one card from Court deck, choose whether to exchange with face-down character. OR Force opponent to show you one card (their choice). May force them to draw new card, shuffling old one back. Block stealing.

Publisher: Indie Boards and Cards. 2-6 players.
