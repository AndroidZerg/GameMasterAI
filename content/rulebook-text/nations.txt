# Game: Nations
# Publisher: Lautapelit.fi
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 40-200 min | Weight: 3.6/5

## OVERVIEW
Nations is a civilization-building game spanning from antiquity to the modern era (4 ages, 8 rounds). Players develop their nations by acquiring cards representing buildings, military units, colonies, wonders, advisors, and wars. The game emphasizes balancing military strength, stability, food production, and cultural achievements across four historical ages.

## COMPONENTS
- Main board with progress track, scoring track, and round/age indicators
- Player boards (one per player) with resource tracks (Military, Stability, Books/Culture)
- Card decks for Ages I through IV (buildings, military, colonies, advisors, wonders, wars, events)
- Resource tokens: Gold, Food, Stone, Books
- Workers (meeples) in each player color
- Victory point tokens
- Architecture tokens (for wonders)
- Heritage tokens
- Score markers and round marker

## SETUP
1. Place the main board in the center of the table.
2. Each player takes a player board and sets Military, Stability, and Books tracks to their starting positions (varies by difficulty chosen — Chieftain/Prince/King/Emperor).
3. Each player receives starting resources based on difficulty: typically 3 gold, some food, and a set number of workers.
4. Each player places their starting cards on their player board (a basic worker, a basic building, a basic military).
5. Separate the card decks by age (I, II, III, IV). Each age has 2 rounds.
6. Set the round marker to Round 1, Age I.
7. Determine first player randomly; give them the first player marker.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game plays over 4 ages, each containing 2 rounds (8 rounds total). Each round has three phases:

### Phase 1: Maintenance
- Reveal new progress cards for the current round and place them on the main board in designated slots.
- Each card has a type: Building, Military, Colony, Advisor, Wonder, War, or Event.
- Resolve the current Event card (if any) — events affect all players (e.g., famine, plague, golden age).

### Phase 2: Action Phase (Player Turns)
Players take turns clockwise, performing ONE action per turn, until all players have passed. Actions include:

**Buy a Card**: Pay the gold cost shown on a progress card and place it in your play area. Cards include:
- **Buildings**: Provide resources, stability, or books when staffed with a worker. You may replace an existing building of the same type.
- **Military Units**: Increase military strength when staffed. Replace existing military cards.
- **Advisors**: Provide ongoing benefits or one-time effects.
- **Colonies**: Pay military strength (not gold) to acquire. Provide ongoing resources.
- **Wonders**: Reserve a wonder by paying gold; it must be built over time using stone.

**Deploy a Worker**: Place an available worker onto a card on your player board to activate it. The card then produces its effect. Each card can hold one worker (some hold more).

**Build a Wonder**: Spend stone to add architecture tokens to a wonder in progress. When fully built, the wonder provides its bonus (VP, resources, etc.).

**Hire a Worker**: Pay the current worker cost (increases through the game: 3/4/5/7 gold by age) to gain a new worker from the supply.

**Pass**: Once you pass, you take no more actions this round. The first player to pass becomes the new first player.

### Phase 3: Resolution
After all players have passed, resolve the round:
1. **War Resolution**: If a War card was drawn, compare each player's military strength to the war's threshold. Players below the threshold suffer penalties (lose VP, resources, stability). Players above may gain bonuses.
2. **Famine Check**: Each player must feed their workers — pay 1 food per deployed worker. Undeployed workers don't eat. If you can't pay, lose VP.
3. **Production**: Players produce resources from their buildings and colonies (gold, food, stone, books).
4. **Scoring**: Players gain VP based on books/culture track position. Advisor bonuses may also trigger.
5. **Heritage**: The player with the highest books/culture gains a heritage token (bonus VP at game end).
6. **Cleanup**: Discard remaining progress cards, advance the round marker.

## SCORING / WINNING
- VP are accumulated throughout the game from: books/culture track, wonders completed, heritage tokens, war bonuses, and certain advisor effects.
- At end of game (after Round 8), final scoring includes:
  - VP from the score track
  - Heritage token bonuses
  - VP printed on completed wonders
  - Some end-game advisor bonuses
- The player with the most VP wins. Ties broken by most remaining resources (gold + food + stone).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Military matters every round**: Wars can devastate underprepared players. Always keep an eye on potential war thresholds.
- **Stability prevents losses**: Low stability means you suffer more from negative events. Keep it above zero.
- **Workers are expensive but essential**: Each new worker costs increasing gold. Plan when to hire carefully.
- **Replacing cards**: When you buy a building/military card, you may replace one of the same type already on your board. The old card is discarded, and if it had a worker, that worker returns to your supply (not deployed).
- **Books/Culture track**: This is the primary source of recurring VP. Investing early pays off over 8 rounds.
- **Food management**: You need 1 food per deployed worker each round. Undeployed workers in your supply don't eat but also don't produce.
- **Difficulty scaling**: Player boards have multiple difficulty levels. Harder difficulties start with fewer resources and lower track positions. Use this to balance experienced vs. new players.
- **Solo mode**: The solo game uses an automa opponent with specific card-drawing rules.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that colonies cost military strength, not gold. You compare your military track to the colony's requirement.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting to feed workers during famine — this happens every round, not just when a famine event appears.
