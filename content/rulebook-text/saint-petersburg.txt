# Game: Saint Petersburg
# Publisher: Hans im Glück
# Players: 2-5 | Time: 45-60 min | Weight: 2.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Saint Petersburg is an economic card game set in the founding era of Russia's great city. Players purchase worker cards that generate income, building cards that generate victory points, and aristocrat cards that do both. The game's elegant structure cycles through four phases each round, and the key challenge is managing your rubles to invest in the right mix of income-generating workers early and VP-generating buildings and aristocrats later.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board (card market display with upper and lower rows)
- Card decks: Workers (green), Buildings (blue), Aristocrats (red), Trading/Upgrade cards (varies by edition)
- Ruble tokens (money — denominations of 1, 5, 10, 25)
- VP tokens (denominations of 1, 3, and 5 in some editions, or scoring track)
- Player markers for score track
- Starting player marker
- Phase indicator

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center. It shows the card market with an upper row (8 slots) and a lower row (8 slots).
2. Each player starts with 25 rubles.
3. Shuffle each card deck separately: Workers, Buildings, Aristocrats, and Trading cards.
4. Place all four decks face-down near the board.
5. Deal worker cards face-up to fill the upper row of the market (8 cards).
6. Set the phase indicator to the Worker phase.
7. Determine first player randomly.
8. In a 2-3 player game, some cards are removed from decks before play (specific counts per player count to balance the game).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each round consists of 4 phases, always in this order: Workers, Buildings, Aristocrats, Trading. Within each phase:

### Phase Structure
1. **Reveal Cards**: At the start of each phase, fill the upper row of the market with cards of the current phase type (any empty spaces in the upper row get new cards).
2. **Player Turns**: Starting with the first player for this phase, players take turns clockwise. On your turn, do ONE of:
   - **Buy a card** from the market (upper or lower row): Pay its cost in rubles, place it face-up in front of you.
   - **Reserve a card**: Take a card from the upper row and place it in the lower row. This is free but the card remains available for anyone to buy. (The lower row holds cards from previous phases that weren't purchased.)
   - **Pass**: You may pass. If all players pass consecutively, the phase ends.
3. **Scoring the Phase**: After all players have passed:
   - **Worker phase**: Each player earns rubles from all workers they own (each worker card shows its income value).
   - **Building phase**: Each player earns VP from all buildings they own (each building shows its VP value).
   - **Aristocrat phase**: Each player earns VP from aristocrats AND rubles from aristocrats (aristocrats generate both).
   - **Trading phase**: No automatic production. Trading cards are upgrades that replace existing cards, typically turning a worker into a better version.
4. **Cleanup**: Any cards remaining in the lower row are DISCARDED. Cards in the upper row slide down to the lower row to make room for next phase's cards.

### Card Details
- **Workers** (green): Cost 3-7 rubles. Produce 3-6 rubles per round in the worker scoring phase. Cheap and essential early.
- **Buildings** (blue): Cost 6-23 rubles. Produce 1-8 VP per round in the building scoring phase. Some are very expensive but high-VP.
- **Aristocrats** (red): Cost 7-17 rubles. Produce both rubles (1-4) and VP (1-4) each round in the aristocrat phase. Additionally, unique aristocrats score bonus VP at game end.
- **Trading/Upgrade cards**: Cost rubles but replace a card you already own (you pay the difference between the upgrade and original). The upgraded card produces more.

### Important Mechanism — Hand Cards
In some editions: Instead of buying or reserving, you may take a card into your HAND (limited hand size, typically 3). Cards in hand can be played in a later phase by paying their cost. This lets you reserve cards privately.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends at the end of any round in which one or more card decks run out.

Final scoring:
- VP accumulated during the game from building and aristocrat phases
- **Aristocrat diversity bonus**: For each UNIQUE aristocrat you own, score bonus VP. The bonus scales significantly:
  - 1 unique aristocrat: 1 VP
  - 2 unique: 3 VP
  - 3 unique: 6 VP
  - 4 unique: 10 VP
  - 5 unique: 15 VP
  - 6 unique: 21 VP
  - 7 unique: 28 VP
  - 8 unique: 36 VP
  - 9 unique: 45 VP
  - 10 unique: 55 VP
  (This is a triangular number progression: n*(n+1)/2)
- Remaining rubles: Every 10 rubles = 1 VP (round down).

Most VP wins. Ties broken by most rubles remaining.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Early income is critical**: Buy workers in the first few rounds. Without income, you can't afford buildings and aristocrats later.
- **Aristocrat diversity is the biggest end-game swing**: Collecting many unique aristocrats can be worth 30+ VP. This is often the deciding factor.
- **Don't overbuy workers**: Workers only generate rubles, not VP. Transition to buildings and aristocrats once your income is sufficient (usually 15-20 rubles per worker phase).
- **Trading cards are powerful**: Upgrading a cheap worker to a more efficient version gives you the improved production for the rest of the game. Upgrades cost the difference in price.
- **Lower row timing**: Cards that slide to the lower row will be DISCARDED at the end of the NEXT phase if not bought. Don't count on a card surviving two phases.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that aristocrats score BOTH rubles and VP each round — they are the most efficient cards in the game but expensive.
- **Common mistake**: Hoarding money too long. Unspent rubles are only worth 1 VP per 10 at game end. Invest early.
- **Common mistake**: Not counting deck sizes to anticipate game end. When a deck looks thin, the game could end soon — pivot to VP.
- **Player count matters**: With 2 players, the market refreshes slowly and you see more cards. With 5 players, competition is fierce and cards vanish quickly.
