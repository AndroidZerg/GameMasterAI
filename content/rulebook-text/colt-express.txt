# Game: Colt Express
# Publisher: Ludonaute
# Players: 2-6 | Time: 30-40 min | Weight: 1.9/5

## OVERVIEW
Colt Express is a programmed-movement game set in the Wild West where players are bandits robbing a 3D cardboard train. Each round, players play action cards into a shared sequence, then all cards are resolved in order — but because actions are planned in advance and the situation changes as cards resolve, chaos and hilarity ensue. The bandit who collects the most loot by the end of 5 rounds wins.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 three-dimensional cardboard train (locomotive + passenger cars — typically 6 cars for a full game; number varies by player count)
- 6 Bandit character standees/meeples (each with a unique special ability)
- 6 Character cards (showing each bandit's special power)
- 6 Bullet cards per bandit (personal deck of 6 bullet cards, each with the bandit's image)
- 10 Action cards per bandit (personal deck): Move, Change Floor, Fire, Punch, Rob, Marshal
- Loot tokens (Purses worth $250-$500, Jewels worth $500, Strongbox worth $1000)
- Round cards (5 round cards that define each round's structure)
- Marshal pawn
- Neutral bullet cards (from the Marshal)
- Cactus token (first player marker)

## SETUP
1. Assemble the 3D train on the table. The number of cars depends on the player count (typically: players + 1 car, or a set number). The locomotive is at the front.
2. Place loot tokens in each car as specified in the rules: each car gets a mix of purses and jewels. The locomotive gets the strongbox ($1000).
3. Place the Marshal pawn in the locomotive.
4. Each player chooses a bandit and takes their Character card, their 6 Bullet cards, and their 10 Action cards.
5. Shuffle each player's 10 Action cards to form their personal draw deck. Draw 6 cards as a starting hand.
6. Place all bandit pawns in the LAST car of the train (caboose), or distribute them among the last two cars (depending on player count — 2-4 players start in one car; with 5-6, split between the last two).
7. Shuffle the 5 Round cards. Reveal the first Round card — it shows how the Schemin' phase works this round (number of turns, any special conditions like tunnel or switching).
8. Give the first player marker to the starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
Each round has two phases:

### Phase 1: SCHEMIN' (Planning)
The Round card shows a sequence of 2-5 turns (represented by train car icons or symbols). Players take turns playing ONE action card each, going around the table. Each turn in the sequence has a condition:

- **Normal turn**: Play one action card FACE-UP on the shared central action pile. Everyone can see it.
- **Tunnel turn** (dark icon): Play one action card FACE-DOWN. Nobody sees what you played.
- **Double turn** (indicated on some round cards): Play TWO action cards instead of one (both face-up or both face-down, depending on the turn).
- **Switching direction**: Some round cards reverse the player order partway through.

If a player cannot or does not want to play a card, they instead **draw 3 cards** from their personal deck into their hand.

### Phase 2: STEALIN' (Resolution)
Flip the entire action pile over (so the first card played is now on top) and resolve each card one at a time, in the order they were played:

#### Action Card Types:
1. **MOVE** (train icon): Move your bandit 1-3 cars in either direction along the train (INSIDE the cars). You choose how far, up to 3 cars.

2. **CHANGE FLOOR** (arrow up/down): Move your bandit between the roof and the inside of the current car. If inside, go to the roof. If on the roof, go inside.

3. **FIRE** (gun icon): Shoot another bandit. Target must be:
   - In an **adjacent car** (if both inside) or
   - **Visible on the roof** (if shooter is on the roof, can shoot any bandit on the roof within range — the range is 1 car for most situations).
   - The shooter takes one of their personal Bullet cards and places it into the TARGET'S draw deck/discard pile. Bullet cards are dead cards that clog the victim's hand (they have no action — drawing one wastes a card draw).

4. **PUNCH** (fist icon): Punch another bandit in the SAME car/roof space:
   - The punched bandit is knocked into an adjacent car (the puncher chooses which direction).
   - The punched bandit drops one piece of loot (the puncher chooses which piece), leaving it in the space they were punched FROM.

5. **ROB** (money bag icon): Pick up one loot token from the floor of the car your bandit is currently in. Add it to your personal loot pile.

6. **MARSHAL** (sheriff badge icon): Move the Marshal 1-2 cars in either direction. Any bandit in the car the Marshal enters (or is already in) is immediately:
   - Shot (receives a neutral Bullet card into their deck).
   - Moved to the ROOF of that car (fleeing the Marshal).
   - The Marshal is always INSIDE the train.

### Special Conditions
- **Roof movement is different**: Bandits on the roof can move up to 3 cars in either direction when playing Move. There are no walls on the roof.
- **The Marshal only moves inside**: Bandits avoid the Marshal by going to the roof.
- **Bullet cards clog your deck**: Each Bullet card in your deck means you might draw it instead of a useful action. Getting shot repeatedly is a serious disadvantage.

### End of Round
- After all action cards are resolved, the round ends.
- Each player draws cards from their personal deck to refill to a hand of 6 (or draws what remains if the deck is running low).
- The round card may trigger an **Event** at the end of the round (hostage rescue, angry marshal, train braking, etc. — varies by card).
- Reveal the next Round card and begin the new round.

## SCORING / WINNING
- After 5 rounds, the game ends.
- Each player counts the total value of their collected loot:
  - Purses: $250 or $500 each
  - Jewels: $500 each
  - Strongbox: $1,000
- **Gunslinger bonus**: The player who FIRED the most bullets (shot the most people) during the game receives a $1,000 bonus. In case of a tie, all tied players receive the bonus.
- **The player with the most money wins.**
- Tiebreaker: the player with fewer Bullet cards in their deck wins (they were shot less).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **This is a chaos game — embrace it**: Plans will go wrong. Other players' actions will move targets, steal loot, and mess up your positioning. Enjoy the unpredictability.
- **Tunnel cards are powerful**: Playing face-down means opponents don't know what you're planning. Save impactful actions for tunnel turns.
- **Bullet cards are a resource**: Shooting opponents doesn't just hurt them immediately — it degrades their deck for the rest of the game. Aggressive shooting is a viable strategy.
- **The roof is safer from the Marshal** but more exposed to gunfire (shooters on the roof have longer range).
- **Punching steals loot AND displaces**: It's often better than robbing because you can take from other players and disrupt their position.
- **The Strongbox is high-risk/high-reward**: It's in the locomotive with the Marshal. Getting it and escaping is a major play.
- **Character special abilities matter**:
  - **Ghost (Tuco)**: Can start on the roof for more flexibility.
  - **Belle**: Cannot be targeted if another bandit is in the same location.
  - **Django**: When shooting, the target is pushed 1 car away.
  - **Cheyenne**: When punching, she takes the dropped loot.
  - **Doc**: Draws 7 cards instead of 6 at the start of each round.
  - **Tuco**: Can fire at bandits on the opposite floor (inside to roof and vice versa).
- **Track what others play**: In non-tunnel turns, you can see what actions others are choosing. Try to anticipate where everyone will be when your card resolves.
- **Don't forget to draw**: If your hand is empty or you have only Bullet cards, skip playing a card and draw 3. Replenishing your hand is important.
