# Game: Alien Frontiers
# Publisher: Clever Mojo Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Alien Frontiers is a dice-placement (worker-placement with dice) game where players colonize an alien planet by using their fleet of ships (dice) to gather resources, research technology, and establish colonies. The planet is divided into territories, and players compete for area control. The game blends classic Euro resource management with dice-rolling luck and direct interaction through alien technology cards.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board depicting the alien planet with 8 territories and orbital facilities
- Custom dice (ships) in player colors — 3 per player to start (can gain up to 6)
- Colony tokens (dome-shaped) in player colors
- Ore tokens (grey cubes)
- Fuel tokens (orange cubes)
- Alien Technology cards deck
- Score track around the board edge
- Territory bonus tokens
- Relic Ship die (grey, neutral)
- Field Generator tokens

## SETUP
1. Place the board in the center. Each territory starts with a territory bonus token face-up.
2. Each player takes 3 dice of their color and all colony tokens of that color.
3. Each player starts with 1 Ore and 1 Fuel.
4. Shuffle the Alien Technology deck; deal 1 card to each player face-down (optional variant: draft instead).
5. Place remaining Ore, Fuel, and colony tokens in the supply.
6. Place all players' score markers on 0 on the score track.
7. Determine starting player randomly.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns in clockwise order. Each turn has 3 phases:

### Phase 1: Roll Your Dice
Roll all of your ships (dice) behind no screen — everyone can see results.

### Phase 2: Assign Dice to Orbital Facilities
Place your dice on the various orbital facilities on the board to perform actions. Each facility has specific requirements for dice placement (number of dice, specific values, matching values, etc.). You may use facilities in any order.

**Orbital Facilities:**

- **Solar Converter** (any 1 die): Gain Fuel equal to the die value. E.g., place a 4, gain 4 Fuel.
- **Lunar Mine** (any 1 die): Gain Ore equal to the die value. E.g., place a 3, gain 3 Ore.
- **Orbital Market** (2 dice, any values): Trade resources — exchange Ore for Fuel or Fuel for Ore. The exchange rate is based on the difference between the two dice. You can trade X of one resource for X of the other where X equals the higher die minus the lower die (minimum 1). Some versions: pay 1 of one to gain 1 of the other per die used.
- **Shipyard** (any number of dice totaling exactly 8): Pay 1 Ore + 1 Fuel to build a new ship (gain 1 additional die of your color). Max 6 dice total.
- **Colonist Hub** (1+ dice, must be a straight of 3 or more, e.g., 1-2-3, 3-4-5-6): Pay 1 Ore + 1 Fuel to place a colony on the planet. This is one of the primary ways to score.
- **Terraforming Station** (1 die of value 6): Pay 1 Ore + 1 Fuel to place a colony on the planet.
- **Raider's Outpost** (matching pair or better): Steal resources or Alien Tech from an opponent. A pair lets you steal 1 resource or 1 tech card. Three-of-a-kind lets you steal 2, etc.
- **Colony Constructor** (3 matching dice): Pay 3 Ore to place a colony on the planet. No Fuel needed.

### Phase 3: Retrieve Dice
After placing all dice you wish to place, your turn ends. Dice remain on the board until the START of your next turn, potentially blocking spaces. You retrieve them all at the start of your next turn before rolling.

### Alien Technology Cards
Cards can be played at various times (on your turn usually) and provide powerful one-time or ongoing effects: re-rolling dice, modifying die values +/- 1, stealing resources, moving colonies between territories, etc. Each card specifies when and how it can be used.

### Colonizing Territories
When you place a colony via Colonist Hub, Terraforming Station, or Colony Constructor, choose any territory on the planet. The player with the most colonies in a territory controls it and gains that territory's bonus (printed on the token), typically an ongoing ability like modifying dice, extra resources, or discounts.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Colony Scoring**: Each colony on the planet = 1 Victory Point on the score track.
- **Territory Control**: Controlling a territory (having the most colonies there) grants +1 VP.
- The first player to place ALL of their colonies on the planet triggers the end of the game — that player wins immediately.
- If no one places all colonies (rare), the player with the most VP at an agreed stopping point wins.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Dice remain on facilities until the START of your next turn, blocking those spaces for other players. This is a key interactive element.
- Facilities have limited slots — you can be blocked from actions. Plan backup strategies.
- Building more ships (dice) is extremely powerful early — going from 3 to 4 or 5 dice dramatically increases your options.
- The Raider's Outpost is the primary attack mechanism — matching dice let you steal. Protect yourself by spending resources quickly.
- Territory bonuses are powerful ongoing effects. Competing for territory control is important.
- Alien Technology cards can swing the game — some allow dice manipulation that unlocks otherwise impossible placements.
- Common mistake: Forgetting to retrieve dice at the start of your turn (not the end).
- Common mistake: Trying to save up resources — they can be stolen. Spend them.
- The Shipyard requires dice totaling EXACTLY 8 — not 8 or more.
- Ties for territory control: no one gets the bonus. You need a strict majority.
- Maximum of 6 dice per player. You cannot build beyond 6 ships.
