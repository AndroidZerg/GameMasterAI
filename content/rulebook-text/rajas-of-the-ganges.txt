# Game: Rajas of the Ganges

# Players: 2-4 | Time: 45-75 min | Weight: 2.8/5

## OVERVIEW
Rajas of the Ganges is a dice-driven worker placement game set in 16th-century India during the Mughal Empire. Players are Rajas and Ranis competing to achieve the greatest fame and fortune by developing their province, trading goods at the market, and sailing along the Ganges River. The game features a unique dual-track victory condition where your fame track and money track race toward each other from opposite directions.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 main game board (worker placement action spaces, Ganges river track, market area)
- 4 player province boards (5x5 grid for tile placement)
- Lots of custom dice in 4 colors (yellow, orange, green, brown — each representing a different good/resource)
- Worker meeples (3 per player in player colors, with option to gain a 4th)
- Province tiles (various sizes and types — generate income, fame, and goods)
- Market tiles
- Bonus tiles
- Kali tokens (wild tokens)
- Fame track markers and Money track markers (one of each per player)
- Coins
- River boats (one per player for the Ganges river track)
- Quarry area with dice

## SETUP
1. Place the main game board centrally.
2. Populate the action spaces with the appropriate tiles, dice, and tokens according to setup rules.
3. Fill the quarry: roll and place dice of each color in the quarry area.
4. Each player takes a province board, 3 workers (meeples) of their color, and a river boat.
5. Each player places their Fame marker on the starting space of the fame track (beginning) and their Money marker on the starting space of the money track (beginning). These tracks start at opposite ends and advance toward each other.
6. Each player takes starting dice (typically 1-2 dice of varying colors depending on turn order) and starting money.
7. Place province tiles in supply stacks near the board.
8. Lay out the market row.
9. Determine starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns in clockwise order. On your turn, you place ONE worker on an available action space and execute that action. When all your workers are placed, you must use a turn to retrieve all of them.

### Action Spaces

#### 1. Quarry
- Take 1 or more dice from the quarry (the available pool of pre-rolled dice).
- Dice come in 4 colors representing 4 types of goods:
  - **Yellow**: Silk
  - **Orange**: Spice
  - **Green**: Tea
  - **Brown**: Wood/Generic
- The VALUE shown on each die matters — higher values are more useful for many actions.
- You choose which dice to take (within the rules of the specific quarry space).

#### 2. Province Development
- Take a province tile from the supply and place it on your 5x5 province grid.
- Tiles come in different shapes (1x1, 1x2, L-shaped, etc.) and must fit on your grid.
- You may need to spend dice of specific colors/values to acquire tiles.
- Province tiles generate resources when activated:
  - **Revenue tiles**: Generate money (advance your money marker).
  - **Fame tiles**: Generate fame (advance your fame marker).
  - **Production tiles**: Generate goods (gain dice of specific colors).
- Placement matters — covering certain printed symbols on your province board triggers bonuses.

#### 3. Market
- Sell goods (dice) at the market for money and/or fame.
- The market has multiple stalls, each demanding specific combinations of dice colors and minimum values.
- More valuable trades (requiring harder-to-get dice combinations) yield better rewards.
- Spent dice are returned to the general supply.

#### 4. Ganges River
- Advance your boat along the Ganges River track by spending dice.
- Each river space provides a bonus when you reach it: money, fame, dice, Kali tokens, province tiles, or special abilities.
- The river is a consistent source of both resources and scoring.
- Some spaces have prerequisites (specific dice colors/values to advance).

#### 5. Palace / Court Actions
- Various action spaces at the palace area provide special abilities:
  - Gain Kali tokens (wilds that substitute for dice or modify die values).
  - Recruit a 4th worker (very powerful — gives you an additional action each cycle).
  - Reroll dice you own.
  - Exchange dice colors.
  - Take bonus tiles with special one-time or ongoing abilities.

#### 6. Karma Actions
- Some spaces allow you to manipulate dice values (increase or decrease die faces).
- Kali tokens can be spent as wild dice of any color with a value of your choice, or used to modify existing dice by +/- pips.

### Worker Retrieval
- When all your workers are placed, you must spend your entire next turn retrieving all of them. No action is taken — you simply take all your workers back.
- This means efficient use of workers is critical. Having a 4th worker delays retrieval.

### Dice Management
- Dice are both resources AND goods. You collect them, store them (limited hand/storage), and spend them for actions.
- The quarry is refilled periodically (when emptied or at certain triggers).
- Dice values matter: a die showing 6 is worth more than one showing 1 for most purposes.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game uses a unique **dual-track convergence** victory system:

- Each player has two markers on two separate tracks:
  - **Fame track**: Starts at one end, advances rightward through gameplay.
  - **Money track**: Starts at the opposite end, advances leftward through gameplay.
- The tracks are arranged so they advance TOWARD each other.

**The game ends immediately when any player's Fame marker and Money marker meet or cross each other** (reach the same position or pass one another on their respective tracks). That player wins!

- If the game reaches a natural endpoint (supply exhaustion or end-of-round trigger), the player whose markers are CLOSEST to meeting wins.
- Closeness is measured by counting the gap between your two markers.
- Tiebreaker: the player with the most remaining dice and Kali tokens.

This system means you must balance BOTH fame and money generation. Focusing on only one track leaves the other marker far behind and you cannot win.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Balance fame and money generation equally. The biggest gap between your two markers is your weakness — address it.
- Getting the 4th worker is one of the most powerful early moves. More workers = more actions before retrieval = more efficiency.
- High-value dice (5s and 6s) are extremely valuable. Prioritize grabbing them from the quarry.
- Kali tokens are incredibly flexible — hoard a few for critical turns.
- The Ganges River provides steady, reliable rewards. Don't neglect it.
- Province tile placement is a spatial puzzle — plan ahead to cover bonus symbols and fit larger tiles.
- The market is strongest when you have accumulated matching sets of high-value dice.
- Watch your opponents' tracks. If someone is close to convergence, you need to race or find a way to catch up.
- The game can end suddenly when markers meet. Don't get caught planning a "big next turn" while your opponent crosses the finish line.
- In 2-player games, there is less competition for action spaces, allowing more strategic planning. In 4-player, adaptability matters more.
