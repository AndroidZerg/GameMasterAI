## OVERVIEW
Lorenzo il Magnifico is a dice-driven worker placement game set in Renaissance Italy. Players lead noble families competing for prestige by acquiring development cards (territories, buildings, characters, and ventures) and managing their relationship with the Church. Three shared dice determine the strength of all players' workers each round, creating a unique strategic tension where everyone shares the same "worker values."

## COMPONENTS
- 1 main game board (with 4 towers of 4 floors each, council area, market, and Church track)
- 96 Development cards (24 each of Territories, Buildings, Characters, Ventures) across 3 periods
- Excommunication tiles (2 per period, 6 total)
- Leader cards (20, dealt at game start)
- Family member pawns: 4 per player (3 colored matching dice + 1 neutral/uncolored)
- 3 dice (Black, White, Orange)
- Player boards (personal tableau for placing acquired cards)
- Resources: Coins, Wood, Stone, Servants, Military Power tokens, Faith Points tokens, Victory Points tokens
- Excommunication markers

## SETUP
1. Place the main board centrally. It has 4 towers (green=Territories, yellow=Buildings, blue=Characters, purple=Ventures), each with 4 floors.
2. Separate Development cards by type and period (I, II, III). Shuffle each set. Period I cards are used in rounds 1-2, Period II in rounds 3-4, Period III in rounds 5-6.
3. Each player receives: a player board, 4 family member pawns, starting resources (varies by turn order — later players get bonus coins/servants).
4. Deal 4 Leader cards to each player. Players draft or select 1 to keep (VERIFY drafting method); remaining are discarded.
5. Place 2 random Excommunication tiles for each period (I, II, III) face up — one will be used each period.
6. Set the round marker to Round 1.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Game Arc
The game lasts 6 rounds divided into 3 periods of 2 rounds each. At the end of each period (rounds 2, 4, 6), a Church Report occurs.

### Round Structure
1. **Roll Dice**: Roll the 3 colored dice (Black, White, Orange). These values apply to ALL players' corresponding colored workers for the entire round. For example, if the Orange die shows 5, every player's Orange family member has a strength of 5.
2. **Place Family Members**: In turn order, players take turns placing one family member at a time on action spaces. Continue until all players have placed all their family members or passed.
3. **Church Report** (end of periods 2, 4, 6 only): Check players' faith against the threshold.

### Placing Family Members (Worker Placement)
On your turn, place ONE family member on an unoccupied action space (with exceptions). The family member's value is determined by the corresponding die roll. You may add servants to boost the value (1 servant = +1 value).

**The Four Towers** (card acquisition):
- Each tower has 4 floors with ascending value requirements: Floor 1 = value 1+, Floor 2 = 3+, Floor 3 = 5+, Floor 4 = 7+.
- To place on a floor, your family member's value (die + servants) must meet or exceed the floor's requirement.
- You take the Development card on that floor and pay its cost (coins, wood, stone, military, etc.).
- **Tower tax**: If any other family member (yours or opponents') is already in the same tower, you must pay 3 extra coins.
- **One per tower rule**: You cannot place more than one of your colored family members in the same tower per round (the neutral member can still go).
- Each tower corresponds to a card type:
  - **Green (Territories)**: Provide harvest bonuses. Require military strength thresholds for 3rd+ territory.
  - **Yellow (Buildings)**: Provide production bonuses. Cost resources to acquire.
  - **Blue (Characters)**: Provide permanent special abilities. Often cost coins.
  - **Purple (Ventures)**: Provide end-game VP. Often require faith points or military.

**Council Palace**:
- Requires value 1+. Gain 1 Council Privilege (choose a bonus: coins, servants, military, faith, or resources).
- Multiple family members can be placed here (no exclusivity).
- The first player to place here claims first-player position next round.

**Market** (4 spaces):
- Each provides a different immediate bonus (coins, servants, military points, council privileges, etc.).
- Exclusive — only one family member per space.
- At 2-3 players, some market spaces are unavailable.

**Harvest Action**:
- Activates all your Territory (green) card bonuses. Your family member's value determines the strength of the harvest — you gain resources from all territories whose harvest value is met.
- One exclusive space (any value) and one shared space (requires value 1+ and -3 penalty to harvest strength).

**Production Action**:
- Activates all your Building (yellow) card bonuses. Same value mechanic as harvest.
- One exclusive space and one shared space with -3 penalty.

### The Neutral Family Member
- Each player has one neutral (uncolored) family member with a fixed value of 0 (can be boosted with servants).
- It can go to any space, even towers where you already have a colored member.

### Church Report (End of Each Period)
- At the end of rounds 2, 4, and 6, players must meet a faith threshold (Period I: 3 faith, Period II: 4 faith, Period III: 5 faith — VERIFY exact thresholds).
- **If you meet the threshold**: You may choose to spend your faith points for VP (or keep them). Your faith resets.
- **If you do NOT meet the threshold**: You are Excommunicated. The Excommunication tile for that period applies a permanent penalty to you for the rest of the game (e.g., lose VP when gaining territories, -1 to all die values, lose resources on harvest, etc.).
- Excommunication penalties are cumulative and can be devastating.

## SCORING / WINNING
**End-Game Scoring:**
- VP accumulated during the game (from cards, actions, conversions)
- VP from Purple (Venture) cards (scored at end of game based on their conditions)
- VP from Territory and Building sets (VERIFY if set bonuses exist)
- VP from Character card effects
- VP from military strength ranking (1st and 2nd place on military track get VP bonuses)
- VP from remaining resources (5 resources = 1 VP, VERIFY conversion rate)
- Penalties from Excommunication tiles

The player with the most VP wins. Ties broken by remaining resources.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Shared dice make planning uncertain**: You know die values AFTER they are rolled, so you cannot plan your entire round in advance between rounds. Adapt each round.
- **Servants are liquid power**: Hoarding servants lets you reach high-value floors and activate powerful harvest/production chains. Never underestimate them.
- **The tower tax (3 coins) adds up fast**: Going second into a tower is expensive. Either go first or avoid contested towers.
- **Faith management is critical**: Being excommunicated in Period I is sometimes acceptable if the penalty is mild, but Period III excommunication can cost 15+ VP.
- **Military thresholds for territories**: You need increasing military strength to hold 3rd, 4th, 5th, and 6th territories (3, 7, 12, 18 military — VERIFY). Plan ahead.
- **Leader cards shape strategy**: Your leader card provides a unique ongoing ability. Draft one that aligns with your planned approach.
- **Purple cards (Ventures) are the biggest VP source**: Many endgame points come from ventures, but they often require meeting conditions by game end.
- **Production and Harvest chains scale**: The more territories/buildings you have, the more powerful these actions become. Investing early pays dividends.
- **Turn order matters**: The Council Palace is the primary way to claim first player. Being first lets you pick the best cards and spaces.
- **2-player games**: Remove 2 market spaces, making the economy tighter.
