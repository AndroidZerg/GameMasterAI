# Game: Vegas Showdown
# Publisher: Hasbro
# Players: 3-5 | Time: 45-75 min | Weight: 2.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Vegas Showdown is an auction and tile-placement game where players compete to build the most prestigious Las Vegas casino resort. Each round, players bid on room tiles (slots, restaurants, lounges, etc.) and place them in their casino floor plan. The goal is to connect your hotel entrance to your casino entrance through a path of rooms while maximizing revenue, population, fame, and end-game bonuses.

## COMPONENTS
- 5 Player casino boards (grid layout with Hotel entrance on one side, Casino entrance on the other)
- Room tiles of various types (Slots, Fancy Slots, Restaurant, Buffet, Lounge, Night Club, Theater, etc.)
- Event cards (drawn each round, affect tile availability and prices)
- Money tokens (starting cash and income)
- Minimum bid markers for each room type
- Score track
- Premier tiles (powerful, expensive unique rooms)

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a casino board and starting money ($35 in some editions, $40 in others — check your version).
2. Set up the room tile supply: arrange tile types in separate stacks at their starting minimum bid prices.
3. Shuffle the Event deck and place it face down.
4. Place minimum bid markers at each room type's starting price.
5. Each player's casino board starts empty. Note the Hotel Entrance (one side) and Casino Entrance (other side) — these must eventually be connected.
6. Determine starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each round follows these steps:

### 1. Event Phase
- Draw an Event card and resolve it. Events may:
  - Introduce a new Premier tile into the auction.
  - Lower or raise minimum bids on certain room types.
  - Add bonus tiles to the supply.
  - Trigger special one-time effects.

### 2. Auction Phase
Players bid on available room tiles in a structured auction:
- Going around the table, each player either:
  - **Places a bid** on any one room tile (must meet or exceed the current minimum bid for that tile type). Place your marker on that tile's bid area.
  - **Raises a bid** on a tile where another player already has a bid (must exceed their bid). The outbid player gets bumped.
  - **Passes** (does nothing this sub-round but remains in the auction).
- Bumped players must immediately either bid on a different tile, raise on another tile, or pass.
- The auction continues in rounds until all players have either won a tile or passed.
- Each player can win at most ONE tile per round.
- Players pay their winning bid amount to the bank.

### 3. Building Phase
- Each player who won a tile places it on their casino board.
- **Placement rules**:
  - Tiles must be placed adjacent (orthogonally) to an existing tile or entrance.
  - Hotel-type rooms (restaurants, lounges, etc.) can only be placed on the Hotel side of the board.
  - Casino-type rooms (slots, tables, etc.) can only be placed on the Casino side.
  - Some rooms are "wild" and can be placed on either side.
  - You are trying to build a connected path from your Hotel Entrance to your Casino Entrance.

### 4. Income Phase
- Each player collects income based on their casino's current Revenue value (determined by placed tiles — each tile adds to Revenue, Population, or both).
- Starting income is a base amount plus bonuses from tiles.

### Round End
- Check if the game-ending condition is met. If not, start a new round.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when either:
- The Event deck runs out, OR
- A certain number of Premier tiles have been purchased (varies by player count).

Final scoring:
- **Revenue score**: Points based on your casino's total Revenue value.
- **Population score**: Points based on your casino's total Population value.
- **Fame score**: Points from specific high-prestige tiles and room types.
- **Connection bonus**: Significant bonus points if you have successfully connected your Hotel Entrance to your Casino Entrance through an unbroken path of rooms.
- **Bonus scoring**: Some tiles grant end-game VP for meeting specific conditions (e.g., "2 VP per Restaurant" or set bonuses).
- **Remaining money**: Small bonus for leftover cash.

Highest total score wins. Ties broken by most remaining money.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Connecting entrances is critical** — the connection bonus is large (often 5-10+ points). Always plan your tile placement to create a path.
- **Don't overbid** — money is tight. Winning a tile at a high price means less income means fewer future tiles. Let opponents overpay.
- **Income compounds** — tiles that boost Revenue pay for themselves over multiple rounds. Get Revenue-generating tiles early.
- **Population vs. Revenue** — you need both for scoring, but tiles tend to give more of one than the other. Balance your strategy.
- **Premier tiles are game-changers** — they're expensive but provide massive VP and abilities. Build toward affording them.
- **Board space is limited** — plan your grid layout from the start. Painting yourself into a corner is a common mistake.
- **Watch the Event deck** — as it thins, the game end approaches. Don't get caught with a half-built casino.
- **Blocking opponents in auctions** — sometimes bidding on a tile you don't want just to force an opponent to pay more is worthwhile, but be careful not to get stuck with a tile you can't use.
- **Wild rooms are flexible** — place them where you need to bridge between Hotel and Casino sides.
