# Game: Tumblin-Dice
# Publisher: Eagle-Gryphon Games
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 30 min | Weight: 1.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Tumblin-Dice is a dexterity game where players flick, roll, or slide dice down a tiered, stepped platform to score points. The value shown on the die face is multiplied by the scoring zone where it lands, so both dexterity (landing in high-value zones) and luck (the die face) determine your score. Players can also knock opponents' dice off the board or into lower-scoring zones, adding a tactical element to the physical skill.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Tumblin-Dice board: a stepped/tiered wooden or plastic platform with 4 scoring zones descending from top to bottom:
  - **Zone 4 (x4)**: The highest, farthest platform — hardest to reach
  - **Zone 3 (x3)**: The second-highest tier
  - **Zone 2 (x2)**: The second-lowest tier
  - **Zone 1 (x1)**: The lowest tier, closest to the launch area
  - **Zone 0 / Off-board**: Dice that fall off the board or land in the gutter score 0
- 4 sets of dice (4 different colors — one set per player): each set contains 4 standard six-sided dice
- Score pad and pencil (optional — for tracking scores across rounds)
- Some editions include a wooden ramp/launch pad at the top

Note: Deluxe editions feature a large wooden board with felt-lined scoring zones and rubber bumper edges. Standard editions may be smaller or made of different materials.

## SETUP
1. Place the Tumblin-Dice board on a flat, stable surface (a table that won't wobble or shift).
2. Each player selects a set of 4 dice of one color.
3. Determine a starting player and play order (clockwise).
4. Decide the number of rounds to play (typically 3-4 rounds, or a target score).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
Players take turns flicking/rolling their dice, one die at a time, in clockwise order.

### On Your Turn
1. **Place your die at the top of the board** (in the designated launch area or at the edge of the highest tier).
2. **Flick, roll, or slide** the die so it tumbles down the stepped board. The die must start from the designated starting area.
   - **House rule variants**: Some groups allow only flicking (no pushing/sliding). Agree on the method before playing.
   - The die must leave the starting area and travel down the board.
3. **The die tumbles** down the steps, bouncing from tier to tier, and comes to rest in one of the scoring zones (or falls off the board).
4. **Leave the die where it lands**. Do NOT move it.

### Interaction with Other Dice
- **Knocking opponents' dice**: Your die CAN hit and move other players' dice already on the board. This is encouraged and strategic!
  - If you knock an opponent's die into a lower zone, that reduces their score.
  - If you knock an opponent's die off the board entirely, it scores 0.
  - If you knock your own die into a better position, that's great (but risky).
- Knocked dice stay wherever they end up after being hit.
- Dice that are knocked off the board are removed and score 0 for that round.

### Turn Order Within a Round
- Each player rolls one die, then the next player rolls one die, continuing clockwise.
- After all players have rolled one die each, continue for a second die each, then a third, then a fourth.
- Total: 4 rolls per player per round (one for each of their 4 dice).
- **Variant for more players**: With 3-4 players, each player still gets 4 dice. Turn order matters because later dice can knock earlier ones.

## SCORING / WINNING

### Scoring Each Round
After all players have rolled all their dice for the round:

1. **For each die on the board**, calculate its score:
   - **Score = Die face value x Zone multiplier**
   - Example: A die showing 5 in Zone 3 (x3) = 15 points
   - Example: A die showing 2 in Zone 4 (x4) = 8 points
   - Example: A die showing 6 in Zone 1 (x1) = 6 points
   - Dice off the board or in Zone 0 = 0 points

2. **Sum all your dice scores** for that round.

3. Record each player's round score.

### Multiplier Zones (typical layout):
| Zone | Multiplier | Description |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 4 | x4 | Farthest/highest platform — premium zone |
| 3 | x3 | Second tier — great score zone |
| 2 | x2 | Third tier — decent zone |
| 1 | x1 | Lowest tier — face value only |
| 0/Off | x0 | Off the board — no score |

### Game End
- Play the agreed number of rounds (typically 3-4).
- The player with the **highest total score across all rounds wins**.
- If playing a single round, the highest round score wins.

### Maximum Possible Score Per Round
- 4 dice, all showing 6, all in Zone 4: 4 x 6 x 4 = 96 points (practically impossible but theoretically maximum).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Aim for Zone 3 or 4**: The multiplier difference is massive. A 3 in Zone 4 (12 points) beats a 6 in Zone 2 (12 points) and is far better than a 6 in Zone 1 (6 points).
- **Knock opponents off the board**: This is one of the most impactful moves. Removing an opponent's 6-in-Zone-4 die eliminates 24 points from their score.
- **Protect your own dice**: Position dice so they're harder to knock off. Dice in corners or against edges are more secure.
- **The die face is random**: You can't control what number lands face-up. Focus on landing in high zones; the multiplier matters more than the die face.
- **Go for it early**: Your first die has no friendly dice to worry about knocking. Aim aggressively for Zone 4.
- **Later dice can be tactical**: Use your 3rd and 4th dice to knock opponents while hopefully landing in good zones yourself.
- **Board condition matters**: Felt surfaces slow dice, wooden surfaces are faster. Adjust your flick strength accordingly.
- **Consistent technique**: Develop a repeatable flick motion. Consistency beats power.
- **Some boards have a center divider/bump**: Learn how the board topology affects dice trajectories.
- **Great party game**: Simple rules, physical skill, and the joy of knocking opponents' dice off the board make this excellent for mixed groups.
- **Kids can play**: The dexterity element levels the playing field. Kids are often surprisingly good at this game.
- **House rules to discuss**: Whether dice can be "pushed" vs. only "flicked," whether re-rolls are allowed if a die doesn't leave the starting area, etc.
- **The board angle matters**: Ensure the board has the correct incline. Too steep and dice fly off; too flat and they don't tumble properly.
