# Game: Legacy of Yu
# Publisher: Garphill Games
# Players: 1 | Time: 45-75 min | Weight: 2.6/5

## OVERVIEW
Legacy of Yu is a solo-only worker-placement and tower-defense game set in ancient China. You play as Yu the Great, tasked with building a grand canal to protect your village from recurring floods while fending off raiders (Barbarians). Over the course of 7 rounds ("years"), you must complete the canal before the village is overrun.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Main board (village/canal area)
- Canal cards (7 sections to build)
- Raider cards (Barbarians that attack each round)
- Townsfolk cards (workers you recruit)
- Building cards (village improvements)
- Resource tokens (food, wood, clay, stone, gold)
- Flood tokens
- Fate deck
- Solo campaign legacy envelope system (for campaign mode)

## SETUP
1. Place the main board in the center of the play area.
2. Lay out the 7 canal section cards in order beside the board.
3. Shuffle the Raider deck and place it face down.
4. Shuffle the Townsfolk deck and deal an initial market of townsfolk.
5. Shuffle the Building deck and deal an initial market of buildings.
6. Take your starting resources as indicated (varies by scenario).
7. Place the Year marker on Year 1.
8. If playing the campaign, open the appropriate legacy envelope and follow its instructions.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each of the 7 years (rounds) follows these phases:

### Phase 1: Draw & Reveal
- Draw new Raider cards equal to the current year's threat level and place them along the raider track. Each raider shows a countdown number — the number of rounds before they attack.
- Reveal new Townsfolk and Building cards for the market.

### Phase 2: Assign Workers
- You have a set of worker tokens (base workers plus any recruited Townsfolk).
- Place workers on action spaces on the board and on cards. Actions include:
  - **Gather Resources**: Gain food, wood, clay, stone, or gold from supply spaces.
  - **Build Canal Section**: Pay the resource cost on the next canal card to complete that section. You must build canal sections in order (1 through 7).
  - **Construct Building**: Pay resources to build a building card from the market, gaining its ongoing benefit.
  - **Recruit Townsfolk**: Pay food to recruit a townsfolk card, adding a worker with a special ability.
  - **Fight Raiders**: Assign workers (and sometimes resources) to fight raider cards before they breach the village.
  - **Trade**: Exchange resources at the trading post (typically at unfavorable rates).

### Phase 3: Resolve Actions
- Resolve all placed workers. Collect resources, build, recruit, and fight as assigned.

### Phase 4: Raider Assault
- Reduce the countdown on all remaining raider cards by 1.
- Any raider whose countdown reaches 0 attacks the village. Undefeated raiders cause damage — typically destroying buildings, stealing resources, or adding flood tokens.
- If you accumulate too many flood tokens (threshold varies, often 7), you lose immediately.

### Phase 5: Upkeep / Feed
- Pay food to feed your workers. Each worker requires 1 food. Unfed workers may be lost or cause penalties.
- Discard used Townsfolk if they are single-use, refresh reusable ones.
- Advance the Year marker.

### Phase 6: Fate
- Draw a Fate card which may introduce a beneficial event, a harmful event, or a choice.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win Condition**: Complete all 7 canal sections before the end of Year 7 without being overwhelmed by floods or raiders.
- **Lose Conditions**: (1) The flood token track fills up. (2) You reach the end of Year 7 without completing the canal. (3) Certain catastrophic raider events in campaign mode.
- In campaign mode, your performance carries forward — buildings and townsfolk may persist or be lost between games, and legacy envelopes introduce new mechanics and story elements.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Canal sections must be built in order** — you cannot skip ahead.
- **Raider management is critical** — ignoring raiders for even one round can cascade into an unrecoverable state. Prioritize raiders with low countdowns.
- **Food is the bottleneck** — recruiting more townsfolk gives you more actions but also costs more food each round. Balance your workforce size.
- **Buildings provide compounding benefits** — early buildings that generate resources or reduce costs pay off enormously over 7 rounds.
- **Campaign mode is the intended experience** — the standalone game works fine, but the legacy envelopes add significant depth, story, and replayability.
- **Gold is a wildcard resource** — it can substitute for other resources in many situations but is hard to obtain. Save it for emergencies.
- **Plan 2 rounds ahead** — look at incoming raiders and canal costs to avoid being caught short on resources.
