## OVERVIEW
Journeys in Middle-earth is an app-driven cooperative adventure game set in J.R.R. Tolkien's Middle-earth. Players control iconic heroes (Aragorn, Legolas, Gimli, Bilbo, Elena, Beravor, and others) as they embark on multi-session campaigns across the map tiles of Middle-earth. The companion app manages the story, enemy AI, map exploration, and campaign progression, while players handle tactical movement, combat, and deck-based skill tests on the physical board.

## SETUP (PER SESSION)
1. Open the companion app and select your campaign (or start a new one).
2. Each player selects a hero. If continuing a campaign, load the saved state.
3. Each player selects a **Role** for this adventure. Roles provide a set of skill cards that are shuffled into the hero's personal deck for this session. Roles can change between adventures.
4. Build each hero's deck: Start with the hero's base cards + role skill cards + any items and skill cards gained during the campaign.
5. Shuffle each hero's deck. Draw a starting hand (VERIFY — no hand in the traditional sense; cards are used from the deck during tests).
6. The app instructs which map tiles to place, where to put heroes and tokens, and provides story text to read aloud.
7. Place enemy figures, exploration tokens, search tokens, and terrain as directed by the app.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
Each round consists of 3 phases:
1. **Action Phase** (heroes take turns)
2. **Shadow Phase** (app-driven enemy actions)
3. **Rally Phase** (heroes reset)

### Phase 1: Action Phase
Each hero takes a turn in any order (players decide). On your turn, you perform **2 actions** (may repeat the same action):

1. **Travel**: Move up to 2 spaces (hexes on the map tiles). Terrain may affect movement (some spaces cost extra or block movement).

2. **Attack**: Attack an enemy in your space or range (if using a ranged weapon).
   - **Skill Test**: Perform an attack test — reveal cards from the top of your deck equal to your attack value (determined by your equipped weapon + bonuses). Count **success icons** (hit/sun icons on the cards). Compare successes to the enemy's armor value.
   - Excess successes deal damage (hits) to the enemy.
   - Some cards have special combat abilities that trigger when revealed.
   - The app tracks enemy HP. When reduced to 0, the enemy is defeated and removed.

3. **Interact**: Interact with a token on your space (search tokens, person tokens, objective tokens). The app resolves the interaction — it may provide items, story text, skill tests, or advance the quest.

4. **Prepare**: Ready an item or skill card that has been exhausted (flipped). Some powerful abilities require exhausting the card.

### Skill Tests (Core Mechanism)
When the app (or a card/ability) calls for a skill test:
1. Determine which **stat** is tested (Might, Agility, Wisdom, Spirit, Wit — VERIFY exact stats).
2. Your stat value determines how many cards you reveal from the top of your personal deck.
3. Count success icons (sun/hit symbols) on the revealed cards.
4. **Inspiration**: Before revealing, you may spend Inspiration tokens to add bonus cards.
5. **Prepared cards**: Some prepared (face-up) cards can be exhausted during tests to add successes or trigger abilities.
6. Report the number of successes to the app. The app determines success or failure and resolves the outcome.

Failed tests may result in damage, fear, or story consequences.

### Phase 2: Shadow Phase
The app controls this phase entirely:
1. **Threat Event**: The app may trigger a story event, spawn new enemies, move existing enemies, or increase a threat meter.
2. **Enemy Activation**: Each enemy group activates according to the app's instructions. The app tells you which enemies move, which attack, and how much damage they deal.
3. **Enemy Attacks**: When an enemy attacks a hero, the hero performs a defense test (reveal cards, count shield icons) to reduce incoming damage. Unblocked damage is applied as Damage tokens and/or Fear tokens.

### Phase 3: Rally Phase
1. Each hero **scouts 2**: Look at the top 2 cards of your deck and decide to put each one on top or bottom of the deck. This lets you prepare for upcoming tests.
2. Each hero draws/prepares cards (VERIFY exact rally mechanism — may involve readying exhausted cards).

### Damage, Fear, and Defeat
- **Damage tokens** represent physical harm. When you take damage, place tokens on your hero.
- **Fear tokens** represent psychological stress. Taken from horror, failed courage tests, etc.
- If you must take Damage or Fear but cannot (you've reached your limit — VERIFY threshold, typically related to deck size), you perform a **Last Stand**: Draw a Last Stand card, which gives you a chance to stay in the fight with a penalty, or you may be knocked out for the rest of the adventure.
- **Weakness cards**: When you take Damage or Fear, you also add Weakness cards to your deck. These cards have NO success icons and clog your deck, making future skill tests harder. This is the primary attrition mechanic.

### Campaign Progression
Between adventures:
- The app saves campaign state.
- Heroes may gain new **Skill cards** (permanent upgrades added to their deck for future adventures).
- Heroes may acquire or upgrade **Items** (weapons, armor, trinkets that modify stats and provide abilities).
- The story branches based on player choices and success/failure outcomes.
- A campaign consists of multiple adventures (typically 10-14 sessions — VERIFY), forming a connected narrative.
- Heroes keep their progression (skills, items, upgrades) across the campaign.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Per Adventure**: Each adventure has objectives set by the app. Complete them before the threat level gets too high or heroes are defeated. Success or failure advances the campaign story (with different consequences).
- **Campaign Level**: The campaign has an overall narrative arc. Winning or losing individual adventures affects the story but does not necessarily end the campaign — the story adapts.
- There is no point scoring. Victory is narrative — completing the campaign's story is the goal.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Deck management is everything**: Your personal deck IS your health, your skill test ability, and your power. As Weakness cards accumulate, you become less effective. Managing deck composition is critical.
- **Role selection matters**: Different roles provide different skill cards. A Pathfinder excels at exploration, a Guardian at defense, a Hunter at combat. Match roles to the adventure's needs.
- **Coordinate hero actions**: Since heroes can act in any order, plan your turn order. Have the Guardian engage enemies first to protect squishier heroes.
- **Scouting is underrated**: The Rally Phase scout (look at top 2 cards) lets you stack your deck for important upcoming tests. Always scout thoughtfully.
- **Use Inspiration wisely**: Inspiration tokens add extra cards to tests. Save them for critical moments (boss fights, key story tests).
- **Explore aggressively**: Many adventures are on a timer (threat level rising). Explore the map quickly to find objectives before enemies overwhelm you.
- **Items and upgrades persist**: Campaign progression rewards investing in hero builds. Consider long-term synergy when choosing skill cards and items.
- **The app is essential**: There is no way to play without the companion app. Ensure devices are charged. The app handles all enemy AI, map reveals, story events, and campaign management.
- **Don't restart failed adventures immediately**: The campaign adapts to failure. Sometimes pressing on with a loss creates a more interesting story than reloading.
- **Party composition**: With fewer players, each player may control 2 heroes. A balanced party (damage dealer, tank, support/explorer) handles varied challenges better.
- **Save frequently**: The app auto-saves, but be aware of your campaign state. Multiple campaigns can run simultaneously.
