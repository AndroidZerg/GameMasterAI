# Game: Kingdom Builder
# Publisher: Queen Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 45-45 min | Weight: 2.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Kingdom Builder is a territory-building strategy game where players place settlements on a modular hex map to score points based on three randomly drawn Kingdom Builder cards that define the scoring conditions each game. The combination of variable scoring, modular boards, and special location abilities creates a different strategic puzzle every time you play. Designed by Donald X. Vaccarino (creator of Dominion), it won the 2012 Spiel des Jahres.

## COMPONENTS
- 8 modular game board quadrants (use 4 per game, each with different terrain and special location hexes)
- 25 terrain cards (5 each of: Forest, Canyon, Desert, Flower Field, Grassland)
- 10 Kingdom Builder cards (use 3 per game — define scoring conditions)
- 160 wooden settlement pieces (40 per player in 4 colors)
- 8 summary tiles for special locations
- 24 special location tiles (various types)
- Score track board

## SETUP
1. Randomly select 4 game board quadrants and arrange them in a 2x2 grid to form the playing area.
2. Shuffle the 10 Kingdom Builder cards and draw 3 — these define the scoring conditions for this game. Place them face up where all players can see.
3. Place the appropriate special location tiles on or near the board quadrants that feature those locations (each quadrant has 2 special location hexes).
4. Shuffle the terrain card deck. Deal 1 terrain card to each player (kept secret).
5. Each player takes all 40 settlements of their color.
6. Determine a starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn, you perform these steps in order:

### 1. Mandatory Action — Place 3 Settlements
- Reveal your terrain card. You MUST place exactly 3 settlements on hexes matching the terrain type shown.
- **Adjacency rule (critical)**: If you already have settlements on the board adjacent to a hex of the required terrain type, you MUST place your new settlements adjacent to your existing settlements (on matching terrain). This significantly constrains placement.
- If there are no hexes of the required terrain type adjacent to your existing settlements, you may place on ANY hex of that terrain type.
- Settlements may never be placed on water or mountain hexes.
- Each hex holds only 1 settlement.

### 2. Optional Action — Use Special Location Abilities
- Before, between, or after placing your mandatory settlements, you may use any special location tiles you have acquired (each once per turn).
- Special location abilities include:
  - **Farm**: Place 1 extra settlement on a Grassland hex.
  - **Oracle**: Place 1 extra settlement on a hex matching your current terrain card.
  - **Tavern**: Place 1 extra settlement at the end of a line of 3+ of your settlements.
  - **Tower**: Place 1 extra settlement on the edge of the board.
  - **Harbor**: Move 1 of your settlements to a Water hex (settlements can be placed on water ONLY via this ability).
  - **Paddock**: Move 1 of your settlements exactly 2 hexes in a straight line.
  - **Barn**: Move 1 of your settlements to a hex of your current terrain card type.
  - **Oasis**: Place 1 extra settlement on a Desert hex.

### 3. Acquire Special Location Tiles
- If any of your 3 newly placed settlements are adjacent to a special location hex that still has tiles remaining, you take 1 tile of that type. You keep this tile for the rest of the game and may use it once per turn on all future turns.
- You can have multiple different special location tiles but only one of each type.

### 4. Draw a New Terrain Card
- Draw the top card of the terrain deck. This will be your terrain for next turn.

### Terrain Types (where settlements can be placed)
- Forest, Canyon, Desert, Flower Field, Grassland
- Water and Mountain hexes are impassable (no settlements, except Harbor for water).

## SCORING / WINNING
At game end (triggered when any player places their last settlement, finish the round), score all 3 Kingdom Builder cards plus a base scoring rule:

### Base Scoring
- Each player scores 3 gold (points) for each settlement built adjacent to a Castle hex (printed on the board quadrants). This ALWAYS scores regardless of Kingdom Builder cards.

### Kingdom Builder Card Examples (3 randomly selected each game)
- **Lords**: 1 gold per settlement in your largest group of connected settlements for each separate sector (quadrant) of the board.
- **Citizens**: 1 gold for each settlement in your single largest connected group.
- **Knights**: 2 gold per settlement on a horizontal line — score only your single best row.
- **Fishermen**: 1 gold per settlement adjacent to a Water hex.
- **Miners**: 1 gold per settlement adjacent to a Mountain hex.
- **Farmers**: 1 gold per settlement in the sector (quadrant) where you have the fewest.
- **Merchants**: 4 gold per connected group of settlements that links 2 or more Castle hexes.
- **Discoverers**: 1 gold per horizontal line on the board containing at least 1 of your settlements.
- **Hermits**: 1 gold per separate (non-connected) group of your settlements.
- **Workers**: 1 gold per settlement adjacent to a special location hex or Castle hex.

The player with the most gold wins. Tiebreaker: most settlements remaining (fewest placed).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- The adjacency rule is the most important constraint: once you settle near a terrain type, you will be forced to keep building there when that terrain card comes up. Spread carefully.
- Special location tiles are extremely powerful — acquiring them early dramatically increases your options. Prioritize getting to special locations.
- Study the 3 Kingdom Builder cards BEFORE your first placement. Your entire strategy should revolve around the scoring conditions.
- Settlements adjacent to Castles always score 3 gold each — don't neglect this.
- Spreading across multiple board quadrants is often valuable but depends on the scoring cards.
- The Paddock and Harbor abilities are among the strongest because they let you reposition settlements, partially bypassing the adjacency constraint.
- With the Hermits card, you want many small disconnected groups — the opposite of most other strategies.
- The game has low downtime and plays quickly once everyone knows the rules.
