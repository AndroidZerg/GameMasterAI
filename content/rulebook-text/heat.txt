# Game: Heat: Pedal to the Metal
# Publisher: Days of Wonder
# Players: 1-6 | Time: 30-60 min | Weight: 2.2/5

## OVERVIEW
Heat: Pedal to the Metal is a racing game where players compete as drivers in a 1960s-style grand prix. Using a hand management and deck-building system, players must carefully manage their speed, braking, and heat to navigate corners and overtake opponents. The game captures the thrill of pushing your car to the limit — go too fast into a corner and you'll spin out; play it too safe and you'll be left in the dust.

## COMPONENTS
- Double-sided game board with 4 different race tracks (varying difficulty)
- Player car miniatures (6 cars in different colors)
- Player decks (12 cards each: Speed cards valued 1-4, Stress cards, Starting Upgrade cards)
- Heat cards (per player, start in engine)
- Upgrade cards (optional module for advanced play)
- Gear markers (gear pawn per player, 1st through 4th gear)
- Player mats (showing gear positions and engine/discard areas)
- Weather and road condition tokens (advanced modules)
- Legend cards (for AI opponents in solo/fewer players)

## SETUP
1. Choose a track and assemble/reveal the board.
2. Each player takes a car miniature, player mat, and starting deck of 12 cards: seven Speed cards (1, 1, 2, 2, 3, 3, 4), three Stress cards, and two Starting Upgrade cards (basic).
3. Each player places 6 Heat cards in their Engine area (on the player mat). These start OUT of the deck.
4. Determine starting positions (randomly or by draft).
5. All players start in 1st Gear.
6. Shuffle your 12-card deck and draw 7 cards as your starting hand.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

The race is played over multiple laps (typically 1-3 depending on the track card). Each round has simultaneous planning followed by sequential resolution.

### 1. Shift Gears
Each player simultaneously adjusts their gear up or down by 0 or 1 step (1st <-> 2nd <-> 3rd <-> 4th). You may shift up or down one gear, or stay in the same gear.

**Gear determines:**
- How many cards you MUST play this turn (1st gear = 1 card, 2nd = 2, 3rd = 3, 4th = 4).
- Your speed flexibility. Higher gears = more cards played = potentially more speed.

### 2. Play Cards
Simultaneously, each player selects exactly the number of cards required by their current gear from their hand and places them face-down.

- **Speed cards (1-4)**: Add their value to your movement.
- **Heat cards**: You shouldn't normally have Heat in your hand, but if you do (from cooling down), they have a value of 0 and waste a card slot.
- **Stress cards**: These have a "?" value — when revealed, you draw the top card of your draw pile. If it's a Speed card, use that value. If it's another Stress or Heat, it counts as 0.

### 3. Reveal & Move
In current race position order (leader first), each player reveals their cards and moves their car forward the total number of spaces.

**Slipstream (Drafting)**: After moving, if your car is directly behind another car (within 2 spaces), you may move up to 2 additional spaces for free. This represents drafting.

### 4. Adrenaline
Players who are currently in last place (or among the last, depending on player count) gain an Adrenaline boost: +1 speed added to their total.

### 5. React (Check Corners)
After all cars have moved, check if any car crossed or landed on a **Corner**:

**Corner Rules:**
- Each corner has a **speed limit** (shown on the board, e.g., "Speed Limit 5").
- Your speed is the total of your played cards (plus any slipstream/adrenaline).
- If your speed **exceeds the speed limit**, you MUST pay Heat equal to the difference. Move that many Heat cards from your Engine to your Discard pile.
- If you don't have enough Heat in your Engine, you **Spin Out**: your car moves back to the first available space of the corner, you gain 1 Stress card into your discard, and your gear shifts to 1st.

### 6. Discard & Draw
Discard all played cards and any remaining cards you choose to discard. Draw back up to 7 cards.

**Cooldown**: Before drawing, you may move Heat cards from your hand to your Engine (cooling them down — they return to the Engine and out of your cycling deck). This is important because Heat in your deck/hand clogs your draws.

### 7. Replenish
When your draw deck runs out, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck.

## SCORING / WINNING
- The first player to cross the finish line after the required number of laps wins.
- If multiple players cross the finish line in the same round, the one who went furthest past the line wins.
- For tournament/league play, positions score points (1st = most points, etc.) across multiple races.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Heat management is the core mechanic**: Heat starts in your Engine and acts as a "health" resource for going fast through corners. Once Heat enters your deck, it clogs your hand with useless 0-value cards. Cooling down (moving Heat from hand back to Engine) is crucial.
- **You MUST play the exact number of cards matching your gear**: In 4th gear, you play 4 cards. If your hand is full of Heat, you'll play 4 cards worth very little.
- **Slipstreaming is powerful**: Positioning yourself 1-2 spaces behind another car gives free movement. On straights, staying just behind the pack is often better than leading.
- **Corner speed limits**: Always check the upcoming corner's speed limit before choosing your gear. Being in 4th gear (4 cards) going into a tight corner (speed limit 3-4) almost guarantees you'll need to spend Heat or spin out.
- **Stress cards are wild**: They can be great (drawing a 4) or terrible (drawing Heat or another Stress = 0). They add uncertainty.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting to check corners mid-movement. If you pass through a corner during your move, its speed limit applies to your total speed for that turn.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting slipstream. Always check if you ended within 2 spaces behind another car.
- **Upgrade cards (advanced)**: These add asymmetric powers to each player. Some let you manipulate Heat, gain extra movement, or have special corner abilities. Highly recommended after learning the base game.
- **Spin outs are devastating**: You lose position, gain a Stress card (deck bloat), and drop to 1st gear. Avoid them at almost any cost.
