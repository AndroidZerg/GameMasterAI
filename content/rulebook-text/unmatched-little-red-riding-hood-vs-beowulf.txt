# Game: Unmatched: Little Red Riding Hood vs. Beowulf
# Publisher: Restoration Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 20-40 min | Weight: 2.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Unmatched is an asymmetric tactical combat card game where legendary heroes fight on modular map boards. Each hero has a unique deck of cards reflecting their fighting style and abilities. Little Red Riding Hood vs. Beowulf is a standalone set featuring two heroes: the cunning Little Red Riding Hood (with her companion the Huntsman) and the mighty warrior Beowulf (with his companion Wiglaf). The system is compatible with all other Unmatched sets for cross-set battles.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 double-sided game board (modular map with zones and spaces)
- Little Red Riding Hood hero card and miniature
- The Huntsman sidekick token
- Little Red Riding Hood's deck (30 cards)
- Beowulf hero card and miniature
- Wiglaf sidekick token
- Beowulf's deck (30 cards)
- Health dials (or health trackers) for each hero and sidekick
- 1 combat token (to mark the active combat)

## SETUP
1. Choose a map side and place the board in the center.
2. Each player selects a hero and takes their hero card, miniature, sidekick token (if applicable), and unique deck of 30 cards.
3. Set each hero's health dial to their starting health:
   - **Little Red Riding Hood**: Typically 13 HP
   - **The Huntsman** (sidekick): Typically 7 HP
   - **Beowulf**: Typically 17 HP
   - **Wiglaf** (sidekick): Typically 5 HP
4. Shuffle each player's deck. Draw an opening hand of **5 cards** (in a 2-player game; hand sizes may vary for 3-4 player variants).
5. Place heroes and sidekicks on their designated starting spaces (as marked on the map or agreed upon by players).
6. Determine the first player (random method, coin flip, etc.).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Turn Structure
On your turn, you must take **2 actions** (they can be the same action twice). The available actions are:

#### 1. MANEUVER
- Draw a card from your deck (draw 1 card).
- Then, you may move your hero and/or sidekick. Each fighter can move up to their movement value (printed on their hero/sidekick card).
- Movement is from space to space along connected paths on the map.
- You can move through spaces occupied by other fighters, but you cannot end movement in a space with another fighter.
- **Zones**: The board is divided into colored zones. Some abilities reference zones (e.g., ranged attacks require being in the same zone but different space).

#### 2. SCHEME
- Play a **Scheme card** from your hand. Scheme cards have an immediate effect described on the card (buffs, movement, card draw, special abilities).
- Scheme cards have the word "Scheme" on them and are always played during this action (never during combat).
- Resolve the card's effect and discard it.

#### 3. ATTACK
- Declare an attack with your hero OR sidekick against an opposing fighter.
- **Melee attacks**: Your fighter must be in the SAME SPACE or an adjacent space as the target (depends on hero — some have melee range, some have ranged).
- **Ranged attacks**: Your fighter and the target must be in the SAME ZONE (but can be in different spaces within that zone). Ranged attacks are indicated by a ranged attack symbol on the card.
- Play an **Attack card** from your hand face-down. The attack card has a value (top number).
- The defender may play a **Defense card** face-down (optional — they may choose to take the hit and save cards).
- Both cards are revealed simultaneously.
- **Resolve combat**:
  - Compare the attack value to the defense value. The defender takes damage equal to the difference (attack minus defense). If defense equals or exceeds attack, no damage.
  - Apply any "During Combat" effects listed on either card.
  - Apply any "After Combat" effects.
  - Discard both cards.
  - If no defense card is played, the full attack value is dealt as damage.

### Combat Cards
Each card in your deck can be one or more of:
- **Attack card** (sword icon + value): Used when attacking.
- **Defense card** (shield icon + value): Used when defending.
- **Versatile card** (both icons): Can be used as either attack or defense.
- **Scheme card**: Played as a Scheme action (not during combat).
- Many cards also have special abilities or effects ("During Combat:", "After Combat:", "Immediately:").

### Sidekicks
- Sidekicks act as separate fighters on the board.
- They can be moved during Maneuver actions and can attack/be attacked.
- Sidekicks typically use generic attack/defense cards from the deck (not the named hero-specific ones).
- When a sidekick is defeated (0 HP), remove them from the board. They do not return.

### Hero-Specific Abilities

**Little Red Riding Hood**:
- Special ability: She has cards that grow stronger based on the number of cards in her discard pile. She starts weak but becomes more dangerous as the fight progresses.
- **The basket mechanic**: Some of her cards reference her "basket" — items she carries that trigger special effects.
- The Huntsman is her ranged sidekick who can attack from a distance.

**Beowulf**:
- Special ability: Beowulf is a powerful melee fighter. He has cards that can be "boosted" by discarding additional cards from hand for increased effect.
- High health pool (17 HP) makes him durable.
- Wiglaf is his loyal sidekick who supports him in close combat.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win condition**: Defeat the opposing hero by reducing their HP to 0.
- Defeating a sidekick does NOT win the game — only the hero's defeat matters.
- **Deck exhaustion**: If you must draw a card and your deck is empty, you take 2 damage instead. This becomes a ticking clock in long games.
- In **3-4 player free-for-all** (combining with other Unmatched sets), the last hero standing wins.
- In **2v2 team variant**, a team wins when both opposing heroes are defeated.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Hand management is critical**: Your deck is your life total AND your options. Every card used for attack/defense is one fewer card available later. And when your deck runs out, you take damage.
- **Don't always defend**: Sometimes it's better to take a hit and save a good card for your own attack.
- **Maneuver is underrated**: Drawing cards and repositioning are essential. Don't spend every action attacking.
- **Know your hero's strengths**: Little Red gets stronger over time (discard pile grows). Beowulf is strongest early with a full hand. This creates interesting pacing dynamics.
- **Protect your sidekick**: Sidekicks extend your board presence and combat options. Losing one reduces your flexibility.
- **Ranged vs. Melee**: Ranged attackers want to maintain distance. Melee fighters need to close the gap. Map positioning matters.
- **Read your cards carefully**: Many Unmatched cards have conditional effects ("During Combat: if you won..." etc.). Triggering these effects is key to mastery.
- **Watch your deck size**: Count your remaining cards. If your opponent's deck is running low, they may be taking damage from empty draws soon.
- **All Unmatched sets are cross-compatible**: You can fight Little Red vs. any hero from any Unmatched set (Jurassic Park, Bruce Lee, Marvel, etc.) using the same core rules.
- **The map matters**: Use zones to your advantage. Choke points, line of sight for ranged attacks, and escape routes all matter tactically.
- **Feint mechanic** (in some Unmatched sets/cards): Some cards let you cancel combat, wasting the opponent's played card. Watch for these.
