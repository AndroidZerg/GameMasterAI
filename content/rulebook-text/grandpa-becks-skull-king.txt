# Game: Skull King
# Publisher: Grandpa Beck's Games
# Players: 2-8 | Time: 30 min | Weight: 1.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Skull King is a trick-taking card game where players bid on exactly how many tricks they will win each round. The game is played over 10 rounds, with each round dealing one more card than the last (1 card in round 1, 10 cards in round 10). Special character cards add chaos and strategy, making this far more dynamic than traditional trick-taking games.

## COMPONENTS
- 66 card deck consisting of:
  - **Numbered suit cards (52)**: 4 suits — Parrots (yellow/green), Treasure Chests (yellow), Treasure Maps (purple), Jolly Roger/Skulls (black) — numbered 1-14 in each suit (some editions: 1-13)
  - **Escape cards (5)**: Played to lose a trick
  - **Pirate cards (5)**: Named pirates that beat all numbered cards
  - **Skull King (1)**: Beats everything except Mermaids
  - **Mermaid cards (2)**: Beat the Skull King, lose to Pirates (in newer editions)
  - **Tigress (1)**: Can be played as either a Pirate or an Escape (player's choice)
  - **Loot cards** (some editions include bonus loot cards)
- Score pad and pencil

Note: Card composition varies between editions. The above reflects the most common version.

## SETUP
1. Shuffle the entire deck.
2. Choose a dealer. Deal proceeds clockwise.
3. Provide each player access to the score pad.
4. Round 1 begins: deal 1 card to each player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
The game is played over **10 rounds**:
- Round 1: each player receives 1 card
- Round 2: each player receives 2 cards
- Round 3: each player receives 3 cards
- ...and so on up to Round 10: 10 cards each

### Bidding Phase
After cards are dealt, ALL players simultaneously bid on how many tricks they expect to win that round:
1. Everyone holds a fist out.
2. On the count of "1, 2, 3," all players simultaneously extend fingers showing their bid (0 to the number of cards in that round).
3. Bids are recorded on the score pad.
4. A bid of 0 means you expect to win NO tricks.

### Playing Phase
1. The player to the left of the dealer leads the first trick.
2. Players MUST follow the suit of the lead card if they can (standard trick-taking rules). If they cannot follow suit, they may play any card.
3. **Exception**: Black (Jolly Roger) cards are ALWAYS trump and can be played at any time, even if you could follow the lead suit. (Note: this varies by edition — in some versions, black is simply the trump suit that must be followed if led.)
4. Special cards (Escape, Pirate, Skull King, Mermaid) can be played at any time regardless of what was led.

### Card Hierarchy (lowest to highest)
1. **Escape cards**: Always lose. If all players play Escapes, the first Escape played wins.
2. **Numbered suit cards (non-trump)**: Standard trick-taking — highest card of the led suit wins among non-trump.
3. **Black (Jolly Roger) suit cards**: Trump suit — any black numbered card beats any non-black numbered card. Highest black card wins among trumps.
4. **Mermaid cards**: Beat all numbered cards (including black). Lose to Pirates.
5. **Pirate cards**: Beat all numbered cards and Mermaids.
6. **Skull King**: Beats everything EXCEPT Mermaids. If a Mermaid is in the same trick as the Skull King, the Mermaid wins.
7. **Tigress**: Functions as either a Pirate or an Escape (declared when played).

### Special Interactions
- If ONLY special cards are played (no numbered cards), the first Pirate or Skull King played wins.
- If multiple Pirates are played with no Skull King, the FIRST Pirate played wins.
- Escapes always lose (unless everyone plays Escapes — then the first one wins).
- **Skull King captured by Mermaid**: The Mermaid player earns a bonus (see Scoring).
- **Pirate captures Mermaid**: Some editions give a bonus for this as well.

### Trick Resolution
- The winner of a trick collects the cards and leads the next trick.
- Continue until all cards in that round have been played.

## SCORING / WINNING

### Scoring a Non-Zero Bid
- **Exact bid**: Earn **20 points per trick bid**. (Bid 3, win exactly 3 = 60 points.)
- **Over or under**: Lose **10 points per trick off** from your bid. (Bid 3, win 5 = -20 points. Bid 3, win 1 = -20 points.)

### Scoring a Zero Bid
- **Successful zero bid**: Earn **10 points multiplied by the current round number**. (Round 7, bid 0, win 0 = 70 points.)
- **Failed zero bid**: Lose **10 points multiplied by the current round number**. (Round 7, bid 0, win any tricks = -70 points.)

### Bonus Points
- **Capturing the Skull King with a Mermaid**: +50 bonus points.
- **Capturing Pirates with the Skull King**: +30 bonus points per Pirate in the same trick.
- **14 of trump (black 14)**: Some editions award +10 bonus per card in the trick when winning with the highest trump.
- Bonuses are only awarded if the player makes their exact bid.

### Game End
- After 10 rounds, the player with the highest total score wins.
- In case of a tie, the player who achieved more exact bids wins (or play a tiebreaker round).

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Bidding 0 is powerful but risky**: The reward scales with round number, but so does the penalty. In round 10, a successful zero bid is worth 100 points but failure costs 100 points.
- **Count your guaranteed losers**: Escape cards and low off-suit cards are reliable ways to lose tricks when bidding 0.
- **The Skull King is not invincible**: Mermaids beat it, so don't assume it's a guaranteed trick. Watch for Mermaids.
- **Pirates are the safest special card**: They beat all numbered cards and Mermaids. Only the Skull King beats them.
- **Tigress flexibility**: Save the Tigress for when you need the option — as a Pirate to win, or as an Escape to duck.
- **Must follow suit applies to numbered cards only**: Special cards can always be played. Use this to strategically play Escapes or Pirates regardless of what was led.
- **In early rounds (1-3 cards), luck dominates**: Don't worry about small scoring misses. The big points come in rounds 7-10.
- **Track other players' bids**: If everyone bids high, some must fail. Adjust your play accordingly.
- **The simultaneous bid reveal prevents gaming**: You can't wait to see what others bid before choosing yours.
- **Lead with strength or weakness intentionally**: Leading a Pirate can flush out the Skull King. Leading an Escape can test the waters.
