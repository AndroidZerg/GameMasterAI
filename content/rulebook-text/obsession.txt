# Game: Obsession
# Publisher: Kayenta Games
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 30-90 min | Weight: 3.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Obsession is a Victorian-era strategy game where players manage a country estate in England, hosting social activities to build prestige, improve their estate, and attract the favor of the Fairchild family (the most eligible family in the county). Players recruit guests, renovate rooms in their manor, and host activities that generate reputation, wealth, and connections. The game combines deck-building-like guest management with tile placement and worker management.

## COMPONENTS
- Player boards (one per player, representing your estate)
- Improvement tiles (rooms/facilities to add to your estate — Parlour, Library, Servants' Hall, etc.)
- Guest cards (Casual, Prestige, and Prominent guests in four families)
- Starter guest cards (your starting family members)
- Starter improvement tiles (rooms each player begins with)
- Service tokens (Butler, Valet, Lady's Maid, Housekeeper, Footman, various specialty servants)
- Reputation track and markers
- Money (pounds)
- Victory point tokens
- Objective cards (end-game scoring goals)
- Courtship cards (Fairchild family — Charles and/or Elizabeth)
- Theme cards (determine which attributes are favored each round)
- Round marker
- Monument/Extension tiles
- Organiser card holders

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a player board, starting improvement tiles (typically a Brushing Room, a couple of basic rooms), starting family guest cards, and starting servants (a Butler, a Housekeeper, a Valet, a Footman, and a Lady's Maid — exact count may vary by player count).
2. Shuffle the improvement tiles by category and lay them out in the market (a row of available tiles to purchase).
3. Shuffle the guest decks (Casual, Prestige, Prominent) and create the guest display.
4. Set the reputation track and place each player's marker at the starting position.
5. Give each player starting money (typically 200-300 pounds depending on player count).
6. Shuffle and reveal Theme cards for each round (these determine the focus attribute for the Courtship phase).
7. Place the Fairchild courtship cards (Charles and/or Elizabeth) near the board.
8. Shuffle and deal Objective cards (each player gets some private end-game objectives).
9. Set up the round track — the game is played over 16 rounds (4 seasons of 4 rounds each, with the 4th round of each season being a special Courtship round).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

The game is played over 16 rounds, organized into 4 seasons. Each season has 3 normal rounds and 1 Courtship round (the 4th round).

### Normal Round (Rounds 1-3 of each season)
On your turn:
1. **Host an Activity**: Select one of your available (face-up/unexhausted) improvement tiles in your estate. This is the room where you're hosting.
2. **Invite Guests**: Each improvement tile requires specific types of guests and servants. Place eligible guest cards from your available hand onto the tile. Guests must match the required guest types shown on the improvement (e.g., "Gentleman + Lady" or "any 2 guests").
3. **Assign Servants**: Place the required servant tokens on the tile. Different rooms require different servants (e.g., a Dinner Party might require a Butler, Footman, and Cook).
4. **Resolve Benefits**: Gain the rewards shown on the improvement tile — typically some combination of: reputation increase, money, victory points, new servants, or the ability to draw new guest cards. Some tiles provide the opportunity to buy new improvement tiles.
5. **Refresh**: The guests you used and the servants you assigned become "exhausted" (unavailable) for the next round. They return after a recovery period (servants return after 1 round of rest; guests return to your available hand after their specified recovery).

**Alternatively**, if you cannot or choose not to host an activity, you may **Pass** and recover all your exhausted servants (they become available again).

### Purchasing Improvements
When a tile's benefit allows you to buy improvements, you may purchase one available improvement tile from the market by paying its cost. Place it on your player board (estate). This tile is now available for future activities.

### Courtship Round (Round 4 of each season)
The Courtship round is special:
- A Theme card is revealed (or was revealed at the start of the season) showing which attribute (Prestige, Service, Reputation, etc.) the Fairchild family values this season.
- Each player's standing in that attribute is compared. The player with the highest value in the relevant attribute wins the Fairchild's favor and receives a Courtship card benefit (bonus VP, guest, or other reward).
- After the Courtship round, ALL servants and guests refresh (everyone recovers for the new season).

### Guest Types
- **Family (starter)**: Your starting guests. Modest abilities.
- **Casual guests**: Easy to attract, provide small benefits.
- **Prestige guests**: Require higher reputation to invite. Provide better benefits.
- **Prominent guests**: The most powerful, require the highest reputation. Some have special ongoing abilities.

### Reputation
- Reputation is tracked on the reputation track (ranges typically 1-25+).
- Higher reputation unlocks the ability to invite more prestigious guests.
- Some improvement tiles and guest abilities increase reputation.
- Certain events or poor hosting can decrease reputation.

## SCORING / WINNING
At the end of 16 rounds, final scoring:
- **Victory Points from activities**: VP accumulated during the game from hosting.
- **Improvement tile VP**: Many improvement tiles in your estate have printed VP values.
- **Objective cards**: Score any achieved private objectives.
- **Courtship favor**: Bonus VP from Fairchild courtship victories.
- **Reputation bonus**: Higher reputation positions may provide end-game VP.
- **Remaining money**: Some money converts to VP (typically at a low rate).
- **Guest prestige**: Some guests in your collection contribute VP.

The player with the most VP wins. Ties broken by reputation, then money.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Servant management is the core puzzle**: You have limited servants, and they need rest after being used. Plan 2-3 rounds ahead — don't exhaust all your servants in one big round unless you can afford to pass.
- **Reputation gates matter**: You MUST have sufficient reputation to invite Prestige and Prominent guests. Invest early in reputation-building improvements.
- **Courtship rounds are pivotal**: The Fairchild bonuses are significant. Try to be strong in the attribute the Theme card favors.
- **Balance money and VP**: Improvements cost money, but VP wins the game. Don't hoard money — invest it in improvements that generate VP.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that guests and servants both exhaust after use. Players often plan a big round and then realize they have nothing to work with for the next 1-2 rounds.
- **Improvement tile synergy**: Build a coherent estate. Some tiles work together (e.g., a Library might synergize with scholarly guests).
- **Don't ignore the Objective cards**: These can swing 10-20 points. Build toward your objectives.
- **Passing is sometimes correct**: If your guests/servants are mostly exhausted, passing to refresh everything is better than hosting a weak activity.
- **The market refills**: New improvement tiles enter the market each round. Don't feel you must buy immediately — but strong tiles get snapped up quickly.
