# Players: 2-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Stuffed Fables is a cooperative storybook adventure game where players control stuffed animals who come alive at night to protect the little girl who loves them from nightmares and the dark forces of the Fall. The game features a unique "Adventure Book" that serves as both the rulebook for each scenario and the game board itself, with pages that unfold the story as you play. It uses a dice-based action system instead of traditional cards or action points.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Adventure Book (large spiral-bound book with map pages and story text)
- 6 Stuffed Animal miniatures (Theodora the bear, Lumpy the elephant, Flops the bunny, Stitch the patchwork cat, Piggle the pig, and others)
- Various Minion miniatures (nightmare creatures)
- 5 custom dice in different colors (white, red, yellow, green, blue, black, purple)
- Stuffie cards (character cards for each stuffed animal with unique abilities)
- Item cards, Status cards, Lost cards
- Heart tokens (health)
- Stuffing tokens (currency/resource)
- Button tokens (special abilities)
- Discovery tokens
- Various cardboard standees and tokens
- Threat track board
- Dice bag (black cloth bag)

## SETUP
1. Choose a story from the Adventure Book. Each story has its own setup instructions.
2. Each player selects a Stuffed Animal and takes its character card, miniature, and starting equipment.
3. Place heart tokens on each character card equal to their starting health (typically 5 hearts).
4. Place all colored dice into the dice bag: the bag contains white, red, yellow, green, blue, and BLACK dice. The number of dice in the bag is specified per story/scenario.
5. Set up the Threat Track — place the threat marker at the starting position.
6. Open the Adventure Book to the starting page of the chosen story.
7. Read the story text aloud and follow any specific setup instructions on that page (place miniatures, tokens, etc. as indicated).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Turn Overview
Stuffed Fables does NOT use a traditional turn order. Instead, players share a communal dice pool drawn from the bag.

### Active Player's Turn
1. **Draw Dice**: The active player draws 5 dice from the bag, one at a time.
   - **Black dice** (Threat dice): When drawn, these are NOT added to your pool. Instead, immediately advance the threat track by 1 for each black die drawn. If the threat track reaches certain thresholds, bad events (Minion activations, surges, etc.) occur. After resolving, the black die goes to a discard pile, not back in the bag until a reset.
   - **Colored dice**: Add these to your personal pool for the turn.

2. **Assign and Use Dice**: On your turn, you assign your colored dice to perform actions. Each die color corresponds to a type of action:
   - **Red die**: Melee attack — roll value determines hit strength.
   - **Yellow die**: Ranged attack — roll value determines hit strength.
   - **Green die**: Search / Interact — used to search areas, pick up items, or interact with story elements.
   - **Blue die**: Defend / Support — used to defend against attacks or help other stuffies.
   - **White die**: Wild — can be used as any color.
   - **Purple die**: Special / Magic — used for special abilities (if in the game).

3. **Movement**: Before or after using dice, you may move your stuffie a number of spaces on the Adventure Book map. Movement costs are typically 1 space per action or free movement up to a set number (usually 3-4 spaces, story-dependent).

4. **Use Items**: Some items require assigning a die to activate.

5. **Pass remaining dice**: If you have dice you don't want to use, you may save up to a certain number (usually 1-2) for a future turn or pass them to other players (group-dependent rules).

### Combat
- **Melee Attack**: Assign a red die. Compare your die value (plus any weapon bonuses) to the enemy's defense. If you meet or exceed it, you deal damage.
- **Ranged Attack**: Same as melee but uses a yellow die and can hit from a distance.
- **Enemy Attacks**: When minions activate (triggered by the threat track), they attack nearby stuffies. The active player rolls defense dice; the result compared to enemy attack value determines if damage is taken.
- **Taking Damage**: Remove heart tokens from your character. If you lose all hearts, your stuffie is "lost" — draw from the Lost deck which has consequences but doesn't eliminate you from the game. Stuffies can be revived by other players.

### Story Progression
- The Adventure Book tells you when to turn pages, often triggered by reaching certain locations, defeating enemies, or making story choices.
- **Story choices**: Players vote on decisions that branch the narrative.
- **Discovery tokens**: When you land on or search a discovery space, draw the indicated discovery card or read the indicated passage.

### Threat Track
- Advances when black dice are drawn.
- At certain thresholds: enemies activate (move and attack), new enemies spawn, or special events trigger.
- If the threat track maxes out, a major negative event occurs (story-specific), then it resets.

### Dice Bag Reset
- When the dice bag is empty, return all dice (including black dice from the discard) to the bag and shake it up. This means more black dice accumulate over time if not managed.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Cooperative victory**: Players win by completing the story's objective (varies per scenario — rescue the child, defeat the boss nightmare, reach a safe haven, etc.).
- **Loss conditions**: Story-specific, but commonly: all stuffies are lost simultaneously, the child is captured, or a timer/threat condition is met.
- There is no point scoring — it's a narrative win/loss.
- The game is designed as a campaign with connected stories that carry consequences forward.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Manage the threat track carefully**: Black dice are the timer and danger source. Discuss strategy about when to push forward vs. when to prepare defenses.
- **Share dice wisely**: Although each player draws their own dice, planning together about who needs which colors is essential for cooperative success.
- **Read the story aloud**: Half the charm of the game is the narrative. Take turns reading and enjoy the storytelling.
- **Don't split up too much**: Stuffies are stronger together. Being spread across the map means you can't help each other in combat.
- **Items matter**: Search whenever reasonable. Good items significantly improve your combat effectiveness and survival.
- **The game scales**: More players means more dice drawn per round but also more black dice encounters. The threat track advances faster with more players due to more draws.
- **Kid-friendly but not trivial**: The game is designed for families and can be played with children (age 7+), but it has genuine strategic depth and can be challenging.
- **Save your white (wild) dice**: They're flexible and valuable. Don't waste them on low-priority actions.
- **Lost stuffies aren't dead**: Being "lost" has penalties but you come back. Don't play too conservatively.
- **Campaign continuity**: Decisions and items carry over between stories. Consider long-term consequences.
