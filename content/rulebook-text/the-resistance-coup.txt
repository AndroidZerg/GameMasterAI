# Game: Coup
# Publisher: Indie Boards & Cards
# Players: 2-6 | Time: 15 min | Weight: 1.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Coup is a fast-paced bluffing and deduction card game set in a dystopian universe. Each player holds two face-down influence cards representing characters in their court. On your turn, you take an action — but you can claim to have ANY character to use its power, whether you actually hold that card or not. Other players can challenge your claims, risking their own influence if wrong. The last player with influence remaining wins.

## COMPONENTS
- 15 Character cards (3 copies each of 5 characters: Duke, Assassin, Captain, Ambassador, Contessa)
- 50 Coins (denominations of 1 and 5)
- Summary/reference cards (1 per player)
- Rules sheet

## SETUP
1. Shuffle all 15 character cards together.
2. Deal 2 cards face-down to each player. Players may look at their own cards but must keep them hidden from others.
3. Give each player 2 coins from the treasury.
4. Place remaining coins in the center as the treasury.
5. Place remaining character cards face-down as a court deck (used by the Ambassador).
6. Choose a starting player (youngest, random, etc.).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Turn Overview
On your turn, you MUST take exactly ONE action. There is no passing. Actions are either **general actions** (available to everyone, cannot be challenged) or **character actions** (require claiming a specific character, can be challenged).

### General Actions (Cannot Be Challenged)
1. **Income**: Take 1 coin from the treasury. Simple and safe.
2. **Foreign Aid**: Take 2 coins from the treasury. Can be BLOCKED by a player claiming Duke (but the action itself cannot be challenged — the block can be challenged).
3. **Coup**: Pay 7 coins to the treasury. Choose a player — they must lose 1 influence (turn one card face-up). This action CANNOT be blocked or challenged. It always succeeds.

**MANDATORY COUP RULE**: If you have 10 or more coins at the start of your turn, you MUST Coup. You cannot take any other action.

### Character Actions (Require Claiming a Character)
4. **Duke — Tax**: Take 3 coins from the treasury. (Claim: "I have the Duke.")
5. **Assassin — Assassinate**: Pay 3 coins to the treasury. Choose a player — they lose 1 influence. Can be BLOCKED by Contessa. (Claim: "I have the Assassin.")
6. **Captain — Steal**: Take 2 coins from another player (or 1 if they only have 1, or 0 if they have 0). Can be BLOCKED by Captain or Ambassador. (Claim: "I have the Captain.")
7. **Ambassador — Exchange**: Draw 2 cards from the court deck, look at them along with your current face-down cards, choose which to keep (keeping the same number you started with), and return the rest face-down to the court deck. (Claim: "I have the Ambassador.")

### Blocking
Certain actions can be blocked by claiming a specific character:
- **Foreign Aid** can be blocked by claiming **Duke** (any player, not just the target).
- **Assassination** can be blocked by the target claiming **Contessa**.
- **Stealing** can be blocked by the target claiming **Captain** or **Ambassador**.

Blocking does NOT cost your turn — it happens in response to another player's action, on their turn.

A block itself CAN BE CHALLENGED (see below).

### Challenging
Any player can challenge a character claim (action or block) by saying "I challenge" or "I don't believe you."

**Challenge Resolution:**
1. The challenged player must reveal whether they actually hold the claimed character.
2. **If they DO have the card**: The challenge fails.
   - The challenger loses 1 influence (flips a card face-up).
   - The challenged player shuffles the revealed card back into the court deck and draws a new card (they do not lose their influence; they get a replacement).
   - The original action/block proceeds as normal.
3. **If they do NOT have the card**: The challenge succeeds.
   - The bluffer loses 1 influence (flips one of their cards face-up — their choice which one).
   - The original action/block is cancelled.

**Assassination + Challenge timing**: If an Assassin action is challenged and the Assassin player was bluffing, they lose an influence AND get their 3 coins back (VERIFY coin return). If the Assassin was NOT bluffing (challenge fails), the challenger loses an influence AND the target still loses an influence to the assassination (potentially losing 2 influences if they challenged and lost, then got assassinated — VERIFY this sequence).

**Block + Challenge**: If someone blocks and the blocker is challenged:
- If the blocker had the card: challenger loses influence, block succeeds.
- If the blocker was bluffing: blocker loses influence, original action goes through.

### Losing Influence
- When you lose influence, choose one of your face-down cards and flip it face-up. It remains visible for the rest of the game.
- Once both your cards are face-up, you are eliminated from the game.
- You cannot take actions or challenge while eliminated, but face-up cards remain visible as public information.

## SCORING / WINNING
- The last player with at least one face-down influence card wins.
- There is no point scoring. The game is elimination-based.
- Games typically last 5-15 minutes, so multiple rounds are common.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Bluffing is the entire game**: You do not need to have a character to claim its action. The risk/reward of bluffing vs. the risk/reward of challenging drives all decisions.
- **Duke is the strongest opening**: Tax (3 coins) is highly efficient and hard to challenge early since there are 3 Duke cards in the deck.
- **10-coin threshold forces action**: Accumulating 10 coins means you MUST Coup. Plan your economy around this.
- **The Contessa is mostly a defensive bluff**: Claiming Contessa to block assassination is one of the most common bluffs in the game, since no one wants to challenge and potentially lose both players' influence.
- **Track revealed cards**: As cards flip face-up, you gain information. If 2 Dukes are visible, someone claiming Duke has only a 1/remaining-cards chance of being truthful.
- **Ambassador is powerful for information**: Exchanging lets you see cards in the deck and choose the best ones. It also protects against Captains.
- **Early challenges are risky**: With little information, challenging is a 50/50 gamble. Late-game challenges with visible cards are much more informed.
- **Assassination is efficient but expensive**: 3 coins for a forced influence loss is strong, but the Contessa block and the challenge risk make it uncertain.
- **Stealing is annoying and powerful**: Taking 2 coins from a rival AND denying them resources is a strong economic swing.
- **With 2 players**: The game becomes intensely psychological with perfect information tracking. Very different feel from 4-6 players.
- **Coup is unstoppable**: When in doubt, save for a Coup. It cannot be blocked or challenged.
