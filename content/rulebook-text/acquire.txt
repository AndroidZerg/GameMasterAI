# Game: Acquire
# Publisher: Avalon Hill
# Players: 2-6 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Acquire is a classic stock trading and hotel chain building game originally designed by Sid Sackson in 1964. Players place tiles on a grid to found, grow, and merge hotel chains while buying and selling stock to maximize their wealth. The player with the most money at the end of the game wins. It is considered one of the greatest board games ever designed and a cornerstone of economic strategy gaming.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 game board (9 rows x 12 columns = 108 spaces, labeled 1A through 12I)
- 108 tiles (one for each board space)
- 7 hotel chain markers (Tower, Luxor, American, Worldwide, Continental, Festival, Imperial — or similar names depending on edition)
- Stock certificates: 25 shares per hotel chain (175 total)
- Money in various denominations
- Information card showing stock prices and majority/minority bonuses
- Tile racks (one per player, holds 6 tiles)

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center of the table.
2. Sort stock certificates into 7 piles by hotel chain.
3. Give each player starting cash: $6,000.
4. Place all 108 tiles face down and mix them.
5. Each player draws 6 tiles to form their hand (placed on their tile rack, hidden from other players).
6. To determine starting player: each player draws one tile and places it on the board. The player who drew the tile closest to 1A goes first. (Alternatively, just choose a starting player.)

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn, perform these steps in order:

### 1. Place a Tile
- Select 1 tile from your rack and place it on the matching space on the board.
- This placement may trigger one of several outcomes:

**a) Isolated tile**: The tile is not adjacent (orthogonally) to any other tile. Nothing further happens from placement.

**b) Adjacent to isolated tile(s) only**: If the placed tile is adjacent to tile(s) that do not belong to any chain, nothing special happens — they are just adjacent tiles.

**c) Founding a chain**: If the placed tile connects 2 or more tiles (including itself) and NONE of them belong to a hotel chain, you FOUND a new hotel chain:
  - Choose any available (unfounded) hotel chain to place on those tiles.
  - Place the chain's markers on all connected tiles.
  - Receive 1 FREE stock certificate of the newly founded chain (the founder's share).
  - Note: Only 7 chains can exist at once. If all 7 are on the board, you cannot found a new one (and cannot place a tile that would require founding).

**d) Growing a chain**: If the placed tile is adjacent to tile(s) belonging to exactly 1 existing hotel chain, that chain grows — place the chain's marker on the new tile and any newly connected orphan tiles.

**e) Merging chains**: If the placed tile is adjacent to tiles belonging to 2 or more different hotel chains, a MERGER occurs (see Merger rules below).

### 2. Buy Stock
- After placing your tile, you may buy 0, 1, 2, or 3 stock certificates from any currently active hotel chains on the board.
- The price per share depends on the chain's size (number of tiles) and its tier:
  - **Budget tier** (e.g., Tower, Luxor): Cheapest — 2 tiles = $200, 3 tiles = $300, 4 tiles = $400, 5 tiles = $500, 6-10 = $600, 11-20 = $700, 21-30 = $800, 31-40 = $900, 41+ = $1,000
  - **Standard tier** (e.g., American, Worldwide, Festival): Mid-priced — same scale but shifted up by $100
  - **Premium tier** (e.g., Continental, Imperial): Most expensive — shifted up by $200
- You pay the bank for the shares.
- If a chain's stock is sold out (all 25 certificates purchased), no more can be bought.

### 3. Draw a Tile
- Draw 1 tile from the supply to refill your rack to 6 tiles.
- If you hold any tiles that are permanently unplayable (would cause an illegal merger of two safe chains), you may reveal them, discard them, and draw replacements.

### Merger Rules (Critical)
When a tile placement causes 2+ chains to be adjacent:
1. **Determine the acquiring chain**: The LARGER chain (more tiles) acquires the smaller. If tied, the active player chooses which acquires which.
2. **Safe chains cannot be acquired**: A chain with 11 or more tiles is "safe" and cannot be taken over. If two safe chains would merge, that tile CANNOT be placed. If one safe chain and one non-safe chain would merge, only the safe chain can be the acquirer.
3. **Pay majority/minority bonuses**: Before disposing of stock, the majority and minority shareholders of the ACQUIRED (defunct) chain receive bonuses:
   - **Majority shareholder** (most shares): Receives the first bonus amount from the price chart.
   - **Minority shareholder** (second-most shares): Receives the second bonus amount.
   - If tied for majority, add both bonuses and split evenly. If tied for minority, split the minority bonus.
   - Bonuses scale with chain size at the time of merger.
4. **Dispose of acquired chain's stock**: Starting with the active player and going clockwise, each player holding stock in the acquired chain must choose for EACH share:
   - **Sell**: Return shares to the supply and receive current market price per share.
   - **Trade**: Exchange 2 shares of the defunct chain for 1 share of the acquiring chain (if available).
   - **Hold**: Keep the shares (they are currently worthless but the chain may be re-founded later).
5. Remove the acquired chain's markers from the board; those tiles now belong to the acquiring chain.
6. If a tile causes 3+ chains to merge, resolve them one at a time (largest acquired last), with bonuses paid for each.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when one of these conditions is met:
- All chains on the board are "safe" (11+ tiles each), OR
- Any single chain reaches 41 or more tiles, OR
- The active player declares the game over (optional) if they believe conditions warrant it — typically when all active chains are safe.

**Final Scoring**:
1. Pay majority/minority bonuses for ALL chains currently on the board (as if each were being acquired).
2. Each player sells all remaining stock at current market prices.
3. Add up all cash. The player with the most total money wins.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Founding chains is very powerful because you get a free share. Look for opportunities to found chains near where you have tiles in your rack.
- Stock in large chains is expensive but earns the biggest bonuses at game end. Stock in small chains is cheap but risky (they may be merged before they grow).
- Always watch what stock other players are buying — this tells you which chains they want to grow and which they might merge.
- Mergers are the primary way to make big money. Being majority shareholder when a chain is acquired is extremely lucrative.
- Trading 2-for-1 during mergers can be very efficient if the acquiring chain is valuable.
- Safe chains (11+ tiles) are powerful investments because they cannot be taken over, guaranteeing end-game bonuses.
- Hold some cash in reserve to buy stock after mergers when new chains may be founded cheaply.
- Tile placement is as much about information as action — knowing which tiles are in your rack gives you an edge in predicting mergers.
- The 3-stock-per-turn purchase limit means you cannot corner a market in one turn. Plan several turns ahead.
