# Game: Telestrations (Party Pack)
# Publisher: USAopoly
# Players: 4-12 | Time: 30 min | Weight: 1.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Telestrations is a party game that combines the telephone game with drawing. Players simultaneously sketch a word, pass their sketchbook, guess what was drawn, pass again, draw that guess, and so on until the book returns to its owner. The hilarious misinterpretations that accumulate through the chain make this one of the best party games available.

## COMPONENTS
- 12 erasable sketchbooks (spiral-bound with multiple pages)
- 12 dry-erase markers
- 12 cleaning cloths
- 1 die (6-sided)
- 1 sand timer (60 seconds)
- 600 cards (with "This Side" and "That Side" words — easier and harder)

## SETUP
1. Each player takes one sketchbook, one marker, and one cleaning cloth.
2. Each player draws a card and places it in the front clear sleeve of their sketchbook.
3. One player rolls the die. The number rolled determines which word on the card each player will use (1-6). If players want easier words, use "This Side"; for harder words, flip to "That Side."
4. Each player writes or reveals their secret word on page 1 of their sketchbook (the GUESS page).
5. All players sit in a circle so books can be passed easily.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Telestrations is played simultaneously — everyone draws/guesses at the same time, then passes.

### Round Flow:
1. **SKETCH IT** (odd-numbered pages): Flip to the next blank SKETCH page. Look at the word or guess on the previous page. Draw a picture representing that word/phrase. You have 60 seconds (use the timer). No letters, numbers, or symbols allowed in drawings.

2. **PASS IT**: When time is up (or everyone is done), pass your sketchbook to the next player:
   - Even number of players: always pass LEFT.
   - Odd number of players: alternate — pass LEFT on sketch rounds, pass LEFT on guess rounds (everyone always passes the same direction).

3. **GUESS IT** (even-numbered pages): Flip to the next GUESS page. Look ONLY at the previous drawing (do not flip back further). Write your best guess of what the drawing represents.

4. **PASS IT** again and repeat: The next player will sketch that guess, and so on.

5. **THE REVEAL**: When your own sketchbook returns to you, the round is over. Each player takes turns flipping through their sketchbook page by page, revealing the hilarious chain of drawings and guesses to the group.

### Number of Pages Used:
- With 4-6 players: each book goes through that many pages (matching player count).
- With 7-12 players: each book goes through that many pages.
- The sketchbook has enough pages for up to 12 players.

## SCORING / WINNING
Telestrations can be played two ways:

### Friendly Mode (Recommended):
No scoring at all. Simply enjoy the reveals and laugh at the misinterpretations. After the reveal, vote on:
- **Favorite Sketch**: Best drawing in any book
- **Favorite Guess**: Funniest or most creative guess

### Competitive Mode:
After the reveal, each player scores their own book:
- 1 point for each player who guessed the original word correctly.
- 1 point if the final guess matches the original word.

Alternatively, on the "guess" pages, each guesser earns 1 point if their guess matches the original secret word or the immediately preceding guess (if it was correct).

Play 3 rounds. Highest total score wins.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **No letters or numbers in drawings.** You can only draw pictures.
- **Only look at the immediately preceding page** when guessing or sketching. Do not flip back to see earlier pages — this ruins the telephone effect.
- **The timer is optional** but recommended to keep the game moving. Some groups skip it for relaxed play.
- **Party Pack supports up to 12 players.** The standard edition supports 4-8. The Party Pack is the better version for larger groups.
- **Erase and reuse:** Sketchbooks are dry-erase. Wipe clean between rounds.
- **House rule suggestion:** For maximum laughs, play without scoring. The joy is in the reveal.
- **Word difficulty:** "This Side" words are common and concrete (e.g., "birthday cake"). "That Side" words are more abstract or challenging (e.g., "déjà vu").
- **The game plays best with 6+ players** — more passes means more room for hilarious drift.
