# Game: Elder Sign
# Publisher: Fantasy Flight Games
# Players: 1-8 | Time: 60-120 min | Weight: 2.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Elder Sign is a cooperative dice game set in the Lovecraftian Cthulhu Mythos where 1-8 investigators explore a museum at midnight, attempting to collect Elder Signs to seal away an awakening Ancient One before it destroys the world. Gameplay revolves around rolling custom dice to match symbols on adventure cards — a Yahtzee-like push-your-luck mechanism layered with thematic horror elements and strategic resource management.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Entrance reference card and Clock token
- 8 Investigator cards with matching Investigator markers
- 8 Ancient One cards (one is chosen as the game's boss)
- 6 custom green dice, 1 yellow die, 1 red die
- 48 Adventure cards (museum locations)
- 12 Other World Adventure cards
- Mythos cards (drawn at midnight to trigger bad events)
- Common Item cards, Unique Item cards, Spell cards, Ally cards
- Elder Sign tokens, Doom tokens
- Sanity tokens, Stamina tokens (health)
- Monster tokens, Gate tokens
- Clue tokens (help with re-rolls)

## SETUP
1. **Choose an Ancient One**: Select one of the 8 Ancient One cards and place it face-up. Each has a unique doom track (number of Doom tokens needed to awaken it), special abilities, and a devastating attack if it awakens. Recommended starter: Yig (easiest) or Azathoth (awakening = instant loss).
2. **Set the Clock** to midnight (12:00) on the clock card.
3. **Deal 6 Adventure cards** face-up — these represent locations in the museum currently available to explore.
4. **Each player chooses an Investigator** and takes their card plus starting items, Sanity, and Stamina as indicated. Each investigator has a unique ability.
5. **Shuffle and place** the Common Items, Unique Items, Spells, Allies, Mythos, and Other World decks in their designated areas.
6. **Place the green dice** in the common pool. The yellow and red dice are only available through special items/abilities.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns clockwise. On your turn:

### 1. Move to a Location (Adventure Card)
Choose one of the 6 face-up Adventure cards to attempt OR the Entrance (to heal/buy items). You cannot attempt an Adventure with a monster on it unless you first defeat the monster.

### 2. Resolve the Adventure (Dice Rolling)
Each Adventure card shows one or more **tasks** — rows of symbols that must be matched to complete the adventure.

**Rolling**:
- Roll all 6 green dice (plus yellow/red if you have items granting them).
- Compare the results to the task requirements on the Adventure card.

**Symbols on dice**:
- **Investigation (magnifying glass)**: 1, 2, or 3 investigation results
- **Peril (skull)**: Matches peril requirements
- **Terror (tentacle)**: Matches terror requirements
- **Lore (scroll)**: Matches lore requirements
- **Wildcard**: Some dice faces have wildcards

**Completing Tasks**:
- You must complete tasks one at a time. Lock dice that match a task onto that task to complete it.
- After locking dice to one task, roll the REMAINING dice to attempt the next task.
- You must complete ALL tasks on the card to succeed.

**If you cannot complete a task**:
- After each roll, if you cannot (or choose not to) assign dice to a task, you must **remove one die** from the pool (it is lost for this adventure).
- Then roll the remaining dice again.
- **Focus**: If you have a Clue token, you may spend it to save a die (add it back to your pool) before re-rolling instead of losing one.

**Failure**: If you run out of dice or cannot complete all tasks, you fail the adventure. Apply the **penalty** shown on the card (lose Sanity, Stamina, add Doom tokens, etc.).

**Success**: If you complete all tasks, claim the **rewards** shown on the card (Elder Signs, items, healing, Clue tokens, trophies). The Adventure card is replaced with a new one from the deck.

### 3. Special Items and Abilities
- **Spells**: Often let you add the yellow or red die to your pool (these have more powerful symbols). Spells are one-use and may have a cost.
- **Common Items**: One-use items that provide re-rolls, extra dice, or symbol locks.
- **Unique Items**: More powerful one-use effects.
- **Allies**: Ongoing assistants that provide passive bonuses.
- **Clue Tokens**: Spend to save a die from being removed on a failed roll (essential resource).

### 4. Advance the Clock
After your turn, advance the clock by 3 hours. When the clock reaches midnight (every 4 turns in a 4-player game):
- **Draw a Mythos card**: These trigger negative events — monsters appear, doom tokens are added, sanity is lost, etc.
- Monsters on Adventure cards must be defeated before the adventure can be attempted. Monsters have their own symbol requirements to defeat.

### 5. Midnight Effects
Some Adventure cards have "At Midnight" effects that trigger when the clock hits midnight, adding ongoing pressure.

## SCORING / WINNING
**Players Win** by collecting enough Elder Sign tokens to seal the Ancient One. The required number is printed on the Ancient One card (typically 8-14 Elder Signs depending on the Ancient One).

**Players Lose** if:
- The Ancient One's Doom Track fills up (enough Doom tokens are placed) — the Ancient One awakens. For some Ancient Ones (like Azathoth), awakening means instant loss. For others, a final battle occurs where players must defeat the Ancient One with dice rolls against extremely difficult requirements.
- All investigators are eliminated (reduced to 0 Sanity or 0 Stamina). A devoured investigator is replaced with a new one from the remaining pool, but if no investigators are left, the game is lost.

**Investigator Defeat**:
- If Sanity reaches 0: The investigator goes insane and is devoured. Choose a new investigator if available.
- If Stamina reaches 0: The investigator is knocked unconscious and is devoured. Choose a new investigator if available.
- Devoured investigators lose all items and trophies.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Elder Signs are the priority**: Always prioritize adventures that award Elder Sign tokens. Do not waste turns on low-reward adventures unless you need items or healing.
- **Clue tokens are extremely valuable**: They save dice from being lost, essentially giving you extra re-rolls. Hoard them for critical adventures.
- **The yellow die is powerful**: It has strong faces including a wildcard. Items and spells that grant the yellow/red die dramatically improve your odds.
- **Manage the Doom Track**: Some Mythos cards and failed adventures add Doom tokens. Keep an eye on how close the Ancient One is to awakening.
- **Heal at the Entrance**: The Entrance location lets you recover Sanity/Stamina and buy items from the shop. Do not let investigators get too low — being devoured is devastating.
- **Coordinate with other players**: Discuss which adventures to attempt. Send investigators with relevant abilities to matching adventures (e.g., a character with bonus lore dice should attempt lore-heavy adventures).
- **Monsters gate adventures**: Always clear monsters before they accumulate. Multiple monsters can lock down the best adventures.
- **The game gets harder over time**: As the deck thins, Mythos events get nastier, and the Doom track fills. Push for Elder Signs aggressively in the mid-game.
- **Ancient One difficulty varies greatly**: Yig is the easiest (fewer doom slots, weaker). Cthulhu, Hastur, and Azathoth are significantly harder.
- At higher player counts (5-8), the clock advances faster relative to each player's turns, creating more midnight events and time pressure. 3-4 players is often considered the sweet spot.
- The game plays well solo — control 2-4 investigators for the best experience.
