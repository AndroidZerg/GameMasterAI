# Game: Lords of Vegas
# Publisher: Mayfair Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 2.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Lords of Vegas is a casino-building game set on the Las Vegas Strip where players buy lots, build casinos, and gamble for control. Players earn points by owning and operating casinos in valuable colors, but casino ownership can shift through hostile takeovers (reorganization) and mergers. The game blends strategic area control with dice-driven gambling mechanics that create tense, dramatic moments at the table.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 game board (the Strip — a grid of numbered lots in different colored zones)
- Lot cards (deck — each corresponds to a specific lot on the board and a color/casino type)
- Casino tiles (colored tiles representing different casino types: silver, gold, purple, etc.)
- Player-colored dice (d6 in 4 colors — used to establish boss status in casinos)
- Money tokens (in various denominations: $1M, $5M, $10M, etc.)
- Player score markers (for the VP track)
- The Strip cards (special event cards shuffled into the lot deck)
- Boss markers
- Sprawl markers (optional expansion markers in some editions)
- Reference cards

## SETUP
1. Place the game board (the Strip) in the center.
2. Shuffle the lot card deck. This deck also contains The Strip scoring cards interspersed within it.
3. Each player receives starting money: $12M for 2 players, $9M for 3 players, $8M for 4 players (amounts may vary by edition — check rules).
4. Each player takes dice of their color and a score marker (placed at 0 on the VP track).
5. Deal 1 lot card to each player as their starting lot. Each player places one of their dice on the corresponding lot on the board (showing a random roll). This is their first property.
6. Determine starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On your turn, perform these steps in order:

### 1. Draw a Lot Card and Collect Income
- Draw the top card of the lot deck.
- The card shows a specific lot number and a COLOR.

**a) If it's a Lot Card:**
- The lot's COLOR determines which casino type pays out this turn.
- **All players who are the BOSS of a casino matching that color score VP.** Victory points scored = the size of the casino (number of tiles). The Boss scores all the VP — other players with dice in that casino score nothing.
- **All players who have ANY dice in casinos of that color receive income.** Each die in a matching casino earns its owner money (typically $1M per pip showing on their die — so a die showing 5 earns $5M). This means you can earn income from a casino you don't boss.
- Then the drawn lot is available — the active player may claim it (see Build/Actions below) or leave it unclaimed.

**b) If it's a Strip Card:**
- A Strip card triggers a major scoring event for a specific casino color.
- All casinos of that color score VP for their bosses (same as above, but may have bonus effects).
- Strip cards also serve as the game timer — when the final Strip card is drawn, the game ends.

### 2. Take Actions (in any order, as many as you can afford)
After resolving the drawn card, you may perform any number of these actions:

**a) Buy a Lot ($varies)**
- You may buy any UNCLAIMED lot on the board by paying its price (lots have printed prices, typically $3M-$6M depending on location).
- Place one of your dice on that lot, rolled to a random value.
- You now own that lot. Lots are the foundation of casinos — you must own lots to build.

**b) Build a Casino ($5M per tile typically)**
- On a lot you own that has no casino tile, you may BUILD a casino.
- Place a casino tile of the lot's printed color on that lot.
- Your die remains on the tile.
- If this casino tile is adjacent (orthogonally) to another casino tile OF THE SAME COLOR, they merge into ONE larger casino automatically. All dice from both casinos are now in the combined casino.
- **Boss determination**: The player whose die shows the HIGHEST value in a casino is the Boss. If tied, the player with more dice wins. If still tied, the player who was Boss before a merge retains it.
- Casino size = number of tiles. Larger casinos score more VP when their color is drawn.

**c) Sprawl ($1M)**
- Place a casino tile of any color on an empty lot you own (regardless of the lot's printed color). This costs only $1M but the tile's color must match the ADJACENT casino you want to expand.
- This lets you grow casinos across lots of "wrong" colors, at a discount.
- The lot's printed color no longer matters for income/scoring — only the casino tile color matters.

**d) Remodel ($$)**
- Change the color of ALL tiles in a casino you are the Boss of.
- Pay $5M per tile in the casino.
- All tiles flip to a new color of your choice.
- This is powerful: a large silver casino can become a large gold casino if you think gold will score more.

**e) Reorganize (Gamble!) ($free but risky)**
- Target a casino where you have at least one die but are NOT the Boss.
- ALL players with dice in that casino reroll ALL their dice simultaneously.
- After the reroll, whoever now has the highest die is the new Boss.
- This is a HOSTILE TAKEOVER attempt. You might seize control... or you might lose your position entirely.
- Reorganizing is the most dramatic and gambling-heavy mechanic in the game.

**f) Trade**
- You may trade lots, money, and/or dice positions with other players at any time during your action phase. All trades must be agreed upon by both parties. You cannot trade VP.

### Boss and Control
- Each casino has exactly one Boss — the player with the highest-valued die in that casino.
- Only the Boss scores VP when that casino's color pays out.
- All players with dice in a casino receive income (money) proportional to their dice values.
- Being the Boss of large casinos is the primary way to win.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when the last Strip card is drawn from the lot deck (the deck is designed so Strip cards appear at intervals and the final one ends the game).

**During the game:**
- VP is scored whenever a lot card or Strip card of a casino color is drawn. The Boss of each casino of that color scores VP equal to the casino's size.
- VP is tracked on the score track throughout the game.

**At game end:**
- The game ends immediately when the end-game Strip card is drawn and resolved.
- No additional scoring — your position on the VP track is your final score.
- The player with the most VP wins.
- **Tiebreaker**: Most money.

(Note: Some editions include a variant where end-game bonus VP is scored for largest casino or most money.)

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- Location matters enormously. Lots adjacent to your casinos let you expand cheaply and build large scoring engines.
- Being Boss is everything for VP. A large casino where you're not the Boss earns you money (income) but zero VP. Consider reorganizing to take control.
- Reorganization is the heart of the game's drama. The dice are random — you might grab a 6-tile casino, or you might lose everything. Risk assessment is key.
- Money is NOT victory points. Money is fuel for actions. Spend it aggressively on lots and building.
- Sprawling ($1M) is much cheaper than building ($5M) but requires owning an adjacent lot. Buy lots near your casinos.
- Remodeling can be devastating to opponents: switching your casino to a color that's about to score (or away from one that's been scored) is a power move.
- Watch the lot card deck composition. As the game progresses, certain colors have fewer remaining scoring opportunities. Invest in colors with more cards remaining.
- Merging casinos (by building adjacent same-color tiles) is powerful but can accidentally hand Boss status to an opponent with a high die.
- In 2-player games, there's less competition for lots but more direct confrontation. In 4-player, the board gets crowded quickly.
- Don't underestimate income from non-boss positions. Having a 6-pip die in someone else's casino earns you $6M every time that color pays — that's significant cash flow.
- The game has a strong "gambling" feel even in the strategic decisions. Embrace the chaos — that's what makes Lords of Vegas special.
- Strip cards are the clock. Pay attention to how many have been drawn to gauge how much game is left.
