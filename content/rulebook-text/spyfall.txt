# Game: Spyfall
# Publisher: Hobby World
# Players: 3-8 | Time: 15 min | Weight: 1.1/5

## OVERVIEW
Spyfall is a social deduction party game where all players except one know the secret location. The spy must figure out the location by listening to questions and answers, while the other players try to identify the spy through their questions — without giving away the location. It is a tense, hilarious game of deduction and bluffing.

## COMPONENTS
- 30 Location cards (each location has a set of role cards for each player)
- Location reference sheet (list of all 30 possible locations)
- Timer (8-minute sand timer or use a phone)
- Ziplock bags for each location set

## SETUP
1. Choose a dealer. The dealer selects one location packet at random without looking.
2. Shuffle that packet's cards (one card per player plus the Spy card).
3. Deal one card face-down to each player. One player will receive the Spy card (which shows "Spy" and no location). All other players receive cards showing the SAME location along with a specific role at that location.
4. Each player looks at their card secretly.
5. Make sure the location reference sheet is visible to all players — it lists all 30 possible locations.
6. Set the timer for 8 minutes.
7. The dealer starts the timer and asks the first question to any other player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
This game has no traditional turns in the board game sense — it's a real-time conversation game with a simple structure:

### Questioning
- The game starts with the dealer asking any player a question.
- After answering, that player then asks any other player a question (you cannot ask back the person who just asked you).
- This continues in a chain — each person who answers then asks someone else.
- Questions and answers should be about the location, but vague enough that the spy can't figure out where you are.

### Question Strategy for Non-Spy Players
- Ask questions that someone who knows the location could answer, but that don't reveal the location to the spy.
- Example (if the location is a Beach): "How did you get here?" A non-spy might answer "I drove." A spy might say something that doesn't fit.
- BAD question: "How's the sand?" — this reveals the location to the spy.
- GOOD question: "What's the weather like?" — someone at a beach would naturally answer "sunny and warm" but it's not a dead giveaway.

### Question Strategy for the Spy
- Listen carefully to what others say. Their answers contain clues about the location.
- Give vague, plausible answers that could fit many locations.
- Use the location reference sheet — try to narrow down which of the 30 locations it might be.
- Don't be too vague or too specific — both are suspicious.

### Accusations
- At ANY point during the game, a player may stop the timer and accuse another player of being the spy.
- A vote is taken — if ALL other players (not counting the accused) unanimously agree, the accused must reveal their card.
  - If the accused IS the spy: Non-spy players win! BUT the spy gets one last chance — if they can correctly guess the location, the SPY wins instead.
  - If the accused is NOT the spy: The spy wins immediately.
- If the vote is not unanimous, the timer resumes and the game continues. The accusing player cannot accuse again this round (some variants just continue normally).

### Spy Reveals
- At any point during the game, the spy may reveal themselves and guess the location.
  - If correct: The spy wins.
  - If incorrect: The non-spy players win.

### Timer Runs Out
- If the 8-minute timer expires with no successful accusation and the spy has not revealed, the spy wins.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game can be played as standalone rounds or as a series with point scoring:

**Point Scoring (optional, across multiple rounds):**
- Non-spy players each earn 1 point if they correctly identify the spy.
- Non-spy players each earn 1 bonus point if the spy fails to guess the location after being caught.
- The spy earns 2 points if not caught (timer runs out).
- The spy earns 4 points if they correctly guess the location (either voluntarily or after being caught).

Play multiple rounds (typically 1 round per player) and highest total points wins.

**Simple mode**: Just play for fun with no scoring. Each round is its own game.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Don't reveal the location in your questions** — the #1 beginner mistake. Be subtle.
- **The spy should ask questions too** — not asking questions is suspicious. Ask things that work for any location.
- **Watch body language** — the spy often hesitates, looks confused, or gives overly generic answers.
- **Use the reference sheet** — both the spy (to narrow down) and non-spy players (to understand what info is safe to share) should reference it.
- **Accusations require unanimity** — if even one player disagrees, the accusation fails. Build consensus before accusing.
- **The spy gets a last-chance guess** — even if caught, the spy can still win by naming the location. So don't reveal too much during the game.
- **Short, specific answers are safest** — long rambling answers either reveal too much (non-spy) or expose you as faking it (spy).
- **Roles on the cards are flavor** — your role (e.g., "Lifeguard" at the Beach) can help you answer in character, but you don't have to follow it strictly.
- **Great with 5-8 players** — the game works with 3-4 but is best with more people for richer deduction.
- **Play multiple rounds** — a single round is very short. The game shines when you play 5-8 rounds and track points.
