# Game: Shadows Over Camelot
# Publisher: Days of Wonder
# Players: 3-7 | Time: 60-80 min | Weight: 2.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Shadows Over Camelot is a cooperative/semi-cooperative game where players are Knights of the Round Table working together to defend Camelot from siege engines, defeat quests, and gather legendary swords. However, one player may secretly be a Traitor working to undermine the group from within. Players must complete quests across multiple locations while managing threats that escalate each turn.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board with Camelot (center) and surrounding quest locations (Grail, Lancelot/Dragon, Excalibur, Saxons, Picts, Black Knight, Tournament)
- 8 Knight figures and matching coats of arms (character cards with unique special powers)
- 12 Siege Engines (catapults)
- White sword cards (Fight cards: 1-5 values, Grail cards, Merlin cards, special white cards)
- Black sword cards (Standard, Special, Morgan, Vivien, etc.)
- 16 Swords of the Round Table (8 white, 8 black)
- Loyalty cards (1 Traitor, remaining are Loyal)
- Life point tokens (4 per knight)
- Relics: Holy Grail, Excalibur, Lancelot's Armor

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center of the table.
2. Place 12 Siege Engines near the board (not on it yet).
3. Shuffle Loyalty cards — 1 Traitor card plus enough Loyal cards for all players (with 3 players, you may optionally play without a Traitor). Deal 1 face-down to each player.
4. Each player chooses a Knight, takes the matching coat of arms card, figure, and 4 Life Points (on the die showing 4).
5. Place all Knight figures in Camelot.
6. Shuffle the white and black card decks separately.
7. Deal 5 white cards to each player (hand limit is 12).
8. Place the 16 swords near the Round Table slots on the board.
9. Set the black cards deck and white cards deck in their designated spaces.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
On each knight's turn, they MUST perform two steps in order:

### Step 1: Progression of Evil (MANDATORY — choose ONE)
- **Draw a Black Card**: Draw one card from the Black deck and resolve it immediately. Most add enemy forces to quest locations; Special Black cards have unique harmful effects. Morgan and Vivien target specific players.
- **Place a Siege Engine**: Add 1 Siege Engine to the space around Camelot.
- **Lose 1 Life Point**: Reduce your life by 1.

### Step 2: Heroic Action (MANDATORY — choose ONE)
- **Move**: Move your Knight to any quest location or back to Camelot. Moving costs nothing but uses your entire Heroic Action.
- **Perform a Quest Action**: Take an action specific to your current quest location:
  - **Holy Grail Quest**: Play 1 Grail card (numbered) to the Grail area. Need 7 Grail cards before 7 Despair (black) cards to win.
  - **Excalibur**: Play 1 Fight card to move Excalibur 1 space toward the Knights. Black cards move it toward the lake. If Excalibur reaches the Knights' side, win a white sword.
  - **Lancelot & the Dragon**: Play Fight cards to either side. Lancelot's side needs 5+ total to win a white sword and Lancelot's Armor.
  - **Black Knight**: Play pairs of same-value Fight cards. Need to play 5 Fight cards total before the Black Knight's side fills.
  - **Saxons/Picts**: Play numbered Fight cards to push back invaders. If 4 Saxon/Pict figures accumulate, it's a lost quest (black sword placed). Win by defeating them with Fight cards totaling required value.
  - **Tournament**: Play Fight cards; highest total at the end wins a white sword.
- **Play a Special White Card**: Play a Merlin card or other special card for its effect (healing, card draw, etc.).
- **Accuse a Knight of Treachery**: Name another Knight. That player reveals their Loyalty card. If they ARE the Traitor, a white sword is placed. If they are Loyal, a black sword is placed. This can only happen in Camelot.
- **Discard 3 cards to heal 1 Life Point** (only in Camelot).

### Quest Resolution
When a quest is completed (won or lost), place a White sword (won) or Black sword (lost) on the Round Table. Some won quests grant relics (Excalibur, Holy Grail, Lancelot's Armor) that provide ongoing bonuses.

### The Traitor
The Traitor tries to sabotage quests subtly — playing suboptimal cards, choosing harmful "Progression of Evil" options, not helping at critical moments. The Traitor may reveal themselves voluntarily at any time to trigger a powerful Traitor action (place siege engines, steal cards, etc.), but can no longer hide.

## SCORING / WINNING
**Loyal Knights Win if:**
- 12 swords are placed on the Round Table AND the majority (7+) are white swords.

**All Players Lose if (at any time):**
- 12 Siege Engines surround Camelot
- 7 or more of the 12 swords on the Round Table are black
- All Loyal Knights are dead (0 Life Points)

**Traitor Wins if the Loyal Knights lose.**

If the Traitor is undetected at game end, flip 2 white swords to black swords before final count.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- You MUST do both steps each turn — no skipping Progression of Evil.
- Hand limit is 12 white cards. Discard down at end of turn.
- Players may NOT show their cards to each other, but may discuss strategy in general terms.
- The Traitor cannot be accused unless the accusing knight is in Camelot.
- Each Knight has a unique special power (e.g., King Arthur can trade cards, Sir Percival looks at an extra black card). Use these powers wisely.
- Siege Engines are the most common loss condition — monitor them carefully.
- Special Black cards can force discards, add extra siege engines, or block quest progress.
- If a Knight dies, they place their hand face-up. Other Knights in Camelot can pick up their cards.
- Common mistake: Forgetting that the Traitor flips 2 swords at game end if undetected.
- Common mistake: Not accusing early enough — but accusing a Loyal Knight costs a sword.
- Relics are powerful: The Holy Grail lets you draw extra white cards, Excalibur adds fight value, Lancelot's Armor absorbs damage.
