## OVERVIEW
Memoir '44 is a light-to-medium tactical wargame depicting World War II battles using the Commands & Colors system. Two players (or teams) take command of Allied and Axis forces across historical battle scenarios, using command cards to order units in different sections of the battlefield. The game combines accessible rules with genuine tactical depth, using custom dice and modular terrain on a hex-based board.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 double-sided game board (beach/countryside, hex-based, divided into 3 sections: left flank, center, right flank)
- 144 Army miniatures:
  - Infantry (foot soldiers, most common)
  - Armor (tanks)
  - Artillery (cannons)
  - In multiple colors (olive for Allies, grey for Axis — VERIFY exact colors)
- Terrain tiles (hedgerows, forests, hills, towns/buildings, rivers, bunkers, barbed wire, sandbags, etc.)
- 60 Command cards (Section cards and Tactics cards)
- 9 Battle dice (custom 6-sided dice with symbols)
- Obstacle and special markers
- Medal tokens (objective/VP markers)
- Scenario booklet (17 historical scenarios in base game)
- Player reference cards/summary cards

## SETUP
1. Choose a scenario from the scenario booklet (or online resources — hundreds of fan-made scenarios exist).
2. Set up the board according to the scenario diagram:
   - Place terrain tiles on the hex grid as indicated.
   - Place unit figures on their designated hexes (scenarios specify exact placement for both sides).
   - Place medal tokens on objective hexes (if the scenario uses them).
3. The board is divided into 3 **sections** by two dotted lines: Left Flank, Center, Right Flank.
4. Each player receives the specified number of **Command cards** (varies by scenario, typically 4-6 cards for each side). The player with fewer cards usually goes first.
5. Place remaining Command cards as a draw deck.
6. Set the medal victory target for the scenario (typically 4-6 medals to win).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Turn Overview
On your turn:
1. **Play 1 Command Card** from your hand.
2. **Order Units** as specified by the card.
3. **Move** ordered units.
4. **Battle** with ordered units that are in range.
5. **Draw 1 Command Card** to replenish your hand.

### Step 1-2: Play a Command Card and Order Units
Command cards come in two types:

**Section Cards**: These order a specific number of units in a specific section of the board.
- Example: "Probe Left Flank" — order 2 units on the left flank.
- Example: "Attack Center" — order 3 units in the center section.
- Example: "General Advance" — order 1 unit in each section.

**Tactics Cards**: These provide special orders or abilities that break normal rules.
- Example: "Behind Enemy Lines" — order 1 infantry unit to move up to 3 hexes regardless of section.
- Example: "Barrage" — order all artillery units to bombard.
- Example: "Medics & Mechanics" — restore eliminated figures to units.

The card you play determines WHICH units you can activate this turn. Units not ordered cannot move or attack.

### Step 3: Movement
Move each ordered unit up to its movement allowance:
- **Infantry**: Move 1 hex. May move 1 AND battle, OR move 2 and NOT battle.
- **Armor (Tanks)**: Move up to 3 hexes. May move and battle.
- **Artillery**: Move 1 hex OR battle (not both). Artillery does NOT move and fire in the same turn.

**Terrain effects on movement:**
- Forests, Towns, Hedgerows: Unit must stop when entering (cannot continue moving).
- Hills: No movement penalty (but provide defensive benefits).
- Rivers: Must stop upon crossing (cannot continue).

### Step 4: Battle (Combat)
Each ordered unit that is in range and eligible may attack ONE enemy unit:

**Range:**
- Infantry: 3 hexes
- Armor: 3 hexes
- Artillery: 6 hexes

**Number of Battle Dice:**
- Base dice depend on unit type and range (close range = more dice):
  - Infantry at 1 hex: 3 dice; 2 hexes: 2 dice; 3 hexes: 1 die
  - Armor at 1-3 hexes: 3 dice (same regardless of range)
  - Artillery at 1-2 hexes: 3 dice; 3 hexes: 2 dice; 4-5 hexes: 1 die; 6 hexes: 1 die (VERIFY exact artillery table)
- Terrain modifiers reduce dice (e.g., target in forest or town: -1 die; target behind hedgerow: -1 die; target in bunker: -2 dice).

**Battle Dice Results:**
Each custom die has these faces:
- **Infantry symbol**: Hit on Infantry
- **Armor symbol (tank)**: Hit on Armor
- **Grenade**: Hit on any unit type
- **Star**: Triggers special unit abilities (or miss in base game)
- **Flag**: Forces retreat
- **BLANK** (VERIFY if this face exists or if one of the above appears twice)

**Resolving Hits:**
- Each matching symbol rolled = 1 hit. Remove 1 figure from the target unit per hit.
- Infantry units have 4 figures, Armor has 3, Artillery has 2 (VERIFY exact figure counts).
- When all figures in a unit are eliminated, the attacking player claims a **medal** (victory point).

**Retreat (Flag results):**
- Each Flag rolled forces the target unit to retreat 1 hex toward its own board edge.
- If the unit cannot retreat (board edge, impassable terrain, etc.), it loses 1 figure per un-retreated flag instead.
- Retreating does not remove figures (unless blocked).
- Some terrain (e.g., towns, fortifications) allows the defender to **ignore 1 flag**.

### Overrun (Armor special rule)
If an Armor unit attacks an adjacent unit and eliminates it or forces it to retreat, the Armor unit may advance into the vacated hex and battle again. This makes tanks devastating in open terrain.

### Close Assault
Infantry attacking an adjacent Armor unit in close assault may score hits with Infantry symbols as well as Grenades (normally Infantry symbols wouldn't affect Armor — VERIFY this rule; in some C&C games, any symbol hits at close range).

## SCORING / WINNING
- Each time you eliminate an entire enemy unit, you claim a **medal** (VP token).
- Some scenarios have **objective medals**: controlling specific hexes (e.g., a town, a bridge) at any point during your turn earns a medal.
- The first player to reach the scenario's medal target (typically 4-6) wins.
- There is no point scoring beyond medals — it's first to the target.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Card management is everything**: You can only order units you have cards for. A powerful unit stuck without orders is useless. Don't overcommit to one section if you lack cards for it.
- **Section balance**: Having units spread across all three sections gives you flexibility to use any Section card you draw.
- **Infantry is versatile**: They're your most numerous unit. Use them to hold terrain and screen flanks.
- **Armor is aggressive**: Tanks hit hard with 3 dice at any range and can Overrun. Use them offensively.
- **Artillery is powerful but fragile**: 6-hex range is enormous but only 2 figures means they die quickly if engaged.
- **Terrain is critical**: Fighting from forests and towns (-1 to enemy dice + ignore 1 flag) is vastly better than being caught in the open.
- **Retreats can be as damaging as hits**: Forcing enemy units off objectives or off the board edge (where flags become casualties) is a legitimate strategy.
- **The scenario determines balance**: Some scenarios intentionally favor one side. Play both sides for a fair match.
- **Multiplayer/Overlord mode**: The 8-player Overlord variant has teams of players each controlling a section of a much larger battlefield, with one Commander-in-Chief distributing Command cards.
- **Don't forget to draw**: Always draw a card at the end of your turn. Hand management is constant.
- **Historical context enhances enjoyment**: Each scenario in the booklet includes historical background. The game is a nice entry point into WWII history.
