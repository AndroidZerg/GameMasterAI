# Game: boop.
# Publisher: Smirk & Laughter Games
# Players: 2 | Time: 20-30 min | Weight: 1.8/5

## OVERVIEW
boop. is a charming abstract strategy game for two players where you place cat pieces on a quilted bed (6x6 grid). Every time you place a cat, it "boops" (pushes) all adjacent pieces one space away from it. Your goal is to get three of your cats in a row — but kittens must first be graduated to adult cats, and only three adult cats in a row wins the game. The push mechanic creates a dynamic, ever-shifting board state.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 game board: a plush quilted bed-shaped board with a 6x6 grid of spaces
- 8 kittens per player (small wooden cat pieces — one set in orange, one in grey): 16 kittens total
- 8 cats per player (larger wooden cat pieces — matching colors): 16 cats total
- The bed/board has slightly recessed squares to hold pieces in place

## SETUP
1. Place the bed (game board) between the two players.
2. Each player takes all 8 kittens and all 8 cats of their color.
3. All pieces start OFF the board, in each player's personal supply.
4. Kittens are placed first. Cats (adults) start in your supply and must be earned by graduating kittens.
5. Choose a starting player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### On Your Turn
1. **Place one piece** from your supply onto any empty space on the 6x6 grid.
   - At the start of the game, you can only place **kittens** (since you haven't graduated any cats yet).
   - Later, you may choose to place either a kitten or a cat (adult) from your supply, if you have any available.

2. **Resolve the "boop"**: After placing your piece, it boops ALL pieces (yours AND your opponent's) that are orthogonally or diagonally adjacent to the piece you just placed:
   - Each adjacent piece is pushed **one space away** from the placed piece, in the direction directly away from it (straight line outward — orthogonal or diagonal).
   - **Key rules for booping**:
     - A piece that would be booped OFF the edge of the board is removed and returned to its owner's supply.
     - A piece that would be booped into an OCCUPIED space does NOT move (it stays where it is — pieces cannot be pushed through or onto other pieces).
     - **Kittens boop only other kittens**. A kitten does NOT boop adult cats — cats are too big to be pushed by kittens.
     - **Cats (adults) boop EVERYTHING** — they push both kittens and other cats.
   - Booping happens simultaneously for all affected adjacent pieces.

3. **Check for three-in-a-row**: After resolving all boops, check the entire board:
   - **Three kittens of YOUR color in a row** (orthogonal or diagonal): Remove those 3 kittens from the board and **graduate** them — place them in your supply as 3 **cat (adult)** pieces, available for future placement.
   - **Three cats (adults) of YOUR color in a row** (orthogonal or diagonal): **You win the game immediately!**
   - If multiple three-in-a-rows occur simultaneously, resolve all of them. If both a kitten line and a cat line form, the cat line wins.
   - Note: A line of 3 that includes both kittens and cats does NOT count. It must be 3 kittens (to graduate) or 3 cats (to win).

4. **End of turn**: Play passes to the other player.

### Graduating Kittens to Cats
- When 3 of your kittens form a line (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal), remove all 3 from the board and add 3 cat (adult) pieces to your supply.
- Graduated cats can be placed on future turns. They are more powerful because they boop everything (including the opponent's cats).
- You can have a mix of kittens and cats on the board at the same time.

### Running Out of Pieces
- If all 8 of your kittens are on the board (and none have been graduated), you MUST remove one of your kittens from the board (your choice) and place it as your turn's placement. (You always need a piece to place.)
- Same applies if all available pieces of a type are on the board.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Win condition**: Get **three of your CAT (adult) pieces in a row** — horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
- Kitten three-in-a-rows do NOT win the game; they only trigger graduation.
- If a player cannot prevent the opponent from forming 3 cats in a row, the game is over.
- There is an alternate win condition in some rules: if you graduate ALL of your kittens (meaning all 8 have been upgraded to cats — requiring at least three separate three-in-a-rows of kittens), you may win by default if the game state allows.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **The boop is both offensive and defensive**: You can use booping to push your own pieces INTO a line, or to break up your opponent's formations. Think about the consequences in all directions.
- **Edge and corner awareness**: Pieces booped off the board return to the owner's supply. This can be used offensively to remove opponent pieces, or it can backfire if your own pieces are near the edge.
- **Cats are much stronger than kittens**: They boop everything and can't be booped by kittens. Graduating kittens quickly gives you a major advantage.
- **Center control matters**: Pieces in the center of the board are harder to boop off the edge and have more adjacent squares for booping.
- **Plan multi-step sequences**: Think about where pieces will end up AFTER the boop. Sometimes placing a piece in a non-obvious location creates a three-in-a-row after the push effects resolve.
- **Watch for opponent graduation setups**: Don't let your opponent get three kittens in a row unchallenged — they'll graduate and gain powerful cat pieces.
- **Blocking with pieces**: Since pieces can't be booped through occupied spaces, you can use your own pieces as "walls" to protect key positions.
- **The board stays dynamic**: Because every placement causes booping, the board state changes significantly every turn. Static defensive positions are hard to maintain.
- **Kittens boop only kittens**: This is easy to forget. An adult cat sitting next to where you place a kitten will NOT be pushed. Use this asymmetry strategically.
- **Quick game, deep strategy**: Games are short (20-30 minutes), but the push mechanic creates surprisingly deep tactical puzzles. Replay value is high.
