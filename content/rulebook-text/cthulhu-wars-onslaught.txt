# Game: Cthulhu Wars
# Publisher: Petersen Games
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 90-120 min | Weight: 3.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Cthulhu Wars is an asymmetric strategy game where players control rival factions of Great Old Ones and their cultists vying for dominance over a doomed Earth. Each faction (Cthulhu, Black Goat, Crawling Chaos, Yellow Sign) has unique monsters, abilities, and spellbooks that play completely differently. The game combines area control, resource management, and combat with enormous highly-detailed miniatures and Lovecraftian horror theme.

## COMPONENTS
- Large game board (map of Earth divided into regions with gates)
- Faction-specific miniatures: Great Old Ones, monsters, cultists (each faction has unique sculpts)
- Spellbook cards (6 per faction, each with unique unlock conditions and powers)
- Power tokens (the universal currency)
- Gate markers
- Doom track and Ritual of Annihilation track
- Elder Sign tokens
- Desecration tokens (Yellow Sign)
- Faction cards/reference sheets
- First player marker

## SETUP
1. Place the board centrally. The map shows various regions (continents and oceans) with some containing starting gate positions.
2. Each player chooses a faction and takes all their miniatures, spellbook cards, and reference sheet.
3. **Starting placement** (varies by faction):
   - **Cthulhu** (Great Cthulhu): Starts in South Pacific with a gate and cultists.
   - **Black Goat** (Shub-Niggurath): Starts in Africa with a gate and cultists.
   - **Crawling Chaos** (Nyarlathotep): Starts in a chosen area with a gate and cultists.
   - **Yellow Sign** (Hastur): Starts in Europe with a gate and cultists.
4. Each player starts with Power equal to their starting cultists and gates (typically 8 Power).
5. Set the Doom track to 0 for all players.
6. Place Elder Signs face-down in a supply.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

The game is played over several rounds until someone reaches the Doom threshold and triggers the end.

### Round Structure:

#### 1. Gather Power
Each player simultaneously gains Power equal to:
- Number of cultists they control (1 Power per cultist)
- Number of gates they control (2 Power per gate)
- Any bonus Power from spellbooks or abilities

#### 2. Action Phase
Starting with the first player and proceeding clockwise, each player takes ONE action, then the next player takes one action, and so on. This continues cycling until all players have passed (usually when out of Power or choosing to stop).

**Actions (each costs Power):**

- **Recruit Cultist** (1 Power): Place a new cultist in a region you have a gate or units.
- **Summon Monster** (varies): Pay the monster's cost to place it in a region with your gate. Costs vary by monster type (e.g., Deep Ones cost 1, Shoggoths cost 2, Star Spawn cost 3, Hunting Horrors cost 2, etc.).
- **Awaken Great Old One** (varies, typically 4-10 Power): Pay the cost and meet faction-specific conditions to bring your GOO into play. Each GOO has unique requirements (Cthulhu requires a gate in an ocean area, Shub-Niggurath requires a certain number of cultists/monsters, etc.).
- **Open Gate** (3 Power): A cultist in a region without a gate can open one there.
- **Move** (1 Power): Move all your units from one region to an adjacent region (or some subset). Cultists can only move to adjacent regions; monsters may have flight or special movement.
- **Attack** (1 Power): Initiate combat against all enemy units in a region where you have units.
- **Capture** (1 Power): If you have a monster/GOO in a region with an unprotected enemy cultist, capture that cultist (they're eliminated and cost the enemy 1 Power to re-recruit).
- **Build Gate** variant actions depending on spellbooks.
- **Ritual of Annihilation** (5 Power): Advance your Doom track. The cost increases each time the Ritual track advances. This is the primary way to score Doom points. Each Ritual also earns an Elder Sign (hidden VP).

### Combat
When you attack:
1. The attacker and defender each roll dice equal to their total combat strength in the region. Each unit contributes: Cultists = 0 dice, most monsters = 1 die, Great Old Ones = varies (Cthulhu = 6 dice!).
2. Each die that rolls a 6 = Kill (eliminate one enemy unit). Each die that rolls a 4-5 = Pain (the enemy must retreat that unit to an adjacent region).
3. Both sides roll simultaneously — combat is a single round (no prolonged battles).
4. Eliminated cultists go back to the player's pool; eliminated monsters are gone until re-summoned.
5. Great Old Ones can't be killed — Kills against them count as Pains instead (they retreat). Exception: some spellbooks modify this.

### Spellbooks
Each faction has 6 spellbook cards that provide unique, powerful abilities. Each spellbook has an unlock condition printed on the faction sheet. Examples:
- "Awaken Cthulhu" — unlock after awakening your GOO.
- "Have 12 Power at the start of a Gather Power phase."
- "Lose a battle where your GOO is present."
Unlocking all 6 spellbooks is a strategic priority, as they dramatically increase your faction's power.

### Faction Highlights:
- **Cthulhu**: Raw combat power. Cthulhu himself rolls 6 dice. Star Spawn are powerful fighters. Devour ability eliminates units. Submerge lets Cthulhu disappear and resurface anywhere.
- **Black Goat (Shub-Niggurath)**: Spawns masses of cheap monsters (Dark Young, Fungi). Fertility abilities generate extra units. Avatar lets Shub-Niggurath appear with a swarm. Ghroth spellbook can devastate opponents.
- **Crawling Chaos (Nyarlathotep)**: Trickery and mobility. Flight lets Hunting Horrors and Nyarlathotep move anywhere. Madness and Emissary of the Outer Gods manipulate opponents. Harbinger forces pre-battle retreats.
- **Yellow Sign (Hastur)**: Subversion and desecration. Desecrate action places tokens that score Doom. The King in Yellow is a non-combat unit that spreads influence. Screaming Dead create cheap sacrificial units. Hastur himself can force enemy units to fight each other.

## SCORING / WINNING
- The game ends when the Ritual of Annihilation track reaches its final space OR a specific Doom threshold is reached.
- Each player's final score = **Doom track points + Elder Sign values** (each Elder Sign is worth 1-3 VP, revealed at end).
- Highest total wins.
- There's also an automatic "instant win" if you unlock all 6 spellbooks, have all your gates, and meet other conditions in some variants — but typically it's scored by total.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Power is everything**: Every action costs Power. More gates and cultists = more Power per round. Early expansion is critical.
- **Unlock spellbooks aggressively**: Your faction is dramatically weaker without spellbooks. Plan your early turns to meet unlock conditions.
- **Ritual of Annihilation timing**: Don't forget to perform Rituals — they're the main way to score. But they cost 5 Power, so balance scoring with board presence.
- **Combat is quick and decisive**: One roll resolves the entire battle. Don't commit to fights you can't win unless a spellbook requires it.
- **Cultists are vulnerable**: Unprotected cultists can be Captured for just 1 Power. Always keep a monster nearby to guard your gates and cultists.
- **Common mistake**: Forgetting that Great Old Ones can't be killed (only retreated). They're nearly impossible to permanently remove from the board.
- **Common mistake**: Not performing enough Rituals of Annihilation. Board control means nothing if you don't convert it to Doom points.
- **Elder Signs are hidden information**: You don't know how much they're worth until the end. This makes final scores unpredictable — don't assume you've won just because your Doom track is highest.
- **Each faction plays completely differently**: Don't apply one faction's strategy to another. Learn each faction's unique win condition path.
