# Game: Legendary: A Marvel Deck Building Game
# Publisher: Upper Deck
# Players: 1-5 | Time: 30-60 min | Weight: 2.5/5

## OVERVIEW
Legendary is a semi-cooperative deck-building game set in the Marvel Comics universe. Players recruit iconic heroes like Spider-Man, Wolverine, and Iron Man to build a powerful deck, then use those heroes to fight villains and ultimately defeat a fearsome Mastermind. While players cooperate to prevent the Mastermind's evil Scheme from succeeding, the player who contributes the most (by defeating the most villains) is the individual winner.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board (HQ row for heroes, Villain row/city spaces, Mastermind area, Scheme area)
- 500+ cards including:
  - Hero cards (14 hero sets in base game, ~60 cards each set with 4 different cards per hero at various costs)
  - Villain groups (multiple groups of villain cards)
  - Henchman groups (weaker villain groups)
  - Mastermind cards (4 in base game: Dr. Doom, Loki, Magneto, Red Skull)
  - Mastermind Tactic cards (4 per Mastermind)
  - Scheme cards (8 in base game)
  - Scheme Twist cards
  - Master Strike cards
  - Bystander cards (civilians to rescue)
  - S.H.I.E.L.D. Officer cards (always-available recruit option)
  - S.H.I.E.L.D. Agent and S.H.I.E.L.D. Trooper cards (starting deck cards)
  - Wound cards

## SETUP
1. Choose a **Mastermind** and place it in the Mastermind space. Shuffle its 4 Tactic cards and place them under the Mastermind card.
2. Choose a **Scheme** card and place it face-up. Read the Scheme's setup instructions — it dictates how many Scheme Twists, Villains, Henchmen, and other components go into the Villain Deck.
3. Build the **Villain Deck**: Combine the specified number of Villain groups, Henchmen groups, Scheme Twists (usually 8), Master Strikes (usually 5), Bystander cards (varies), and any Scheme-specific additions. Shuffle thoroughly.
4. Build the **Hero Deck**: Choose 5 hero sets (at standard player counts). Shuffle all their cards together. This is the Hero Deck. Draw 5 cards to fill the HQ row (face-up hero market).
5. Each player takes a **starting deck** of 8 S.H.I.E.L.D. Agents (1 Recruit each) and 4 S.H.I.E.L.D. Troopers (1 Attack each). Shuffle and draw 6 cards as your starting hand.
6. Place S.H.I.E.L.D. Officer cards in a separate stack (always available for purchase).
7. Place Wound cards in a stack nearby.
8. The city has 5 spaces (Sewers, Bank, Rooftops, Streets, Bridge) from right to left. Villains enter at the Sewers and push forward.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Turn Overview
On your turn, in this order:
1. **Play Hero Cards**: Play all the hero cards you want from your hand, generating **Recruit Points** (star icon) and **Attack Points** (claw/burst icon). Activate any special abilities printed on the cards.
2. **Recruit Heroes**: Spend Recruit Points to buy hero cards from the HQ row (5 face-up cards) or the S.H.I.E.L.D. Officer stack. Purchased cards go to your discard pile. Refill the HQ immediately when a card is taken.
3. **Fight Villains/Mastermind**: Spend Attack Points to defeat Villain cards in the city or attack the Mastermind.
   - Each Villain has a Fight value (attack needed to defeat it).
   - Defeated Villains go to your personal Victory Pile (face-down, scored at end).
   - When you defeat a Villain, resolve its "Fight" effect (if any — some reward you, some punish).
4. **Discard and Draw**: Discard all played cards AND remaining hand cards into your discard pile. Draw 6 new cards. (Your deck reshuffles when empty.)

### Villain Deck and the City
- At the **start of each player's turn** (before they play cards), draw 1 card from the Villain Deck:
  - **Villain card**: Place in the Sewers (rightmost city space). If there's already a villain there, push all villains one space to the left. If a villain is pushed off the Bridge (leftmost space), it **escapes** — resolve its Escape effect (often bad: KO heroes from HQ, each player discards, gain Wounds, etc.).
  - **Scheme Twist**: Resolve the Scheme's Twist effect immediately. These advance the Mastermind's evil plan.
  - **Master Strike**: The Mastermind attacks all players. Resolve the Mastermind's Master Strike ability (usually each player suffers a penalty — Wounds, discards, etc.).
  - **Bystander card**: A Bystander is captured by the Villain in the Sewers. If that Villain escapes, the Bystander is lost (bad for scoring and possibly triggering Scheme failure).

### The Mastermind
- The Mastermind has a high Attack value (typically 8-12).
- To fight the Mastermind, you need Attack Points equal to or exceeding its value.
- When you successfully attack the Mastermind, draw one of its face-down Tactic cards, resolve its "Fight" ability, and add it to your Victory Pile.
- **The Mastermind has 4 Tactics.** Defeating all 4 means the Mastermind is fully defeated and the players WIN.

### Wounds
- Wounds are cards that go into your deck and do nothing when drawn — they clog your deck.
- You gain Wounds from Master Strikes, Villain abilities, Scheme effects, or escaping Villains.
- Some hero abilities let you heal (remove) Wounds from your hand or discard pile.

### Hero Card Abilities
Cards may have:
- **Recruit/Attack values**: Added to your pool for the turn.
- **Superpower abilities**: Triggered if you play another card of the same hero class/color (e.g., "Strength: If you played another Strength hero, draw a card").
- Hero classes/colors include: Strength (green), Instinct (red), Covert (yellow), Tech (blue), Ranged (grey) — VERIFY exact classes.

## SCORING / WINNING
### Cooperative Victory
- Players WIN together if they defeat the Mastermind (all 4 Tactic cards are claimed).
- Players LOSE if the Scheme's failure condition is met (varies by Scheme — e.g., "If 12 Scheme Twists are resolved," "If X Villains escape," "If the Hero Deck runs out," etc.).

### Individual Winner
- After a cooperative victory, each player counts VP in their Victory Pile:
  - Defeated Villains: VP printed on card (typically 2-7 VP each)
  - Mastermind Tactics: VP printed on card (typically 5 VP each)
  - Rescued Bystanders: 1 VP each
  - Minus VP from Wounds still in your deck (VERIFY — some versions do not count Wound penalties)
- The player with the most VP is the individual winner.

### If Players Lose
- Everyone loses together. Some groups track scores anyway for bragging rights, but officially it's a shared loss.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Don't ignore the Villain row**: Letting villains escape triggers nasty Escape effects and may advance the Scheme toward failure. Clear them proactively.
- **Recruit wisely**: Buy heroes that synergize with your existing deck. Stacking one or two hero classes triggers more Superpower abilities.
- **Deck thinning is strong**: Removing Wounds and weak starting cards makes your deck more consistent. Some heroes offer this ability.
- **Coordinate Mastermind attacks**: Communicate about who has enough Attack to take on the Mastermind. Wasting a big turn when no one can finish a Tactic is costly.
- **Scheme Twists are the real threat**: Most game losses come from Scheme Twist effects accumulating. Understand your Scheme and plan around its failure condition.
- **S.H.I.E.L.D. Officers are versatile**: At 3 Recruit cost, they provide 2 Recruit. They are solid early purchases to boost economy.
- **Bystanders have dual importance**: They are worth 1 VP if rescued (by defeating the villain holding them), and their loss may trigger Scheme effects.
- **Master Strikes scale with Mastermind difficulty**: Harder Masterminds have more punishing Strikes. Be prepared.
- **Setup variety is the game's strength**: The combination of Mastermind + Scheme + Hero selection creates enormous replay variety. Experiment with different setups.
- **Solo mode**: Works well as a single player controlling 1 hand. Draw from Villain Deck as normal.
