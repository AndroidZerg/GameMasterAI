# Game: Ingenious (Einfach Genial)
# Publisher: KOSMOS
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 45 min | Weight: 1.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Ingenious is an abstract tile-laying game designed by Reiner Knizia where players place double-hex tiles onto a hexagonal board, scoring points in six different colors. The critical twist is that your final score equals your LOWEST-scoring color — so you must balance development across all six colors rather than maximizing any single one. This elegant scoring mechanism creates constant tension and strategic depth.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 hexagonal game board with a grid of hexagonal spaces (varies by player count — smaller for 2 players)
- 120 double-hex tiles (domino-shaped, each half showing one of 6 colored symbols: circle, star, cross, diamond, hexagon, triangle — in red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple)
- 4 tile racks (holds 6 tiles each, hidden from other players)
- 1 scoreboard with 6 color tracks (0-18 each)
- 4 sets of scoring markers (one per player, 6 markers each — one per color)
- Cloth bag for drawing tiles

## SETUP
1. Place the game board in the center of the table. For 2 players, use the marked smaller area. For 3-4 players, use the full board.
2. Place all 120 tiles face-down or into the cloth bag and mix them.
3. Each player takes a rack and draws 6 tiles, placing them on the rack hidden from other players.
4. Each player takes a set of 6 scoring markers and places them on the "0" space of each color track on the scoreboard.
5. Determine a starting player (youngest, random, etc.).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### On Your Turn
1. **Place one tile** from your rack onto the board:
   - The tile must be placed so both hexes fit onto empty spaces on the board.
   - Both hexes must be on valid board spaces (within the boundary).
   - Tiles can be placed in any orientation.
   - The first tile of the game can go anywhere on the board.

2. **Score the tile**:
   - For EACH hex of the tile you just placed, look outward in all directions from that hex (there are up to 5 directions from each hex, excluding the direction toward the other hex of the same tile — actually each hex has 6 neighbors, but you score in lines radiating outward).
   - For each hex, count lines of MATCHING colored symbols radiating outward in straight lines (up to 5 lines per hex, since one direction is blocked by the tile's other hex — see below).
   - Specifically: from each placed hex, look in each of the 5 outward directions (not toward the partner hex). Count consecutive symbols in a straight line that match the COLOR of the hex you placed. Each matching symbol = 1 point in that color.
   - Move your scoring marker up on the corresponding color track.
   - You score for BOTH hexes of your tile separately.
   - **Maximum 18**: No color can score above 18. If you would go above, stop at 18.

3. **"Ingenious!" bonus**: If any of your color scores reaches exactly 18 (the maximum), you immediately take another turn (place another tile and score it) before drawing. You can chain multiple Ingenious bonuses in one turn.

4. **Draw tiles**: Refill your rack back to 6 tiles from the bag. If the bag is empty, continue without drawing.

5. **Tile exchange (special rule)**: If at the START of your turn (before placing), ALL 6 of your tiles would score zero in your lowest color, you MAY exchange all 6 tiles: return them face-down, shuffle them in, and draw 6 new tiles. This is optional.

### Scoring Details
- Each hex of the placed tile scores independently.
- You look in straight lines radiating from the placed hex.
- Count consecutive matching-color symbols in each line. A gap (empty space or different color) stops the count in that direction.
- Example: You place a tile with a red hex. In one direction from that red hex, there are 3 consecutive red symbols already on the board. You score 3 points in red for that line. Check all other directions from that hex too. Then score the other hex of your tile separately.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Your score is your LOWEST color**. At the end of the game, look at all 6 of your color scores — the lowest one is your final score.
- The game ends when:
  - No player can place a tile legally (board is full or no valid placements), OR
  - A player achieves 18 in ALL 6 colors (instant win — "Perfect Ingenious").
- The player with the **highest lowest score** wins.
- **Tiebreaker**: If tied on lowest score, compare the second-lowest, then third-lowest, etc.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Always watch your lowest color**: It doesn't matter if you have 18 in five colors — if your sixth color is 3, your score is 3. Prioritize raising your weakest color.
- **Block opponents' weak colors**: If you notice an opponent is low in a color, place tiles to block scoring opportunities in that color near them.
- **The Ingenious bonus is powerful**: Reaching 18 gives you an extra turn. Try to set up chains where reaching 18 triggers bonus turns.
- **Plan your rack**: You have 6 tiles visible to you — plan several turns ahead. Don't waste tiles that could help your weakest color.
- **Tile exchange is rare but vital**: If you're stuck with tiles that can't help your lowest color, the exchange rule can save you.
- **Corner and edge positions** score in fewer directions, so center placements generally offer more scoring potential.
- **Early game**: Spread your scoring across colors. Don't tunnel on one color.
- **Late game**: Focus heavily on your weakest color. Every point there matters most.
- **2-player games** use a smaller board, making blocking more impactful and the game tighter.
- **This is a Reiner Knizia game**: The "score your lowest" mechanism appears in many of his designs (Tigris & Euphrates, etc.). Understanding this philosophy is key — balance is everything.
