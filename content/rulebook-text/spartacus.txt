# Game: Spartacus: A Game of Blood & Treachery
# Publisher: Gale Force Nine
# Players: 3-4 | Time: 120-180 min | Weight: 2.7/5

## OVERVIEW
Spartacus: A Game of Blood & Treachery puts players in the roles of scheming Roman dominus (masters of gladiatorial houses) vying for influence in the city of Capua. The game blends backroom dealing, treachery, and negotiation with exciting gladiatorial arena combat. Each round, players scheme with intrigue cards, bid on new slaves and gladiators at market, and then host bloody arena fights where fortunes are won and lost through combat and betting.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board with arena combat grid, market area, and influence track
- House boards (4 different dominus houses, each with unique abilities and starting assets)
- Gladiator cards (with combat stats: Attack, Defense, Speed, special abilities)
- Slave cards (provide gold income or special abilities)
- Intrigue cards (schemes, reactions, and guards)
- Market cards deck
- Combat dice (custom dice for attack and defense)
- Gold tokens
- Influence markers (track on main board, 1-12)
- Injury tokens (wounds, exhaustion)
- Favor tokens
- Betting tokens
- Guard tokens
- Arena hex grid for combat

## SETUP
1. Each player selects a dominus house and takes the corresponding house board, starting gladiators, slaves, and gold as indicated.
2. Place influence markers on the influence track at each house's starting position (varies by house, usually 1-4).
3. Shuffle the intrigue deck and deal 3 intrigue cards to each player.
4. Shuffle the market deck and place it near the board.
5. Place gold supply, injury tokens, and other components within reach.
6. Determine the first host of the arena (usually the player with the highest starting influence).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each round has 4 phases:

### Phase 1: Upkeep
- Each player collects income: gold from slaves, gladiators that generate income, and house abilities.
- Heal one injury from each gladiator (if wounded from previous combat).
- Draw intrigue cards (typically 3 per player, hand limit varies — usually 3 in hand at phase start, some houses modify this).
- Check for influence — certain conditions or card effects may adjust influence.

### Phase 2: Intrigue
This is the scheming, dealing, and backstabbing phase. Starting with the host and going clockwise, each player may:

- **Play Scheme cards**: These have effects like stealing gold, injuring opponents' gladiators, gaining influence, forcing discards, etc. Many can be countered by Reaction cards.
- **Play Guard cards**: Attach to your assets (gladiators/slaves) to protect them from schemes.
- **Trade and negotiate**: Players may freely trade gold, intrigue cards, gladiators, slaves, market cards, and even promises/deals. Deals are NOT binding — treachery is explicitly encouraged. You can promise anything and break your word.
- **Form alliances**: Temporary cooperation against a leading player is common. But betrayal is expected.

Players continue playing schemes and making deals until everyone passes.

### Phase 3: Market
- Reveal a number of market cards equal to the number of players + 1. Market cards include new gladiators, slaves, equipment, and sometimes events.
- **Open Auction**: Each revealed card is auctioned one at a time. The host sets the auction order (choosing which card to auction first). All players may bid with gold. Highest bidder wins. Ties broken by influence.
- Players may also sell assets during this phase (back to supply for reduced gold).

### Phase 4: Arena
This is the dramatic gladiatorial combat phase.

1. **The Host invites fighters**: The player currently hosting the games (the one with the most influence, or rotating) invites two OTHER players to each send a gladiator into the arena. The host may offer gold or other incentives to encourage participation. Players can refuse, but social pressure and offered gold usually ensure a fight.
2. **Betting**: All players (including combatants and the host) place secret bets on which gladiator they think will win, and how much gold they wager.
3. **Combat**: Gladiators fight on the hex grid.
   - Each round of combat: Both gladiators roll attack dice based on their Attack stat, and defense dice based on their Defense stat. Net hits (attack successes minus defense successes) become wounds.
   - **Speed** determines initiative (who strikes first if it matters) and movement on the grid.
   - Gladiators have special abilities that modify combat (e.g., extra dice in certain situations, re-rolls).
   - **Equipment**: Weapons and armor modify stats during combat.
   - Combat continues until one gladiator is wounded enough to be defeated (typically 3 wounds, or their health is depleted based on their stats).
4. **Defeat**: When a gladiator falls, the HOST decides: **Thumbs Up** (the gladiator lives, returns to owner injured) or **Thumbs Down** (the gladiator is killed and removed from the game permanently). The crowd's favor and betting may influence this.
5. **Betting Resolution**: Players who bet on the winner collect their wager amount as winnings (double their bet). Losers forfeit their wager.
6. **Influence**: The host gains 1 influence for hosting. The winner's owner may gain influence.

### Winning the Game
The game continues round after round until a player reaches **12 influence** on the influence track, immediately winning the game.

## SCORING / WINNING
- There is no traditional scoring — the game is a race to 12 influence.
- Influence is gained through: hosting arena games, winning fights, intrigue cards, and certain market cards/events.
- Influence can be LOST through: intrigue cards, certain events, and failed schemes.
- First player to reach 12 influence wins immediately.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Deals are NOT binding**: This is a core rule. You can promise to do something and then not do it. Only physical exchanges of cards/gold at the time of the deal are real.
- **Intrigue is the real game**: The arena is exciting but influence is mainly won and lost through schemes and political maneuvering. Never neglect your intrigue card play.
- **Guard your best gladiator**: A killed gladiator is gone forever. Guards and defensive intrigue cards protect valuable assets.
- **The host has significant power**: Choosing who fights and the thumbs up/down decision gives the host leverage. Use it for political gain.
- **Common mistake**: Not negotiating enough. This game rewards dealmaking, temporary alliances, and creative trades. If you play silently, you'll fall behind.
- **Common mistake**: Betting too conservatively. Winning bets are how you fund your schemes. Assess gladiator matchups carefully and bet aggressively when you have an edge.
- **Ganging up on the leader**: When someone reaches 9-10 influence, the table should cooperate to slow them down. The game is designed around kingmaker dynamics.
- **Slave economy**: Slaves generate reliable income every round. Having 3-4 good slaves funds everything else. Don't just buy gladiators.
- **Player count**: Base game supports 3-4 players. Expansions add 5th and 6th player. The game shines at 4 players with full negotiation dynamics.
