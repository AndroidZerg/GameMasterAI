# Game: Deal with the Devil
# Publisher: Czech Games Edition
# Players: 4 | Time: 120-150 min | Weight: 3.4/5

## OVERVIEW
Deal with the Devil is a hidden-identity Euro game set in a medieval city where one player is secretly the Devil, one is a Cultist allied with the Devil, and two are Mortals trying to prosper honestly. Players build structures and gain resources through a unique anonymous trading mechanism powered by an app, where the Devil tempts players by offering resources in exchange for pieces of their soul. The Inquisitor investigates to discover who has been dealing with the Devil.

## COMPONENTS
- Game board (medieval city with building slots, event track)
- Player screens and secret identity cards (Devil, Cultist, 2 Mortals)
- Resource tokens (wood, stone, gold, food, influence)
- Building tiles (various types with costs and VP)
- Soul piece tokens (each mortal starts with a complete soul)
- Companion app (REQUIRED — manages secret deals and trading)
- Production boards (behind screens)
- Chest boxes with compartments (used for anonymous resource exchange via the app)
- Event cards
- Trial/Inquisition components
- VP track
- Round marker

## SETUP
1. Download and launch the companion app on a phone/tablet.
2. The app secretly assigns identities: 1 Devil, 1 Cultist, 2 Mortals (in a 4-player game). Only you see your identity.
3. Each player takes a screen, production board, starting resources (as indicated by the app), and a chest box.
4. Mortals receive a complete soul (multiple soul pieces). The Devil and Cultist have different starting conditions as indicated by the app.
5. Set up the city board with available buildings for the current era.
6. Place event cards in order on the event track.
7. Set the round marker to Round 1.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game is played over 5 rounds, each with the following phases:

### Phase 1: Production
All players simultaneously produce resources based on their production board and any buildings they've constructed. Production is done behind screens.

### Phase 2: Secret Deals (App-Driven)
This is the core unique mechanic. The app orchestrates anonymous trades:
- The Devil player makes OFFERS through the app — tempting other players with resources they need in exchange for soul pieces.
- Players use their chest boxes to pass resources back and forth anonymously (following app instructions — put resources in specific compartments, pass the box, take from specific compartments).
- Mortals can accept or reject offers. Accepting gives them resources but costs soul pieces.
- The Cultist may also participate in deals to help disguise the Devil's activity.
- All trades are anonymous — you don't know who you're trading with.
- The app tracks who has made deals and who has soul pieces.

### Phase 3: Action Phase (Building & City Actions)
Players take turns performing actions:
- **Build**: Pay resource costs to construct buildings in the city. Buildings provide VP, ongoing production bonuses, or special abilities.
- **Gain Resources**: Take basic resource actions available on the board.
- **Influence Actions**: Spend influence for special effects or to gain advantages in trials.
- **Other Actions**: Varies by round — may include hiring workers, advancing on tracks, etc.

Building types include:
- **Residential**: Provide ongoing resource production.
- **Commercial**: Provide gold and trade advantages.
- **Religious**: Provide influence and protection from the Inquisitor.
- **Military**: Provide VP and defensive benefits.
- **Monuments**: High VP but expensive.

### Phase 4: Event
Resolve the current round's event card, which affects all players (resource costs, threats, bonuses, etc.).

### Phase 5: Inquisition / Trial
At certain points (typically rounds 3 and 5), the Inquisitor investigates:
- Players may be examined — the app reveals partial information about their soul status.
- Players with missing soul pieces appear suspicious.
- The group may vote to put a player on trial. If found guilty, they suffer penalties (lost VP, resources, etc.).
- The Devil wants to avoid detection. Mortals who dealt with the Devil will also appear suspicious.
- The Cultist tries to deflect suspicion from the Devil.

## SCORING / WINNING
At the end of 5 rounds, score VP from:
- **Buildings constructed**: Each building has a printed VP value.
- **Remaining resources**: Some convert to VP.
- **Soul status**: Mortals with intact souls gain bonus VP. Mortals with missing soul pieces lose VP.
- **Identity bonuses/penalties**:
  - **Mortals** win by having the most VP among Mortals. They benefit from keeping their soul intact and building efficiently.
  - **The Devil** scores VP differently — gains VP from collected soul pieces and from the Cultist's success. The Devil wants to collect as many soul pieces as possible while avoiding detection.
  - **The Cultist** scores a blend of normal VP and a bonus based on the Devil's success. They want to help the Devil while building up their own position.

The player with the most VP wins. Each identity type has a different scoring formula, creating asymmetric win conditions.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- The companion app is MANDATORY — it manages all secret dealing and identity information.
- The game is designed for EXACTLY 4 players. There is no variant for other player counts.
- The anonymous dealing phase is the heart of the game. As a Mortal, accepting deals gives you a short-term resource boost but long-term soul penalties.
- The Devil's offers are tempting by design — resist unless you absolutely need the resources.
- The Cultist knows who the Devil is but not vice versa (in most configurations).
- During trials, examine players' behavior: who has been building a lot without visible resource production? They may have been making deals.
- Common mistake: Mortals taking too many deals early, then getting caught by the Inquisitor and losing massive VP.
- Common mistake: The Devil being too aggressive with offers in early rounds, making it obvious who they are.
- Religious buildings provide protection during Inquisition — they're valuable for players who've made deals.
- The game has a strong social deduction element layered over Euro-game resource management — both aspects matter.
- Information is gradually revealed through the app and Inquisition — early rounds are more mysterious, later rounds involve more deduction.
