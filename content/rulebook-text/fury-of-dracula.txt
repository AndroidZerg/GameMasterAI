## OVERVIEW
Fury of Dracula is a one-versus-many hidden movement game set in Victorian Europe. One player controls Count Dracula as he secretly moves across the continent, laying traps and creating new vampires. The other players control four iconic hunters (from Bram Stoker's novel) who must deduce Dracula's location and destroy him before he plunges Europe into darkness. The game combines deduction, hand management, and thematic combat.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 game board (map of Europe with cities connected by roads, railways, and sea routes)
- Dracula figure and 4 Hunter figures (Lord Godalming, Dr. Seward, Van Helsing, Mina Harker)
- Dracula's trail cards (location cards played face-down to track Dracula's secret movement)
- 71 Location cards (for Dracula and Hunters)
- Dracula's Power cards (special abilities)
- Encounter cards (traps, ambushes, vampires that Dracula places)
- Event cards (split deck: Hunter events and Dracula events)
- Item cards (weapons and equipment for Hunters)
- Combat cards (separate decks for Dracula and Hunters)
- Influence track (Dracula's victory progress)
- Health/damage tokens
- Time track and day/night indicator
- Ticket tokens (for Hunter travel)
- Consecrated Ground tokens
- Rumor/Newspaper tokens

## SETUP
1. Place the board centrally.
2. The Dracula player takes all Dracula-specific components: trail cards, power cards, encounter cards, and combat cards.
3. Dracula secretly chooses a starting city (cannot be a sea zone) and records it (does NOT place a trail card yet — VERIFY).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Round Structure
Each round represents one day/night cycle and consists of:
1. **Hunter Phase** (all Hunters take turns)
2. **Dracula Phase**
3. **Time Advancement**

### Hunter Phase
Each Hunter, in turn order, performs these steps:
1. **Day Action / Night Action**: The available actions differ slightly based on whether it is currently Day or Night.
2. **Movement**: Move to an adjacent city via road (free), railway (costs a ticket or requires a die roll — VERIFY), or sea route (risky — Hunters at sea cannot do much and may take damage).
3. **Search**: If in a city, you may search for Dracula's trail. If Dracula has been in this city (it's in his trail), the corresponding trail card is revealed.
4. **Resupply**: Draw Event cards or Item cards (depending on city type — large cities give better options).
5. **Trade**: If sharing a city with another Hunter, you may trade items.
6. **Special Actions**: Use item abilities, rest to heal, or take unique Hunter actions.

**Day vs. Night**:
- During Day, Hunters are stronger — Dracula fights at reduced power.
- During Night, Dracula is at full power and certain Dracula abilities are enhanced.
- The time track alternates through Day and Night phases each round.

### Dracula Phase
1. **Movement**: Dracula secretly moves to an adjacent city (by road only — Dracula cannot use railways). He places a new Location card face-down on his trail (a row of up to 6 cards, the most recent on the left).
   - As a new card is added, the oldest card slides off the trail. If it had an Encounter on it, that Encounter matures (vampires may become permanent, etc.).
   - Dracula may also use **Power Cards** instead of normal movement (e.g., Wolf Form for double movement, Mist to escape, Dark Call to summon encounters, Hide to stay in the same city). Power cards go on the trail like location cards.
   - **Sea movement**: Dracula can move to sea zones but takes 1 damage each time he is at sea during the Dracula phase. He can stay at sea for multiple turns but it is costly.
2. **Place Encounters**: Dracula may place 1 Encounter card face-down on his current trail card (or a previous trail card — VERIFY). Encounters include:
   - Traps (damage Hunters who arrive)
   - New Vampires (score Influence if they mature off the trail)
   - Ambushes (force combat)
   - Various other hazards
3. **Mature Trail Cards**: When a card slides off the 6th position of the trail, it is revealed. If it has a mature Vampire encounter, Dracula gains Influence.

### Combat
When a Hunter enters Dracula's actual current location (or is caught by an ambush), combat occurs:
1. Each round of combat: both sides simultaneously choose a combat card from their hand.
2. Cards are revealed — some cards counter specific opposing cards.
3. Damage, bites, or effects are applied based on the matchup.
4. Combat continues until one side is defeated or escapes.
5. **Dracula's Bite**: If a Hunter is bitten, they gain a Bite card. Multiple bites are cumulative. If a Hunter has enough bites, they are eliminated (VERIFY threshold).
6. **Dracula's Health**: Dracula has a blood pool (15 HP, VERIFY). If reduced to 0, the Hunters win.

**Day vs. Night Combat**: Dracula has a weaker combat deck during the day and a stronger one at night. Hunters should prefer to fight during day.

### Influence Track (Dracula's Progress)
Dracula gains Influence by:
- Maturing Vampires (encounter cards that survive the full trail)
- Defeating or biting Hunters
- Advancing the time track (slow drip of influence over time)
- Certain event cards

If Influence reaches 13, Dracula wins.

### Event Cards
- Hunters draw from the Hunter Event deck — these provide items, information, or special abilities.
- Dracula draws from the Dracula Event deck — these provide powers, misdirection, or combat advantages.
- Some events are "Ally" cards that provide ongoing benefits.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **Dracula wins**: If the Influence track reaches 13.
- **Hunters win**: If they reduce Dracula's blood (HP) to 0 through combat.
- There is no point scoring — the game is a binary win/loss between the two sides.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Deduction is the Hunters' primary weapon**: Track Dracula's trail reveals, known locations, and eliminated possibilities. Use logic to narrow down his location.
- **Communication is key for Hunters**: Share information freely. Coordinate movements to cover the map and cut off Dracula's escape routes.
- **Dracula should keep moving**: Staying in one area too long makes it easier for Hunters to corner you. Use the full map.
- **Sea travel is a double-edged sword**: Dracula takes damage at sea but can cross the Mediterranean to escape. Hunters at sea are vulnerable and limited.
- **Night vs. Day matters enormously in combat**: Hunters should strongly prefer to fight during Day. Dracula should try to force Night encounters.
- **Mature those Vampires**: As Dracula, getting Vampire encounters to mature off the trail is a primary victory path. Protect them with traps.
- **Consecrated Ground blocks Dracula**: Hunters can place Consecrated Ground tokens in cities, preventing Dracula from entering. Use these to block key intersections.
- **Dracula's Power cards are limited**: Wolf Form and Hide are powerful but go on the trail and can be deduced.
- **With 2 players**: One player controls all 4 Hunters. This works well but is more demanding.
- **The trail is your deduction tool**: When trail cards are revealed (either through search or maturation), reconstruct Dracula's path to predict his current location.
- **Dracula should bluff and misdirect**: Leave encounters on trail cards in unexpected locations. Make Hunters waste time searching the wrong area.
