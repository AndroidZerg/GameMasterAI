# Game: Mystic Vale
# Publisher: AEG (Alderac Entertainment Group)
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 45-60 min | Weight: 2.2/5

## OVERVIEW
Mystic Vale is a deck-building game that pioneered the "Card Crafting System" — instead of adding new cards to your deck, you slide transparent plastic advancement cards INTO clear card sleeves, upgrading existing cards with new abilities. Each base card has three slots, and over the course of the game you layer advancements onto them to create increasingly powerful custom cards. A push-your-luck mechanism drives the core gameplay: reveal cards until you choose to stop or bust.

## COMPONENTS
- 20 base cards per player (in clear card sleeves — these have 3 blank zones: top, middle, bottom, with some starting with Cursed Land symbols (red decay) or Fertile Soil symbols (green growth))
- Starting advancement cards (pre-printed transparent overlays already inserted in some base cards)
- Advancement card supply (large pool of transparent plastic cards in 3 levels: Level 1, Level 2, Level 3)
- Vale cards (large cards providing ongoing powers and end-game victory points)
- Mana tokens (currency to buy advancements and vale cards)
- Victory Point tokens (green VP tokens in various denominations)
- Spirit tokens (blue tokens used for special abilities)
- Decay tokens (red — penalty markers)
- On-Deck area / Field area (player play zones)
- Advancement card display (shared market rows for Levels 1, 2, and 3)
- Vale card display (shared market of available vale cards)

## SETUP
1. Each player takes a deck of 20 base cards in clear sleeves. Of these:
   - 9 cards have a **Cursed Land** symbol (red decay, top slot) — these start with 1 decay icon each.
   - 3 cards have a **Fertile Soil** symbol (green, with 1 mana each).
   - 8 cards are completely blank.
2. Shuffle your 20-card personal deck.
3. Set up the Advancement display: create rows of face-up Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 advancement cards (typically 4 cards per level available at a time, refilled from their respective decks).
4. Set up the Vale card display: lay out a number of Vale cards (typically 4 face-up, drawn from the Vale deck).
5. Place Mana tokens, VP tokens, and Spirit tokens in a shared supply.
6. Each player begins the **Planting Phase** for the first turn (see below).

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE

### Phase 1: PLANTING (Push Your Luck)
This is the core mechanism of the game.

1. **On-Deck Area**: At the start of your turn, you have some cards already face-up in your field from the previous turn's prep. Flip cards from your deck into your **on-deck area** (a staging zone) one at a time.
2. **Count Decay symbols**: Each red decay symbol (Cursed Land and any added decay advancements) on your face-up cards in your field AND on-deck area counts toward your decay total.
3. **Spoil threshold**: If you have **3 or more decay symbols** showing across ALL your revealed cards, you **SPOIL** (bust):
   - You lose your turn. You cannot buy advancements or vale cards.
   - You gain 1 VP token as consolation.
   - Discard all revealed cards and prep for next turn.
4. **Choose to stop**: If you have NOT spoiled, you may choose to stop revealing cards. Push the on-deck cards into your field (they're now committed). The decay symbols on your field are "locked in" — if exactly 2 decay, you're safe but can't push further.
5. **Prep for next turn**: After your field is set, flip cards from your deck until you have a card with at least one decay symbol in your on-deck area — this is your starting point for next turn. (This "prep" step means you always start a turn with some risk already showing.)

**Key nuance**: You are tracking decay across ALL face-up cards. Cards already in your field from prep count. You're deciding whether to flip one more card or stop.

### Phase 2: HARVEST (Gain Resources)
If you did NOT spoil:
1. **Count mana symbols** on all your field cards. This is your mana for the turn.
2. **Count spirit symbols** on all your field cards. Gain that many spirit tokens.
3. **Activate any "on harvest" abilities** printed on advancement cards in your field.
4. **Gain VP tokens** from any VP-generating advancement cards in your field.

### Phase 3: BUYING (Spend Resources)
Use your mana to buy:

1. **Advancement Cards**: Pay the mana cost shown on an advancement card in the display. Take the transparent card and **slide it into one of your base cards' empty slots** (top, middle, or bottom). The advancement becomes a permanent part of that card for the rest of the game.
   - **Slot restrictions**: Each card has 3 slots. You cannot overlap — one advancement per slot. Choose wisely which card gets which advancement.
   - **Level 1 advancements** are cheap (1-3 mana) and provide basic benefits.
   - **Level 2 advancements** are mid-range (3-5 mana) and more powerful.
   - **Level 3 advancements** are expensive (5-8 mana) and very powerful.

2. **Vale Cards**: Pay the mana cost to buy a Vale card. Vale cards go into your personal tableau (NOT into your deck). They provide:
   - Ongoing abilities (triggered each turn)
   - End-game VP bonuses
   - Special powers

3. You may buy **multiple** advancements and/or vale cards per turn, as long as you can afford them.

### Phase 4: DISCARD AND PREP
1. Discard all field cards to your personal discard pile.
2. **Prep for next turn**: Flip cards from your deck to the on-deck area until you reveal a card with at least 1 decay symbol. This card (and any non-decay cards flipped before it) sit in your on-deck area, ready for your next Planting phase.
3. If your deck runs out, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck.

### Turn Summary
Planting (push your luck) -> Harvest (gain resources) -> Buy (spend mana on advancements/vales) -> Discard and Prep

## SCORING / WINNING

### Game End Trigger
The game ends when the **VP token pool runs out** (all VP tokens have been claimed from the supply). Finish the current round so all players have equal turns.

### Final Scoring
Add up:
1. **VP tokens** collected during the game.
2. **VP values on Vale cards** in your tableau.
3. **VP symbols on advancement cards** in your deck (some advancements have VP icons).
4. Any end-game scoring bonuses from Vale cards or special advancements.

**The player with the most VP wins.** Tiebreaker: most mana remaining, then most spirit tokens.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Card crafting is the core strategy**: Think carefully about which advancements go on which base cards. A card with a great top-slot advancement and a great bottom-slot advancement creates a powerhouse when you draw it.
- **Reduce decay early**: Advancements that remove or blank out decay symbols are extremely valuable. They reduce your spoil risk AND free up space for productive symbols.
- **Mana generation is essential**: You need mana to buy things. Prioritize mana-producing advancements early so you can afford better purchases later.
- **Push your luck intelligently**: Count your decay. If you have 2 showing, every additional card flip risks spoiling. Know the ratio of decay to non-decay cards in your remaining deck.
- **Vale cards provide endgame VP**: Don't ignore them. A well-chosen vale card can be worth 5+ VP.
- **Thin your risk**: As you add advancements that replace decay symbols with productive ones, your deck becomes safer to push through. Late-game turns should involve flipping many cards without spoiling.
- **Spirit tokens**: Some powerful abilities require spirit tokens. Stockpile them if your strategy depends on activated abilities.
- **Level 3 advancements are game-changers**: They're expensive but dramatically powerful. Building toward affording them is a solid strategy.
- **Watch the VP pool**: When it's getting low, the game is ending soon. Shift to maximizing immediate VP rather than long-term deck building.
- **Deck familiarity**: You always have exactly 20 cards. Over time, you'll know what's in your deck. Track which powerful cards have been played and which are coming.
- **Blank cards are opportunities**: Those 8 blank base cards are canvases for building exactly what you need. Don't waste slots on weak advancements.
