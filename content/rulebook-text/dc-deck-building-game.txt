## OVERVIEW
The DC Deck-Building Game is a competitive card game using the Cerberus engine where players take on the roles of iconic DC heroes (Batman, Superman, Wonder Woman, etc.), each with a unique special ability. Players purchase cards from a shared lineup to build a powerful deck, defeat Super-Villains for victory points, and aim to accumulate the most points by game's end. It blends accessible deck-building mechanics with DC Comics theme.

## COMPONENTS
- Over 200 cards total:
  - 7 oversized Super Hero cards (Batman, Superman, Wonder Woman, Aquaman, The Flash, Green Lantern, Cyborg)
  - 16 Super-Villain cards (including a starter villain)
  - Main deck cards (~130+) of various types: Heroes, Villains, Equipment, Super Powers, Locations
  - Kick cards (16 basic attack cards, always available to purchase)
  - Weakness cards (~20 negative point cards)
  - Punch cards (basic starting cards with 1 Power)
  - Vulnerability cards (starting cards worth 0 Power and negative VP)
- Rulebook

## SETUP
1. Each player randomly selects (or drafts) one oversized Super Hero card and places it face-up in front of them. Each hero has a unique ongoing ability.
2. Each player takes a starting deck of 7 Punch cards (1 Power each) and 3 Vulnerability cards (0 Power, worth -1 VP each), shuffles them, and places the deck face-down.
3. Separate the Super-Villain stack: Place the specific starter villain (e.g., "Ra's al Ghul" or similar) on top. Shuffle the remaining Super-Villains and stack them face-down. Flip the top one face-up — this is the current Super-Villain available to fight.
4. Shuffle the main deck. Deal 5 cards face-up in a row — this is the **Line-Up** (the shared card market).
5. Place the Kick card stack and Weakness card stack nearby (these are always available).
6. Each player draws 5 cards from their personal deck as their starting hand.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Players take turns clockwise. On your turn:

### 1. Play Cards
Play any number of cards from your hand face-up in front of you. Cards generate **Power** (the game's currency, used to buy/defeat cards) and may have special abilities:
- **+Power**: Most cards add Power to your total for this turn.
- **Card draw**: Some cards let you draw additional cards from your deck.
- **Attacks**: Some cards force opponents to gain Weakness cards or discard cards.
- **Defense**: Cards with "Defense" keyword can be revealed from hand to block attacks.
- **Special abilities**: Various effects — destroy cards, look at the Line-Up, gain extra buys, etc.

### 2. Buy/Defeat Cards
Spend your accumulated Power to acquire cards:
- **Buy from the Line-Up**: Pay the card's cost (printed on the card) using your Power total. The purchased card goes to your discard pile. Immediately refill the Line-Up slot from the main deck.
- **Buy a Kick card**: Costs 3 Power. Kick cards provide +2 Power when played. Always available regardless of Line-Up.
- **Defeat the Super-Villain**: If you have enough Power to meet or exceed the Super-Villain's cost, you may defeat them. The defeated villain goes to your discard pile (and is worth significant VP). Flip the next Super-Villain face-up. When a new Super-Villain is revealed, its "First Appearance — Attack" triggers, affecting all players.
- You may buy/defeat multiple cards per turn as long as you have enough Power.
- Unspent Power is lost at end of turn (no saving between turns).

### 3. Discard and Draw
Place ALL played cards and all remaining hand cards into your discard pile. Draw 5 new cards from your deck. If your deck is empty, shuffle your discard pile to form a new deck and continue drawing.

### Card Types
- **Hero cards**: Characters like Green Arrow, Hawkman — provide Power and abilities. Often synergize with specific card types.
- **Villain cards**: Antagonists you purchase (not the Super-Villains). They provide Power and often have attack effects.
- **Equipment cards**: Bat-Signal, Utility Belt, Power Ring — provide Power and special abilities. Batman's hero power specifically benefits from Equipment.
- **Super Power cards**: Heat Vision, Super Speed — provide Power and effects. Superman's hero power benefits from Super Powers.
- **Location cards**: Arkham Asylum, Fortress of Solitude — ongoing effects that stay in play. Most provide Power each turn.
- **Weakness cards**: Worth -1 VP. Can be destroyed (removed from the game) by certain card effects.

### Super Hero Abilities (Examples)
- **Batman**: Equipment cards you play cost 1 less Power to buy.
- **Superman**: +1 Power for each Super Power card you play.
- **Wonder Woman**: When you play/buy a Villain card, draw an extra card.
- **Aquaman**: Whenever a card lets you draw, draw an additional card the first time each turn.
- **The Flash**: When you buy or gain a card costing 5 or less, you may put it on top of your deck instead of discard pile.
- **Green Lantern**: The first time you play a Super Power each turn, +3 Power.
- **Cyborg**: The first time you play an Equipment or Super Power each turn, draw a card.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when:
- The last Super-Villain is defeated, OR
- The main deck runs out and you cannot refill the Line-Up.

Each player totals the Victory Points (VP) printed on all cards in their deck (including hand, discard pile — everything):
- Most cards have a VP value printed in the star icon (ranging from 0 to 5+).
- Super-Villains are typically worth 5 VP each.
- Weakness cards are worth -1 VP each.
- Starting Vulnerability cards are worth -1 VP each (Punch cards are worth 0).

The player with the highest VP total wins. Ties are broken by the player with the fewest cards in their deck.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Thin your deck**: Destroying Weakness and Vulnerability cards is extremely valuable. Cards that let you "destroy" a card remove it from the game entirely, improving your deck's consistency.
- **Match your hero ability**: Build around your Super Hero's strength. Batman should prioritize Equipment; Superman should grab Super Powers; Green Lantern wants at least one Super Power per hand.
- **Super-Villain First Appearance attacks** can be devastating. Be aware of what the next villain might do — sometimes it is worth defeating the current one quickly (or slowly) depending on the next in line.
- **Kick cards** are a reliable early purchase. 3 Power for +2 Power is efficient and gets your engine started.
- **Do not over-buy cheap cards**: A bloated deck with many low-value cards will draw poorly. Quality over quantity.
- **Locations stay in play**: Location cards provide their bonus every turn without being drawn. They are very powerful if acquired early.
- **Defense cards** must be in your hand when an attack happens. They are revealed (not played) to block.
- **Card destruction** abilities can target cards in hand, discard, or sometimes opponents' cards depending on the specific effect.
- At 2 players, the game is more strategic with less Line-Up competition. At 5 players, popular cards disappear quickly.
- Average game length is about 45 minutes once all players know the rules.
