# Game: Galaxy Trucker
# Publisher: Czech Games Edition
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 60 min | Weight: 2.2/5

## OVERVIEW
Galaxy Trucker is a chaotic two-phase game: first, players frantically build spaceships in real-time by grabbing and placing tiles; then, they fly their (usually poorly constructed) ships through space, encountering pirates, meteors, abandoned stations, and other hazards. The comedy comes from watching your hastily assembled ship fall apart. It's a game that rewards good construction but celebrates hilarious failure.

## COMPONENTS
- Ship boards (Class I, II, and III — progressively larger grids: 4x4, then larger)
- Component tiles (approximately 140+ face-down tiles):
  - Cabins (crew quarters — single, double)
  - Engines (single, double — provide thrust)
  - Cannons (single, double — front, side-facing for combat)
  - Shields (protect against specific directions)
  - Cargo holds (small, large — carry goods)
  - Batteries (power shields and double weapons)
  - Structural connectors (smooth, single, double, universal connectors on edges)
  - Special components (alien life support, etc.)
- Crew figures (human and alien astronauts)
- Cargo cubes (different colors = different values)
- Adventure cards (3 decks for rounds I, II, III)
- Credit tokens (victory points)
- Flight order track
- Timer (sand timer for building phase)

## SETUP
1. Place all component tiles **face-down** in the center of the table as a shared pool.
2. Each player takes the appropriate **ship board** for the current round:
   - Round 1: Class I ship (smallest grid)
   - Round 2: Class II ship (medium grid)
   - Round 3: Class III ship (largest grid)
3. Prepare the adventure deck for the current round by shuffling the appropriate adventure cards.
4. Place crew figures and cargo cubes in supply areas.
5. Determine starting flight order randomly.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each round has two distinct phases:

### PHASE 1: BUILDING (Real-Time)
This phase is simultaneous and frantic:

1. **Flip the sand timer** to start building.
2. All players simultaneously grab tiles from the face-down pool, **one at a time**:
   - Pick up a tile and look at it.
   - **Keep it:** Place it on your ship board, following connection rules.
   - **Put it back:** Return it face-UP (now visible to all players) and grab a different face-down tile.
3. **Connection rules are strict:**
   - Each tile edge has a connector type: **smooth** (no connection), **single connector** (one pipe), **double connector** (two pipes), or **universal connector** (fits any).
   - Adjacent tile edges MUST be compatible: single connects to single, double to double, universal to anything. Smooth edges can only touch other smooth edges (or board edge).
   - All tiles must be connected to the central starting block through a chain of valid connections.
   - Cannons and engines have directional arrows — cannons point outward (front or sides), engines point backward.
4. **While building, you may peek at adventure cards** for the upcoming flight. Flip cards from the adventure deck to see what's coming (meteors, pirates, planets, etc.). This costs building time but helps you prepare.
5. **When you're satisfied with your ship (or time is up)**, grab a numbered flight order token. Lower numbers go first in flight order (advantage: first pick at planets) but you stop building sooner.
6. **After all players stop or time expires**, building ends. You cannot modify your ship after this point.

### PHASE 2: FLIGHT (Adventure)
Reveal adventure cards one at a time. Each card is resolved for all players:

#### Adventure Card Types:
- **OPEN SPACE**: Nothing happens. Advance on the flight track.
- **PLANETS**: Stop to pick up cargo cubes (goods). Players in front (flight order) get first pick. Cargo goes into your cargo holds. Goods have different values (green < yellow < red < blue roughly).
- **METEOR SWARM**: Meteors hit your ship from specified directions (front, sides, or rear). For each meteor:
  - If you have a **shield** facing that direction (and battery power), block it.
  - If you have a **cannon** facing that direction, shoot it down.
  - Otherwise, the meteor **destroys the outermost exposed component** on that side of your ship. If a component is destroyed that was connecting other pieces, those pieces **fall off too** (chain destruction).
- **PIRATES / SMUGGLERS**: Combat encounter. Compare your **total cannon power** (all front-facing and relevant side-facing cannons) against the pirate's strength. If you match or exceed, you win (gain credits). If not, you lose cargo or crew or take damage.
- **COMBAT ZONE**: Similar to pirates, but based on crew count or specific conditions.
- **ABANDONED STATION**: Opportunity to gain cargo or crew.
- **SABOTAGE**: Lose components from your ship based on crew count or specific criteria.
- **STARDUST**: Beautiful but causes components on exposed edges to be knocked off.
- **EPIDEMIC**: Lose crew members.

#### Flight Order:
- Your position in the flight order track determines when you act on choice-based encounters (planets, stations).
- Faster ships (more engines) can advance in flight order during certain events.
- Being first is advantageous for picking cargo but makes you a bigger pirate target in some variants.

### End of Round:
After all adventure cards are resolved:
1. **Cargo scoring**: Sell cargo cubes for credits (prices vary by color).
2. **Flight order bonus**: Credits awarded based on final flight order position.
3. **Penalties**: Lose credits for each exposed connector (unfinished edges where a tile should be but was destroyed or never placed — you pay for shoddy construction).
4. **Lose additional credits** if your ship lost its cabin with crew (if you lost all crew, your ship is "lost" — heavy penalty).
5. Move to the next round with a bigger ship.

## SCORING / WINNING
After all 3 rounds, total credits:

- **Cargo credits**: From goods delivered across all rounds.
- **Flight bonuses**: From finishing positions.
- **Adventure rewards**: Credits from defeating pirates, etc.
- **Penalties**: Deduct for exposed connectors, lost crew, etc.

**The player with the most credits wins.** Tiebreaker: best finish position in round 3.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Connector compatibility is the #1 rule.** Double-check every tile edge. An illegal connection discovered during flight means that tile and everything beyond it falls off your ship immediately.
- **Engines should point BACKWARD.** They determine your speed. More engines = better flight position.
- **Cannons should point FORWARD and to SIDES.** Front-facing cannons fight pirates; side cannons help against side meteor swarms.
- **Don't neglect shields** — they protect against meteors without using ammunition.
- **Batteries power shields and double weapons.** Without batteries, double cannons and shields are just dead weight.
- **Crew is essential.** You need at least one crew member (in a cabin) for your ship to survive. Some adventures target crew. Have backup cabins.
- **Build from the center out.** Your starting block is the anchor — expand symmetrically for structural integrity.
- **Peeking at adventure cards during building is a powerful trade-off.** Knowing meteors come from the left means you should armor that side. But peeking costs precious building time.
- **It's okay to have an ugly ship.** Perfection is rarely achievable. Focus on structural integrity (no single-point-of-failure connections) and covering key threats.
- **Exposed connectors at game end cost you credits.** Try to cap open edges with smooth-sided tiles or plan your layout to minimize open connectors.
- **The game is designed to be funny.** Watching your ship disintegrate is part of the experience. Don't take it too seriously.
- **Class III ships are enormous** and nearly impossible to build well in the time limit. Embrace the chaos.
