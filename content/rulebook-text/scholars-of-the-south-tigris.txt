# Game: Scholars of the South Tigris
# Publisher: Garphill Games
# Players: 1-4 | Time: 60-90 min | Weight: 3.2/5

## OVERVIEW
Scholars of the South Tigris is set during the Abbasid Caliphate, circa 830 AD, in the House of Wisdom in Baghdad. Players take on the roles of scholars working to translate texts from Greek, Persian, and Indian sources into Arabic. The game combines worker placement with a unique deck-building mechanism featuring translucent card overlays, where cards physically change as you translate them by placing colored overlay cards on top.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 main game board (with worker placement action spaces)
- Player boards (1 per player, with tableau area)
- Translucent overlay cards (blue, gold, and clear — representing translated knowledge)
- Base cards (representing untranslated texts in your deck)
- Worker meeples (in player colors)
- Dice (used on certain action spaces)
- Influence markers (for 3 influence tracks: Astronomy, Literature, Law)
- Journal tokens
- Coins
- VP tokens
- Solo mode components (automa)

## SETUP
1. Place the main board centrally.
2. Each player takes their player board, starting workers, starting hand of base cards, and initial resources (coins, influence markers at starting positions).
3. Shuffle the card supplies and place them in their designated spots on the board.
4. Place worker spaces with appropriate icons visible.
5. Set out coins, journals, and other tokens in supply piles.
6. Each player draws their starting hand from their personal deck.
7. Determine first player.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
The game uses a worker-placement system where players take turns placing workers to perform actions. The game ends when a certain number of translations have been completed collectively.

### On Your Turn:
You perform **one main action** by placing a worker on an available action space on the board:

#### Key Action Spaces:
1. **ACQUIRE** — Take a new card from the display and add it to your deck/discard. Cards have various abilities and VP values. You may need to pay coins.

2. **TRANSLATE** — The signature mechanism. Take a translucent overlay card and physically place it on top of a base card in your tableau. This "translates" the text:
   - Cards have colored pip requirements showing which overlays are needed.
   - A fully translated card (all required overlays placed) scores VP and may grant bonuses.
   - Overlays are colored (blue, gold) and must match the required translation slots.

3. **RESEARCH** — Advance on one of the three influence tracks (Astronomy, Literature, Law). These tracks provide ongoing bonuses and end-game scoring. Reaching milestones on tracks triggers rewards.

4. **JOURNAL** — Collect journal tokens which provide VP and may unlock abilities.

5. **WORK** — Gain coins or other basic resources.

6. **HIRE** — Recruit additional workers, expanding your placement options in future turns.

### Deck-Building Element:
- Your personal deck of cards cycles through draw, play, and discard as in typical deck-builders.
- Cards in your hand can be played to your tableau (translation area) or used for their action effects.
- Translated cards in your tableau are permanent and score at game end.

### Worker Strength / Bumping:
- Workers have different levels of strength or specialization that affect which spaces they can use.
- Some spaces allow bumping — placing your worker returns the previous occupant's worker to their owner (who may gain a small benefit from the bump).

### Influence Tracks:
- Three tracks (Astronomy, Literature, Law) are advanced through the Research action.
- Each track has milestone bonuses at certain thresholds.
- End-game VP is awarded based on final positions.
- Tracks may also unlock abilities or discounts for other actions.

## SCORING / WINNING
The game ends when a set number of translations are completed (varies by player count).

**Final scoring includes:**
- VP from fully translated cards in your tableau (the primary source of points)
- VP from partially translated cards (reduced value)
- Influence track positions (milestones and final position bonuses)
- Journal tokens collected
- VP from special card abilities and bonuses
- Remaining resources may convert to small VP amounts

The player with the most VP wins. Tiebreaker: most translated cards.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **The translucent overlay mechanic is the heart of the game.** Plan your translations carefully — you need specific colored overlays, and they may be contested.
- **Deck management matters.** A lean deck cycles faster, getting your best cards back sooner. But acquiring powerful cards is also important.
- **Influence tracks are not optional.** They provide crucial bonuses and significant end-game points. Neglecting them is a losing strategy.
- **Balance short-term and long-term.** Acquiring cards and translating early sets up your engine, but the most valuable translations come later.
- **Watch your opponents' progress on translation counts** — the game end can sneak up on you.
- **Coins are tight.** Don't over-spend early. The Work action feels wasteful but is sometimes necessary.
- **In solo mode,** the automa opponent follows a decision card system to simulate competitive play.
- **Card synergies** between your acquired cards and translation targets are key to high scores.
- **The game shares a visual style and some mechanical DNA with the other South Tigris games** (Wayfarers, Architects) but plays as a standalone experience.
