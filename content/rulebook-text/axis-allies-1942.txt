# Game: Axis & Allies 1942
# Publisher: Avalon Hill
# Players: 2-5 | Time: 180-360 min | Weight: 3.3/5

## OVERVIEW
Axis & Allies 1942 is a grand strategy board game set in World War II where players control the major powers — the Allies (Soviet Union, United Kingdom, United States) and the Axis (Germany, Japan). Players manage economies, produce military units, and wage war across a map of the entire world. The game begins in the spring of 1942 and plays out the conflict through strategic maneuvering, resource management, and dice-based combat.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 large world map game board divided into territories and sea zones
- Hundreds of plastic unit sculpts in 5 colors (one per nation): infantry, artillery, tanks, fighters, bombers, battleships, aircraft carriers, destroyers, submarines, cruisers, transports, anti-aircraft guns, industrial complexes
- National control markers
- Industrial Production Certificate (IPC) paper money in various denominations
- Combat dice (standard 6-sided)
- National reference cards (one per power) showing unit costs, attack/defense values
- Setup charts listing starting unit placements
- Turn order card

## SETUP
1. Unfold the map on a large table (the board is very large).
2. Each player (or team) takes control of one or more nations. With 5 players, each controls one nation. With fewer, players double up (e.g., one player runs both Germany and Japan).
3. Following the setup chart, place all starting units for every nation on the board. This takes 15-30 minutes.
4. Distribute starting IPCs (money) to each nation as indicated.
5. The turn order is fixed: Soviet Union -> Germany -> United Kingdom -> Japan -> United States. This order repeats every round.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Each nation takes a full turn in the fixed order. A complete turn consists of these phases:

### 1. Purchase Units
Spend IPCs to buy new units from the price chart. Units are not placed yet — set them aside. You cannot spend more IPCs than you have. Key unit costs (approximate — verify):
- Infantry: 3 IPC (Attack 1, Defense 2, Move 1)
- Artillery: 4 IPC (Attack 2, Defense 2, Move 1; supports infantry giving +1 attack)
- Tank (Armor): 6 IPC (Attack 3, Defense 3, Move 2)
- Fighter: 10 IPC (Attack 3, Defense 4, Move 4)
- Bomber: 12 IPC (Attack 4, Defense 1, Move 6)
- Submarine: 6 IPC (Attack 2, Defense 1, Move 2; special abilities)
- Destroyer: 8 IPC (Attack 2, Defense 2, Move 2; negates sub abilities)
- Cruiser: 12 IPC (Attack 3, Defense 3, Move 2; shore bombardment)
- Aircraft Carrier: 14 IPC (Attack 1, Defense 2, Move 2; carries 2 fighters)
- Battleship: 20 IPC (Attack 4, Defense 4, Move 2; takes 2 hits to sink, shore bombardment)
- Transport: 7 IPC (Attack 0, Defense 0, Move 2; carries 1 infantry + 1 other land unit or 2 infantry)
- Anti-Aircraft Gun (AA Gun): 5 IPC (Defense only vs. aircraft; fires before combat)
- Industrial Complex: 15 IPC (placed in a territory to enable unit production there)

### 2. Combat Movement
Move units into enemy-occupied territories or sea zones to initiate combat. Key movement rules:
- Land units move 1 space (tanks move 2).
- Air units have extended range (fighters 4, bombers 6) but must have a valid landing zone at the end of the turn.
- Naval units move 1-2 spaces depending on type.
- Transports carry land units across sea zones (load, move, unload).
- Amphibious assaults: Land units carried by transports attack coastal territories.
- Units cannot move through enemy-controlled territories (except air units flying over).

### 3. Conduct Combat
Resolve all battles, one territory/sea zone at a time (attacker chooses order):
- **Combat rounds**: Each battle consists of rounds until one side is eliminated or the attacker retreats.
- **Attack roll**: Each attacking unit rolls 1 die. If the result is equal to or less than the unit's Attack value, it scores a hit.
- **Defense roll**: Each defending unit rolls 1 die against its Defense value.
- **Apply casualties**: Each side assigns hits to their own units (player chooses which units to lose). Casualties are removed simultaneously — units destroyed this round still get to fire.
- **Retreating**: After any combat round, the attacker may retreat all surviving units to an adjacent friendly territory/sea zone. Defenders never retreat.
- **Special combat abilities**:
  - AA Guns fire before combat at each attacking aircraft (1 die per plane, hits on 1).
  - Submarines get a first-strike ability (casualties from sub attacks are removed before they can fire back) UNLESS a destroyer is present.
  - Submarines can submerge instead of fighting.
  - Shore bombardment: Battleships and cruisers supporting an amphibious assault fire once before the land battle.
  - Strategic bombing: Bombers can attack enemy Industrial Complexes directly to destroy IPCs (the bomber rolls 1d6; that many IPCs are lost by the defender). AA guns at the complex fire at the bomber first.

### 4. Non-Combat Movement
Move units that did NOT participate in combat. This is for:
- Repositioning land units to reinforce.
- Landing fighters and bombers (they MUST land in friendly territory/carrier).
- Moving naval units that did not enter combat.
- Moving AA guns (they never attack; only move in non-combat).

### 5. Mobilize New Units
Place all units purchased in Phase 1 on the board:
- Land and air units are placed in territories with YOUR Industrial Complexes.
- Naval units are placed in sea zones adjacent to territories with your Industrial Complexes.
- Each Industrial Complex can produce units up to the IPC value of the territory it occupies (e.g., a territory worth 3 IPCs can produce 3 units per turn from its complex).

### 6. Collect Income
Count the IPC value of all territories you currently control and collect that amount from the bank.

## SCORING / WINNING
There are two ways to win:
- **Total Victory**: The Axis wins by controlling 14 or more victory cities (marked on the map) at the end of a complete round. The Allies win by controlling all Axis capitals (Berlin and Tokyo).
- **Standard Victory**: The Axis wins by controlling any 10 victory cities at the end of a round. The Allies win by controlling Berlin AND Tokyo at the end of a round.

Victory cities include: Washington, London, Leningrad, Moscow, Calcutta, Sydney, Los Angeles, Berlin, Paris, Rome, Shanghai, Manila, Tokyo, and others.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Economy is everything**: Control territory to earn IPCs, spend IPCs to build units, use units to control more territory. The economic engine drives the game.
- **Infantry are the most cost-efficient defenders** in the game (2 defense for 3 IPCs). Mass infantry is a proven strategy for holding territory.
- **Artillery pairs with infantry**: 1 artillery boosts 1 infantry's attack from 1 to 2 — always pair them.
- **Air power is expensive but flexible**: Fighters have excellent defense (4) and long range. Bombers enable strategic bombing raids to cripple enemy economies.
- **Germany typically pushes east** toward Moscow while defending against UK/US invasion from the west. Japan expands in the Pacific and Asia.
- **The US takes 2-3 turns to mobilize** due to distance from both fronts. The early game is about Soviet and UK survival.
- **Transports are essential but defenseless**: Always escort them with warships. Losing loaded transports is devastating.
- **Control of key chokepoints** (Gibraltar, Suez, the English Channel, sea zones around Japan) is critical for naval strategy.
- **Do not ignore the Pacific**: Japan left unchecked will dominate Asia and threaten to win on victory cities.
- Games typically run 3-6 hours. Agree on a time limit or victory condition before starting.
- With fewer than 5 players, one person often plays both Axis powers (Germany + Japan) while others split the Allies.
