# Game: Rhino Hero: Super Battle
# Publisher: HABA
# Players: 2-4 | Time: 10-20 min | Weight: 1.0/5

## OVERVIEW
Rhino Hero: Super Battle is a dexterity stacking game where players build a towering skyscraper out of cards and walls, then race their superhero figures to the top. It is the sequel to the original Rhino Hero, adding battling mechanics where heroes fight for position on the highest floor. The player whose hero is highest when the tower falls (or the cards run out) wins.

## COMPONENTS
- 1 Ground floor (large base card)
- 28 Floor cards (roof/platform cards showing wall placement guides)
- 28 Wall cards (folded cards that act as pillars)
- 4 Superhero figures (Rhino Hero, Giraffe Boy, Big E., Batguin) — each a different height/weight
- 3 Spider Monkey hanging tokens
- 1 Battle die (numbered for hero battles)
- 3 Floor medal tokens (1st, 2nd, 3rd floor markers)

## SETUP
1. Place the Ground Floor card in the center of the table as the foundation.
2. Shuffle all Floor cards and deal 3 face-down to each player as their hand.
3. Place remaining Floor cards as a face-down draw pile.
4. Place all Wall cards in a shared supply within reach of all players.
5. Each player chooses a Superhero figure and places it beside the board (not on the tower yet).
6. Set the Spider Monkey tokens and Battle die nearby.
7. Place Floor medal tokens near the tower.

## GAMEPLAY / TURN STRUCTURE
Play proceeds clockwise. On your turn:

### 1. Place Walls
- Look at the top Floor card currently on the tower (or the Ground Floor at the start). It shows lines indicating where walls must be placed — these lines show the required positions and how many walls (1, 2, or 3).
- Take the required number of Wall cards from the supply, fold them, and carefully stand them upright on the marked positions on the current top floor.

### 2. Place a Floor Card
- Choose 1 Floor card from your hand and carefully place it on top of the walls you just built, creating a new floor/platform.
- The floor card must rest on the walls — it becomes the new top of the tower.
- Floor cards have symbols that may trigger special effects (see below).

### 3. Move Your Hero (if applicable)
- If the Floor card you placed shows a **superhero symbol**, you MUST move your hero figure onto that new floor.
- Heroes can also be placed on any floor at or below the new top floor.
- Your hero starts off the tower and enters the first time a superhero symbol appears on a card you play.

### 4. Resolve Floor Card Effects
Floor cards may show special symbols:
- **Superhero symbol**: Move your hero to this floor (as above).
- **Spider Monkey**: Hang a Spider Monkey token on one of the walls on the current floor. This adds instability to the tower. If all 3 Spider Monkeys are already placed, move one from a lower floor.
- **Battle symbol**: If another player's hero is on the SAME floor as yours (or the floor immediately below), a battle occurs. Both players roll the Battle die — higher number wins. The loser must move their hero DOWN one floor. If tied, both stay.
- **Double turn arrow**: The next player must take two consecutive turns.

### 5. Draw a Floor Card
- Draw 1 Floor card from the draw pile to refill your hand to 3.
- If the draw pile is empty, play continues without drawing.

### Tower Collapse
- If at any point the tower (or part of it) collapses during your turn, you LOSE.
- The player whose hero is on the HIGHEST floor among the remaining players wins.
- If multiple heroes were on the same highest floor, they share the victory.

## SCORING / WINNING
- **If the tower collapses**: The player who caused the collapse loses. Among remaining players, the one whose hero is on the highest floor wins.
- **If the Floor card draw pile runs out AND a player successfully plays their last Floor card**: That player wins immediately.
- **Ties**: If heroes are on the same floor, they share the win.

## KEY RULES & TIPS
- **Walls must be placed exactly where shown** on the floor card — you cannot choose alternate positions.
- **One hand only** — you must place walls and floors using only one hand at a time (you may switch hands between pieces).
- **If you knock the tower**, even slightly, and pieces fall — you lose. Be very careful.
- **Spider Monkeys are mean** — they add real wobble. Place them on walls that are already stable if you can, to minimize your own risk.
- **Hero weight matters** — heavier heroes make the tower less stable when placed high. Consider timing.
- **Battles are luck-based** — don't over-invest in getting to the top early since you might get knocked down by a die roll.
- **Play low-risk Floor cards early** — save the cards with fewer wall requirements (more stable) for when the tower is tall and wobbly.
- **Table surface matters** — play on a flat, non-slippery surface for the best experience.
- **Great for kids and families** — the rules are very simple, and the dexterity challenge is the main event.
